# Is the Amex Platinum Card Worth It in Canada? 2026 Review

The American Express Platinum Card costs $799 per year in Canada. That's the first thing most people hear, and it's the last thing they investigate before walking away. This review does the opposite — we start with the fee and work backward, credit by credit, benefit by benefit, to answer the real question: does the math work for you?

Spoiler: for some Canadians, this card returns significantly more than $799 in documented, calculable value. For others, it's an expensive flex they'll never fully use. The difference comes down to your lifestyle, not your income.

---

## What You're Actually Paying For

**Annual fee:** $799 CAD  
**Supplementary card fee:** $250 per additional cardholder  
**Type:** Charge card (pay in full each month)

The Platinum is a charge card, not a credit card. There's no preset spending limit, but you're expected to pay the balance in full every statement. There's no interest rate to worry about if you do — but unlike a credit card, you can't carry a balance.

## The Credits: Where the Math Starts

This is where most reviews get lazy. They list the credits without telling you which ones you'll actually use. Let's be specific.

### $200 Annual Travel Credit

Amex gives you a $200 credit applied to your first $200 in eligible travel charges each calendar year. This covers flights, hotels, vacation packages, and car rentals booked through Amex Travel Online.

**Real-world usefulness:** High. If you take one trip per year — a flight to Vancouver, a hotel for a long weekend in Montréal, a ski trip in Whistler — you'll use this. The credit applies automatically; you don't need to redeem it manually.

**Annual value:** $200 CAD

### $200 Annual Dining Credit

This credit applies when you spend $200 or more in a single transaction at eligible Canadian restaurants or dining establishments on your Amex Platinum.

**Catch:** It's a single-transaction credit — you need to spend $200+ in one go to trigger it. A $180 dinner doesn't count. But a $220 meal out with friends or a catered event does.

**Annual value:** $200 CAD (if you have one qualifying dinner out per year, which isn't a stretch for someone paying $799 for a premium card)

### $100 NEXUS Statement Credit

NEXUS membership costs $50 USD (~$70 CAD) for a 5-year card, renewed every 4 years. Amex gives you a $100 statement credit toward a NEXUS application or renewal every four years.

**Is this worth it?** NEXUS is genuinely one of the best values in Canadian travel. It gets you expedited customs and immigration at 42 land and airport border crossings between Canada and the US, plus TSA PreCheck access at US airports. If you cross the border more than twice a year, it's worth having.

**Annual value (amortized over 4 years):** ~$25 CAD/year

## Lounge Access: The Big Ticket Item (With a 2027 Catch)

This is where the Platinum card separates itself from everything in the $120–$400 range.

### Through December 31, 2026: Unlimited Access

For all of 2026, Platinum cardholders get **unlimited access** to the Global Lounge Collection, which includes:

- **Centurion Lounges** — Amex's own premium lounges, available in major airports including YYZ (Toronto Pearson Terminal 1), and US hubs (New York JFK, LAX, SFO, Seattle, etc.). Food and drinks are complimentary. The JFK Centurion Lounge is genuinely excellent.
- **Priority Pass lounges** — 1,300+ airport lounges globally. This is the most widely-used lounge network in the world.
- **Escape Lounges** — Found at mid-tier US airports where Priority Pass lounges are scarce.
- **Plaza Premium Lounges** — Particularly relevant for Canadians; excellent lounges at YYZ, YVR, YYC, and YEG.
- **Delta Sky Clubs** (when flying Delta)
- **Lufthansa Business Lounges** (when flying Lufthansa)

Supplementary cardholders get their own access. This is meaningful if you're a couple — both partners get lounge access without having to share one card or pay a separate Priority Pass membership.

**What's a single lounge visit worth?** Day passes for Priority Pass lounges sell for $35–$50 USD (~$47–$68 CAD) at the door. Plaza Premium at Toronto Pearson is $49 CAD. Centurion Lounges don't sell day passes at all.

If you use airport lounges 10 times per year (not unusual for someone with a transatlantic flight, a connecting flight in the US, and a few domestic trips), that's $470–$680 in value at retail.

**Annual value (conservative estimate):** $400–$600+ CAD for frequent travelers

### Starting January 1, 2027: The Access Structure Changes

This is the most important thing to know when deciding whether to apply in 2026 vs. waiting.

Beginning in 2027, the lounge access structure shifts:
- **6 complimentary lounge visits per year** (base)
- Supplementary cardholders: 2 visits per year
- After the 6 visits, additional visits cost $35 USD each
- **Exception 1:** Centurion Lounges remain completely unlimited
- **Exception 2:** Escape Lounges remain completely unlimited
- **Way to restore unlimited:** Spend $20,000 CAD on eligible purchases in a calendar year, and you unlock unlimited access for the rest of that year plus all of the following year

**Bottom line:** If you're a road warrior who uses lounges 30+ times per year, the value calculation changes significantly in 2027 unless you hit the $20K spend threshold. If you primarily fly through Pearson (where Plaza Premium is excellent) and maybe a few US airports with Centurion Lounges, the impact is softer.

## Hotel Benefits

### Fine Hotels + Resorts

When you book a 2-night minimum stay at 1,600+ properties through the Fine Hotels + Resorts (FHR) program, you get:

- Room upgrade at check-in (subject to availability)
- **Complimentary breakfast for two** (daily)
- Guaranteed 4 PM late check-out
- **$100 USD hotel credit** per stay (for dining, spa, or resort charges)
- Noon early check-in (subject to availability)
- Welcome amenity (varies by property)

The **breakfast for two** is worth calling out specifically. At a boutique hotel in New York or a luxury resort in the Maldives, breakfast for two can run $80–$150 USD per day. On a 5-night stay, that's $400–$750 in included meals. The math on a single FHR booking can net you $500+ in tangible value.

**Best use case:** Honeymoons, anniversary trips, and international stays where you'd otherwise be spending heavily on meals anyway.

**Annual value:** Highly variable — anywhere from $0 (if you don't book luxury hotels) to $1,000+ per stay

### Hilton Honors Gold Status

Automatic enrollment in Hilton Honors Gold Status gets you:

- **80% bonus on base Hilton Honors points** earned on stays
- Room upgrade (when available)
- Space-available fifth-night free on award stays
- Complimentary bottled water

Hilton has over 7,000 properties across 18 brands, including Hampton, DoubleTree, and Curio Collection hotels you'd actually book for business travel. The Gold status is worth something if you stay at Hilton properties even a few times per year.

**Annual value:** Modest unless you're a Hilton loyalist; call it $50–$150 in upgrade and bonus point value

### The Hotel Collection

For stays that don't qualify for FHR (shorter or at non-FHR properties), The Hotel Collection provides:

- **$100 USD hotel credit** per 2-night stay
- Room upgrade (if available)
- Early check-in/late check-out

Essentially: more properties, fewer perks than FHR. Still useful for a 2-night business trip.

## Earning Membership Rewards Points

The Platinum earns in the following categories:

- **2X MR points** on eligible dining and food delivery in Canada (restaurants, cafés, DoorDash, Uber Eats, etc.)
- **2X MR points** on eligible travel purchases
- **1X MR point** on everything else

Amex Membership Rewards points are Canada's most flexible points currency. Transfer partners include:

- **Aeroplan** (Air Canada's frequent flyer program) — 1:1 transfer ratio. This is the cornerstone of Canadian travel hacking. 60,000 Aeroplan points can book a business class flight to Europe.
- **British Airways Avios** — 1:1 transfer. Valuable for short-haul redemptions on American Airlines through the BA website.
- **Marriott Bonvoy** — 2:3 transfer (you get 1.5 Marriott points per Amex point, which is actually a good deal given Marriott's earn/redemption rates).

**What's an MR point worth?** Conservatively, 1 cent CAD each. If you're transferring to Aeroplan for a business class redemption, closer to 2–3 cents. Most analyses land at 1.3–1.8 cents per point for above-average redemptions.

If you spend $3,000/month on your card across dining, travel, and everyday categories, you're earning roughly 36,000–40,000 MR points per year. At 1.5 cents per point: ~$540–$600 in points value annually.

## Other Benefits Worth Mentioning

### Premium Concierge Service

The Platinum Concierge is available 24/7 to handle restaurant reservations, event tickets, gift sourcing, travel bookings, and almost anything requiring time and connections. For people who actually use it — sourcing sold-out concert tickets, getting a reservation at a no-reservations restaurant, booking a specific villa in Europe — this service has real value. For most cardholders, it goes unused.

### Amex Offers

Rotating offers (usually 10–30 active at any time) give you statement credits or bonus points when you spend at specific merchants. Common offers include 10–20% back at specific hotel brands, bonus points at airlines, and credits at retailers you'd shop at anyway.

Actively using Amex Offers can add $200–$400 in annual savings for someone who pays attention to them. This requires effort — you need to add the offers to your card and remember to use them.

**Annual value (active user):** $200–$400 CAD

### Travel Insurance Package

The Amex Platinum includes:

- **Emergency medical insurance** while traveling (up to $5 million)
- **Trip cancellation and interruption insurance**
- **Flight delay insurance** (if your flight is delayed 4+ hours, you can claim up to $1,000 in meals and accommodations)
- **Baggage delay and loss insurance**
- **Hotel burglary insurance**
- **Car rental collision and damage waiver**
- **Purchase protection** and extended warranty

The car rental CDW alone saves the $30–$50/day rental companies charge for their own coverage. On one 7-day rental, that's $210–$350 saved.

## Who Should Get the Amex Platinum Canada?

### The card makes clear sense if you:

- **Travel internationally at least twice per year** — lounge access, travel insurance, and hotel credits all compound in value with international travel
- **Dine out at nicer restaurants regularly** — 2X MR on dining plus the $200 dining credit is a meaningful stack
- **Value airport lounge access** — even at 10 visits per year, you're getting $470+ in value at retail prices
- **Stay at upscale hotels on vacation** — one Fine Hotels + Resorts booking can deliver $500+ in breakfast, credits, and upgrades
- **Already use Amex Offers actively** — $200–$400 in additional annual savings for organized cardholders

### The card is overkill if you:

- **Fly economy domestically** once or twice a year and never stay at premium hotels
- **Don't fly through airports with Centurion, Plaza Premium, or Priority Pass lounges**
- **Can't use the $200 dining credit** in one transaction (you prefer casual dining or cooking at home)
- **Have no interest in Membership Rewards** — you'd be leaving the main value driver of the card on the table

## How Does It Compare?

**vs. Amex Cobalt ($155.88/year):** The Cobalt earns 5X on food and groceries, making it an exceptional everyday earner. But it has no lounge access, no travel insurance, no hotel benefits. The Platinum is a different product for a different user.

**vs. TD Aeroplan Visa Infinite Privilege ($599/year):** The TD card earns Aeroplan points directly and includes a $100 Nexus credit plus 6 regional airport lounge passes. The Platinum has richer hotel benefits and a better lounge program (unlimited vs. 6 passes). The TD card earns at a stronger rate for Aeroplan-loyal travelers.

**vs. RBC Avion Visa Infinite ($120/year):** No competition at this price point. The Avion is an on-ramp to the Avios/Aeroplan ecosystem; the Platinum is the destination.

## The 2026 Window Is Real

This is worth saying plainly: **the 2026 version of the Platinum is a better card than the 2027 version will be** for most cardholders, due to the lounge access changes. If you've been on the fence about applying, doing it now means you get one full calendar year of unlimited lounge access before the rules change.

After January 1, 2027, the card still makes sense — especially if you concentrate your stays in Centurion Lounges (which stay unlimited) or hit the $20K annual spend threshold. But the unlimited-everything version of the card has a finite window.

---

## Bottom Line

The Amex Platinum Card in Canada is worth it for travelers who use what it offers. If you take 2–4 trips per year, dine out occasionally, and have any interest in maximizing your credit card points, the $799 fee returns more than $1,000 in calculable annual value for most users — often significantly more.

It's not worth it for casual cardholders who'll treat it like a status symbol and ignore the credits. A $155 Cobalt card will outperform the Platinum every time for someone who doesn't travel.

The honest advice: model your own usage. Add up the credits you'll actually use, the lounge visits you'll realistically take, and the point earn rate on your typical spending. If the total lands above $799, apply. If it doesn't, there are better options for your lifestyle.

For Canadians who travel regularly and want a single premium card that covers medical insurance, lounge access, hotel upgrades, and points accumulation — the Platinum remains the benchmark.
