Best Credit Cards for Phone & Mobile Bills in Canada 2026

Most Canadians pay $50–$150/month in phone and internet bills. That's $600–$1,800/year — and the right card earns 2–5% back on every recurring payment. Set it and forget it.

How Phone Bills Are Coded on Credit Cards

Mobile phone bills from Rogers, Telus, Bell, Fido, Koodo, and most MVNOs (Public Mobile, Fizz, Lucky Mobile) code as MCC 4812 (Telephone Companies) or MCC 4814 (Telecommunications). Not all credit cards treat these the same as "recurring bills" — some cards have explicit recurring bill bonus categories, while others earn base rate only.

Key distinction: Cards that earn bonus on "recurring bills" or "subscriptions" may include telco MCC 4812/4814. Cards that bonus only "streaming" or "entertainment" typically DO NOT include phone bills — Netflix is MCC 7841/7999, Rogers wireless is MCC 4812. These are different codes. Always verify with your card's terms or call the bank.

Best Cards for Phone Bills

Frequently Asked Questions

Does Scotia Momentum VI earn 4% on ALL recurring bills or just specific ones?
The Scotia Momentum VI earns 4% on "eligible recurring bill payments" — defined as pre-authorized charges from the same merchant billed at the same interval. This includes phone, internet, cable, insurance, streaming subscriptions, gym memberships, and most utility services. The key requirement: the merchant must charge your card automatically (not you logging in and paying). One-time online bill payments typically do not qualify for the 4%.
Does BMO eclipse rise count phone bills as a bonus category?
The BMO eclipse rise's bonus categories are grocery, dining, gas, drugstores, and streaming/subscriptions. Phone bills from wireless carriers (MCC 4812) are classified as "telecommunications" — a separate MCC from streaming (Netflix, Spotify). Whether wireless bills qualify under the "streaming/subscriptions" bonus depends on your specific card agreement and the transaction coding. As of 2026, the eclipse rise's 5x category is confirmed for streaming services, not confirmed for all telecommunications billing. Check your transactions to verify.
Can I earn points on device purchases (new phone)?
Yes — buying a new phone at a carrier store earns credit card rewards at the rate applicable to the purchase location (electronics store, carrier store). However, phone device purchases typically code differently than monthly bill payments and may not qualify for recurring-bill bonus rates. You earn the category rate for where you buy the phone, not the recurring-bills rate.
