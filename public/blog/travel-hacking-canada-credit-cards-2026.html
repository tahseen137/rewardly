Travel Hacking Canada 2026 — Free Flights with Credit Card Points

Canadian travel hackers earn enough Aeroplan miles for a business class trip to Europe every 12–18 months — on normal spending, with the right card strategy. Here's how.

What Is Travel Hacking?

Travel hacking is the practice of systematically collecting airline miles and hotel points through credit card welcome bonuses and optimized everyday spending — then redeeming those points for premium travel at a fraction of the cash price. It's legal, widely practiced in Canada and the US, and the main tools are credit cards.

A business class flight from Toronto to London costs $3,000–$6,000 CAD in cash. It can be booked for 55,000–70,000 Aeroplan miles in Air Canada business class. At 1.5–2¢/mile value, that's $825–$1,400 in value from ~67,000 miles. A single Amex Cobalt welcome bonus (up to 30,000 MR pts, transferable to Aeroplan 1:1) covers half of this — before any spend.

The Foundation: Welcome Bonuses

Welcome bonuses (signup bonuses) are the largest single source of points for most Canadian travel hackers. They're far larger than ongoing earn rates — a 30,000-point welcome bonus equals what you'd earn spending $6,000 at a 5x card on dining, or 2+ years of 1x base earn at $1,500/month.

12-Month Canadian Travel Hacking Plan — Beginner

This plan is conservative and realistic for someone with a 700+ credit score, $2,500–$3,000/month spend, and who isn't applying for a mortgage in the next 24 months.

Month 0

Apply for Amex Cobalt

30,000 MR pts welcome bonus over 12 months (2,500 pts/month if $500+ spend). No income requirement. Earns 5x on food/dining. This is your core ongoing earn card.

Month 3

Apply for TD Aeroplan VI or CIBC Aeroplan VI

Wait 3 months after Cobalt application. Welcome bonus: up to 50,000 Aeroplan miles with $5,000 spend in 90 days. Put upcoming larger purchases on this card (appliances, phone upgrade, back-to-school). This is your direct Aeroplan earn card.

Month 6

Transfer Cobalt MR Points to Aeroplan

By month 6, you should have accumulated 15,000–25,000+ Cobalt MR pts from the welcome bonus + ongoing earn. Transfer to Aeroplan at 1:1 via the Amex Transfer portal. Now combine with your Aeroplan card miles: total Aeroplan balance = 65,000–75,000+ miles.

Month 9

Search Award Availability and Book

Use the Aeroplan search tool to find available award seats. Book Air Canada business class to London/Europe for 55,000–75,000 miles. Or: book two economy seats (partner airlines) for 12,500–25,000 miles each. Premium redemptions deliver 1.5–2.5¢/mile. Economy redemptions are fine for domestic Canada trips.

Month 12

Reassess — Cancel or Downgrade Underperforming Cards

Review each card. If the annual fee renewal isn't justified, call and ask for a fee waiver (see our fee waiver guide). If denied, downgrade to the no-fee version. Rinse and repeat with a new card's welcome bonus.

Credit Score Considerations for Travel Hackers

Each new credit card application causes a hard inquiry (typically -5 to -15 points temporary dip). Applying for 3–4 cards per year as a travel hacker will temporarily suppress your score. Key rules:

Don't apply for a mortgage or major loan within 12–18 months of card churning activity. Wait 3–6 months between new applications to allow score recovery. Keep your oldest cards open to maintain average account age. Pay every card in full, every month — a missed payment catastrophically undermines the entire strategy (both credit score and ability to get future cards).

Frequently Asked Questions

Is travel hacking allowed in Canada?
Yes — earning and using credit card points for travel is entirely legal and widely practiced. Banks offer welcome bonuses to attract customers; collecting those bonuses is exactly what they're designed for. What's not allowed is "manufactured spending" — using credit cards to purchase cash equivalents (gift cards, money orders) purely to hit spend thresholds. Stick to legitimate purchases and your strategy is completely above board.
How many credit cards should a Canadian travel hacker have?
Most Canadian travel hackers maintain 2–5 active cards at any time: 1 core everyday earner (Amex Cobalt), 1–2 Aeroplan or transferable points cards, 1 no-fee catch-all (Rogers Red or Tangerine), and optionally 1 new card being used to hit a welcome bonus spend requirement. Beyond 5 cards, the management complexity increases without proportional benefit for most people. The sweet spot is 2–3 cards used regularly.
