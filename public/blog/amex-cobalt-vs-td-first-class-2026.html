Earn rates compared

Where Amex Cobalt wins

  • Dining & food delivery: 5x MR — Restaurants, bars, cafes, Uber Eats, DoorDash, SkipTheDishes. TD First Class earns only 3x at restaurants.
  • Groceries: 5x MR — Every grocery store including Walmart Supercentre. TD earns 3x.
  • No minimum income — Anyone can apply regardless of income. TD requires $60K.

Where TD First Class wins

  • Expedia for TD: 8x TD Points — Book flights, hotels, and packages through Expedia for TD and earn 8 points per dollar. This is a significant multiplier for dedicated Expedia users.
  • Visa network — Accepted everywhere, including Costco and places that don't take Amex.
  • Gas & transit: 3x — Slightly better than Cobalt's 2x on travel-adjacent spending.
  • Lower annual fee — $139 vs $155.88 for Cobalt ($16.88 cheaper).

The key difference: TD Points cannot be transferred to any airline loyalty program. You're locked into Expedia for TD for travel, or accepting lower values for other redemptions. At best, TD Points are worth 0.5¢ each — meaning the 8x at Expedia for TD = 4% return. Cobalt's 5x at dining, transferred to Aeroplan at 2¢, = 10%.

Verdict on points: Amex MR points are 2–3x more valuable than TD Points when redeemed strategically. The Cobalt's higher earn rates and more valuable points make it better for most travel-reward seekers, despite the higher fee.

Who wins each scenario

Get Amex Cobalt if:

  • You eat at restaurants frequently
  • You order food delivery (Uber Eats, DoorDash)
  • You want Aeroplan points for flights
  • Your income is under $60K
  • You value flexibility across multiple loyalty programs
  • You shop at grocery stores regularly

Get TD First Class if:

  • You book travel frequently through Expedia
  • You already bank with TD
  • You need a Visa (e.g., to use at Costco)
  • You spend more on transit/gas than dining
  • You want the better medical insurance (21 days)
  • You don't transfer to airline programs

Overall winner: For most Canadians — especially those who prioritize flexible reward value — the Amex Cobalt wins. The Aeroplan transfer option and superior dining/grocery earn rate outweigh the $16.88 fee premium and limited Amex acceptance. The TD First Class is a better fit for existing TD customers who book heavily through Expedia.

FAQ

Can I transfer Amex Cobalt points to Aeroplan?

Yes — Amex MR points transfer to Aeroplan at 1:1. This is one of the most valuable redemptions available on any Canadian card. Business Class awards to the US using Aeroplan points regularly provide 3–5¢ per point in value, turning Cobalt's 5x dining into 15–25% effective return.

Is TD First Class Travel accepted at Costco?

Yes — it's a Visa card, and Costco Canada only accepts Mastercard and Visa. Amex Cobalt is not accepted in-store at Costco Canada.

Can I have both cards?

Yes. Some people get both: use Cobalt for dining and groceries (5x), and TD First Class for Expedia for TD bookings (8x) and anywhere Amex isn't accepted. The combined annual fee is ~$295/year, which makes sense if you're spending $20,000+ on both categories combined.

Does the Amex Cobalt earn 5x at Tim Hortons and Starbucks?

Yes — Tim Hortons and most Starbucks locations code as restaurants and earn 5x on the Amex Cobalt. The exception is Tim Hortons or Starbucks inside a grocery store, which may code as grocery (also 5x!) rather than restaurant.

Is there a TD equivalent to Aeroplan transfers?

No — TD Rewards Points cannot be transferred to any airline program. You're limited to Expedia for TD redemptions (0.5¢/point) or lower-value options. This is TD's biggest disadvantage versus Amex and is unlikely to change given TD and Aeroplan compete directly.
