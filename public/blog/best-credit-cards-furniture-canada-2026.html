How Furniture Stores Code in Canada

Furniture and home furnishing retailers typically code under one of these MCCs:

  • MCC 5719 — Miscellaneous Home Furnishing Specialty Stores: Most furniture-only retailers (EQ3, Structube, Article, BoConcept, West Elm, Restoration Hardware)
  • MCC 5021 — Commercial Furniture: Some office/commercial furniture sellers
  • MCC 5211 — Lumber & Building Material Dealers: RONA, Reno-Depot, some Home Depot furniture purchases
  • MCC 5999 — Miscellaneous and Specialty Retail: IKEA commonly codes under 5999 in Canada
  • MCC 5065 / 5999 (online): Wayfair typically codes as general merchandise or specialty retail
The bottom line on MCCs: None of MCC 5719, 5999, or 5021 are specifically bonused by any major Canadian credit card. There's no "furniture bonus" equivalent to 4% on gas (CIBC/Amex) or 5x on dining. Your strategy is to use the best flat-rate card available, or route purchases through Amazon.ca where possible to trigger an online/marketplace bonus.

Store-by-Store Breakdown

IKEA Canada

IKEA codes as MCC 5999 (miscellaneous retail) in Canada. No credit card bonuses this specifically. Best card: Rogers Red (2%) or BMO Eclipse Rise (5x / ~1.7% for travel). IKEA does not sell on Amazon.ca. For large IKEA purchases timed with a welcome bonus, use whichever card has the highest pending minimum spend. IKEA's own IKEA Visa (if available) earns IKEA rewards points but typically at low effective rates versus a general rewards card.

Wayfair Canada

Wayfair codes as specialty retail or general merchandise. No major Canadian credit card has an "online shopping" category that captures Wayfair — BMO Eclipse VI's 5x applies only to dining, grocery, gas, and transit. Rogers Red (2% flat) is the best card for Wayfair purchases. Wayfair frequently runs 15–20% off sales — stack with Rogers Red for guaranteed 2% on any size order.

HomeSense / HomeGoods (TJX)

HomeSense is owned by TJX Companies and codes as MCC 5331 (variety stores) or 5999. No bonus category. Rogers Red (2%) or BMO Eclipse Rise are the best options. HomeSense pricing is already discounted (off-price retail), so the rewards rate compounds the savings.

Article (article.com)

Article sells direct-to-consumer furniture online. Available on Amazon.ca as well as their own website. Both article.com direct and Amazon.ca purchases code as general retail — Rogers Red (2% flat) is the best option in both cases. No major Canadian card has an online shopping category that captures furniture retail.

Structube

Structube (Canadian furniture chain) codes as MCC 5719. No bonus category. Best approach: Rogers Red (2%) for in-store, or order any Structube products available on Amazon.ca for potential 5x. Structube does not have a store credit card.

EQ3, BoConcept, West Elm, Restoration Hardware (RH)

All high-end furniture retailers code as MCC 5719 or similar. No Canadian credit card bonuses these. Rogers Red (2%) or BMO Eclipse Rise (5x, ~1.7%) for ongoing earn. For very large purchases at RH or BoConcept ($3,000–$15,000+), welcome bonus spend is your biggest lever — time major furniture purchases within the first 3 months of a new card to satisfy minimum spend requirements.

The Amazon.ca Furniture Strategy

Amazon Canada carries an increasingly broad furniture selection — including brands like Zinus, Flash Furniture, Baxton Studio, and Novogratz — that compete with IKEA and Wayfair on price. Amazon.ca purchases code as online marketplace (general retail), which earns base rate on all major Canadian credit cards. No card has a dedicated "Amazon.ca" or "online shopping" bonus category, so the best earn rate on Amazon furniture is Rogers Red's 2% flat.

Price match first, then use Rogers Red: Check Wayfair.ca, Structube.ca, and Amazon.ca for the same or equivalent items. If Amazon.ca has the best price, use Rogers Red (2% flat) — it earns the same 2% on Amazon as anywhere else, and beats any points card at base rate. On a $2,500 sofa: $50 back with Rogers Red vs. roughly $25 with a typical 1% base-rate card.

Amex Offers for Furniture Retailers

American Express Canada periodically issues Amex Offers — targeted discounts and bonus point promotions — for furniture and home decor retailers including IKEA, West Elm, Pottery Barn, and Wayfair. These offers appear in your Amex app or online account and typically run for 2–4 months at a time.

Historical Amex Offers in the furniture space have included: $50 back on $300+ at IKEA, 10x MR points on West Elm, and similar promotions during Back to School and fall home refresh seasons. Stack an Amex Offer with your base earn rate for compounding return. If you're furnishing a space, check your Amex account first before shopping — a targeted offer can represent $50–$150+ in value on a large purchase.

0% Store Financing vs. Rewards Card — Which Wins?

IKEA, Leon's, The Brick, Ashley HomeStore, and other furniture retailers commonly offer 0% interest financing for 12–36 months on large purchases. Should you take the financing or use your rewards card?

Scenario 0% Financing Rewards Card (2%) Winner
$3,000 sofa, pay in full immediately $0 back $60 cashback Rewards card
$3,000 sofa, need 12 months to pay $0 cost (true 0% if paid before promo ends) $60 back but $3,000 invested = potential $90–150 return Financing (invest the cash)
$3,000 sofa, 24-month 0% then card rate applies $0 cost if paid off in 24 months $60 cashback; no interest if paid at statement date Either — depends on discipline
The hybrid move: Put the furniture purchase on a credit card with a strong welcome bonus (satisfying $3,000–$5,000 minimum spend), then pay the full statement balance before interest accrues. You get the welcome bonus value (often $200–$500) plus the per-dollar earn rate. Far superior to either 0% financing or base earn rate alone.
