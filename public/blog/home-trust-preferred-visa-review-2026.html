Most Canadians don't realize they're losing 2.5% on every foreign currency purchase they make. That Amazon.com order, that Airbnb booking in Euros, that hotel reservation in US dollars — all of it gets hit with a foreign transaction fee from every Canadian bank card except a small handful.

The Home Trust Preferred Visa is one of those exceptions, and it's the only card in Canada that offers both no annual fee AND no foreign transaction fee while earning cash back. For Canadians who shop online from US merchants, travel internationally, or subscribe to US-based services, this card quietly saves meaningful money on every transaction.

The No-FX-Fee Advantage — What It's Actually Worth

Standard Canadian card: $100 USD purchase → charged at spot rate + 2.5% fee → ~$138.50 CAD
Home Trust Preferred: $100 USD purchase → charged at spot rate (no fee) → ~$135.00 CAD + 1% cash back

Difference: $3.50 on every $100 USD spent. For someone spending $500 USD/month on US merchants, that's $210 saved per year.

The 2.5% FX fee is so standard in Canada that most Canadians simply accept it. But that fee compounds significantly for:

  • Online shoppers buying from US retailers (Amazon.com, Etsy USD listings, US software subscriptions)
  • Travelers spending in the US, Europe, or any non-CAD currency
  • Canadians with US Netflix, Spotify, or other USD-billed subscriptions
  • Remote workers invoiced or paid in USD who put business expenses on a card
  • Snowbirds spending 3–6 months in the US per year

The Home Trust card is the only no-fee card with a true 0% FX fee (no surcharge at all — you get the Visa exchange rate). Rogers Red is also no-fee but uses a different mechanic: they charge the 2.5% FX fee and give you 3% cash back on foreign spend, which nets to roughly a 0.5% gain. Cash Back Details

The Home Trust Preferred earns 1% cash back on all eligible purchases — both domestic (CAD) and foreign currency transactions. Because there is no FX fee, your effective return on foreign purchases is 1% on top of the raw exchange rate, with no surcharge eating into it.

Cash back is calculated monthly and applied as a statement credit. There is no minimum redemption threshold and no annual cap.

What the Card Lacks

The Home Trust Preferred Visa is deliberately lean. It is a pure "no-fee, no-FX, cash back" product without the extras that come with annual fee cards:

  • No travel medical insurance
  • No trip cancellation or interruption coverage
  • No car rental collision protection
  • No airport lounge access
  • No concierge service
  • No purchase protection beyond basic Visa zero liability

For travel abroad, you should always carry a separate travel insurance policy (or pair with a credit card that has emergency medical) to cover the gaps. The Home Trust card is a spending card, not a travel insurance card.

The Ideal Two-Card Wallet for International Travelers

For Canadians who travel or shop internationally, Home Trust pairs well with a domestic cash back card:

  • All foreign currency spending → Home Trust Preferred Visa (no FX fee, 1% back)
  • All CAD spending in Canada → Rogers Red, Simplii, or another high-earn card
  • Travel insurance → Separate policy, or keep a travel card like Scotia Passport for the insurance umbrella

This setup eliminates all FX fees and maximizes domestic category rewards without paying for card features you don't need.

Home Trust as an Institution

Home Trust Company is a Canadian federally regulated trust company (not a chartered bank, but similarly regulated). It is based in Toronto and focuses on residential mortgage lending and alternative credit products. Its credit cards (Home Trust Preferred Visa and the secured Home Trust Secured Visa) are accessible through the Home Trust website and through mortgage brokers.

Because Home Trust is not a Big 5 bank, the application process is online-only and there are no branches. Customer service is available by phone and digital channels. For people with slightly non-traditional credit profiles (newly arrived Canadians, self-employed individuals), Home Trust is often more accessible than big bank alternatives.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the Home Trust Preferred Visa available across Canada?

Yes — Home Trust accepts applications from all Canadian provinces and territories. The application is done entirely online at hometrust.ca. There are no physical branches.

Does the 1% cash back apply to foreign currency purchases?

Yes. You earn 1% cash back on all eligible purchases regardless of currency. Because there is no FX fee, your effective return on foreign purchases is simply 1% on the Visa-rate converted amount.

How does Home Trust compare to the Rogers Red for foreign spending?

Both waive the effective FX cost. Home Trust does it by charging no FX fee at all and returning 1% cash back. Rogers Red charges the 2.5% FX fee but gives 3% back on foreign USD spending (net +0.5%). Rogers Red wins marginally on foreign spend but only matches this through a rebate mechanism. For domestic CAD spending, Rogers Red (1.5%) comfortably outperforms Home Trust (1%). Many travelers carry both.

Is there a secured version for people building credit?

Yes — Home Trust also offers the Home Trust Secured Visa, which requires a security deposit and helps Canadians build or rebuild credit history. The unsecured Preferred Visa requires a reasonable credit score and Canadian residency.

Can I use this card at US online retailers?

Yes. Any USD-priced purchase on a US website processes through Visa's exchange rate with no additional FX fee. This is one of the best use cases for this card — shopping at Amazon.com, US subscription services, US software, etc.
