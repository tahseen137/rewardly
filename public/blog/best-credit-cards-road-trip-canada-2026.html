Best Credit Cards for Road Trips Canada 2026

Gas, hotels, roadside diners — all earn rewards with the right cards in your wallet. Plus: which card includes roadside assistance and rental car insurance.

What You're Spending on a Road Trip

A typical Canadian road trip budget breaks down roughly as: gas (40–50%), accommodation/hotels (30–35%), dining and food (15–20%), and activities/misc (5–10%). Since these span different merchant categories, the ideal road trip wallet uses 2–3 cards to maximize each category rather than one catch-all card.

Top Cards by Road Trip Category

#1 For Gas: TD Cash Back Visa Infinite

3% on gas · 3% on grocery · Travel insurance included

Annual fee: $139 (first year often free). 3% on gas is one of the highest flat gas earn rates in Canada from a general-purpose card. On a road trip burning $200–$500 in fuel, that's $6–$15 in cashback — vs. $4–$10 from Rogers Red. Also earns 3% on groceries if you're stocking up for camping or packing coolers. Includes travel insurance (out-of-province medical, trip interruption) which road trips need.

#2 For Hotels: Amex Cobalt

2x MR on hotels · 5x MR on dining · 3x on streaming

Annual fee: $156. Cobalt earns 2x Amex MR on hotel purchases (coded as "accommodation/lodging") and 5x on the roadside diner, pub, or food stop. MR points transfer to Aeroplan and Marriott Bonvoy. A long road trip with $600 in hotels earns 1,200 MR points (~$18 in travel value) — not huge, but it adds up on multi-night trips. Note: Cobalt is Amex, not accepted everywhere. Bring a backup Visa or Mastercard.

#3 For Everything Else (and US Road Trips): Rogers Red WE Mastercard

2% cashback everywhere · 3% on foreign currency (USD purchases)

Annual fee: $0. The fallback card when Cobalt isn't accepted and when the gas category doesn't apply. For a US road trip (or crossing into the US for a section), Rogers Red's 3% on foreign-currency purchases makes it the clear winner — gas, hotels, and dining in USD all earn 3%, effectively making the FX fee a wash. Works everywhere Mastercard is accepted, including many small towns and rural areas where Amex acceptance is spotty.

Road Trip Insurance: What Your Card Covers

Out-of-Province Medical Insurance

TD, CIBC, Scotiabank premium Visa cards include emergency medical insurance (typically $5M) for travel outside your home province. Essential for any Canadian driving interprovincially. Activate by charging the trip to the card.

Rental Car Insurance

Most premium Visa Infinite and World Elite Mastercard cards include CDW/LDW collision coverage for rental vehicles when you pay with the card and decline the rental agency's damage waiver. Call your card's benefits line to confirm before you pick up the car.

Trip Interruption Insurance

If your road trip is disrupted by illness, vehicle breakdown, or weather, some cards reimburse additional hotel stays and meals. Covered under TD Cash Back VI, Scotiabank Passport VI, and most Amex cards.

Roadside Assistance

Some premium Canadian cards include roadside assistance (towing, flat tire, dead battery). Scotiabank Passport VI and some RBC cards include this. Amex cards don't usually include it. Check your benefits guide before leaving CAA out of the glove box.

The two-card road trip setup: TD Cash Back VI (for gas 3% + out-of-province medical insurance) + Amex Cobalt (for hotels 2x and restaurant meals 5x) + Rogers Red as backup Mastercard for US purchases and anywhere Amex isn't accepted. Three cards, all earning above-baseline on the road.
