Best Credit Cards for Bad Credit in Canada (2026)

Getting a credit card with bad credit IS possible — and the right card is your fastest path back to a good score.

Updated July 2026 · 9 min read

A bad credit score in Canada typically means a score below 580 on the Equifax or TransUnion scale. Causes range from missed payments and collections to bankruptcy, consumer proposals, or simply no credit history at all. Whatever brought you here, the path forward is the same: a credit card used responsibly is the single fastest tool to rebuild your score.

This guide covers the specific options available for Canadians with damaged credit — no judgment, no complexity, just a clear path forward.

If your score is below 620, you'll be declined for most standard credit cards. Between 620–659 (Fair), you may qualify for some entry-level unsecured options with lower limits. Below 620, secured credit cards are your best and sometimes only option.

The Three Paths for Bad Credit

1. Secured credit cards: You deposit cash as collateral (typically $200–$2,500) that becomes your credit limit. Approval is essentially guaranteed. The bank holds your deposit as security and reports your payment history to Equifax and TransUnion monthly — building your credit score.

2. Credit-builder cards: Certain issuers (KOHO, Refresh Financial/Refresh Card) have products specifically designed for credit rebuilding with features to help you build history safely.

3. Prepaid credit cards with credit-building features: Some prepaid card products (like KOHO's "Credit Building" subscription) report payment history to the credit bureaus even though they're not technically credit cards — functioning as a credit-building tool without traditional credit risk.

How to Rebuild Your Credit Score Effectively

The mechanics of credit rebuilding are simple, but they require consistency:

1

Get one secured card and use it lightly

Put one small recurring charge on it — a streaming subscription, a monthly transit pass, or groceries. Don't use more than 30% of the credit limit at any time (e.g., keep balance under $150 on a $500 limit card).

2

Pay the FULL balance every single month

This is non-negotiable. Paying only the minimum is better than nothing, but paying the full statement balance every month creates the most positive payment history signal. Set up autopay for the full balance.

3

Don't apply for anything else for 6+ months

Every new credit application is a hard inquiry that temporarily reduces your score. While rebuilding, resist the temptation to apply for other cards. One card, used perfectly, does more than five cards used carelessly.

4

Monitor your score monthly (free, soft pull)

Use Borrowell (Equifax scores) or Credit Karma (TransUnion scores) — both free, both soft pulls, no score impact. Watch your score improve monthly as your positive payment history compounds.

How fast can you rebuild? With a secured card used responsibly, most Canadians see their score increase 50–100 points within 12 months and 100–150+ points within 24 months. Someone starting at 500 can realistically reach 650+ (qualifying for standard rewards cards) within 18 months. The key is zero missed payments.

Realistic Credit Rebuilding Timeline

Month 1–3: Score may not change much as new accounts take time to process. You might see a slight dip from the secured card's hard inquiry (if one was required). Focus on making on-time payments and keeping utilization low.

Month 3–6: Score typically starts improving as consistent on-time payments accumulate. 10–30 point improvement is common in this window.

Month 6–12: Stronger improvement — often 30–60 points above starting score. If you started at 520, you might reach 570–580 by month 12.

Month 12–24: Continued improvement as account age grows and negative items age off your report. Starting at 520: expect 620–680 by month 24 with perfect payment history.

6 years: Most negative items (late payments, collections, NSF) are automatically removed from your credit report after 6 years in Canada. Bankruptcies are removed after 6–7 years from discharge.

Critical Mistakes to Avoid While Rebuilding

Do NOT use credit repair services: Companies that promise to "fix" your credit score for a fee are almost universally ineffective or outright fraudulent. Legitimate negative information on your credit report cannot be removed early — it ages off naturally after 6 years. No service can accelerate this. The only legitimate credit repair is responsible behavior over time.

Don't max out your secured card: High utilization (using more than 30% of your limit) negatively affects your score even if you pay in full. A $500 limit card used at $400/month = 80% utilization = negative signal. Keep it under $150 on a $500 limit.

Don't close old accounts: Even if a credit card has a high interest rate or no rewards, an old open account with good history is valuable. Account age is a component of your score. Only close accounts if they have an annual fee you can't justify — never for "simplicity."

Don't apply for multiple cards at once: Each application is a hard inquiry. When you're rebuilding, every point counts. Apply for one secured card, use it for 12 months, then apply for a rewards card when your score has improved.
