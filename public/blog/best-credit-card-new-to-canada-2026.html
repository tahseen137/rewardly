No Canadian credit history? Here's how to get your first card and build credit fast

When you arrive in Canada, you start with a blank Canadian credit history — even if you had excellent credit in your home country. This guide explains your best options in 2026: newcomer programs at the Big 6 banks, secured cards, and which credit card to get first to build credit the fastest.

The Challenge: No Canadian Credit History

Canadian credit scores are maintained by Equifax Canada and TransUnion Canada — separate from any credit bureaus in your home country. Even if you had an 800+ credit score in India, the US, UK, or anywhere else, that history doesn't transfer to Canada. You need to start building a Canadian credit profile from scratch.

This doesn't mean you can't get a credit card right away — most major Canadian banks have dedicated newcomer programs specifically for people who have recently arrived and have no Canadian credit history.

Bank Newcomer Programs — Best Option

Key insight: Apply within your first year

Most bank newcomer programs define "newcomer" as someone who has been in Canada for less than 1–3 years (varies by bank). Once you're past that window, you'll need Canadian credit history to qualify for cards. The first year is the critical time to apply.

When you visit a branch, bring: your passport, work permit or PR card/confirmation, and proof of address in Canada (rental agreement or utility bill).

Common Newcomer Credit Mistakes to Avoid

1. Paying only the minimum payment. You'll pay 19.99–25% interest on the remaining balance. Always pay the full balance — a credit card should be a convenience tool, not a loan.

2. Applying for too many cards at once. Each application triggers a "hard inquiry" that lowers your score by ~5–10 points temporarily. Apply for one card, use it well for 6–12 months, then apply for a second if needed.

3. Closing your first credit card. Your oldest card contributes most to "length of history." Even after upgrading, keep your first card open — switch it to a no-fee option if needed but don't close it.

4. Carrying a balance for rewards points. No amount of rewards is worth 19.99% interest. Points earned while carrying a balance are vastly outweighed by interest charges.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use my credit score from India/US/UK in Canada?
No — Canadian credit bureaus (Equifax Canada and TransUnion Canada) don't share data with credit bureaus in other countries. Your foreign credit score doesn't transfer. You need to build a Canadian credit profile from scratch. Some banks (Nova Credit partners) can access US credit reports in limited cases, but this isn't yet universal in Canada.
How long until I have a credit score in Canada?
Equifax and TransUnion typically need 3–6 months of credit history before generating your first score. Once you have an account open and active, your score will appear after your first billing cycle or two is reported to the bureaus (usually 30–60 days after opening the account).
What's a good credit score in Canada?
Canadian credit scores range from 300–900 (Equifax and TransUnion scale). General ranges: 300–579 poor; 580–669 fair; 670–739 good; 740–799 very good; 800+ excellent. For premium credit cards, aim for 700+. For the best mortgage rates, aim for 760+.
Do I need a Social Insurance Number (SIN) to get a credit card?
Not always — some banks will open a credit card account with just your passport and work permit/PR documentation. However, having a SIN helps because it's the unique identifier that connects your accounts at the credit bureau. Apply for your SIN as soon as you're eligible.
