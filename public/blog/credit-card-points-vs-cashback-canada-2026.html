Credit Card Points vs. Cash Back Canada 2026 — Which Is Better?

A 2% cash back card sounds simple and good. But 5x points on dining redeemed via Aeroplan can deliver 7–10% return. The answer depends on how you spend and redeem.

Points vs. Cash Back — The Core Difference

✈️ Travel Rewards Points

  • Earn: Amex MR, Aeroplan, Avion, TD Rewards, Scene+
  • Value: variable (0.5¢ to 3¢+ per point depending on redemption)
  • Best redemptions: business/first class flights
  • Worst redemptions: merchandise, Amazon cash
  • Example: 5x Amex MR → Aeroplan = potential 7.5%+ return on spend
  • Requires: active management, travel intent

💵 Cash Back

  • Earn: Direct % back on purchases
  • Value: fixed and predictable (1–3% depending on category)
  • Best redemptions: automatic statement credit or bank deposit
  • No worst redemption — cash is always 100% cash
  • Example: 2% Tangerine Money-Back = $2 back per $100 spent
  • Requires: minimal management

The Real Math — When Points Win

The key insight: travel points have variable value. The same number of points can be worth 0.5¢ or 3¢ depending on how you redeem them. This is what makes points potentially much more valuable than cash back — and also more complex.

When Cash Back Wins

Cash back is superior in several common scenarios:

1. You rarely travel: If you don't fly at least once a year, you'll struggle to redeem travel points efficiently. Points with low redemption value (merchandise, statement credits through the bank's own portal) often perform worse than 2% cash back. Cash is always 1¢ per cent — no devaluation risk, no program changes.

2. You want simplicity: Cash back requires zero management. Points cards require tracking earn rates, understanding transfer partners, finding award availability, and planning redemptions. For people who don't want to spend time optimizing, cash back delivers reliable value automatically.

3. You have significant non-travel spend: Most premium points cards earn 5x only on specific categories (food, travel) and 1x elsewhere. A 2% flat cash back card (like SimplyCash Preferred at 2% everywhere) beats a 1x points card on non-bonus spend every time.

4. You're paying off debt or in financial stress: Points are irrelevant if you're carrying a balance at 19.99%. A low-rate card (MBNA True Line at 12.99%) saves far more than any points program until the balance is cleared.

Who Should Choose Points? Who Should Choose Cash Back?

✈️ Choose Points if:

  • You fly at least once per year
  • You're willing to spend 1–2 hours/year learning about Aeroplan sweet spots
  • Your top spend categories match 5x bonus earn (dining, groceries)
  • You want to fly business or first class without paying cash prices
  • You'll actually redeem points within 2–3 years (avoid devaluation)
  • You spend $500+/month on bonus categories

💵 Choose Cash Back if:

  • You rarely travel or can't predict when you'll redeem
  • You want zero management — automatic rewards
  • Your spending is spread across many categories (benefits from flat-rate cards)
  • You're building an emergency fund and want liquid rewards
  • You're unsure about travel programs and don't want to learn
  • You occasionally carry a balance (first priority: low-rate card)

The Winner for Most Engaged Canadians

Points cards win at high spend volumes and premium redemptions — cash back wins on simplicity and non-travel spending

For Canadians who spend $1,000+/month on dining and groceries, consistently fly at least once per year, and are willing to learn Aeroplan basics, a 5x points card like Amex Cobalt easily outperforms the best 2% cash back card by a factor of 3–5x when redeemed for Aeroplan flights. For those who fly rarely, prefer simplicity, or have highly varied spending, the best 2% cash back card (Amex SimplyCash Preferred at 2–4%, or even free Tangerine at 2% on chosen categories) is often the better practical choice.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do travel points expire in Canada?
Most major Canadian travel points programs do NOT expire if your account is active (meaning you earn or redeem points at least once within a set period). Aeroplan points never expire as long as your account has activity within 18 months. Amex Membership Rewards never expire while your card is open. Scene+ points expire after 12 months of inactivity. Always check your program's specific expiry policy — inactivity expiry is the main risk for infrequent travellers.
What's the best cash back card in Canada with no annual fee?
The Tangerine Money-Back Mastercard (2% on up to 3 chosen categories, 0.5% elsewhere) is the most flexible no-fee cash back card in Canada. The Rogers Red World Elite Mastercard (1.5% on all CAD purchases, $0 fee) is the best flat-rate no-fee option. BMO CashBack World Elite MC offers 5% on groceries (first 3 months, then 3% ongoing) with the annual fee partially waived first year.
