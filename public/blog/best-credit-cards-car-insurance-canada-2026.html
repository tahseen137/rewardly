Best Credit Cards for Paying Car Insurance Canada 2026

Car and home insurance is $2,000–$4,000+/year for most Canadians. Most insurers accept credit cards. That's real rewards money sitting on the table.

Can You Pay Car Insurance With a Credit Card in Canada?

Yes — most major Canadian auto and home insurance providers accept credit card payments, either online or by phone. This includes Intact, Aviva, TD Insurance, Desjardins, Co-operators, Economical, and most brokerages. A handful of insurers charge a credit card processing fee (typically 1.5–2.5%), which may offset rewards — but many accept cards with no additional fee. Always confirm with your insurer before switching payment methods.

How insurance premiums code: Car insurance premiums typically code as "insurance" on your credit card statement. This is a base-rate category on most cards — no card has a specific insurance bonus category (unlike groceries or dining). The best earn strategy is a high flat-rate card that earns 2% everywhere, since no category card offers a bonus on insurance.

#2 Any Card During Welcome Bonus Period — Annual Payment Strategy

All spend counts toward welcome bonus minimum threshold

The biggest win on insurance is using an annual lump-sum premium payment to hit a welcome bonus spend threshold. If you switch from monthly to annual insurance payment when you open a new card, $2,000–$3,000 in insurance premiums can fulfil much of a $3,000–$5,000 minimum spend requirement. The 60,000 Aeroplan welcome bonus from TD Aeroplan VI is worth $600–$1,200 in travel — far exceeding a year's worth of 1–2% rewards on the same premium.

Insurers That Charge Credit Card Fees

Some insurance companies pass card processing fees to consumers — typically 1.5–2.5%. If your insurer charges 2.5% to use a credit card, and your card only earns 2%, you'd be losing money. In these cases, stick to direct debit or bank transfer. Before switching your insurance to a credit card, call your insurer and specifically ask: "Is there a fee to pay by credit card?" The answer determines whether the math works.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does paying insurance by credit card affect my policy?
No — your insurance policy terms don't change based on your payment method. Coverage begins and remains in force as long as payment is received on time, regardless of whether it came from a credit card, bank account, or cheque. The only consideration is that if your credit card is declined, your policy could lapse if the insurer doesn't receive payment. Always have a backup payment method on file if paying by credit card.
Can I use any credit card, or is there a specific type required?
Most insurers accept any major Visa or Mastercard. American Express acceptance varies — call your insurer to confirm. Prepaid cards (Visa debit) are generally not accepted for recurring insurance payments. If you're paying via an insurance broker's portal, Visa and Mastercard are almost universally accepted; Amex less so.
