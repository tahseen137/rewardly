Effective Annual Cost Comparison

Scotia Passport VI: $150/year flat. No travel credits, no rebates. You pay $150.
HSBC WE MC: $149/year − $100 annual travel credit = $49 effective annual fee. The $100 travel credit applies as a statement credit when you make eligible travel purchases. If you travel at all (flight, hotel, rental car), you will use the $100 credit, making the effective fee $49.

On fee alone, HSBC wins significantly: $49 effective vs. $150 is a $101/year difference. Even if the $100 credit requires a travel purchase to unlock, virtually anyone carrying this card for travel will trigger it. The HSBC WE MC costs 1/3 as much as the Scotia Passport VI in practical terms.

When to Choose Scotia Passport Visa Infinite

Pick Scotia Passport VI if:

You're a heavy grocery spender (3x Scene+ on all grocers is meaningfully better than HSBC's 1.5% on groceries). You value Scene+ points for Canadian redemptions (movie theatres, Cineplex, Scotia travel booking). You want Visa's marginally broader global acceptance — particularly helpful in some parts of Europe and Asia where Mastercard acceptance is slightly less consistent. You already bank with Scotiabank and prefer keeping financial products together. You want trip cancellation insurance that's clearly bundled.

When to Choose HSBC World Elite Mastercard

Pick HSBC WE MC if:

You travel 1+ times per year (the $100 travel credit reduces your effective fee to $49 — a massive value advantage). You spend significantly on gas and travel (3% earn beats Scotia Passport's 2x on travel). You want points that transfer to airline programs (Cathay Asia Miles, British Airways Avios, Singapore Airlines KrisFlyer — all accessible from HSBC Rewards). You want slightly longer emergency medical coverage (31 days vs. 25 days). You want the lower effective annual cost. You don't value Scene+ points specifically.

Can You Have Both?

The Two-Card Power Move

Both cards together: $49 (HSBC net) + $150 (Scotia Passport) = $199/year combined. Use HSBC WE MC for: travel and gas purchases (3% earn), base travel spending, and Priority Pass lounge access on international trips. Use Scotia Passport VI for: groceries at all chains (3x Scene+), dining (2x Scene+), and as the Visa backup when Mastercard isn't accepted. This covers all major categories efficiently. However, if you only want ONE travel card for 0% FX + lounge access, HSBC WE MC wins on cost-effectiveness.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is DragonPass the same as Priority Pass?
They provide access to largely overlapping networks of ~1,300 airport lounges worldwide. Both are Collinson Group products. The same physical lounges typically participate in both programs — when you arrive at an airport lounge, you present either your DragonPass card or Priority Pass card and they provide access. For practical purposes in Canada and at major international airports, the difference is negligible. HSBC's Priority Pass may have a slight edge at some Asian airports with Priority Pass–exclusive lounges not in DragonPass. For most Canadian travellers flying to Europe, the US, or Caribbean, they're interchangeable.
How does the HSBC WE MC $100 travel credit work?
The $100 annual travel credit is applied automatically as a statement credit when eligible travel purchases appear on your account. Eligible purchases typically include airlines, hotels, car rentals, cruises, and travel agencies. The credit appears within 1–2 billing cycles after the qualifying purchase. It's not a voucher you need to redeem — it just appears on your statement. If you book a $200 flight on your HSBC WE MC, you'll see a $100 statement credit typically within 30–60 days.
