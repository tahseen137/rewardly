Amex Membership Rewards Guide Canada 2026

How to earn, transfer, and maximize American Express Membership Rewards points in Canada — including the Aeroplan 1:1 transfer with 10% bonus.

📖 12 min read

American Express Membership Rewards (MR) is Canada's most versatile loyalty currency. Unlike Aeroplan or WestJet Dollars — which are earned directly in one program — MR points are a hub currency: you accumulate them across Amex cards and then transfer to whichever loyalty program serves your next trip best.

The flexibility is the point. A stockpile of MR points can become Aeroplan miles for a business class redemption to Europe, British Airways Avios for a short-haul partner flight, or cash against your Amex statement. Understanding how MR works unlocks significantly more value than any single-program approach.

The $15.99/month fee structure ($191.88/yr) is slightly unusual but lets newer cardholders cancel after any month rather than committing to a full year. Amex frequently offers welcome bonuses of 15,000–25,000 MR points on the Cobalt to new applicants.

Transfer Partners — Where to Send Your MR Points

The power of Membership Rewards lies in its transfer partnerships. Canadian Amex members can transfer MR points to these programs:

✈️ Aeroplan (Air Canada) — BEST FOR MOST CANADIANS

1 MR point → 1 Aeroplan mile + 10% bonus

Transfer at 1:1 ratio and Amex adds 10% more miles on top. 10,000 MR → 11,000 Aeroplan. This 10% bonus is unique to Amex — no other Aeroplan transfer partner offers a bonus. Aeroplan's Star Alliance access and business class sweet spots make this the highest-value transfer for most Canadian travelers. Minimum transfer: 1,000 MR. Transfers process within 1–5 business days.

✈️ British Airways Avios

1 MR point → 1 British Airways Avios

1:1 transfer to BA Avios (Executive Club). Particularly valuable for short-haul partner award bookings — American Airlines flights within the continental US or Canada from 7,500 Avios one-way. Also useful for BA partner routes. No transfer bonus.

✈️ Air France / KLM Flying Blue

1 MR point → 1 Flying Blue mile

1:1 transfer to Flying Blue. Flying Blue regularly offers "Promo Awards" with significant discounts (up to 50% off) on specific routes in specific months. If your destination matches a Promo Award month, Flying Blue can offer exceptional value on European and transatlantic routes.

✈️ Cathay Pacific Asia Miles

1 MR point → 1 Asia Mile

1:1 transfer to Asia Miles. Valuable for premium cabin redemptions on Cathay Pacific routes — particularly Hong Kong and Southeast Asia. Also bookable on partner airlines (oneworld members).

🏨 Marriott Bonvoy

1 MR point → 1.2 Marriott Bonvoy points

Unusual ratio — you get 1.2 Marriott points per 1 MR point. Marriott points are worth approximately 0.7¢ each, so this transfer gives you roughly 0.84¢ per MR point. Less valuable than Aeroplan transfers but useful if you have a specific high-value Marriott redemption in mind.

💳 Statement Credits / Gift Cards

1 MR point → ~0.67¢

You can use MR points for statement credits at approximately 1¢ per 1.5 MR points (0.67¢/pt) — far below the value you'd get by transferring to Aeroplan or Avios. Avoid using MR for statement credits unless you have no better use for the points.

The Aeroplan Transfer — Canada's Best Points Move

Why the Aeroplan transfer is exceptional:

Amex gives you a 10% bonus when transferring to Aeroplan. No other transfer partner provides a bonus. This means:

• 10,000 MR → 11,000 Aeroplan miles
• 50,000 MR → 55,000 Aeroplan miles
• 100,000 MR → 110,000 Aeroplan miles

At scale, this bonus is meaningful. A round-trip to Europe in business class might require 110,000 Aeroplan miles — you could use 100,000 MR (from the Cobalt) to get there, with 10,000 Aeroplan miles to spare.

The strategic move for many Canadian travelers: earn MR on the Amex Cobalt (5× on dining/grocery) and accumulate until you have enough for a specific redemption, then transfer to Aeroplan in one batch. This avoids having Aeroplan miles sitting idle and ensures you're transferring with a booking in mind.

⚠️ Transfer timing: Always confirm Aeroplan award availability BEFORE transferring your MR points. Transfers are one-way and cannot be reversed. Identify your desired award flight, confirm the seats are available, then transfer the exact amount needed.

MR Points Expiry and Account Rules

Amex Membership Rewards points do not expire as long as your account is open. However:

  • If you cancel all of your MR-eligible Amex cards, your MR points are forfeited within a short window
  • Points earned on a supplementary card belong to the primary cardholder's MR account
  • You cannot transfer MR points between different Amex accounts (e.g., from one cardholder's account to another)
  • If you downgrade from a MR card to a non-MR card (e.g., SimplyCash), your MR balance may be at risk — always check before downgrading

Building an Amex MR Strategy

For someone starting from scratch, a practical Amex MR strategy:

  1. Get the Amex Cobalt — earns 5 MR pts/$ on dining and grocery, which is most Canadians' highest spending categories
  2. Capture the welcome bonus — usually 15,000–25,000 MR after $750 in first 3 months; that's already a meaningful Aeroplan transfer
  3. Accumulate MR for a specific goal — decide on a redemption before building points (e.g., round-trip economy YYZ-LHR requires ~60,000 Aeroplan; identify a target and earn toward it)
  4. Transfer to Aeroplan when ready to book — confirm seat availability first, then transfer the exact amount needed plus a small buffer
  5. Consider supplementing with a TD or CIBC Aeroplan card — direct Aeroplan earning on non-MR-bonus-eligible spending (gas, general purchases) rounds out the strategy

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I pool MR points from multiple Amex cards?

Yes — if you hold multiple MR-eligible Amex cards (e.g., Cobalt and Gold Rewards), all points go to one Membership Rewards account. You can transfer the pooled balance to a partner program at once.

Do supplementary cardholder purchases earn MR?

Yes. Purchases made by supplementary cardholders (e.g., a spouse or family member on an additional card) earn MR points into the primary cardholder's account at the same category rates.

What's the minimum transfer amount to Aeroplan?

The minimum transfer to Aeroplan from Amex MR is 1,000 points (becoming 1,100 Aeroplan miles with the 10% bonus). There is no published maximum, and you can transfer in any increment above the minimum.

How long do Aeroplan transfers take?

Transfers from Amex MR to Aeroplan typically process within 1–5 business days. Do not book an award before the transfer completes — confirm the miles have arrived in your Aeroplan account before proceeding.

Is the Amex Cobalt good for earning if I don't want to travel?

Yes — you can redeem MR points for statement credits (at ~0.67¢/pt) or through Amex's "Pay with Points" feature on any travel purchase at ~1¢/pt. The Cobalt still generates value even without airline redemptions, though the return is lower than transfer-based redemptions.
