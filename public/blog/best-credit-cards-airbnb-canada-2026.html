How Airbnb Charges Code on Canadian Credit Cards

The rewards you earn on an Airbnb booking depend on your card's merchant category code (MCC) classification for Airbnb transactions. Airbnb is headquartered in the US, and its Canadian charges typically come through as either Airbnb, Inc. (a distinct merchant) or code under travel accommodation MCCs. This matters enormously because a card that earns 3x on travel will treat Airbnb very differently than a card with a specific "hotels" category.

The key distinction: Airbnb is NOT a hotel for credit card purposes. Cards that offer bonus rates on "hotels" (like the TD First Class Travel Visa Infinite's 8x for travel booked through Expedia for TD) typically do NOT give that bonus rate for Airbnb. Airbnb charges usually fall into a generic travel or accommodation MCC — or sometimes just base rate.

Important: MCC coding can vary by card issuer and change over time. The patterns below are based on general community experience as of 2026. Always check a small transaction to verify how your specific card codes Airbnb before a large booking.

Best Cards for Airbnb Bookings

BEST OVERALL — CONSISTENT TRAVEL EARN

Scotiabank Gold American Express

The Scotia Gold Amex earns 5x Scene+ points on all dining, food delivery, and entertainment, and crucially, earns 3x Scene+ on all other travel purchases — which includes Airbnb. Scene+ points are worth 1¢ each for most travel redemptions, making that 3x = 3% back on Airbnb stays.

This makes Scotia Gold one of the best cards for Airbnb in Canada: no foreign transaction fee (Airbnb charges often come through in USD), 3% effective rewards on the stay, and a broad travel category that consistently includes vacation rental platforms. Annual fee: $120/yr.

Earn on Airbnb: 3x Scene+ (~3%)  |  FX fee: None  |  Annual fee: $120

BEST FOR AMEX POINTS

American Express Gold Rewards Card

The Amex Gold Rewards earns 2x Amex Membership Rewards points on travel purchases, which includes Airbnb. Amex MR points are worth approximately 1–2¢ depending on how you redeem them — at 1.5¢ average, that's 3% effective return on Airbnb stays, comparable to Scotia Gold.

The advantage: if you're already earning Amex MR points and want to concentrate them in one program, this card keeps Airbnb in the 2x travel bucket. Also earns 2x on groceries at participating stores. Annual fee: $250/yr (offset by annual travel credit and companion voucher).

Earn on Airbnb: 2x Amex MR (~2–3%)  |  FX fee: 2.5%  |  Annual fee: $250

BEST NO-FEE OPTION

Rogers Red World Elite Mastercard

Rogers Red earns 2% cashback on all purchases with no category restrictions. Airbnb earns 2%, US-based Airbnb charges avoid foreign transaction fees (Rogers Red has a 0% FX fee), and there's no annual fee. The only limitation is income requirements ($80K personal or $150K household for World Elite).

If Airbnb bills you in USD (common for US properties), Rogers Red nets you 2% back with no FX surcharge — a clear win over cards that earn 3x but also charge a 2.5% FX fee. For Canadian properties billed in CAD, any 2% card works equally well.

Earn on Airbnb: 2% flat  |  FX fee: 0%  |  Annual fee: $0

BEST FOR FLEXIBLE POINTS

TD First Class Travel Visa Infinite

The TD First Class earns 8x Expedia for TD points on travel booked through Expedia for TD, but only 2x on all other purchases — including Airbnb booked directly. Expedia for TD points are worth 0.5¢ each, so 2x = 1% effective return, which is below Rogers Red's 2%.

TD First Class is NOT the best card for Airbnb specifically, but if you hold it for other travel benefits (annual $100 travel credit, emergency medical insurance), the 2x on Airbnb is acceptable. Don't hold it solely for Airbnb rewards.

Earn on Airbnb: 2x Expedia for TD pts (~1%)  |  FX fee: 2.5%  |  Annual fee: $139

The Foreign Transaction Fee Problem

When you book an Airbnb for a Canadian property, you're often still being charged by Airbnb, Inc. (a US entity), and the transaction may process in USD. This means a 2.5% FX surcharge applies if your card charges foreign transaction fees — including nearly all major bank rewards cards.

Does Airbnb Travel Insurance Apply?

Most travel credit card insurance benefits require you to book the trip on that card. For Airbnb stays, the relevant insurance to check is trip cancellation/interruption and travel medical. Most Canadian premium cards cover these, but there are conditions:

Travel cancellation typically covers you if you pay for the Airbnb with an eligible card and cannot travel due to a covered reason (severe illness, family emergency, etc.). Airbnb also has its own AirCover cancellation policy — understand both before assuming coverage. Trip cancellation on your credit card is secondary coverage; you must attempt to get a refund from Airbnb first.

Bottom line: For Canadian Airbnb stays, use Scotia Gold Amex (3x travel, no Airbnb-specific caveat). For US Airbnb bookings, use Rogers Red (2% flat, no FX fee). Hold your Amex primarily for what it excels at — dining (5x) — and use the travel earn as a bonus.
