#1 Pick: TD Aeroplan Visa Infinite

The TD Aeroplan Visa Infinite is the most popular Aeroplan card in Canada for good reason: it offers a strong earn rate on everyday categories (groceries and gas), a meaningful welcome bonus, and a comprehensive suite of travel insurance.

Why It Wins

The grocery and gas bonus rate (1.5 pts/$) is particularly valuable because it's uncapped — unlike some competing products. A Canadian family spending $800/month on groceries and $200/month on gas earns 15,000 Aeroplan points per year just from those two categories.

#2 Pick: CIBC Aeroplan Visa Infinite

Nearly identical to the TD version. Same fee, same welcome bonus structure, and nearly identical travel insurance. If you bank with CIBC, this is your best entry-level premium choice.

Premium Tier: Is the $599 Fee Worth It?

For high-income frequent flyers, the Privilege tier cards from TD and CIBC offer Priority check-in, lounge access, and free first checked bags. If you fly Air Canada 6+ times per year, these perks easily outweigh the $599 fee.

How to Choose the Right Aeroplan Card

Choose TD or CIBC Aeroplan Visa Infinite if:

  • You spend heavily on groceries and gas
  • You fly Air Canada 2–5 times per year
  • Your household income qualifies ($60K+ personal)

Choose Amex Aeroplan if:

  • You book Air Canada directly (2x on those purchases)
  • You use food delivery apps regularly
  • You don't meet Visa Infinite income requirements

The Bottom Line

The right Aeroplan card depends on how much you spend, where you spend it, and how often you fly Air Canada. Don't leave points on the table by using the wrong card for your biggest expenses.
