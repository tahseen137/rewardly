Point Value

à la Carte Rewards points are valued at approximately 1¢ per point when redeemed through National Bank's travel platform. So 5× on dining = 5% effective return, and 2× on groceries/gas/pharmacy = 2% return. This is strong for a Canadian travel card.

Travel Insurance — National Bank's Crown Jewel

National Bank World Elite has the most comprehensive travel insurance package of any credit card in Canada in its $150 tier. The emergency medical, baggage, and rental car coverage in particular outperforms most competitors.

The baggage loss coverage of $3,000 per insured is exceptional — most competitors offer $500–$1,000. For a couple travelling, that's $6,000 in coverage. Similarly, 48 consecutive days of rental car coverage is more than double most competitors' 31-day limits.

The graduated medical coverage by age (60 days under 54, 31 days at 55–64, 15 days at 65+) is worth noting — older travellers should supplement with separate travel medical insurance for longer trips.

à la Carte Travel Program

National Bank's travel redemption platform allows you to book travel and pay for it with points. Unlike some programs with rigid airline partner lists, the à la Carte platform lets you redeem for any travel purchase — flights, hotels, car rentals, vacation packages — on any airline at any price.

Points are applied as statement credits against travel purchases, at approximately 1¢ per point. This means your points are essentially worth a fixed cash value toward travel — no blackout dates, no seat restrictions, no partner limitations. Book the cheapest flight you can find, then pay with points.

Who Should Get This Card?

Best for: Quebec residents who bank with National Bank, frequent diners and entertainment-goers, travellers who prioritize insurance coverage over lounge access, and those who rent cars often (48-day coverage is exceptional).

Not ideal for: International travellers who need no FX fees, frequent flyers who value lounge access, or those who prefer airline/hotel loyalty program transfers.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the National Bank World Elite available outside Quebec?
Yes — the card is available to eligible Canadians nationwide, not just in Quebec. However, National Bank has fewer branches outside Quebec, so online applications are the primary channel for most Canadians. Credit underwriting requirements apply everywhere.
Can I transfer à la Carte Rewards points to airline miles?
No — National Bank's à la Carte Rewards points are not transferable to airline loyalty programs. They're redeemed through National Bank's travel platform for travel purchases or as statement credits against travel charges.
What counts as "entertainment" for the 5× earn rate?
Entertainment includes movie theatres, concert venues, live sports events, amusement parks, and similar purchases typically coded as entertainment by Mastercard. Streaming services (Netflix, Spotify) typically code as recurring subscriptions, not entertainment — they earn at the base rate.
Is the emergency medical coverage enough for older travellers?
The 15-day limit for those 65 and older may be too short for longer trips. National Bank recommends purchasing a top-up policy for extended travel. For travellers 54 and under, the 60-day window is exceptional.
