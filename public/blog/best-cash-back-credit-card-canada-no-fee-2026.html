🥇 Best Flat-Rate No-Fee Card

Best Flat-Rate

Rogers Red World Elite Mastercard — $0/yr

1.5% cash back on all purchases. That's the simplest pitch. No category restrictions, no quarterly rotations, no partner stores needed. Every dollar earns the same rate. Plus: 3% cash back on all purchases in foreign currencies (USD, EUR, etc.) — effectively a 0.5% net gain after the 2.5% FX fee, making it one of the best no-fee cards for online USD shopping.

  • 1.5% on all purchases in CAD
  • 3% on all foreign currency purchases (net ~0.5% after FX fee)
  • Cash back credited to your Rogers bill OR deposited as cash
  • Must have a Rogers, Fido, or Shaw product to get full 1.5% (otherwise 1.25%)
  • World Elite Mastercard perks (Mastercard Travel Rewards, cell phone protection)

Best for: Rogers/Fido/Shaw customers who want simplicity. Also the best no-fee card for Amazon.ca (US inventory billing) and other online USD retailers.

🥈 Best for Loblaws / Shoppers Drug Mart Shoppers

Best Partner Earn

PC Financial Mastercard — $0/yr

30 PC Optimum points per dollar at Loblaws-banner grocery stores. 45 points per dollar at Shoppers Drug Mart. At 1,000 points = $1 in value, that's 3% at Loblaws-family stores and 4.5% at Shoppers — dramatically higher than any other no-fee card at those specific retailers. For Canadians who do most of their grocery and pharmacy shopping at PC-family stores, this is the highest-return no-fee card available.

  • 45 pts/$ at Shoppers Drug Mart (~4.5%)
  • 30 pts/$ at Loblaws, No Frills, Real Canadian Superstore, Zehrs (~3%)
  • 10 pts/$ at PC Travel, PC Express, PC Insurance
  • 10 pts/$ everywhere else (~1%)
  • No annual fee, no minimum income

Best for: Anyone who shops regularly at Loblaws, No Frills, or Shoppers Drug Mart. The highest grocery cash-back rate among no-fee cards at those stores.

Best for Grocery-Heavy Households

Best Groceries (No Fee)

BMO CashBack Mastercard — $0/yr

3% cash back on groceries. No annual fee. For a household spending $600–$800/month on groceries, 3% = $216–$288/yr in free cash back. 1% on recurring bills, 0.5% base. The 3% grocery rate on a no-fee card is among the best available in Canada (the Scotiabank Momentum charges $120/yr for 4%).

  • 3% on groceries (any grocery store)
  • 1% on recurring bills
  • 0.5% on all other purchases
  • Cash back paid annually as statement credit
  • No annual fee

Best for: Families or households with significant grocery spending who don't want to pay an annual fee. Pairs well with a dining-focused card for restaurant spending.

Best Dining (No Fee)

Simplii Financial Cash Back Visa — $0/yr

4% cash back on restaurant, bar, and coffee shop purchases (up to $5,000/yr, then 0.5%). 1.5% on gas, groceries, and drugstores. 0.5% base. For Canadians who spend heavily on dining out and want a no-fee card, 4% on restaurants beats every paid card except the Scotiabank Gold Amex (6%).

  • 4% on restaurants, bars, coffee shops (first $5,000/yr)
  • 1.5% on gas, groceries, drugstores
  • 0.5% on everything else
  • No annual fee
  • CIBC-backed (Simplii is CIBC's direct banking brand)

Best for: Young professionals, urban dwellers, or anyone who eats out frequently and wants maximum dining cash back without paying an annual fee.

Should You Pay an Annual Fee?

The breakeven math is simple. If you spend $1,000/month on groceries and a paid card earns 4% vs. a free card earning 1.5%, the paid card earns an extra $300/yr — well above the typical $120 annual fee.

Rough breakeven spending levels (paid card advantage vs. free card):

Spending LevelNo-Fee Often BetterPaid Often Better
Total monthly spend < $1,500✓
Grocery $300–$500/mo✓
Grocery $600–$800/mo✓ (Scotia Momentum)
Heavy dining $400+/mo✓ (Scotia Gold, Cobalt)
Frequent international travel✓ (no-FX cards)
The best strategy for most Canadians: One paid rewards card for your highest-spend categories + one no-fee card as a backup (or for categories the paid card doesn't cover well). Don't pay two annual fees unless both clearly deliver value above their cost.
