Best Credit Cards for Dining Out in Canada 2026

Restaurants and food delivery are the highest-earning categories on multiple Canadian credit cards. The right card turns every meal into 4–10% back in rewards.

How Dining Earns on Credit Cards

Dining transactions code with MCC 5812 (Eating Places & Restaurants) or MCC 5814 (Fast Food Restaurants). Food delivery platforms (Uber Eats, DoorDash, SkipTheDishes) typically code as MCC 5812 as well — meaning most cards that earn bonus points at restaurants also earn at food delivery services. A few cards distinguish between the two; we note this in each review below.

Canadian households spend an average of $3,000–$6,000/year on dining out and food delivery combined. At 5x points vs. 1x base rate, that's a difference of $150–$300/year in extra rewards on dining alone — just from choosing the right card.

Best Credit Cards for Dining in Canada 2026

#1

American Express Cobalt Card

$156/yr · 5x MR at Restaurants & Delivery · Best Overall
Best Dining Card
5x Membership Rewards on all Dining & Food Delivery

The Amex Cobalt earns 5x MR at restaurants, bars, cafés, and food delivery (Uber Eats, DoorDash, SkipTheDishes) — the highest earn rate on dining of any Canadian card. At $3,000/yr dining spend → 15,000 MR/year just on dining. Transferred to Aeroplan at 1:1 + 10% bonus → 16,500 Aeroplan points → worth $165–$330+ toward flights.

Combined with the monthly welcome bonus (up to 15,000 MR/month for 12 months), the Cobalt is the undisputed best card for heavy dining spenders who also want to accumulate travel rewards. Annual fee: $156.

Best for: Anyone who dines out or orders delivery regularly. 5x MR at every restaurant and delivery app makes this the #1 dining card in Canada by a wide margin. Pair with a Visa/MC for restaurants that don't accept Amex.
#2

Scotiabank Gold American Express

$120/yr · 6x Scene+ at Eligible Dining · Best Scene+ Earn
Best Scene+ Dining
6x Scene+ at eligible dining & entertainment

The Scotia Gold Amex technically earns more Scene+ points on dining than the Cobalt earns MR — 6x Scene+ vs. 5x MR — but Scene+ points are worth 1¢ each (fixed), while MR points can be worth 1.5–3¢ each via Aeroplan transfers. So Scotia Gold = 6% Scene+ on dining vs. Cobalt = 7.5–15% in Aeroplan/travel value on dining.

For Scotiabankers who want simplicity (Scene+ redeems easily at Cineplex, Empire theatres, and Scene+ Travel), the Gold Amex at $120/yr beats the Cobalt. Also: 0% foreign transaction fee makes it strong for dining internationally.

Best for: Scene+ collectors and those who value simplicity over maximizing Aeroplan value. The 6x on dining + 0% FX is particularly powerful when dining abroad.
#3

National Bank World Elite Mastercard

$150/yr · 5x NB Rewards on Dining & Entertainment
Best Visa/MC for Dining
5x NB Rewards on Dining, Entertainment & Streaming

The best non-Amex dining card in Canada. 5x NB Rewards on dining and entertainment at any Visa-accepting restaurant — critical because Amex is declined at many small restaurants, food trucks, and delivery services. NB Rewards are worth ~0.5¢ each toward travel/merchandise, so 5x = ~2.5% return on dining. Also includes the best travel insurance in Canada (60-day emergency medical, unlimited Dragon Pass).

Best for: Dining at restaurants that don't accept Amex, or as a backup to the Cobalt/Scotia Gold. The 5x NB Rewards is the best Visa/Mastercard dining rate in Canada.
#4

BMO eclipse rise Visa Card

$0/yr · 5x BMO Points on Dining & Delivery · No-Fee Winner
Best No-Fee Dining
5x BMO Points on Dining, Delivery, Grocery & Gas

The BMO eclipse rise earns 5x BMO Points on dining, food delivery, gas, and groceries at zero annual fee — an extraordinarily generous earn rate for a no-fee card. BMO Points are worth ~0.5¢ each toward travel, so 5x = ~2.5% return on dining. On $3,000/yr dining spend → 15,000 BMO points → $75 in travel value, for free.

The eclipse rise is ideal as a no-fee companion to a premium travel card — use the premium card for travel insurance and point transfers, and the eclipse rise for everyday dining when you want to avoid annual fees.

Best for: Cost-conscious diners who want strong dining rewards without an annual fee. 5x on dining, delivery, grocery, AND gas — all with $0/yr — is genuinely exceptional for a no-fee product.
#5

CIBC Dividend Visa Infinite

$120/yr · 4% Cash Back on Dining

4% cash back on dining (restaurants & food delivery)

If you want straightforward cash back on dining rather than points, the CIBC Dividend VI earns 4% cash back at restaurants and food delivery — the best cash-back dining rate in Canada. On $3,000/yr dining spend → $120/yr in cash back. With the $120 annual fee, the card breaks even on dining alone. Everything else (grocery, transit, recurring bills at 4%, gas at 4%) builds the value further.

Food delivery earn rates:

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the highest dining earn rate on any Canadian credit card?
In raw point terms, the Scotia Gold Amex earns 6x Scene+ on dining — the most points per dollar of any widely available card. However, in effective dollar value, the Amex Cobalt wins: 5x MR that can be transferred to Aeroplan at 1:1 + 10% bonus, worth 1.5–3¢/point, delivers 7.5–15% effective return on dining compared to 6% for Scene+ (at 1¢/point). For pure cash value, CIBC Dividend VI at 4% cash back is the top performer.
Does the Amex Cobalt work at all restaurants?
Amex acceptance at Canadian restaurants has improved significantly but remains imperfect. Major chains, sit-down restaurants, bars, and delivery apps accept Amex broadly. Some independent restaurants, food trucks, and counters at food courts may not. If you dine at Amex-limited venues regularly, pair the Cobalt with a Visa or Mastercard that also earns dining bonuses (BMO eclipse rise at 5x, or NB World Elite MC at 5x) to ensure you never miss out on dining rewards.
Does fast food earn at the restaurant rate?
Yes — fast food restaurants (McDonald's, Tim Hortons, Subway, Popeyes, etc.) code as MCC 5814 (Fast Food Restaurants) or MCC 5812, both of which earn dining bonus rates on the Cobalt, Scotia Gold Amex, NB WE MC, BMO eclipse rise, and CIBC Dividend VI. Your Tim Hortons runs earn 5x just like a sit-down dinner.
