The MBNA True Line Mastercard is built on a single, powerful premise: the lowest interest rate of any major credit card in Canada. At 8.99% per year on purchases, it costs less than half what standard credit cards charge — and there is no annual fee.

This is not a rewards card. It earns no cash back, no points, no miles. If you pay your balance in full every month, there is no reason to have it over a cash back card. But if you occasionally carry a balance — or if you're looking to consolidate high-interest debt — the True Line Mastercard can save hundreds or even thousands of dollars per year in interest.

The Interest Savings — Real Numbers

Scenario: You carry a $3,000 balance for 6 months.

Standard card at 19.99%: $3,000 × 19.99% / 2 = ~$300 in interest
MBNA True Line at 8.99%: $3,000 × 8.99% / 2 = ~$135 in interest

Savings: $165 on a single $3,000 balance over 6 months.

The arithmetic becomes more compelling at higher balances and longer carry periods. A $10,000 balance carried for a year at 19.99% costs $2,000 in interest. At 8.99%, that drops to $899 — a savings of over $1,100 per year on the same balance.

Balance Transfer Offers

MBNA frequently runs promotional balance transfer offers on the True Line Mastercard, including 0% for 12 months (with a transfer fee, typically 1–3%) for new applicants. This means:

  1. You transfer your existing high-interest credit card debt to the True Line
  2. You pay 0% interest on that balance for up to 12 months
  3. After the promotional period, the ongoing 8.99% rate applies — still far below the 19.99% you were paying before

Check MBNA's current promotional terms at the time of application — these offers change. The balance transfer option makes this card a legitimate debt consolidation tool, not just a low-rate spending card.

💡 Strategy for carrying a balance: Use the True Line for large purchases you know you'll need to pay off over several months (appliances, emergency expenses, home repairs). The 8.99% rate means you're borrowing at a rate competitive with some personal loans.

What the Card Does Not Include

The True Line Mastercard deliberately strips away everything that drives cost in premium cards:

  • No rewards program (no cash back, no points, no miles)
  • No travel insurance (no emergency medical, trip cancellation, etc.)
  • No airport lounge access
  • No purchase protection beyond standard Mastercard zero liability
  • No extended warranty benefits
  • No rental car collision coverage

For people who pay their balance in full every month, these omissions make the True Line a poor everyday card. Use a rewards card if you always pay in full — the rewards outweigh zero interest cost and you're not paying interest anyway.

Who Should Get the MBNA True Line Mastercard

This card is not for everyone. It is specifically excellent for:

  • People who sometimes carry a balance — If your bill is occasionally unpaid in full, 8.99% beats 19.99% meaningfully every single month
  • People consolidating high-interest credit card debt — Transfer high-rate balances during the 0% promotional window
  • People on fixed incomes — Predictable, low interest charges make budgeting easier than volatile high-rate cards
  • Snowbirds and seniors — Low rate as a backup card for larger US expenses that may take 2–3 months to fully pay off
  • People rebuilding credit — No income requirement and accessible approval criteria

The True Line Gold Mastercard — Is the $39 Version Worth It?

MBNA also offers the True Line Gold Mastercard at $39/yr. Both cards carry the same 8.99% purchase rate. The Gold version typically offers a longer or more aggressive balance transfer promotional rate for new cardholders. If you are specifically doing a large balance transfer, the Gold version might recoup its $39 fee through a better promotional offer. For ongoing everyday low-rate use, the no-fee True Line is sufficient.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is 8.99% really the lowest credit card interest rate in Canada?

Yes, as of 2026 the MBNA True Line Mastercard (and its Gold variant) hold the lowest standard purchase interest rate offered by a major Canadian credit card issuer. Most other low-rate cards from major banks charge 12.99%–13.99%.

Does the balance transfer 0% offer include a fee?

Yes — balance transfers at 0% typically carry a transfer fee of 1–3% of the transferred amount. Even with that fee, a 0% rate for 12 months is far cheaper than continuing to pay 19.99% on a rewards card. Calculate: (balance × transfer fee%) vs (balance × 19.99% × months/12) to see your savings.

Can I use this card alongside a rewards card?

Yes — many Canadians use a two-card approach: a rewards card for everyday purchases paid in full each month, and the True Line as a "safety net" or for planned large purchases they will pay off over time. This avoids paying 19.99% on balance carryover while still earning rewards on day-to-day spending.

Does MBNA do a hard credit check when I apply?

Yes — like all credit card applications, MBNA will perform a hard credit inquiry when you apply. This temporarily impacts your credit score by a small amount. If approved, responsible use of the card improves your score over time through on-time payments and credit utilization management.

What happens after the 0% balance transfer period ends?

After the promotional period, any remaining balance on the transferred amount begins accruing interest at the standard 8.99% purchase rate. This is still far better than the 19.99% rate on your original card, giving you additional flexibility if you haven't fully paid off the balance.
