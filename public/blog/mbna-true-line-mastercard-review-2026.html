What Is the MBNA True Line Mastercard?

The MBNA True Line Mastercard is a low-interest credit card issued by MBNA (a subsidiary of TD Bank Group in Canada). It charges a 12.99% annual interest rate on purchases and balance transfers — compared to the standard 19.99–22.99% on most rewards cards. It has no annual fee and earns no rewards points. It is purely a tool for reducing interest costs.

Interest savings example: On a $5,000 balance carried for 6 months —
Standard 19.99% card: ~$500 in interest
MBNA True Line (12.99%): ~$325 in interest
Savings: $175 in 6 months, or $350/year on a persistent $5,000 balance.

Which True Line to choose? The Gold pays off if you carry more than ~$975 in balance persistently. The rate difference (4%) × $975 balance = $39 annual fee savings. If you regularly carry $2,000+, the Gold saves ~$80/yr net. If your balance fluctuates and you pay in full some months, the no-fee base version avoids the fee in months you're at $0 balance.

Interest on a rewards card is always a bad deal: If you carry a $1,000 balance on a 19.99% rewards card for 12 months, you'll pay ~$200 in interest. That card's 2% cash back on $1,000 spend only returned $20. You lost $180 net. A no-fee low-rate card like the True Line eliminates this equation — pay less interest, then graduate to a rewards card once you're consistently paying in full each month.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I do a balance transfer to the MBNA True Line?
Yes — the MBNA True Line accepts balance transfers at 12.99% (the same as the purchase rate), which is significantly lower than the 19.99–22.99% on most Canadian rewards cards. Balance transfer offers (sometimes 0% for a promotional period) are occasionally available at application. A $5,000 balance transfer from a 19.99% card to the True Line's 12.99% saves $350/year in interest. Check current promotional balance transfer offers at application — MBNA occasionally runs 0% for 6–12 months on transfers for new cardholders.
Is MBNA in Canada affiliated with Bank of America?
MBNA Canada is owned by TD Bank Group, not Bank of America (despite the same name in the US). TD acquired MBNA Canada in 2011. MBNA credit cards in Canada are issued by TD and are distinct from US MBNA products. This means MBNA Canada cards are backed by TD's customer service infrastructure.
Does the MBNA True Line have any insurance?
The True Line offers basic purchase security (90 days against theft and damage) and extended warranty (doubles manufacturer warranty up to 1 year) on eligible purchases. It does not include travel insurance, trip cancellation, car rental CDW, or any travel-related benefits. If you also travel, hold a separate travel card (even a no-fee travel card) to cover insurance needs.

Rewardly Verdict

The MBNA True Line Mastercard is the best no-fee low-interest credit card in Canada — clear, simple, and effective for what it does. If you carry a balance even 2–3 months per year, it saves more than a rewards card earns. Once you've paid off your balance and can reliably pay in full, add a rewards card (Tangerine, BMO eclipse rise, or Amex Cobalt) and leave the True Line open to preserve your credit history. Best of both worlds: low-rate safety net + rewards on purchases you pay off monthly.
