What Is the BMO eclipse rise Visa Card?

The BMO eclipse rise Visa Card is BMO's entry-level rewards card, positioned as the stepping stone below the BMO eclipse Visa Infinite (which costs $120/year). Launched in 2022 and updated since, the eclipse rise earns 5 BMO Rewards points per $2 on five everyday categories and 1 point per $2 on everything else — all with a $0 annual fee.

What makes it stand out among no-fee cards is the breadth of its 5x categories: groceries, dining, drugstores, streaming subscriptions, AND gas — covering nearly all discretionary everyday spending. Most no-fee cards pick 1–2 elevated categories; the eclipse rise covers five.

BMO also throws in a $25 annual eco cashback credit for purchases at eligible sustainable businesses, and a 10% bonus on every point earned when you add an authorized user to the account.

What Are BMO Rewards Points Worth?

BMO Rewards points are worth approximately 0.67¢ per point when redeemed for travel through BMO's travel portal, and 0.5¢ for statement credits and gift cards. At 5 points per $2 on 5x categories:

  • 5 pts per $2 × 0.67¢ = ~1.7% effective return on 5x categories (travel redemption)
  • 5 pts per $2 × 0.5¢ = ~1.25% effective for statement credits
  • 1 pt per $2 × 0.67¢ = ~0.33% on base spending

Key insight: BMO Rewards points are most valuable for travel redemptions via BMO's travel portal. If you want cash back, the Tangerine World MC's 2% is simpler and higher. The eclipse rise shines when you redeem for travel.

The $25 Annual Eco Credit

Free money: BMO credits you $25 once per year for purchases at eco-friendly businesses. Eligible businesses include those selling organic food, electric vehicles, solar panels, and similar sustainable products/services. Check BMO's site for the approved merchant list. Effectively reduces the card's cost to -$25/year — they're paying you to hold it.

The eco credit isn't just marketing fluff — it's a real rebate credited to your statement. For many cardholders, $25/year in grocery store organic purchases or at a bike shop will qualify. Worth claiming every year.

Authorized User 10% Bonus

Adding an authorized user (spouse, family member, adult child) earns you a 10% bonus on ALL points earned by the primary cardholder. So if the primary cardholder earns 5,000 points in a month, they get 5,500 points. On a couple that both spend on the account, this adds up.

Authorized user cards are free on the eclipse rise. For couples, this is a straightforward way to boost effective earn rates by 10% at no cost.

Insurance & Benefits

For a no-fee card, the eclipse rise includes meaningful protections:

  • Mobile Device Insurance — Up to $1,000 if your phone is lost, stolen, or accidentally damaged. Pay your monthly wireless bill with the eclipse rise.
  • Purchase Protection — 90 days against theft and accidental damage on purchases made with the card.
  • Extended Warranty — Doubles the manufacturer's warranty up to 1 additional year.
  • Zero Liability — Full fraud protection on unauthorized charges.

What's not included: travel medical insurance, trip cancellation, rental car CDW, airport lounge access, or any travel benefits. This is a domestic everyday card, not a travel card.

Who Should Get This Card?

  • Students and new graduates — No income requirement, no fee, easy approval, daily category coverage.
  • First credit card — Better rewards than a standard secured card while building credit history.
  • Couples — The AU 10% bonus is a genuine uplift, and two authorized user cards costs $0.
  • Occasional travellers — Stack 5x points from everyday spending; redeem via BMO's travel portal for flights and hotels.
  • People who spend across 5 categories — If you have material spend on groceries AND dining AND gas AND streaming AND drugstores, this covers them all at once.

Who should look elsewhere: Heavy travel spenders needing travel medical insurance (get Scotiabank Gold Amex or TD Aeroplan Visa Infinite instead). Those focused on pure cash back (Tangerine World MC is simpler). Those who spend mostly at Loblaws (PC Financial WE MC earns more there).

The BMO eclipse Rise → eclipse Visa Infinite Upgrade Path

BMO designed the eclipse rise as an entry point into the eclipse product line. Once your income or credit score qualifies, you can upgrade to the BMO eclipse Visa Infinite ($120/year), which adds:

  • Complimentary Priority Pass lounge memberships (4 visits/year)
  • Travel emergency medical insurance
  • Trip cancellation & interruption insurance
  • $50 lifestyle credit (in addition to $25 eco, which continues)
  • Out-of-province medical ($5M coverage)

The 5x earn structure is identical. So if you outgrow the rise, upgrading doesn't change how you earn — just how you're protected when travelling.

Strategy: Start with the eclipse rise at $0/year to build your BMO Rewards balance. When you're ready to travel and want coverage, upgrade to eclipse Visa Infinite. Your points carry over.

Verdict

4.0 / 5

The BMO eclipse rise Visa is one of the best starter credit cards in Canada. No other no-fee card gives you 5 elevated earn categories, a built-in annual credit, and mobile device insurance in one package with zero minimum income.

Its main limitation is the BMO Rewards ecosystem itself — points are worth more through BMO's travel portal than as cash back, and there are no airline or hotel transfer partners to unlock outsized value. For pure cash back simplicity, the Tangerine World MC (2% in your chosen categories) is more straightforward.

But as a $0-fee card for everyday Canadians who eat out, fill up, grocery shop, and subscribe to streaming services — and who occasionally travel — the eclipse rise is hard to beat. It earns a solid 4.0.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the minimum income requirement for the BMO eclipse rise? BMO does not publish a minimum personal or household income requirement for the eclipse rise. This makes it accessible for students, part-time workers, and new Canadians. You still need to pass a basic credit check.
How do I get the $25 eco credit? Make purchases at eligible eco-friendly businesses (BMO publishes a list). Once your cumulative eligible spend reaches a threshold, BMO credits $25 to your statement once per calendar year. Review BMO's website for the current eligible merchant list.
What can I redeem BMO Rewards points for? Travel bookings via BMO's travel portal (best value, ~0.67¢/pt), statement credits (~0.5¢/pt), gift cards, and merchandise. Unlike TD Points (Expedia) or RBC Avion Rewards (British Airways), BMO Rewards does not have airline transfer partners — all travel must go through BMO's portal.
Does the eclipse rise have a welcome bonus? BMO periodically offers welcome bonuses. As of 2026, check BMO's current promotions — offers change frequently. Welcome bonuses have historically ranged from 10,000 to 30,000 BMO Rewards points for the eclipse lineup.
Is the BMO eclipse rise better than the BMO eclipse Visa Infinite? For everyday spending, the earn rates are identical (5x on the same categories). The Visa Infinite adds travel insurance, lounge access, and a larger lifestyle credit — worth the $120/year only if you travel regularly and will use the insurance and lounge benefits.
How does food delivery count? Is DoorDash and Uber Eats 5x? Yes — food delivery services like DoorDash, Skip the Dishes, and Uber Eats are categorized under dining/restaurants at Mastercard/Visa MCC level and earn 5x points on the eclipse rise.
