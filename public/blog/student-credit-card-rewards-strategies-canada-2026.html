Groceries are one of the most rewarding categories in the Canadian credit card market ‚Äî and for students, groceries are often the single biggest discretionary expense. Yet most students run grocery spending through a general-purpose card earning 1% or less.

Optimize the Way You Pay Tuition

Tuition payments are typically the largest single transaction a student makes ‚Äî often $3,000‚Äì$8,000 per semester ‚Äî and most students pay them by online banking or cheque, earning exactly zero rewards. With some creativity, you can capture meaningful value from these payments.

Direct credit card payments to universities are rare in Canada, but there are legal workarounds:

  • Plastiq (where available): Pay tuition via credit card through Plastiq's service for a ~1.75% fee. If your card earns 2%+ on purchases, you still come out ahead on large balances.
  • Gift card arbitrage: Some universities accept payment via gift cards purchased with credit cards. Buy the gift cards at a grocery or drug store to earn 3‚Äì5%, then pay tuition with the gift cards.
  • Parent card charging: If a parent puts tuition on their premium travel card and you repay them, the rewards go to your family's card ‚Äî still a win if you're sharing finances.
  • OSAP reimbursement timing: When OSAP arrives, use a credit card to float 30 days of expenses you'd pay anyway, earning points, then pay it off immediately when funding lands.
Caveat: Never carry a balance. The interest on a credit card (19.99%) will evaporate any rewards you earn in days. These strategies only work if you pay in full every month.
