Parks Canada Discovery Pass and Campsite Bookings

The Parks Canada Discovery Pass ($72.25/adult or $145.25/family in 2026) grants unlimited entry to over 80 national parks, national marine conservation areas, and historic sites. This is a government purchase and typically codes as government/recreation MCC rather than travel — which means it won't trigger "travel" bonus categories on most cards. Use your flat-rate card (Rogers Red 2% or BMO Eclipse Rise ~1.7%) for the Discovery Pass purchase.

Parks Canada campsite reservations through reservation.pc.gc.ca code variably — often as hotel/accommodation (7011) or recreation (7999). If they code as travel (7011), a card with a travel bonus category (like Scotiabank Gold Amex's 3x on travel) could earn bonus points. Test with a small reservation first.

Provincial park passes: Ontario Parks, BC Parks, Alberta Parks, and other provincial park systems accept credit cards for day passes and campsite fees. These typically code as recreation (7999) or government (9311) — not as travel. Use a flat-rate card for these payments rather than assuming a travel category bonus applies.
