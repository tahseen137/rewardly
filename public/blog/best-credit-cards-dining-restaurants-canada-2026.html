Best Credit Cards for Dining & Restaurants in Canada 2026

Canadians spend an average of $4,000–$8,000/year dining out. The right card earns 4–5%+ back on every restaurant bill — including food delivery apps, with top transfer-value cards unlocking even more. Here's what earns the most.

What Counts as "Dining" for Credit Cards?

Credit card dining categories are defined by Merchant Category Codes (MCCs). Most cards include MCC 5812 (Eating Places and Restaurants), MCC 5814 (Fast Food Restaurants), and increasingly MCC 5499 (Misc. Food Stores). Food delivery apps (DoorDash, Skip the Dishes, Uber Eats, SkipTheDishes) are typically classified as restaurant MCCs and earn at the dining rate.

Does Costco Food Court count? No — Costco is coded as a warehouse club, not a restaurant. Coffee shops (Starbucks, Tim Hortons) typically earn at the dining rate if coded as restaurants, but some large chain locations code as fast food or even retail. When in doubt, run a small test transaction and check the category on your statement.

Scotiabank Gold American Express Card

Best Overall
5 Scene+ pts/$ (~5%)

The Scotiabank Gold Amex earns 5 Scene+ points per dollar at restaurants and food delivery (including DoorDash, Uber Eats, Skip) — effectively 5% back since Scene+ points are worth 1¢ each. No annual cap on the dining rate. Also earns 5× on entertainment, 5× at most grocery stores (6× at Sobeys-banner stores: Sobeys, IGA, FreshCo, Safeway), and 3× on gas and transit.

Critical dining advantage: 0% foreign transaction fee — when dining abroad, you earn 5× Scene+ with no 2.5% FX drag. Compared to cards where foreign dining erases all rewards, this is a major win for travellers who eat out internationally.

$120 annual fee (waived in year 1). Amex acceptance is slightly less universal than Visa/Mastercard — a small number of restaurants may not accept it.

The Amex Cobalt earns 5 Membership Rewards points per dollar at dining and food delivery. The value unlocked via transfers to Aeroplan (1:1 + 10% bonus) can yield 2–3¢ per point on premium redemptions — making the effective dining earn rate 10–15% for sophisticated redeemers. Both cards earn 5x on dining, but the key difference is point value: Scotia Gold Scene+ points are fixed at 1¢ each (~5% back on dining), while Cobalt MR points can be transferred to Aeroplan at 1:1 with a 10% bonus — unlocking 2–3¢/pt on premium redemptions and making the Cobalt worth 10–15% on dining for expert redeemers.

Best for: Points optimizers who want to transfer Amex MR to Aeroplan and redeem for premium travel. The Cobalt's 5x MR on dining can unlock 2–3× more value than cash-equivalent programs when redeemed cleverly.

Food Delivery: Does It Count as Dining?

In almost all cases, yes. DoorDash, Uber Eats, Skip the Dishes, and SkipTheDishes are classified under restaurant MCCs and earn at your card's dining rate. One exception: Instacart is coded as grocery/delivery service, not a restaurant — it earns at the grocery rate (if applicable) rather than dining.

Important tip: if you order through a restaurant's own app (e.g., McDonald's app, Swiss Chalet app), it typically earns at the restaurant dining rate. If the payment goes through a third-party processor coded differently, verify on your statement.
