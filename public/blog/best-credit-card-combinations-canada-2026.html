Best 2-Card Credit Card Combinations in Canada 2026

No single card maximizes every spending category. The right two-card combination covers groceries, dining, travel, recurring bills, and international spending — while eliminating Amex gaps with a Visa or Mastercard backup.

Why Use Two Cards Instead of One?

The best single card earns ~4–6% on one or two categories and 1% on everything else. A well-chosen pair can earn 3–6% across multiple categories simultaneously. The key is choosing cards with complementary strengths rather than overlapping ones.

The three gaps to fill: (1) Amex acceptance — pair any Amex with a Visa/MC backup. (2) Category coverage — your primary card's weak categories become the secondary card's strong ones. (3) International use — pair domestic cards with a 0% FX card for travel.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many credit cards should I have?
Two is the sweet spot for most Canadians. One card maximizes your top spending categories; the other covers gaps (Amex backup, 0% FX, or category fill). Having 3–4 cards can improve optimization but adds complexity — you need to remember which card to use where. Beyond 4 cards, the marginal benefit rarely justifies the mental overhead.
Does holding two cards hurt my credit score?
Each new card application triggers a hard inquiry, which temporarily dips your credit score by a few points. However, having two cards generally improves your credit utilization ratio (more available credit → lower utilization %) over time, which positively impacts your score. Space applications 6+ months apart, and the net effect is typically neutral or positive.
Should both cards have the same reward currency?
Not necessarily — having two programs (e.g., Aeroplan + cash back) is fine. Having two programs that transfer TO the same place (e.g., Amex MR + Aeroplan → both convertible to Aeroplan) can be powerful: you maximize earn in multiple categories but consolidate points in one account for redemption. Avoid programs that conflict or have incompatible redemptions.
