Credit Card Foreign Transaction Fee in Canada 2026

Most Canadian cards charge 2.5% on every USD, EUR, or other foreign-currency purchase. On a $5,000 international trip, that's $125 in hidden fees. Here's exactly how to avoid it.

What Is a Foreign Transaction Fee?

A foreign transaction fee (also called an FX fee or cross-border fee) is a surcharge added to credit card purchases made in a foreign currency or processed through a foreign bank. In Canada, virtually all major bank credit cards charge approximately 2.5% of the transaction amount on any purchase not billed in Canadian dollars.

This fee applies in two scenarios: physically making purchases in another country, and making online purchases where the merchant charges in USD, EUR, GBP, or any non-CAD currency — even while sitting at home in Canada. Amazon.com (as opposed to Amazon.ca), Netflix US, Airbnb for foreign properties, international online retailers, and US software companies often charge in USD, triggering the FX fee without you ever leaving Canada.

The Math: Why 2.5% Is a Big Deal

FX Fee Real-World Cost Examples

Two-week US road trip: $3,000 USD spending
FX fee at 2.5%: $75 CAD (at ~1.36 USD/CAD exchange) = approximately $102 CAD in fees

European vacation: €4,000 spending
FX fee at 2.5%: €100 = approximately $150 CAD in fees

Annual Amazon.com purchases (USD): $500 USD
FX fee at 2.5%: $12.50 USD = approximately $17 CAD per year — even without leaving home

Annual US software subscriptions (Netflix, Adobe, Spotify USD): $300 USD
FX fee at 2.5%: $7.50 USD = approximately $10 CAD per year, hidden in your bill

The combined FX fees from international purchases and US-billed subscriptions can easily add up to $200–400 per year for a moderately active Canadian traveller and online shopper — easily erasing the value of rewards earned at a 1% earn rate.

The Online Shopping Trap: FX Fees at Home

FX fees don't require international travel. Many Canadians unknowingly pay FX fees on routine online purchases at home:

Amazon.com vs Amazon.ca: If you're an Amazon Prime member and shop at Amazon.com (not .ca), purchases are in USD — FX fee applies. US-based streaming services priced in USD: some Netflix plans, Hulu if accessible, Disney+ USD pricing. US software: Microsoft 365 from US site, Adobe Creative Cloud from US, Shopify fees if billed in USD. US online retailers: ASOS US, Revolve, SSENSE (often USD), any US store shipping to Canada.

Rule of thumb: Any time the price shows USD, EUR, or any non-CAD symbol at checkout, you'll be charged an FX fee if your card has one. For USD purchases, Rogers Red, Scotiabank Passport, or Home Trust Preferred all work. For EUR, GBP, or other non-USD currencies, use Scotiabank Passport or Home Trust Preferred — Rogers Red still charges 2.5% FX on non-USD purchases.
