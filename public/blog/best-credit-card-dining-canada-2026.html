Dining out and ordering in are two of the top three spending categories for most Canadians under 40. The right credit card can earn 5x points or 2–4% cashback on every restaurant bill, delivery order, and coffee run — with no extra effort.

How dining purchases code

Credit card categories are assigned by Merchant Category Codes (MCCs), not by you. Dining rewards typically apply to:

  • MCC 5812 — Eating Places and Restaurants (most sit-down and fast food)
  • MCC 5814 — Fast Food Restaurants
  • MCC 5411 coded orders — Some grocery stores with restaurants inside

Food delivery apps (Uber Eats, DoorDash, Skip) typically code as MCC 5812 and earn dining rates. Tim Hortons and Starbucks inside grocery stores may code as grocery rather than restaurant — check your statement to confirm.

Best card for Uber Eats, DoorDash, and Skip

Food delivery apps (Uber Eats, DoorDash, SkipTheDishes) code as restaurant/dining (MCC 5812) for most Canadian cards. This means your dining card's full earn rate applies to delivery orders — not just restaurant visits.

The best cards for food delivery:

  1. Amex Cobalt — 5x MR points. At 50L per order, 10+ orders/month = 60,000+ points annually just from delivery
  2. CIBC Dividend — 2% cashback, confirmed to work on Uber Eats
  3. TD Cash Back Visa Infinite — 3% on dining including delivery
  4. Tangerine — 2% if restaurants is one of your selected categories, no annual fee
Uber Eats Amex Cobalt hack: If you spend $500+/month on Uber Eats (common for families ordering dinner 3–4 nights/week), the Amex Cobalt earns 5x = 2,500+ MR points per order. Transferred to Aeroplan 1:1, that's real travel value.

FAQ

Does Tim Hortons count as dining or grocery?

Tim Hortons standalone locations code as restaurant (MCC 5814 — Fast Food) and earn dining rates. Tim Hortons inside a Walmart or grocery store may code as grocery. Check your statement if you're unsure.

Does Starbucks count as dining?

Usually. Standalone Starbucks locations code as restaurants or cafes and earn dining rates. Starbucks purchases inside bookstores (Chapters/Indigo) or grocery stores may code differently.

Does DoorDash count as dining?

Yes. DoorDash, Uber Eats, and SkipTheDishes all use MCC 5812 (Eating Places) and earn dining category rates on cards with dining categories. Confirmed on Amex Cobalt, CIBC Dividend, and TD Cash Back.

Is the Amex Cobalt accepted everywhere?

American Express has lower acceptance than Visa/Mastercard in Canada. Most sit-down restaurants take Amex, but some fast food, food courts, and food delivery platforms (SkipTheDishes) historically preferred Visa/Mastercard. Uber Eats and DoorDash both accept Amex.

What if I eat at both nice restaurants and fast food regularly?

Any card with a broad "dining/restaurant" category will cover both MCC 5812 and 5814. Amex Cobalt and CIBC Dividend both earn dining rates across sit-down and fast food alike.
