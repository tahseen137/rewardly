The Big Differentiator: 0% Foreign Transaction Fee

Most Canadian credit cards charge a 2.5% foreign transaction fee on every purchase made in a non-CAD currency. The Scotiabank Passport VI is one of the few major bank Visa Infinite cards to waive this entirely — paying 0% on any foreign currency transaction.

Airport Lounge Access

The Passport VI includes 6 complimentary DragonPass airport lounge visits per year. DragonPass provides access to 1,300+ airport lounges worldwide, including Priority Pass network lounges.

Each lounge visit is typically worth $30–$45 USD. Six visits = $180–$270 USD in annual lounge value — nearly offsetting the $150 annual fee before counting FX savings or Scene+ earn. Additional visits beyond 6 can be purchased at ~$32 CAD each.

Tip: Book your DragonPass visits at the right lounges. North American domestic terminals often have lower-quality lounges. Use your visits on international departures at major hubs (Pearson Terminal 3, Vancouver, Montreal) for the best food, drinks, and shower facilities.

Who Should Get the Scotiabank Passport Visa Infinite?

The Passport VI is purpose-built for frequent international travelers who regularly make purchases in USD, EUR, or other foreign currencies. If you travel internationally 2+ times per year, the 0% FX fee alone saves more than the annual fee costs — and you get the lounge access and travel insurance on top.

It's not the best choice if your primary goal is maximizing rewards on Canadian everyday spending (groceries, gas, dining). The Scotia Gold Amex earns significantly more on those categories — and also has 0% FX. Many travelers pair the two: Passport VI when spending abroad, Scotia Gold Amex for domestic categories.

Verdict: Excellent for International Travelers

The Scotiabank Passport Visa Infinite is a strong, well-rounded travel card — the 0% FX fee is the standout feature, and the 6 lounge visits plus 25-day medical make the $150 fee easy to justify for anyone who travels internationally at least once a year. The main weakness is the earn rate on domestic spending, where the Scotia Gold Amex dominates. Best used as a travel-specific card paired with a higher-earning domestic card.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does the Scotiabank Passport VI have a minimum income requirement?
The standard Visa Infinite income requirement is $60,000 personal or $100,000 household. Scotiabank does not always enforce this strictly — applicants with strong credit profiles have been approved below these thresholds. Apply and let Scotiabank's decision stand.
Is the annual fee waived in the first year?
As of 2026, the annual fee is typically not waived in the first year for standard applicants, though Scotiabank runs occasional promotional offers (particularly through referral links or branch applications) with first-year fee waivers. Check current promotions before applying.
Can I use the Passport VI at Costco?
Yes — Costco Canada accepts Visa, so the Passport VI works there. However, Costco MCC (5300) may not earn the 3x Scene+ rate in some categories — most likely earns at the base 1x rate. Verify with Scotiabank.
How do I access DragonPass lounges?
Register your Scotiabank Passport VI at DragonPass.com or through the DragonPass app to receive your digital membership card. Present the app at lounge reception. Your 6 complimentary visits are loaded per calendar year, not per anniversary year — unused visits do not roll over.
