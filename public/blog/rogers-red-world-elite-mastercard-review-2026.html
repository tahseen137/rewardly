The Rogers Red World Elite Mastercard is uniquely positioned in the Canadian credit card market: it's the only no-fee card that combines a flat 1.5% cashback on ALL Canadian dollar purchases with zero foreign transaction fees on eligible foreign currency purchases. For Canadians who spend in USD regularly — whether through US shopping, Amazon.com, US subscriptions, or cross-border trips — the combination is genuinely unmatched at zero cost.

The no-FX-fee nuance: The Rogers card charges zero FX fees on USD purchases only (US dollars). Purchases in other currencies (EUR, GBP, MXN, etc.) still incur the standard 2.5% FX fee. For a dedicated no-FX card for all international currencies, the Scotiabank Passport Visa Infinite is the better choice.

At 1.5% on all CAD purchases with no cap, the Rogers card earns more than Tangerine's 0.5% base rate on non-bonus purchases. It's the best flat-rate no-fee option in Canada for pure simplicity: one card, no category selection, no tracking — just 1.5% back on everything.

Who Actually Benefits Most

Rogers and Fido wireless customers

If you're already a Rogers or Fido customer and pay your bill with this card, you earn 1.5% and can apply that cashback directly to your Rogers/Fido bill. For a $120/month Rogers wireless plan, that's $21.60/year back — plus whatever you earn on other spending. Not huge, but zero-cost.

Canadians who shop on US websites

This is the card's most compelling use case. Canadians who regularly buy from Amazon.com, US online stores, US subscription services (if billed in USD), or make purchases while in the US benefit from two things simultaneously: 1.5% cashback earned AND no 2.5% FX fee paid.

On a $2,000 USD spend: most Canadian cards would charge $50 in FX fees and earn ~$20 in rewards → net cost: $30. Rogers Red: $0 FX fees + $30 in cashback → net gain: $30. That's a $60 swing per $2,000 USD spent.

Simple spenders who don't want to optimize

Not everyone wants to pick categories (Tangerine), maximize travel points (Aeroplan), or worry about annual fees (Scotia Momentum). For people who want one card that earns a decent flat rate on everything with no annual fee — the Rogers Red World Elite delivers exactly that.

The Income Requirement Catch

The Rogers Red World Elite requires $80,000 personal income or $150,000 household income — the same as the BMO World Elite Mastercard and PC Insiders World Elite. This is the main barrier. If you don't meet the income threshold, you won't be approved for this card.

Lower-income Canadians looking for a simple no-fee cashback card should look at: Tangerine Money-Back (no income minimum), the basic PC Mastercard (no income minimum), or Home Trust Preferred Visa ($15,000 income). Rogers does not offer a lower-tier cashback card.

Cashback Redemption

Rogers cashback can be redeemed in two ways:

  • Statement credit: Redeem $10+ as a statement credit against any purchase. Simple and flexible.
  • Rogers/Fido/Shaw bill credit: Apply cashback directly to your Rogers, Fido, or Shaw bill. Same $10 minimum, equally simple if you're a Rogers customer.

No points to track, no partners to manage, no minimum redemption period. Cashback accumulates and can be redeemed when you choose.

The Best Two-Card Setup with Rogers Red

The Rogers Red World Elite works exceptionally well as a "fallback" card in a two-card setup:

  • Primary: Tangerine Money-Back (2% in chosen categories — groceries + recurring bills) OR PC World Elite (4.5% at Loblaws/Shoppers)
  • Rogers Red: Everything else at 1.5%, plus all USD purchases with no FX fee

This two-card combination covers every spending scenario — bonus rates where your top spend happens, plus 1.5% on all the leftovers — at zero annual fee total.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to be a Rogers customer to get the Rogers Mastercard?

No — you don't need to be a Rogers or Fido customer to apply for the Rogers Red World Elite Mastercard. Anyone who meets the income requirement can apply. However, Rogers customers get the additional benefit of applying cashback directly to their Rogers/Fido bill.

Does Rogers Red have no FX fees on ALL currencies?

No — the zero FX fee on Rogers Red applies only to purchases in US dollars (USD). Purchases in other foreign currencies (Euro, British Pound, Mexican Peso, etc.) still incur the standard 2.5% foreign transaction fee. For zero FX fees on all currencies, consider the Scotiabank Passport Visa Infinite or Scotiabank Gold American Express (both have no FX on all currencies, but with annual fees).

Is there a Rogers World Mastercard (lower income tier)?

As of 2026, Rogers only offers the World Elite Mastercard at the $80,000/$150,000 income threshold. There is no lower-income version of the Rogers rewards Mastercard. Eligible Rogers/Fido customers who don't meet the World Elite threshold should consider the Tangerine Money-Back Mastercard or PC Financial basic Mastercard as alternatives.

How much cashback can I earn monthly?

There is no monthly or annual cap on cashback earned with the Rogers Red World Elite. If you charge $10,000/month to the card, you earn $150/month in cashback. Accumulate and redeem when it suits you (minimum $10).
