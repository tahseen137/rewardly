Priority Pass vs. DragonPass — What's the Difference?

In practice, for Canadian travellers: almost nothing. Both networks provide access to 1,300+ airport lounges worldwide. The same physical lounges are typically accessible through either program — you present your card (DragonPass card or Priority Pass card issued with your credit card) at the lounge entrance.

The minor differences: Priority Pass is more established globally and has slightly broader coverage in some Asian airports. DragonPass has a stronger app with real-time lounge information and digital access. For access to Canadian airport lounges specifically, both programs work identically.

Air Canada Maple Leaf Lounges are NOT in Priority Pass or DragonPass: To access Air Canada's own lounges, you need: Amex Platinum's 6 annual Maple Leaf passes, an Air Canada Executive Platinum/Altitude status, or to pay $75 CAD at the door. The Centurion Lounge at YYZ (Toronto Pearson) is Amex-only (Amex Platinum / Centurion card required).

Frequently Asked Questions

Does credit card lounge access work when flying economy?
Yes — lounge access through credit cards works regardless of the cabin class you're flying. Priority Pass, DragonPass, and Centurion lounge access is granted based on your credit card membership, not your ticket class. This is one of the biggest advantages of card-based lounge access vs. status-based access (where economy flyers are typically excluded). As long as you're departing from an airport with a covered lounge, you can enjoy it even on a budget economy flight.
Can I bring guests into the lounge with my credit card?
Guest access varies by card. Amex Platinum includes the cardholder + 2 guests free at Centurion Lounges. At Priority Pass and DragonPass lounges, guests typically cost an additional $30–$35 USD per person per visit (charged to your credit card). Some premium cards offer 1 free guest visit — check your specific cardholder guide. For frequent family travellers, the per-guest fee can add up — in that case, the Amex Platinum's family-friendly unlimited Centurion access (2 free guests) may be worth the higher annual fee.
