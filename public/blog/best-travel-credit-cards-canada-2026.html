Best Travel Credit Cards in Canada 2026 (Compared)

Amex Cobalt, TD First Class, CIBC Aventura, and RBC Avion side-by-side on earn rates, fees, insurance, and real-world value.
February 28, 2026 · 12 min read · By Rewardly Team

Canada has some of the best travel credit cards in the world — but also some of the most confusing. Multiple currencies (Scene+, Aeroplan, Avion, Aventura, MR points), varying redemption values, and wildly different earn structures make it genuinely hard to figure out which card actually earns you the most.

This guide compares the top four Canadian travel credit cards in 2026: the American Express Cobalt, TD First Class Travel Visa Infinite, CIBC Aventura Visa Infinite, and RBC Avion Visa Infinite. Side-by-side on the numbers that matter.

#1 Pick: American Express Cobalt Card

Our Top Pick

Annual fee: $155.88/year ($12.99/month)
Welcome bonus: Up to 15,000 MR points per month in year 1 (when spending $500+/mo)
Point value: 1.5–2.0 cents/point for flights via Aeroplan transfer

Earn Rates

  • 5x Membership Rewards points on food & dining (restaurants, food delivery, cafes)
  • 3x MR points on streaming, transit, and gas
  • 2x MR points on travel purchases
  • 1x MR point on everything else

Why the Cobalt Wins

The Cobalt earns 5x Membership Rewards on food — and MR points transfer 1:1 to Aeroplan. That means you’re effectively earning 5 Aeroplan points per dollar on every restaurant meal, DoorDash order, and grocery run. At 1.5–2 cents per Aeroplan point for business class redemptions, that’s 7.5–10 cents of value per dollar spent on food. No other mainstream Canadian card comes close.

The broader MR ecosystem is also the most flexible in Canada. Transfer to Aeroplan, Avios (British Airways), Marriott, and a dozen other programs. Or redeem for travel through Amex’s travel portal at 1 cent/point. You’re never locked in.

Travel Insurance

  • Emergency medical (up to $5M, 15-day trips)
  • Flight delay and baggage coverage
  • Car rental theft & damage
  • Hotel burglary

Best For

Canadians who spend heavily on food, dining, and delivery. The ideal Cobalt cardholder is a millennial or urban professional spending $600–$1,200/month on restaurants and groceries who wants to convert that into flights.

#2 Pick: TD First Class Travel Visa Infinite

Strong Contender

Annual fee: $139 (often waived year 1)
Welcome bonus: Up to 100,000 TD Points in year 1 (varies by offer)
Point value: 0.5–0.56 cents/point via ExpediaForTD

Earn Rates

  • 8 TD Points/$ on travel booked through ExpediaForTD.com
  • 3 TD Points/$ on groceries, dining, and streaming
  • 2 TD Points/$ on everything else

Why It’s Strong

The 8x earn rate on ExpediaForTD is the highest travel-on-travel earn rate of any card on this list. If you book hotels, rental cars, and flights through that portal, you accumulate TD Points fast. The welcome bonus (often 100,000 points in promotional periods) is one of the largest first-year offers available from any Canadian bank.

The 3x on groceries and dining keeps pace with most competitors. And as a Visa Infinite card, it’s accepted everywhere — solving Amex Cobalt’s acceptance gaps at Costco and some small retailers.

The Key Limitation

TD Points are worth ~0.5–0.56 cents/point when redeemed through ExpediaForTD. That’s lower than Aeroplan or Avion for premium flight redemptions. The flexibility is there, but the ceiling on points value is lower than the Cobalt for sophisticated travelers willing to optimize transfers.

Travel Insurance

  • Emergency medical (up to $2M, 21-day trips)
  • Trip cancellation and interruption
  • Flight delay and baggage delay coverage
  • Rental car insurance
  • Common carrier accidental death

Best For

TD banking customers who want a large welcome bonus, book hotels and rental cars through Expedia-style portals, and want full Visa acceptance without Amex gaps.

#3 Pick: CIBC Aventura Visa Infinite

Annual fee: $139 (often waived year 1)
Welcome bonus: Up to 45,000 Aventura points
Point value: ~1–2 cents/point depending on redemption

Earn Rates

  • 3 Aventura pts/$ on travel booked through the CIBC Rewards Centre
  • 2 pts/$ on groceries, gas, and drug stores
  • 1.5 pts/$ on everything else

Why It’s Different

The Aventura’s strong suit is everyday grocery and gas earn (2x) plus a genuinely useful perk that the others lack at this fee tier: 4 complimentary Priority Pass airport lounge visits per year. For travelers departing Canadian airports 3–4 times annually, those lounge visits alone are worth $130–$200 in saved access fees.

Aventura points redeem for any travel purchase as a statement credit — no portal required, no blackout dates, no airline-specific constraints. The simplest redemption model on this list.

Best For

CIBC banking customers who fly 2–4 times/year, want lounge access at this fee tier, and prefer straightforward travel credits over transfer optimization.

#4 Pick: RBC Avion Visa Infinite

Annual fee: $120 (often waived year 1)
Welcome bonus: Up to 35,000 Avion points
Point value: Up to 2+ cents/point via airline transfers

Earn Rates

  • 1.25 Avion pts/$ on travel
  • 1.0 pt/$ on everything else
  • Petro-Canada bonus (extra points + per-litre discount when linked)

Why It’s on the List

The Avion’s flat earn rates look modest, but Avion Rewards has valuable airline transfer partners — including British Airways Avios and WestJet Rewards — that can unlock high-value redemptions unavailable on TD or CIBC’s programs. If you know how to find British Airways Avios sweet spots (short-haul North American flights at 7,500–15,000 Avios), the ceiling on Avion value is higher than most Canadians realize.

At $120/year, it’s also the cheapest card on this list — meaningful if you want a lower-cost entry to travel points without a $139+ annual fee.

Best For

RBC customers, Petro-Canada regulars, and travelers who want to explore airline transfer partner redemptions (especially British Airways Avios) to extract maximum value from their points.

How to Choose

Pick the Amex Cobalt if you spend heavily on food and dining and want maximum flexibility with your points. The 5x earn rate on MR points (which transfer to Aeroplan 1:1) is the strongest food-to-travel conversion in Canada.
Pick TD First Class if you want a massive welcome bonus, book hotels and travel through portals, and need full Visa acceptance. Best for TD customers with promotional offers.
Pick CIBC Aventura if you want Priority Pass lounge access at the $139 fee tier, prefer simple statement-credit redemptions, and bank with CIBC. The 2x on groceries and gas is also competitive.
Pick RBC Avion if you’re an RBC customer who banks primarily at Petro-Canada, wants transfer partner flexibility (especially British Airways Avios), or prefers the lowest annual fee on this list.

The Bottom Line

For most Canadians: Amex Cobalt

The 5x on food & dining is simply unmatched at this fee tier, and the MR points ecosystem is the most flexible in Canada. If you spend at restaurants and want your daily coffee to pay for your next flight — this is your card.

But the right card depends on your spending.

A household spending $600/month on groceries, $200 on gas, and flying Air Canada quarterly will get completely different results than a frequent traveler booking through Expedia. The math is different for everyone.
