PC Financial Mastercard Review 2026

World Elite vs World vs Basic — which PC Mastercard earns the most PC Optimum points at Loblaws, No Frills, and Shoppers Drug Mart?

PC Financial Mastercards are the credit cards of choice for Canadians who shop at Loblaws, Real Canadian Superstore, No Frills, Shoppers Drug Mart, or Joe Fresh. They earn PC Optimum points that you can redeem for free groceries — and the stacking opportunities between the card's earn rate, in-store PC Optimum offers, and bonus events can push your return to 20%+ on a single grocery run.

There are three tiers of PC Financial Mastercard, all with no annual fee. The difference is earn rates and income requirements.

The PC World Elite is one of the best no-fee credit cards in Canada — period. The 45 pts/$1 (4.5% return) at Loblaws-family stores is the highest grocery earn rate of any no-fee card in Canada. It even beats the no-fee BMO Air Miles and the no-fee TD Aeroplan cards on their best categories.

The Shoppers Drug Mart 30 pts/$1 (3% return) is exceptional. Most Canadians spend $100–$300/month at Shoppers on prescriptions, beauty, and household items — that's $3–$9 back per month just at Shoppers without a single grocery trip.

The World Elite also includes travel insurance — rare for a no-fee card:

  • Out-of-province emergency travel medical (up to $1 million, 10 days)
  • Trip cancellation / trip interruption insurance
  • Car rental collision waiver (32 consecutive days)

The income requirement ($80,000 personal) is the barrier. If you meet it, this card is one of the easiest approval decisions in Canada — it's no-fee, earns more than most paid cards at your primary grocery store, and includes travel insurance.

PC Optimum Stacking — How to Earn 20%+ at Loblaws

The PC Financial Mastercard's earn rate is just the baseline. The real power is stacking it with in-store PC Optimum offers:

  1. PC Optimum app offers: Every week, the PC Optimum app shows personalized "Offers" that trigger bonus points (e.g., "Earn 5,000 bonus PC Optimum points when you spend $40 on produce"). Activate these before shopping.
  2. PC Optimum Insiders membership: A paid membership ($99/yr) that gives higher everyday earn rates and exclusive bonus offers — worth it if you spend $400+/month at Loblaws stores.
  3. Shoppers Drug Mart Thursday events: Shoppers regularly runs "Spend $50, earn 5,000 bonus points" or "Spend $75, earn 15,000 bonus points" events on specific Thursdays. Stack with your PC World Elite card's base 30 pts/$1 earn.
  4. PC Travel offers: Booking travel through PC Travel sometimes triggers 5× or 10× bonus points. PC Travel is powered by Expedia.
  5. Loblaws bonus weekends: Periodically Loblaws runs "20× points" weekends on all purchases. Combined with your PC World Elite base rate (4.5%), this can deliver 20%+ return on a single grocery trip.
Stack example: A typical well-stacked Shoppers Thursday:
• PC World Elite base: 30 pts/$1 = 3%
• Thursday promo: "Earn 15,000 bonus pts on $75 spend" = 200 pts/$1 at that spend level
• Personalized in-app offer: "Earn 2,000 bonus pts on fragrance" = extra 2,000 pts
Effective return on a $75 Shoppers run: 25–30%

Who Should Get a PC Financial Mastercard?

  • Loblaws/Real Canadian Superstore/No Frills regular shoppers — the 4.5% base earn at PC stores (World Elite) is unmatched for grocery earn at zero annual fee
  • Shoppers Drug Mart frequenters — 3% at Shoppers with World Elite is exceptional for prescriptions, beauty, and household items
  • PC Optimum point collectors — if you already use the PC Optimum app and collect points, this card accelerates your earn dramatically vs. using another card at PC stores
  • Students and new Canadians — the basic PC Mastercard has no income minimum and still earns 2.5% at PC stores (better than most student credit cards)

Who Should Look Elsewhere?

  • Costco shoppers — Costco accepts Mastercard but doesn't participate in PC Optimum. You earn only 10 pts/$1 (1%) at Costco on a PC card. The Triangle World Elite from Canadian Tire earns cashback at Costco.
  • Walmart shoppers — Walmart doesn't participate in PC Optimum either. Use a flat-rate cashback card at Walmart.
  • International travelers — PC Financial cards charge 2.5% FX fee. Use Scotiabank Passport Visa Infinite for international purchases.
  • Travel rewards seekers — PC Optimum points can be redeemed for travel through PC Travel, but the value is typically lower than Aeroplan or Avion for flights. If maximizing travel is the goal, a dedicated travel card serves better.
Bottom line: The PC Insiders World Elite Mastercard is one of the best no-fee credit cards in Canada — full stop. 4.5% return at Loblaws-family stores, 3% at Shoppers Drug Mart, travel insurance included, and no annual fee. If you shop at Loblaws-brand stores and meet the $80,000 income threshold, this is a no-brainer card to hold. Stack it with the PC Optimum app offers and you'll routinely achieve 10–20% returns on grocery trips that would earn 4% cashback on competitor cards.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the PC Financial World Elite the same as PC Insiders World Elite?

Same card, same earn rates. "PC Insiders" was added to the name as part of a rebranding to connect the card with the PC Insiders loyalty program tier. If you have the PC Financial World Elite Mastercard, you already have the highest-tier card in the PC Financial lineup.

How much is a PC Optimum point worth?

10,000 PC Optimum points = $10 off at Loblaws-family stores (including Loblaws, No Frills, Real Canadian Superstore, Fortinos, Zehrs, Maxi, Provigo) and Shoppers Drug Mart. That means 1 PC Optimum point = 0.1¢. At 45 pts/$1 (PC World Elite at PC stores), you're earning 4.5% back in grocery discounts.

Can I use PC Optimum points for travel?

Yes — PC Travel (powered by Expedia) accepts PC Optimum points at a rate of 1,000 pts = $1 for flights, hotels, and car rentals (same as the grocery value). You won't get above-average travel value from PC points (unlike Aeroplan in business class), but it's consistent and flexible across any airline/hotel on Expedia.

Does the PC World Elite have a credit limit minimum?

PC Financial sets credit limits based on your income and credit history. World Elite Mastercards typically require a minimum credit limit of $5,000 as per Mastercard's World Elite standards. If PC Financial approves you for World Elite, your limit will be at least $5,000.

Can I hold a PC Financial Mastercard and a Scotia Momentum card at the same time?

Yes — and this is actually a popular two-card strategy. Use the PC World Elite at Loblaws/Shoppers for the highest grocery earn, and Scotia Momentum at all other grocery stores (4% cashback) and gas stations (4%). This covers you optimally at PC-brand stores and everywhere else.
