The Tangerine Money-Back Credit Card is one of Canada's most popular no-fee cashback cards — and for good reason. It lets you earn 2% cashback in 2 spending categories of your choice (or 3 categories if you deposit your cashback into a Tangerine savings account), with no annual fee. The catch: everything outside your chosen categories earns only 0.5%.

This is simultaneously the card's strength and weakness: at 2%, your chosen categories are competitive with premium paid cards. But at 0.5% on everything else, your non-category spending earns 4× less than the Amex SimplyCash Preferred's 2% base rate. The key is identifying your top 2 categories and routing almost all spending there.

Category Selection Strategy

The 2 (or 3) categories you choose should cover the highest monthly spend categories for your household. For most Canadian families, the optimal picks are:

  • Best overall pick: Groceries (Category 1) + Recurring Bills (Category 2) → covers food + all your monthly subscriptions/telecom
  • For foodies: Restaurants + Groceries → 2% on all food spending
  • For commuters: Gas + Groceries → 2% on driving costs + food
  • For renters with high telecoms: Recurring Bills + Restaurants → subscriptions + dining

With 3 categories (deposit cashback to Tangerine savings account), add: Gas, Dining, or Drugstore as your 3rd category depending on your spending profile.

Pro tip: Add a 3rd category for free. If you direct your cashback to a Tangerine savings account (instead of a statement credit), you automatically unlock a 3rd bonus category at 2%. This effectively gives you grocery + recurring bills + restaurants all at 2% — a powerful sweep of most everyday spending, at no annual fee.

Cashback Value — Monthly vs Annual Payout

Tangerine pays cashback monthly — credited to your statement (or savings account) the month after you earn it. This is an advantage over CIBC Dividend (annual January payout) and Amex SimplyCash (annual February payout).

The cashback is credited as real money with no redemption process. No points, no catalogs, no minimums. Every dollar of cashback is immediately usable.

Tangerine is the only no-fee card that lets you choose your bonus categories. If your highest spend is in an unusual category (home improvement, furniture, hotels), Tangerine is one of the few cards that offers 2% there with zero fee. CIBC Dividend VI offers 4% on groceries and gas; Scotia Momentum offers 4% on groceries, streaming, and recurring bills (2% on gas) — but both are annual-fee cards ($120/yr), and all other categories drop to 1–2%.

However: if you spend heavily outside your 2 chosen categories, Tangerine's 0.5% base rate is a drag. Someone spending $2,000/month with $500 in groceries and $1,500 in varied expenses earns: $10 + $7.50 = $17.50/month on Tangerine vs $30 on Amex SimplyCash Preferred (2% on all). The $99 fee on Amex is covered in 8 months for a high-volume spender.

Welcome Bonus

Tangerine frequently offers a welcome bonus of 10% cashback on purchases in your first 2 months (up to $150 back, capping out at $1,500 in spend). This is simple and achievable for most applicants — spend $1,500 in your first 2 months and earn $150 back.

At $150 bonus + zero annual fee, the first year is clearly worthwhile for almost anyone who spends $750+/month on any type of purchase.

Who Should Get the Tangerine Money-Back Card?

  • No-fee seekers who want a higher rate than 1% on their biggest categories — if grocery and recurring bills are your top spend, 2% with no fee beats most cards with annual fees on those specific categories
  • People with non-standard spending patterns — if you spend heavily on home improvement, furniture, or hotels (categories most other cards pay 1%), Tangerine's choice model lets you earn 2% there
  • New Canadians and students — $12,000/year income requirement is among the lowest in Canada; easy to get approved; builds credit history
  • Tangerine banking customers — seamless integration with a Tangerine savings/chequing account if you bank there
  • Monthly cashback preference — monthly payout is better than CIBC's annual January dump

Who Should Look Elsewhere?

  • Heavy-everything spenders — if 60%+ of your spend falls outside your 2 categories, Amex SimplyCash Preferred ($99/yr, 2% on everything) earns more
  • Gas + grocery maximizers — Scotiabank Momentum earns 4% on both (vs Tangerine's 2% on each); worth the $120 fee if you spend $500+/month on those categories
  • International travelers — 2.5% FX fee wipes out the 2% earn on any foreign purchase
  • Travel rewards collectors — no points program, just pure cashback; if you want Aeroplan or BMO Rewards, look elsewhere

Final Verdict

Rating: 4.1/5. The Tangerine Money-Back Card is excellent for no-fee cashback within your chosen categories — and the category flexibility is genuinely unique in Canada. If your top 2 spending categories match the available options (and they usually do), you get 2% cashback with zero cost. The main limitation is the 0.5% base rate, which means you should always use a different card (ideally one that earns 1%+ on everything) for purchases outside your chosen categories. Combined with a card like Rogers World Elite (1.5% on everything) or the no-fee PC Mastercard, Tangerine becomes the category booster in a two-card setup.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I change my Tangerine cashback categories?

Yes — you can change your bonus cashback categories twice per year (January and July). Log in to your Tangerine online account or the app and select new categories. Changes take effect at the start of the following month.

How does the 3rd category work with a Tangerine savings account?

If you choose to deposit your cashback earnings into a Tangerine savings account (instead of taking it as a statement credit), Tangerine automatically adds a 3rd bonus category for free. The 3rd category is chosen by you at signup. To switch to savings deposit, log in and change your cashback destination in the Rewards section.

Does the Tangerine card have a credit limit?

Yes — Tangerine sets your credit limit based on your income and credit history. There's no stated minimum limit, but typical approvals for new applicants range from $1,000–$10,000. You can request a limit increase after 6+ months of good payment history.

Is the Tangerine card a Mastercard or Visa?

The Tangerine Money-Back Credit Card is a Mastercard. It's accepted anywhere Mastercard is accepted — including most major retailers, Costco in-store (Costco doesn't take Visa), and online merchants.

What's the Tangerine World Mastercard?

There's also a Tangerine World Mastercard with the same 2% category structure but adding mobile device insurance, rental car collision waiver, and extended warranty. It requires $60,000 personal or $100,000 household income. If you qualify and want those extras, it's worth getting the World over the base card — same earn rates, better insurance, still $0 annual fee.
