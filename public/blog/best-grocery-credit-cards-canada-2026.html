Best Credit Cards for Groceries in Canada 2026

Canadian families spend $800–$1,500/month on groceries. Earn 4–6% back on every shopping trip. Here's what earns the most at Loblaws, Sobeys, Metro, Walmart, and Costco.

Why Groceries Are the #1 Category to Optimize

Groceries are the largest single spending category for most Canadian households — typically $800–$1,500/month ($9,600–$18,000/year). Earning 1% vs. 6% on that spend is the difference between $96/year and $1,080/year. No other credit card category optimization comes close in absolute dollar value for the average family.

Real-world math: A family spending $12,000/year on groceries earns: 1% card = $120 · 2% = $240 · 4% = $480 · 6% = $720. Upgrading from a 1% to a 6% grocery card means an extra $600/year in rewards — more than enough to justify a $120 annual fee.

Top Grocery Credit Cards in Canada (2026)

#1

Scotiabank Gold American Express Card

$120/yr · Scene+ · Any Grocery Store
Highest Grocery Rate
5 Scene+ pts/$ (~5%)

The Scotiabank Gold Amex is Canada's highest-earning grocery card at major Canadian supermarkets. 5 Scene+ points per dollar at most grocery stores — no cap. Scene+ = 1¢/pt, so 5x = 5% effective return. Earn 6 pts/$ (6%) at Sobeys-banner stores: Sobeys, IGA, FreshCo, Safeway, and Farm Boy.

Works at 5x: Metro, Loblaws, No Frills, Whole Foods, Costco (Amex accepted), independent and ethnic grocery stores. Works at 6x: Sobeys-banner stores only (Sobeys, IGA, FreshCo, Safeway, Farm Boy). Tip: Present your Scene+ card at Sobeys/IGA to earn Scene+ loyalty points ON TOP of the 6x credit card points.

$120/year annual fee (waived year 1). 0% FX fee — if you buy imported or specialty items at foreign-priced grocery stores, no extra fee. Travel insurance included. Also earns 5x at entertainment, 3x at gas and transit.

Best for: Most Canadian households. Highest flat grocery earn rate, no cap, no chain restriction, and a strong all-around card for dining and travel too. The default recommendation for grocery rewards in Canada.
#2

PC Financial World Elite Mastercard

$0 Annual Fee · PC Optimum · At Loblaws/Superstore/No Frills
Best for PC Stores
45 pts/$10 = 4.5% (PC stores)

Earns 45 PC Optimum points per $10 at Loblaws-banner grocery stores — Loblaws, Real Canadian Superstore, No Frills, Zehrs, Fortinos, Maxi, Provigo, and T&T Supermarket. Effective return: 4.5% in PC Optimum points redeemable for free groceries at those same stores.

$0 annual fee, $80,000 household income requirement. Also earns 45 pts at Shoppers Drug Mart and 30 pts/$10 (3%) everywhere else. PC Optimum bonus events (20X points, etc.) stack on top for exceptional short-term earn rates. Travel insurance included (unusual for a no-fee card).

Limitation: PC Optimum points only redeemable at PC-banner stores. If you don't shop at Loblaws/No Frills/Superstore regularly, this card loses most of its value.

Best for: Families who do their primary grocery shopping at Loblaws, Real Canadian Superstore, No Frills, or Maxi. The highest grocery earn rate on a no-fee card in Canada.
#3

Scotiabank Momentum Visa Infinite

$120/yr (waived yr 1) · Cash Back · Any Grocery Store
Best Cash Back
4% cash back (up to $25K/yr)

The Scotia Momentum VI earns 4% pure cash back on groceries — also 2% on gas and recurring bills. Annual cap of $25,000 across all 4% categories (gas + grocery + bills combined). Most families won't hit this cap.

Cash-back simplicity is the main draw: 4% cash at groceries, paid as a statement credit each February. No points program to track. Also includes strong travel insurance package and rental car CDW for 48 days.

Best for: Families who want pure, predictable cash back on groceries without a points program. The best overall cash back grocery card in Canada.
#4

BMO eclipse rise Visa Card

$0 Annual Fee · BMO Rewards · Any Grocery Store
Best No-Fee Any Store
5x BMO pts (~1.7% travel)

The BMO eclipse rise earns 5 BMO Rewards points per $2 at any grocery store — no chain restriction, no cap, $0 annual fee. At ~0.67¢/pt for travel redemptions = ~1.7% effective. For cash-back equivalent, closer to 2.5%.

No income requirement. Also 5x at dining, gas, drugstores, streaming. For families who want a single no-fee card covering all daily categories, this is the broadest coverage available. The $25 annual eco credit and 10% AU bonus are unique perks.

Best for: No-fee shoppers who want strong rewards at any grocery store (including Costco, Walmart grocery, ethnic grocers) without a points program lock-in.
#5

CIBC Dividend Visa Infinite

$120/yr (waived yr 1) · Cash Back · Any Grocery Store
4% + 2% on Drugstores
4% cash back (up to $20K/yr)

The CIBC Dividend VI earns 4% on groceries (same as Scotia Momentum), but with a lower annual cap of $20,000 rather than $25,000. For families spending under $20K on groceries + gas combined, the difference is irrelevant.

CIBC's advantage: mobile device insurance ($1,500) and 2% on dining and drugstore (vs Scotia's 1%). Better for households with high dining or Shoppers Drug Mart spend. Same $120 annual fee, waived year 1.

Best for: CIBC banking customers, or households with significant dining/drugstore spend who also want 4% grocery earn.

Stacking Grocery Loyalty Programs with Credit Cards

Many Canadian grocery chains have their own loyalty programs that stack with your credit card rewards:

At Loblaw stores: PC Optimum in-app scan + PC Financial WE MC = base 45 pts + card 45 pts + any bonus event points. During 20X bonus events, your 45 pts card + 20X in-store = massive return on a single trip.

At Sobeys/Empire stores: Scan your Scene+ card + pay with Scotia Gold Amex = 3 pts Scene+ in-store (standard) + 6 pts from card = 9 Scene+ pts per dollar on the same purchase.

The principle: A credit card earn rate is a floor, not a ceiling. Loyalty program stacking can add 50–200% on top of your base card rate during promotional periods.
