Purchase Protection During the Holidays

Many premium Canadian credit cards include purchase protection (90–120 days) and extended warranty on items purchased with the card. This is particularly valuable for holiday gifts. If you buy an electronic or appliance as a gift and it breaks within 90 days, your credit card may cover the repair or replacement at no cost — even as a gift for someone else.

Cards with strong purchase protection: Amex Cobalt (90-day purchase protection, 1-year extended warranty), RBC Avion VI, TD Aeroplan VI (same coverage). See our purchase protection guide for full details.

Frequently Asked Questions

Should I use credit card points to buy holiday gifts?
Only if you're redeeming at a high-value rate. Travel redemptions (Aeroplan miles for flights) deliver 1.5–8¢ per point. Redeeming for gift cards or merchandise typically delivers 0.5–1¢ per point — a fraction of travel value. If you have points sitting unused and want to offset holiday costs, redeeming for statement credits or direct cashback is better than merchandise redemptions, but still lower-value than saving for travel.
Is it safe to shop online with a credit card during Black Friday?
Yes — credit cards have strong fraud protection and chargeback rights. If you're charged for something you didn't receive, or if your card is used fraudulently, your bank is obligated to investigate and reverse unauthorized charges. Using a credit card (rather than debit or e-Transfer) for online shopping provides this protection layer. Use virtual card numbers (available through some Canadian banks) for extra security on unfamiliar merchants.
