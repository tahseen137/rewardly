Aeroplan is Air Canada's loyalty program — and the most versatile travel rewards currency in Canada. Points can be used on Air Canada flights, 45+ Star Alliance partners (United, Lufthansa, Singapore Airlines, ANA, Swiss, and more), and hotel/car redemptions. Unlike many programs, Aeroplan has a distance-based award chart that rewards smart routing significantly more than simple "points = dollar" programs.

2. Amex Membership Rewards Transfer (1:1 + 10% Bonus)

Amex Membership Rewards points transfer to Aeroplan at 1:1 with a 10% transfer bonus (you receive 1,000 Aeroplan for every 1,000 MR transferred, plus 100 bonus Aeroplan). The Amex Cobalt earns 5x MR on dining/grocery — transferred to Aeroplan, that becomes 5.5x Aeroplan points per dollar on dining.

The Cobalt + Aeroplan strategy: Earn 5x MR on dining/grocery with the Amex Cobalt, then transfer to Aeroplan at 1:1+10% = 5.5 Aeroplan per dollar. Redeem for business class at ~4¢/pt = 22% effective return on dining spend. This is the highest-value Aeroplan earn path for sophisticated collectors.

3. Flying on Air Canada and Star Alliance

Earn Aeroplan miles on every Air Canada or Air Canada Express flight — based on miles flown, fare class, and Elite status tier. Higher Elite tiers earn status miles and base miles simultaneously. Star Alliance flights (United, Lufthansa, ANA, Swiss, Singapore, etc.) also earn Aeroplan points when booked through Aeroplan or Air Canada.

How to Redeem Aeroplan Points — Best and Worst Options

🏆 #1: Air Canada Business / First Class (Best Value)
3–4¢+ per point
Redeeming for Air Canada business or first class is the highest-value use of Aeroplan points. A round-trip Toronto–London in business class can require 95,000–130,000 points that would cost $6,000–$10,000 in cash — yielding 4–7¢/pt. Always search for saver award space first.
✈️ #2: Star Alliance Flights (Premium Cabins)
3–5¢+ per point
Aeroplan can book seats on United, Lufthansa, ANA, Singapore Airlines, and 40+ other Star Alliance carriers. Some of the world's best premium cabin products (Singapore Suites, ANA First Class) can be booked with Aeroplan points at rates that beat Air Canada's own pricing. Search at the partner's website first, then book through Aeroplan.com.
🛫 #3: Air Canada Economy — Short Haul
1.3–1.5¢ per point
Standard economy redemptions for short Air Canada domestic or North American routes yield about 1.3–1.5¢/pt — the baseline for most Canadian collectors. Still better than many cash-back cards. Best for last-minute travel when cash prices spike.
🏨 Hotels and Car Rentals (Mediocre Value)
0.8–1¢ per point
Hotel and car rental redemptions through Aeroplan typically yield 0.8–1¢/pt — below the flight redemption value. Better to book hotels with a cash-back card and save Aeroplan for flights. Exception: occasional hotel promotions can offer better value.
🛍️ Merchandise, Gift Cards (Worst Use)
0.4–0.8¢ per point
Redeeming Aeroplan points for merchandise, gift cards, or "store credit" is the worst use — yields 0.4–0.8¢/pt vs. 1.5–4¢ for flights. Avoid these redemptions entirely unless you have points that would otherwise expire.

Aeroplan Family Sharing

Aeroplan Family Sharing lets up to 8 family members combine their points balance into a single pool for redemptions. Key rules: all accounts must be in the member's name (no duplicates), all accounts must have been active for 90+ days, and points pools count toward redemptions immediately.

Family Sharing is particularly powerful for households where one person flies frequently on Air Canada (earning status miles + base miles) while others earn through credit card spending. The pooled balance can reach flight thresholds faster than individual accounts.

Tip: If multiple family members hold Aeroplan co-branded credit cards (TD Aeroplan VI for one, CIBC Aeroplan VI for another), both earn streams flow into the Family Sharing pool — effectively doubling the household's Aeroplan accumulation rate.

Do Aeroplan Points Expire?

Aeroplan points do not expire as long as you have at least one qualifying activity every 12 months. Qualifying activity includes: any flight on Air Canada or partner, any Aeroplan partner purchase (hotel, car rental), any credit card spend on an Aeroplan co-branded card, or any Aeroplan transfer. Points reset on inactivity after 12 months with no qualifying activity.

Watch for inactivity: If you stop using your Aeroplan account entirely — no card activity, no flights, no partner transactions — your points will expire after 12 months. The simplest prevention: hold a TD or CIBC Aeroplan credit card and use it at least once a year.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the sweet spot in the Aeroplan award chart?
For most Canadians, the best value is in transatlantic business class: Toronto–Europe typically requires 75,000–95,000 Aeroplan points in saver business class, while cash prices range from $3,000–$8,000. At 75K points for a $5,000 seat, that's 6.7¢/point — exceptional value. Finding saver space on Lufthansa or SWISS operated flights is particularly good.
Can I combine Aeroplan points from multiple credit cards?
Yes — all TD Aeroplan and CIBC Aeroplan cards deposit into your single Aeroplan member number. Amex MR transfers also go to the same account. If you have multiple Aeroplan earning products, all points land in one place automatically.
Is Aeroplan better than WestJet Dollars?
For domestic travel: WestJet Dollars (1 WJD = $1 CAD toward WestJet flights) offer simplicity and competitive value. For international travel in premium cabins: Aeroplan is dramatically better due to Star Alliance access and the ability to earn outsized value on business/first class redemptions. WestJet Dollars are fixed at 1¢; Aeroplan points can be worth 1.5–4¢+ depending on how you redeem.
What is Aeroplan's "Points + Cash" option?
Points + Cash lets you use a mix of points and cash to pay for a flight or upgrade. The ratio is typically fixed by Aeroplan (e.g., you must use at least 7,500 points + some cash). The effective points value in these redemptions is usually at the lower end (1–1.3¢/pt) — better to save points for pure award travel where value is higher.
