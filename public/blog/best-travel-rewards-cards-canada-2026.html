Best Travel Rewards Credit Cards in Canada 2026

Aeroplan, Avion, Aventura — which program gets you to your destination for fewer points?
Updated February 27, 2026 • 10 min read

Travel rewards cards are where the real value lives — if you know how to use them. We're talking business-class flights for the price of economy, hotel stays that don't drain your bank account, and lounge access that makes airport delays almost bearable.

But here's the catch: not all points are equal. An Aeroplan point redeemed for a domestic flight is worth ~2¢. Same point redeemed poorly? Maybe 0.8¢. The card matters, but how you use it matters more.

The Big Four Canadian Travel Programs

Aeroplan (TD, CIBC)

Sweet spot: Long-haul premium cabin flights. Aeroplan dynamic pricing has made domestic flights less appealing, but business-class to Europe/Asia still delivers 2-3¢ per point.

Watch out: Fuel surcharges on certain routes (especially British Airways-operated flights) can kill the value.

Avion (RBC)

Sweet spot: Fixed-value redemptions (2.1¢/point on RBC Travel) and hotel bookings. No blackout dates, no airline restrictions.

Watch out: Lower ceiling than Aeroplan for premium redemptions. Great for flexibility, not necessarily for maximum value.

Aventura (CIBC)

Sweet spot: Flexible travel bookings at 1¢/point. Simple, predictable, no award chart complexity.

Watch out: Lower earn rates than competitors. You need to spend more to get the same trip.

American Express Membership Rewards

Sweet spot: Transfer to Aeroplan or Marriott Bonvoy for premium redemptions. Fixed-value travel bookings at 1¢/point as a fallback.

Watch out: Annual fees on Amex cards are high. Need to travel regularly to justify the cost.

How to Choose Your Travel Card

Step 1: Where do you actually go?

Aeroplan dominates if you're flying to Europe or Asia. For US trips, a flexible program like Avion or a co-branded US airline card might be better. Domestic Canada flights? Honestly, cashback cards often beat points.

Step 2: Do you value flexibility or maximum value?

Avion and Aventura let you book any seat on any airline. Aeroplan requires award availability but delivers higher value when you find it. Different strategies for different travelers.

Step 3: Can you hit the minimum spend for the welcome bonus?

Welcome bonuses are the real prize. A 60,000 Aeroplan point bonus is worth $1,200+ if redeemed well. Don't sign up for a card with a $5,000 minimum spend if you normally spend $2,000/month.

Analyze Your Spending

Premium Cards: Are They Worth It?

Cards with $400-$500 annual fees sound insane. But run the math:

  • Lounge access: Priority Pass membership alone is $400/year. If you travel internationally 3+ times/year, you've already broken even.
  • Travel credits: Many premium cards offer $200-$300 in annual travel credits (hotel bookings, airline fees, etc.)
  • Insurance: Trip cancellation, interruption, lost baggage, car rental coverage — this stuff adds up fast if you pay separately.
  • Higher earn rates: Premium cards typically earn 1.5-2x more points per dollar than entry-level cards in the same program.

If you travel 4+ times per year, premium cards often deliver more value than they cost. If you take one trip annually, stick with mid-tier cards.

Common Travel Card Mistakes

1. Hoarding points forever

Points get devalued. Airlines change award charts. Banks adjust programs. Sitting on 200,000 points waiting for "the perfect trip" means you're losing value every year. Use them.

2. Ignoring transfer partners

Amex Membership Rewards at 1¢/point is fine. Transferred to Aeroplan and redeemed for business class? 3¢/point. Learn the transfer ratios.

3. Paying annual fees on cards you don't use

That Aeroplan card from 2022 that you never carry? Cancel it or downgrade to a no-fee version. Paying $120/year for a card collecting dust is a tax on forgetfulness.

The Bottom Line

The best travel card isn't the one with the highest earn rate. It's the one that matches where you actually go and how you actually travel.
