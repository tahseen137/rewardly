Best Credit Cards for Back to School Canada 2026

Whether you're the student buying your first card or the parent spending $1,000–$3,000 on supplies and tuition, there's a card that earns meaningfully on every dollar.

For Students: Best First Credit Cards (No Income Required)

#1

BMO CashBack Mastercard (Student)

$0 annual fee · No income requirement · Available to students
3% on groceries 1% on recurring bills 0.5% everywhere else

No income requirement makes this accessible to students with part-time income. The 3% grocery rate is the highest available on a no-fee student card. Cashback redeemable in January each year. Perfect for students managing their own grocery budget.

#2

Scotiabank Scene+ Visa (Student)

$0 annual fee · Build Scene+ for movie rewards · Good starter card
5x Scene+ at Cineplex 3x at participating grocery (Sobeys family) 1x everywhere

Best for students who go to movies regularly — 5x Scene+ at Cineplex translates to roughly 1 free movie per 4-5 tickets purchased. The 3x grocery rate applies at Empire/Sobeys/FreshCo/Foodland stores specifically. Good gateway to the wider Scene+ ecosystem.

#3

TD Cash Back Visa (Student)

$0 annual fee · 2% on grocery and gas · Simple cashback
2% on grocery 2% on gas 0.5% everywhere else

Clean and simple. Students who drive or frequently grocery shop will see strong value. Cashback is automatically applied as a statement credit in February each year. TD's app is highly rated for expense tracking, which is helpful for students learning to budget.

Student card tip: The goal of a student credit card is to build your credit history — not to maximize rewards. Pay your balance in full every month. Keep utilization below 30%. In 2–3 years with a clean history, you'll qualify for premium cards with much higher earn rates and welcome bonuses. The rewards difference between a student card and a premium card ($200+ per year) is worth building toward.

For Parents

For Parents: Best Cards for Back-to-School Spending

Back-to-school spending for a family typically hits $500–$2,000 in August and September: school supplies, clothing, textbooks, grocery stocking, and extracurricular fees. The right card earns significantly on this concentrated spend.

#1

Amex Cobalt

$191.88/yr ($15.99/mo) · 5x on all food and dining
5x on ALL grocery stores 5x on restaurants 3x on streaming 2x on travel

Back-to-school means groceries surge — stocking up on snacks, lunches, pantry staples. Cobalt earns 5x at every Canadian grocery store, no exceptions. On $500 in August grocery spend, that's 2,500 MR points (worth ~$25–$50 when redeemed for travel). Also earns 5x on school restaurants/dining and 3x on subscription services your student might start using.

#2

PC Financial World Elite Mastercard

$0 annual fee (with $80K income) · 3% PC Optimum at Loblaws banner
3% PC Optimum at Loblaws/Shoppers 4.5% at Esso/Mobil 30 pts/litre Esso

Best no-fee option for parents who shop at Loblaws/Real Canadian Superstore/Shoppers Drug Mart. Back-to-school shopping at Shoppers earns 3% in PC Optimum points. Stack with Shoppers PC Optimum bonus days (20x events) for exceptional return. PC Optimum redeems at 1 pt = $0.001 ($10 per 10,000 pts).

#3

Scotia Gold American Express

$120/yr · 5x on grocery (Sobeys), dining, entertainment · 0% FX
6x at Sobeys/Empire stores 5x on dining/entertainment 0% foreign transaction fee

If you're shopping at Sobeys/FreshCo/Foodland for back-to-school supplies, this card's 6x rate is unmatched. The 5x entertainment rate also covers school event tickets, sporting goods, and some recreation purchases. Good for families with a mix of grocery and entertainment spending.
