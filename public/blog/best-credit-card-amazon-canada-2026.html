Best Credit Card for Amazon Canada 2026

Amazon codes as "online shopping" — and some cards earn 3–4% there. Here's which one wins.
Updated July 12, 2026 • 6 min read

Amazon.ca is one of the most common places Canadians spend money — and most people use the wrong card there. The difference between 1% and 4% cashback on a $2,000/year Amazon habit is $60 in your pocket every year. This guide ranks the best cards for Amazon Canada purchases, with no fluff.

How Amazon.ca codes with credit cards

Amazon Canada typically codes as Online Shopping / E-commerce or General Merchandise at most Canadian card issuers. It does NOT code as grocery, gas, or travel. Cards that offer elevated rates on "online shopping" or "everyday purchases" are your best bet. We verified coding with each issuer below.

The Amex Problem with Amazon

Amazon.ca does accept American Express. However, the popular Amex Cobalt only earns 1x (1%) on Amazon — online shopping doesn't qualify for the 5x dining/grocery rate. If your goal is maximizing Amazon spend, the Cobalt is actually your worst premium card option for this specific merchant. Use the Cobalt for restaurants and groceries; use a dedicated online shopping card for Amazon.

Pairing Strategy: The Optimal Amazon Setup

The best setup for most Canadian Amazon shoppers:

  • For heavy Amazon.ca spending: CIBC Dividend Visa Infinite (4% cashback) as your primary Amazon card
  • For Amazon.com USD purchases: Rogers Red World Elite Mastercard (3% on foreign currency)
  • For no-fee simplicity: Tangerine World Mastercard with "Online Shopping" as a chosen category (2% cashback)

Pro tip: Amazon Prime membership payments also earn at the online shopping rate — so your $99/year Prime fee earns 4% back ($3.96) on the CIBC Dividend. Small but it adds up if you're stacking it with your regular Amazon purchases.

Amazon Business Purchases in Canada

If you're buying for a business, consider the American Express Business Edge card. It earns 3x (3%) on office supplies and online purchases, with no foreign transaction fees. It's aimed at business owners who stock up on Amazon Business regularly.

Does Amazon Canada Have Its Own Credit Card?

Unlike the US (which has the Amazon Prime Visa and Amazon Store Card), Amazon Canada does not issue its own co-branded credit card as of mid-2026. The optimal strategy is to use a third-party Canadian credit card that offers bonus rates on online shopping, as described above.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does Amazon.ca code as for credit cards?

Amazon.ca typically codes as "Online Shopping," "Internet/Mail Order," or "General Merchandise" at most Canadian card issuers. It does not typically code as grocery, gas, or travel. Cards with bonus rates on online shopping or everyday purchases are your best bet.

Is there a no-fee card that earns more than 2% at Amazon Canada?

Not consistently. The Tangerine World Mastercard at 2% (chosen category) is the highest no-fee rate available for Amazon.ca. Cards earning 4%+ at Amazon all charge annual fees in the $120-$139 range.

Does Amazon.ca accept Amex?

Yes. Amazon Canada accepts American Express cards. However, Amex cards typically earn only base rates (1%) on Amazon purchases since it doesn't qualify as dining, grocery, or most elevated categories. Use a Visa or Mastercard with online shopping bonuses instead.

Is Amazon.ca purchases eligible for the Scotiabank Gold Amex 5x rate?

No. The Scotiabank Gold Amex earns 5x on food and dining, and 3x on transit, streaming, and entertainment. Amazon.ca purchases earn only 1x on this card. The Scotiabank Momentum Visa Infinite also earns only 1% on Amazon.ca — its 4% tier covers grocery, streaming, and recurring bills, not general online shopping. For Amazon, a Tangerine Money-Back or similar card with an online/other category is the better Scotiabank-adjacent option.
