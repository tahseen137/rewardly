Marriott Bonvoy Points — What Are They Worth?

Marriott Bonvoy points are generally valued at 0.7–0.9 cents CAD per point for typical hotel redemptions. At peak value (Category 1–2 hotels, premium rooms with points + cash), you can squeeze more. At large urban flagship hotels, you'll get less.

CategoryEarn RateEffective Return (at 0.8¢/pt)

All other purchases2× Bonvoy pts~1.6% in hotel value

The 2× on everyday purchases is competitive for a hotel card. It means you're continuously accumulating points toward free nights even when you're not traveling.

The Annual Free Night Award

Every year after your card anniversary, you receive a free night award valid at properties costing up to 35,000 Bonvoy points. This covers a wide range of Marriott properties globally — typically mid-tier properties (Courtyard, Fairfield, Four Points, AC Hotels) and many Sheraton/Westin locations in off-peak periods.

At 0.8¢ per point, a 35,000-point free night is worth approximately $280. Against a $120 annual fee, the free night alone more than covers the cost if you use it at a property you'd otherwise pay $200+ for. Find a Category 4–5 Marriott property on a weeknight and you've gotten exceptional value.

Note: The free night award does not include taxes and fees at the hotel, which typically run $25–$60 per night. Still, the net value is substantial.

Automatic Silver Elite Status

Simply holding the card grants you Marriott Bonvoy Silver Elite status automatically. Silver Elite benefits include:

  • 10% bonus on Bonvoy points earned during hotel stays
  • Priority late checkout (subject to availability)
  • Dedicated Bonvoy customer service line
  • Early check-in (subject to availability)

Silver is the entry-level status, but the 10% point bonus on stays is meaningful — it compounds your in-hotel earning.

The card also credits you with 15 elite night credits per calendar year. Gold Elite status requires 25 nights; Platinum requires 50. Cardholders start 15 nights ahead, meaning 10 real paid nights puts you at Gold Elite (enhanced room upgrades, lounge access at many properties, 25% point bonus, complimentary breakfast at some brands).

Bonvoy's Global Portfolio — Why It Matters

Marriott Bonvoy is the largest hotel loyalty program in the world: 8,000+ properties across 30+ brands in 139 countries. This includes:

  • Luxury: Ritz-Carlton, St. Regis, W Hotels, Luxury Collection, EDITION
  • Premium: Westin, Sheraton, Marriott Hotels, Le Méridien, Renaissance
  • Select: Courtyard, Fairfield, Four Points, AC Hotels, SpringHill
  • Extended Stay: Residence Inn, TownePlace Suites, Element

This breadth means Bonvoy points are useful almost everywhere in the world. Canadian travelers heading to the U.S., Europe, the Caribbean, Southeast Asia, or Japan will find Marriott properties in nearly every destination.

Amex Membership Rewards Transfer Option

This card earns Marriott Bonvoy points directly, not Amex Membership Rewards. However, Amex MR points from other Amex cards can transfer to Bonvoy at a 1:1.2 ratio (1,000 MR → 1,200 Bonvoy points). If you hold the Amex Gold or Cobalt cards in addition, you can funnel MR points into Bonvoy at a slight bonus.

What This Card Lacks

Important limitations: This card does not include travel insurance, airport lounge access, or foreign transaction fee waivers. It is a rewards accumulation card, not a travel benefits card. For travel protection, use a dedicated card like Scotiabank Passport VI alongside this card on hotel bookings.

Who Should Get This Card?

Get it if: You stay at Marriott properties at least a few nights per year, want to accumulate Bonvoy points on everyday spending, and will use the annual free night award. The card is especially valuable if you can reach Gold Elite with a combination of card nights (15) + real hotel stays (10+).

Pair it with: A comprehensive travel insurance card (Scotiabank Passport VI or National Bank World Elite) and a no-FX-fee card for international spending. Use the Marriott Bonvoy Amex specifically at Marriott properties and for everyday accumulation.

Skip it if: You rarely stay at Marriott properties, prefer Hilton or IHG (no Canadian co-brand cards exist for either), or want a single all-in-one card that does everything.

Rewardly Verdict — 3.8 / 5

The Marriott Bonvoy American Express is a solid hotel co-brand card held back by the absence of any travel insurance or FX fee waiver. The annual free night award is genuinely valuable and covers the annual fee for anyone who uses it. If you're a Bonvoy loyalist, it's a no-brainer add-on to your wallet. If you want a primary card that does everything, look elsewhere.

Best for: Marriott Bonvoy members wanting to accelerate points, business travelers staying at Marriott properties, families targeting Gold Elite status.
