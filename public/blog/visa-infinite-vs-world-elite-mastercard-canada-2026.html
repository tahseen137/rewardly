Visa Infinite vs World Elite Mastercard Canada 2026

Both are Canada's top credit card tiers. But they have different income requirements, benefits, and acceptance. Here's exactly what each tier offers — and which is better for you.

What Are Card Tiers?

Canadian credit cards come in tiers — basic, standard, premium, and ultra-premium. Each tier unlocks better benefits and higher earn rates, but also requires higher income and credit scores. Visa Infinite and World Elite Mastercard are the two main premium tiers from their respective networks — one step below ultra-premium (Visa Infinite Privilege and World Elite Mastercard Black/Infinite).

The tier is set by Visa or Mastercard as a network-level standard. Individual banks then issue specific cards at each tier — for example, RBC offers the RBC Avion Visa Infinite (VI tier), and BMO offers the BMO World Elite Mastercard (WE tier).

Key Practical Differences for Canadians

The income requirement difference is the most meaningful practical distinction. Visa Infinite cards typically require $60K personal income, while World Elite Mastercard cards require $80K. Both are more accessible than you might think — the income requirement is often self-reported and based on gross income (not after-tax).

Acceptance is nearly identical in Canada, with one critical exception: Costco Canada exclusively accepts Mastercard. If you shop at Costco regularly ($2,000–$5,000/year is common for families), a World Elite Mastercard is practically required to earn rewards there. Visa cards work at Costco only for prepaid Visa gift cards or Costco's own Visa-based products in some regions.

Costco Canada rule: Costco Canada only accepts Mastercard in-store and at the gas bar. The best Mastercard for Costco is the Rogers Red World Elite Mastercard (2% everywhere, no annual fee, $80K income). Many Canadians who shop at Costco specifically choose a WE Mastercard as their primary card for this reason.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Visa Infinite better than World Elite Mastercard?
Neither is universally better — it depends on the specific card. The "Visa Infinite" or "World Elite Mastercard" designation is a tier label from the payment network; the actual benefits (earn rates, insurance, lounge access) are determined by the issuing bank. Compare the specific cards you're considering, not just the tier names. A Visa Infinite from one bank may be far better or worse than a World Elite Mastercard from another.
What income do I need for a Visa Infinite card?
Most Visa Infinite cards require $60,000 personal annual income or $100,000 household income. Some cards are more or less strict in practice. Income is typically self-reported on the application — banks may do a hard pull but usually don't verify income documents for standard credit card applications. If you don't meet the stated minimum, you may still be approved, but it's less likely.
Can I have both a Visa Infinite and a World Elite Mastercard?
Yes — many Canadian rewards optimizers carry one of each. A common setup: TD Aeroplan Visa Infinite for Air Canada flights + travel insurance, and Rogers Red World Elite Mastercard for Costco spending and flat cashback. The two cards are complementary, covering different merchant acceptance gaps and earn categories.
