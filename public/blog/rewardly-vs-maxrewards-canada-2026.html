Canada Support: The Core Problem with MaxRewards

MaxRewards is transparent about this: it was built for American cardholders. Its headline features — auto-activating Chase Offers, Bank of America Offers, and Citi card bonuses — don't apply in Canada because those banks don't operate here.

What it does support in Canada:

  • Some Amex Canada cards and offers
  • Benefits tracking for Amex Platinum, Amex Gold (Canadian versions)
  • Best card recommendations across connected cards

What it doesn't support in Canada:

  • TD Bank — Canada's largest bank by assets. No TD Aeroplan, no TD Cash Back, no TD First Class.
  • RBC Royal Bank — RBC Avion, RBC Cash Back Mastercard, RBC Ion Visa. None of these work.
  • CIBC — Aventura cards, CIBC Dividend Visa. Not in MaxRewards.
  • Scotiabank — Scene+ cards, Momentum Visa Infinite. Not supported.
  • BMO — BMO CashBack, BMO Ascend World Elite. Not supported.
  • National Bank, Desjardins — Same story.

This matters because the average Canadian has 2–3 credit cards, and it's very common to have a mix: maybe an Amex Cobalt plus a TD Aeroplan Visa. MaxRewards can only see half your wallet at best.

MaxRewards Canada Reality Check

Great app. Wrong market. If you only carry Amex Canada cards and want auto-offer activation, MaxRewards delivers that. But if your credit card drawer has any Big Five bank card in it — and most Canadians' do — MaxRewards is only partially useful to you.
