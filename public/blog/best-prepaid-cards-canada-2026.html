Who Should Use a Prepaid Card in Canada?

New immigrants or newcomers to Canada who don't yet have established credit history. Prepaid cards provide immediate purchasing power without a credit check — and KOHO with the credit-building add-on can start building your credit file from day one.

People rebuilding credit after bankruptcy or consumer proposal. Prepaid cards don't report to credit bureaus (except KOHO's optional credit builder) — useful for spending while rebuilding credit separately via a secured credit card.

Teenagers and young adults who don't qualify for credit cards (must be 18+ for credit in most provinces). KOHO can be used by those 16+ in some provinces.

Budget management. Since you can only spend what you load, prepaid cards are genuinely effective for people who struggle with credit card overspending.

Prepaid cards don't build credit by default: If building your credit score is important, a secured credit card (e.g., Home Trust Secured Visa, Capital One Secured MC) is usually better than a prepaid card — because secured cards do report your payment history to the credit bureaus. KOHO's credit builder ($7/mo extra) is the exception.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does KOHO build credit in Canada?
KOHO's standard prepaid card does NOT build credit. However, KOHO offers an optional "Credit Building" add-on for $7/month that works alongside the prepaid card: KOHO opens a secured credit line, reports monthly on-time payments to Equifax and TransUnion, and this builds your credit history. If you're using KOHO specifically to build credit, the $7/month credit builder add-on is required — simply having the prepaid card itself has no credit-building effect.
Are prepaid cards safe for online shopping in Canada?
Yes — Visa and Mastercard prepaid cards are accepted at virtually all online merchants that accept those networks (which is most major retailers). They offer similar fraud protection to debit cards through the Visa/Mastercard zero liability policies, though the specific coverage varies by issuer. For security, prepaid cards are actually useful for online shopping because if the card number is compromised, you lose only what was loaded — not your full bank account.
