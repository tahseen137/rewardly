Neo Financial is a Canadian fintech founded in Calgary in 2019. It offers a Mastercard through a partnership with ATB Financial (in Alberta) and Concentra Bank (nationally). The key differentiator: Neo has negotiated boosted cashback rates with 10,000+ Canadian merchants — typically 5–15% at partner locations, with a guaranteed floor of 0.5% everywhere else.

The fine print on partner rates: The 5%+ rates at partner merchants are negotiated offers, not a fixed card feature. Merchants can change their offer. Some advertised rates are promotional. The "guaranteed minimum" of 0.5% applies to all other spending. If you don't shop at Neo's partner network regularly, your effective earn rate on most spending will be 0.5% — significantly below the Rogers Red (2%), Amex Cobalt (1–5x), or PC Financial WE (3–4.5%).

Foreign Transaction Fee

This is a common point of confusion: the Neo Mastercard standard card DOES charge a 2.5% foreign transaction fee on non-Canadian purchases. This is the same as most Canadian credit cards. Neo is NOT a 0% FX card — don't use it for US shopping or international travel without checking the current terms. The Scotia Passport VI, HSBC WE MC, or Home Trust Preferred Visa are better choices for 0% FX on all currencies. Rogers Red WE MC waives FX only on USD purchases — EUR/GBP/etc. still incur 2.5% FX.

Pros and Cons

✅ Pros

  • $0 annual fee — no-fee card with upside
  • 5–15% cashback at 10,000+ partner merchants
  • Clean, modern app with real-time cashback tracking
  • Easy approval — accessible for newer credit files
  • Neo Card+ ($4.99/month) raises the floor to 1% everywhere

❌ Cons

  • 0.5% minimum earn rate is low for non-partner spending
  • 2.5% foreign transaction fee — not a travel card
  • No welcome bonus
  • No travel insurance included
  • Partner cashback rates can change at any time
  • Mastercard — not accepted everywhere Amex is (though better accepted than Amex)
  • Relatively new company — less track record than Big Six

Our Verdict: 4.0 / 5

Neo Financial Mastercard is a solid no-fee card IF you regularly shop at its partner network. The 5%+ cashback at partner merchants is genuinely excellent. But for Canadians who primarily shop at grocers, Amazon, or merchants outside Neo's network, the 0.5% minimum makes it a weak everyday card. We recommend it as a secondary card — use it at partner merchants and a higher flat-rate card (Rogers Red 2%) everywhere else. Not a replacement for a dedicated travel card or premium category card like Amex Cobalt.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Neo Financial a real bank?
Neo Financial is a federally regulated fintech company. The Neo Mastercard is issued through a banking partnership with ATB Financial (in Alberta) or Concentra Bank (nationally), both of which are CDIC member institutions. Your Neo deposits and card services are backed by regulated Canadian institutions. Neo itself is not a bank but is regulated by FINTRAC and applicable provincial regulations.
Does Neo have a credit limit?
Yes — Neo sets credit limits at approval, typically based on your credit score and income. Limits range from $500 to $20,000+. Unlike prepaid cards (KOHO, Wealthsimple Cash), the Neo Mastercard is a true revolving credit card that reports to Equifax and TransUnion, helping build your credit history.
Can I use Neo at any grocery store?
You can use it anywhere Mastercard is accepted, but you'll only earn the 5%+ rate at Neo's partner grocery stores (like Save-On-Foods). Most Canadian grocery chains (Loblaws, Sobeys, Metro, Walmart, Costco) are not Neo partners — at these you'd earn 0.5%. For grocery earn, the Amex Cobalt (5x at ALL grocers) or PC Financial WE MC (3–4.5% at Loblaws/Shoppers) are stronger options.
