Best Credit Cards for Paying Rent in Canada 2026

Rent is your biggest monthly expense and earns you nothing — until now. Services like Chexy let you pay rent by credit card. Here's when the math works.

Can You Pay Rent With a Credit Card in Canada?

Most Canadian landlords and property management companies don't accept credit cards directly — they require e-Transfer, cheque, pre-authorized debit, or bank draft. However, third-party services have emerged that accept your credit card and pay your landlord by e-Transfer on your behalf. You earn credit card rewards on the full rent amount; they charge a processing fee (typically 1.5–2.5%).

The key question is whether the rewards you earn exceed the processing fee. For most Canadians, this depends heavily on which credit card you use and what your rent is.

Canadian Rent Payment Services (2026)

Chexy

The most widely used rent credit card service in Canada. Pay rent with any major credit card; Chexy sends an e-Transfer to your landlord. Also reports your rental payments to Equifax (credit building benefit).

Fee: ~1.75% of rent payment · Visa & Mastercard accepted · Amex not currently supported

Example: $2,000/month rent → $35 fee. If your card earns 2% cashback ($40), you net $5/month or $60/year. If your card earns 1% cashback ($20), you're losing $15/month — negative value.

Zenbase

Primarily a "rent splitting" tool that lets you pay rent in two installments mid-month. Some credit card payment functionality available depending on your landlord's setup. Also offers credit building via rental reporting.

Fee: varies by plan, typically monthly subscription model · Check current pricing at zenbase.ca

Less commonly used for pure credit card rewards earning than Chexy. Better for people who need payment flexibility (splitting into two payments) as the primary benefit.

The Best Use Case: Welcome Bonus Minimum Spend

The highest-value use of rent payment via credit card is achieving a welcome bonus minimum spend threshold. If you just got a new TD Aeroplan Visa Infinite (60,000-point bonus for $3,000 in 90 days), and your regular spending doesn't reach $3,000/month, putting rent through Chexy fills the gap easily. Even at 1.75% fees, paying $2,100 in processing fees over three months is worth it to unlock a welcome bonus worth $600–$1,200 in Aeroplan travel value.

After the welcome bonus period: Once you've hit your minimum spend, reassess. Only continue paying rent via card if your effective earn rate exceeds the Chexy fee — which typically requires a 2%+ cashback card or strong transfer-partner redemptions.

Credit Building Bonus: Rental Payment Reporting

Chexy reports your rental payments to Equifax Canada, which means paying rent through Chexy builds your credit history — even beyond the rewards benefit. For renters who don't have a lot of credit products, this is valuable: on-time rent payments appear as positive credit history, which can help you qualify for better cards and rates over time. This is a real secondary benefit that isn't available when paying rent by e-Transfer or cheque directly.

Frequently Asked Questions

Will my landlord know I'm using Chexy?
Your landlord receives an Interac e-Transfer from Chexy, not from you directly. Some landlords don't know or care how you fund the transfer; others have preferences. It's worth checking your lease — most residential leases don't restrict how you source the funds for rent, but commercial leases may have specific payment method clauses. Chexy's own FAQ addresses this and confirms that most landlords accept the e-Transfer without issues.
Does rent payment count toward credit card category bonuses?
Rent payments via Chexy typically code as "financial services" or "payment services" on your credit card statement — not as a standard grocery, dining, or travel purchase. This means they earn base earn rates (1–2%), not accelerated category rates (3x, 5x). The exception is during welcome bonus minimum spend periods, where all spending counts toward the threshold regardless of category.
Is this the same as the US Bilt Mastercard?
Bilt Mastercard in the US allows direct rent payment with no fees and earns travel rewards — it's widely considered one of the best rent reward tools in North America. Canada doesn't currently have a Bilt-equivalent product. Chexy and Zenbase are the closest approximations, but they charge fees rather than being fee-free. If Bilt or an equivalent launches in Canada, it would change this equation significantly.
