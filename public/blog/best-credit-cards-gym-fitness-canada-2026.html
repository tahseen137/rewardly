Best Credit Cards for Gym & Fitness in Canada 2026

Monthly memberships, equipment, sports gear, yoga studios — your fitness spend is larger than you think. Here's how to earn on every dollar of it.

How Fitness Spend Codes on Credit Cards

Like clothing and dental, fitness has no dedicated bonus category on any major Canadian credit card. Gym memberships, personal training, yoga studios, and sports facilities code under "Recreation Services," "Health Clubs," or "Athletic Clubs" — merchant categories that earn base rate (1% or 1x) on virtually every card.

Fitness equipment bought online codes as sporting goods or general retail (not a bonus category on any major Canadian card). Understanding the distinction between fitness services (always base rate) and fitness products (base rate, best handled by a flat 2% card) is the key to setting the right expectations for this spending category.

Fitness spend breakdown: Monthly gym membership ($40–80/mo) → base rate (Rogers Red 2% wins). Personal training sessions ($80–150/session) → base rate. Online fitness apps (Peloton app, iFIT) → may code as streaming — earns 3x on Amex Cobalt (~4.5% value) or 4% on Scotia Momentum (recurring bills). Home fitness equipment online (Amazon.ca, MEC, Decathlon) → base rate; Rogers Red 2% is best. Sports gear at specialty stores → base rate.

Fitness Expense by Category — Best Card for Each

🏋️

Gym Memberships (GoodLife, Planet Fitness, Anytime Fitness, etc.)

Monthly auto-billing at gym chains codes as "Athletic Clubs and Gyms" (MCC 7997). No bonus category exists on Canadian cards. Best option: Rogers Red (2% flat cashback) or Tangerine (if you select Recreation in your 2% categories). On a $70/month GoodLife membership, Rogers Red earns $16.80/year — small but automatic.

Best card: Rogers Red (2% flat)
🧘

Yoga, Pilates, Dance Studios

Boutique studios typically code the same as gyms — Recreation/Athletic Clubs. Base rate on most cards. Rogers Red 2% is again the top performer. If your yoga studio is a small business and charges via Square or Stripe, it still codes as fitness services. Amex is accepted at most urban fitness studios.

Best card: Rogers Red (2% flat)
💻

Online Fitness Apps (Peloton, iFIT, Beachbody, Sweat)

Digital fitness subscriptions often code as "Streaming" or "Digital Subscriptions" — which earns bonus rates on several cards. Amex Cobalt earns 3x MR on streaming (~4.5–6% value via Aeroplan). Scotia Momentum VI earns 4% cashback on recurring bills/streaming. BMO eclipse rise earns 5 pts per $2 (~1.7% travel) on streaming. Test your specific card with a small transaction to verify categorization before banking on the bonus.

Best card: Amex Cobalt (3x MR on streaming) or Scotia Momentum VI (4% recurring bills)
🏃

Running Gear, Sports Shoes (Online)

Running shoes from Nike.com, Brooks, Hoka, or New Balance online code as online retail or sporting goods — base rate on virtually all Canadian cards, including BMO Eclipse VI (whose 5x categories are dining, grocery, gas, and transit only). Rogers Red's 2% flat is the best available rate for both online and in-store athletic footwear purchases. In-store at Running Room or SportChek also earns base rate.

Online & in-store: Rogers Red (2% flat — no card bonuses athletic footwear)
🏊

Home Gym Equipment (Dumbbells, Resistance Bands, Yoga Mat)

Amazon.ca purchases of fitness equipment earn at the base rate on most cards (1% on CIBC Dividend/Scotia Momentum, 1.5% on TD Cash Back, 2% on Tangerine if online shopping selected as a category). Rogue Fitness, Titan Fitness (US sites) charge in USD — use Rogers Red (2%, no FX fee) for US fitness equipment sites to avoid the 2.5% FX surcharge erasing your rewards.

Amazon.ca: Tangerine World MC (2% — online shopping category) | US fitness sites: Rogers Red (2%, no FX)
🎽

Athletic Apparel (Lululemon, Nike, Adidas online)

Online athletic apparel (Lululemon.com, Nike.com Canada, Adidas.ca, Roots) codes as clothing or general retail — no bonus category on any major Canadian card. BMO Eclipse VI's 5x applies only to dining, grocery, gas, and transit. Rogers Red's 2% flat is the best available rate for both in-store and online athletic apparel.

Online & in-store: Rogers Red (2% flat — no card bonuses athletic apparel)

Best Overall Cards for Fitness Spending

BEST FOR GYM MEMBERSHIPS — FLAT RATE

Rogers Red World Elite Mastercard

For recurring gym memberships and in-person fitness services (personal training, yoga, studios), Rogers Red's 2% flat cashback is the consistent winner. No category tracking, no annual fee, automatic 2% on your GoodLife, Anytime Fitness, YMCAs, or any other fitness provider. Wide Mastercard acceptance ensures you can set up pre-authorized credit card billing at virtually any gym in Canada.

Annual fee: $0  |  Earn on gym memberships: 2% flat

BEST FOR DIGITAL FITNESS SUBSCRIPTIONS

Amex Cobalt / Scotia Momentum VI

For Peloton, iFIT, Beachbody, and similar digital fitness subscriptions that code as streaming or recurring bills, dedicated subscription-bonus cards beat a flat-rate card. Amex Cobalt earns 3x MR on streaming (~4.5–6% value via Aeroplan transfer). Scotia Momentum VI earns 4% cashback on recurring bills and streaming. Both beat Rogers Red's 2% on this specific sub-category.

For all other fitness purchases (equipment, apparel, memberships), Rogers Red 2% flat is the winner — no card has a dedicated "sporting goods" or "online fitness retail" bonus.

Cobalt fee: $156/yr  |  Momentum VI fee: $120/yr  |  Earn on digital fitness subs: 3–4%

BEST IF TANGERINE ELIGIBLE

Tangerine Money-Back Credit Card

Tangerine offers a "Recreation" bonus category for its 2% cashback selector — which includes gym memberships, sports clubs, and recreational activities. If you select Recreation as one of your two 2% categories, your gym membership earns 2% — same as Rogers Red but with no income requirement. Useful if you don't qualify for World Elite income thresholds.

Annual fee: $0  |  Earn on gym memberships: 2% (if Recreation selected as 2% category)
