Credit Card Rental Car Insurance Canada 2026

Rental car companies charge $20–$35/day for collision damage waiver coverage. The right credit card covers this for free — saving you $140–$245 on a one-week rental.

What Is CDW/LDW and Why You Need It

When you rent a car, you're financially responsible for any damage to the vehicle — even if it's not your fault. Collision Damage Waiver (CDW) or Loss Damage Waiver (LDW) is the rental company's optional insurance product that waives this financial liability. Without it, a fender bender or windshield crack could cost you $5,000–$20,000+.

The good news: most premium Canadian credit cards include CDW coverage automatically when you pay for the rental with the card and decline the rental company's own CDW. You must typically decline the rental company's CDW to activate your card's coverage.

The savings are significant: At Enterprise, Hertz, Budget, or Avis, CDW runs $20–$35/day. On a 7-day rental: $140–$245 in CDW fees you can eliminate by paying with the right credit card.

How to Use Your Credit Card's Rental Insurance

1

Charge the entire rental to the eligible credit card

The full rental must be paid with the qualifying card — not split between cards. The initial deposit authorization must also go on the card.

2

Decline the rental company's CDW/LDW at the counter

At the rental counter, explicitly decline the Collision Damage Waiver (CDW) or Loss Damage Waiver (LDW). You may receive pushback from the rental agent — stay firm. Say: "I'm covered by my Visa/Amex credit card." The card coverage is primary — it pays before any other insurance.

3

Inspect the vehicle thoroughly before driving

Walk around the car and document every existing scratch, dent, or chip. Take photos or video with a timestamp. Note everything on the rental agreement before signing. This protects you from being charged for pre-existing damage upon return.

4

If damage occurs, do NOT accept responsibility prematurely

Report damage to your card issuer's insurance line immediately — do not admit liability to the rental company until you've spoken with the card's insurer. Save all documentation: rental agreement, damage photos, police report (if needed), repair receipts.

Your Own Auto Insurance + Credit Card — Which Pays First?

Most Canadian credit card rental coverage is primary — it pays first, before your personal auto insurance. This is a huge advantage: your own insurance premiums won't increase, and you don't pay a deductible on your personal policy. Amex Cobalt, TD Aeroplan VI, and most premium Visa Infinite and World Elite Mastercard policies are primary rental coverage.

Your personal auto insurance policy may also cover rental cars (check your policy's "temporary replacement vehicle" or "rental car" section). But using it would trigger a claim on your own policy — potentially raising your premiums and depleting your claims-free discount.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to carry a physical copy of my credit card's certificate of insurance for car rentals?
Yes, this is strongly recommended. Your credit card's Certificate of Insurance (COI) document explains the specific terms of your rental coverage — which vehicles are covered, the duration limit, and the claims process. Download or print the COI before your trip. Rental counter agents may ask to see proof of coverage, and the insurer will need the COI details if you file a claim. The COI is usually available through your card issuer's website or insurance portal (login required).
Does credit card rental insurance cover rentals in the US?
Yes — most Canadian credit card rental insurance covers rentals in Canada, the United States, and internationally. Some cards exclude specific countries (Cuba, Iran, etc. due to sanctions). Check the geographic coverage in your COI. The rental car must be rented from a commercial rental company (Hertz, Enterprise, Budget, etc.) — peer-to-peer car sharing (Turo, Getaround) is typically NOT covered by credit card rental insurance.
