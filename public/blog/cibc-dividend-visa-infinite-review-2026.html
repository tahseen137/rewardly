What Is the CIBC Dividend Visa Infinite?

The CIBC Dividend Visa Infinite is CIBC's flagship everyday cash back credit card, earning 4% back on groceries and gas, 2% on drugstore, dining, and recurring bill payments, and 1% on all other spending. Annual fee of $120, waived in the first year.

Cash back is paid annually as a statement credit each February for the preceding calendar year — one lump deposit rather than monthly. Requires $60,000 personal income or $100,000 household income (Visa Infinite standard).

This card is often directly compared with the Scotiabank Momentum Visa Infinite, which has an identical fee, similar earn rates, and targets the same buyer. Understanding the differences between these two cards is the most common question Canadians ask when choosing a cash back Visa Infinite.

Key caveat: The CIBC Dividend VI's 4% cap is $20,000/year on the combined grocery + gas tier (NOT $25,000 like the Scotia Momentum). For families spending $15K+ on groceries and $5K+ on gas, this cap is hit faster. Once capped, those purchases earn 1% instead of 4%.

The 2% categories ($80,000 annual cap) are essentially uncapped for almost everyone. The 4% cap is the most important limitation to understand.

Choose CIBC Dividend VI if:

  • You drive a lot — 4% on gas (vs Scotia's 2%) makes a real difference for commuters
  • You dine out or order food regularly — 2% on dining vs Scotia's 1%
  • You shop at Shoppers/Rexall — 2% drugstore vs Scotia's 1%
  • You want mobile device insurance ($1,500 — Scotia has none)
  • You prefer a card from CIBC where you already bank

Choose Scotia Momentum VI if:

  • Your recurring bills (utilities, streaming, phone, insurance) are high — 4% vs CIBC's 2%
  • Your combined grocery + gas spend exceeds $20,000/year (Scotia's $25K cap is 25% more)
  • You stream a lot — Netflix/Spotify/Disney+ earn 4% on Scotia vs 1% on CIBC
  • You prefer Scotia's extended CDW (48 days vs CIBC's 31 days)

Verdict

4.3 / 5

The CIBC Dividend Visa Infinite is a strong cash back card that trades blows with the Scotiabank Momentum VI across every category. Its edge is mobile device insurance and 2% on dining and drugstore — meaningful for families and urban professionals who eat out and shop at Shoppers Drug Mart.

Its weakness vs. Scotia: the 4% tier cap is $5,000 lower per year ($20K vs. $25K), and recurring bills earn only 2% rather than 4%. For families with high streaming/utility bills, the Scotia Momentum is the better card. For those who dine out frequently or want mobile device protection, CIBC wins.

If you bank with CIBC, this is the natural home card. If you don't have a strong bank preference, compare your actual spending against both earn structures to determine which pays you more.

Frequently Asked Questions

When is cash back paid out on the CIBC Dividend VI? Cash back accumulates throughout the year and is automatically deposited to your CIBC account once annually, typically in February for the previous year. Unlike the Scotia Momentum VI (monthly deposits), CIBC pays in one annual lump sum.
Does the CIBC Dividend VI work at Costco? Yes — Costco Canada accepts all Mastercard and Visa cards. The CIBC Dividend VI (Visa Infinite) earns 4% at Costco if Costco is coded as a grocery store by CIBC. Verify the actual earn rate on your first Costco purchase as MCC assignment varies.
Is the mobile device insurance worth having? Mobile device insurance covers your smartphone for up to $1,500 if it's lost, stolen, or accidentally damaged — provided you pay your monthly wireless plan with the card. At $12–15/month for device insurance from your carrier, this coverage saves up to $180/year. One of the CIBC Dividend VI's genuine competitive advantages over the Scotia Momentum.
What happens when I hit the $20,000 4% cap? Grocery and gas purchases revert to earning 1% for the remainder of the calendar year. The cap resets on January 1. If you anticipate hitting $20,000 in grocery + gas, consider the Scotia Momentum VI instead ($25K cap) or pair with a second card for overflow spending.
Can I get this card without a CIBC bank account? Yes — you don't need a CIBC chequing account to hold the Dividend Visa Infinite. However, CIBC banking customers receive some enhanced integration for managing the card and redeeming cash back.
