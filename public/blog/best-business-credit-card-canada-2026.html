How to Choose a Business Credit Card

Incorporated business vs. sole proprietor

Most business cards require incorporation or a business number (BN). Sole proprietors can often qualify using their SIN + business documentation. American Express is notably flexible — they assess based on personal creditworthiness alongside business documents, making their cards accessible to newer businesses.

Points vs. cashback

Points (MR, TD Points, Avion) can deliver 2¢+ per point when redeemed strategically through airline partners — up to 3× the value of cashback. But they require redemption effort. If your bookkeeper, not you, manages the card, cashback (Scotia, Rogers) is more practical.

Employee cards and expense management

Every card above offers supplementary cards. Amex Business Platinum offers unlimited free employee cards (a $0 add-on that competitor programs charge $29–$299 for). Amex also provides category spend controls per employee card — useful for capping dining or restricting purchases to approved categories.

Income and revenue requirements

Unlike personal cards, business cards typically don't require a personal income minimum — they look at business revenue or credit history. New businesses (under 1 year) typically qualify most easily for Amex cards and Rogers. TD, RBC, and Scotia generally want at least 1–2 years of operating history for their premium business cards.

Business Card Strategy: The Two-Card Setup

The highest-value approach most Canadian businesses use is a two-card setup:

  • Primary card — a high-earn card for your largest categories (Amex Business Gold for advertising + dining, or TD Business Travel for travel)
  • Fallback card — a Visa/Mastercard for suppliers who don't take Amex, with at least 1% earn (Rogers Business at $0 fee is ideal)

This covers all spend scenarios while maximizing earn on your highest categories.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can a sole proprietor get a business credit card in Canada?

Yes. Most major banks accept sole proprietors. You'll need your SIN, business name, and some form of business documentation (HST registration or a business bank account). American Express is the easiest issuer for newer businesses.

What's the difference between a business card and a personal card?

Business cards typically have higher credit limits, offer employee card controls, and provide itemized spending reports useful for accounting. They also usually report to the business credit bureau (Dun & Bradstreet) rather than your personal credit file, which can help build business credit separately.

Do business credit card rewards count as business income in Canada?

The CRA's general position is that cashback and rewards earned on business credit cards used for deductible business expenses are a reduction of the expense — not income. However, this is nuanced and you should confirm with your accountant. Credit card bonuses for meeting minimum spend thresholds are generally non-taxable.

Which business card has the best travel insurance?

The American Express Business Platinum has the most comprehensive coverage: 31 days emergency travel medical, trip interruption up to $5,000/person, car rental collision, baggage delay, and flight delay. TD Business Travel Visa Infinite is second at 25 days travel medical plus trip cancellation.

How many business credit cards can I hold?

No hard limit. Many businesses run 2–3 cards (one Amex for travel/dining, one Visa/MC for suppliers, one no-fee for USD). The key constraint is credit utilization — holding cards across multiple issuers gives you the highest aggregate limits while keeping individual utilization low.
