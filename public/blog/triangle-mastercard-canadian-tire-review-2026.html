Canadian Tire is one of Canada's most beloved retailers — hardware, sporting goods, automotive, seasonal products, and everything in between. The Triangle Mastercard is Canadian Tire Financial Services' co-branded credit card, and it earns "Canadian Tire Money" on purchases at CT and its family of stores.

There is no annual fee. If you shop at Canadian Tire regularly, this card is worth adding to your wallet. If you don't, there are better no-fee options for general spending.

The Triangle Ecosystem — Stores That Earn 4%

The 4% rate applies at the following Canadian Tire retail banners:

  • Canadian Tire — Hardware, automotive, outdoor, seasonal
  • Sport Chek — Athletic gear, footwear, equipment
  • Mark's (formerly Mark's Work Wearhouse) — Workwear, casual clothing
  • Party City — Party supplies
  • Gas+ stations — Canadian Tire's own gas stations (1.5% here, not 4%)

If your household shops at any combination of CT, Sport Chek, and Mark's regularly, 4% in CT Money adds up. A family spending $300/month at these stores earns $144/year in CT Money — redeemable on virtually everything in-store.

Canadian Tire Money — How It Actually Works

Canadian Tire Money (CTM) is a loyalty currency that functions like a voucher or rebate at any Canadian Tire family store. It is NOT transferable to airline programs, cash, or other retailers. It is redeemable only at CT-banner stores. Think of it as a store credit, not a flexible points currency.

CTM earned on the Triangle Mastercard is loaded to your Triangle account (linked via phone number or Triangle app). You can redeem it at checkout — the cashier applies it like a coupon. There is no minimum redemption amount.

The effective cash value of 1% CTM is exactly $0.01 per dollar (100 points = $1) at any CT store. The 4% rate at CT stores is therefore literally 4 cents per dollar in redeemable store credit.

The 1.5% on gas is competitive with many premium cash back cards for gas specifically. However, the payout is in CT Money (redeemable only at CT stores), not flexible cash. If you regularly shop at Canadian Tire, that distinction is minor — CT Money is almost as useful as cash for CT shoppers.

Who Should Get the Triangle Mastercard

  • Frequent Canadian Tire, Sport Chek, or Mark's shoppers (the 4% rate earns meaningfully)
  • Anyone who uses Gas+ stations regularly (1.5% on gas with no fee)
  • People who want a dedicated CT-loyalty card separate from their main rewards card
  • Canadians who don't qualify for the World Elite income threshold

Rewardly Verdict — 3.6 / 5

The Triangle Mastercard is a solid store-loyalty card for Canadian Tire shoppers. Four percent back at CT, Sport Chek, and Mark's is genuinely good — and there's no fee to hold it. If you spend $150+/month at those stores, the card pays for itself in CT Money quickly.

It scores lower than general-purpose cards because CT Money is less flexible than cash back, and the 0.5% base rate is among the weakest in its fee tier. The ideal strategy: hold the Triangle card for CT purchases, and use a strong general cash back card (Rogers Red, BMO Cash Back, or Simplii) for everything else. Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use CT Money at Sport Chek and Mark's?

Yes — CT Money earned on the Triangle Mastercard can be redeemed at any Canadian Tire family store, including Sport Chek, Mark's, and Canadian Tire retail locations. It cannot be redeemed at non-CT-affiliated stores.

Does Canadian Tire Money expire?

CT Money on the Triangle Mastercard does not expire as long as your account remains in good standing. The digital balance stays in your Triangle account indefinitely.

Is there a Triangle World Elite Mastercard version?

Yes. The Triangle World Elite Mastercard is also no-fee but requires $80K personal or $150K household income. It adds 3% at grocery stores and 7¢/L at Gas+ stations (much better than the base card for gas). If you qualify, the World Elite version is strongly preferred.

Who issues the Triangle Mastercard?

The Triangle Mastercard is issued by Canadian Tire Financial Services, which is a subsidiary managed in partnership with Scotiabank. The card runs on the Mastercard network.

Does the 4% rate apply to Canadian Tire online purchases?

Yes — purchases made on canadiantire.ca count as Canadian Tire purchases and earn the 4% rate. However, you need to be logged in to your Triangle account and the payment must be made with the Triangle Mastercard to earn the bonus rate.
