Aeroplan Family Sharing Canada 2026 — Complete Pooling Guide

Pool Aeroplan points with your partner or family — for free. Here's how it works, who qualifies, and the redemptions it unlocks.

What Is Aeroplan Family Sharing?

Aeroplan Family Sharing is a free program that lets up to 8 Aeroplan members pool their points together when booking flights. You keep earning points in your own individual account — but at the time of redemption, you can draw on all family members' balances combined to make a single booking.

This is one of the most powerful tools in Canadian travel rewards because it lets couples or families unlock high-value redemptions (business class, long-haul flights) that neither person could reach on their own balance alone.

Free flight unlocked example: Partner A has 55,000 pts. Partner B has 50,000 pts. Individually, neither can book a roundtrip to Europe in economy (60,000–70,000 pts). Together via Family Sharing, they have 105,000 pts — enough to book one roundtrip economy to Europe with points left over, or split into two one-ways.

Eligibility: Who Can Join Your Family Sharing Group?

Aeroplan's rules require that members in the same Family Sharing group either (a) live at the same residential address, OR (b) be related as parent/child, grandparent/grandchild, or siblings. Members who qualify under the family relationship rule can be in different cities or provinces.

How to Set Up Aeroplan Family Sharing (Step by Step)

  1. 1

    Log into your Aeroplan account at aeroplan.com
    Go to your profile. You'll find "Family Sharing" under the account management section.

  2. 2

    Choose who will be the "Family Sharing account holder"
    This is the person who creates the group and sends invitations. Typically the person with the most points or the more active Aeroplan member. The account holder can change later.

  3. 3

    Send an invitation to each family member's Aeroplan account
    You'll need each person's Aeroplan number or email address. They'll receive an email invitation and must accept within 30 days.

  4. 4

    Each invited member accepts through their own Aeroplan account
    They log in, find the invitation, review the terms, and accept. Once accepted, their balance is immediately visible to all group members for booking purposes.

  5. 5

    Book flights using combined balances
    When booking on Air Canada or partner airlines via aeroplan.com, select "Use Family Sharing points" to access all member balances. You choose how many points to draw from each member's account.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can we leave the Family Sharing group if we break up or move?
Yes — any member can leave the group at any time from their Aeroplan profile. Points return to their individual account immediately. The account holder can also remove members. There's no penalty or waiting period for leaving. Any bookings already made using pooled points are not affected.
Do points expire differently in Family Sharing?
No — standard Aeroplan expiry rules apply. Points expire after 18 months of inactivity (no earn or burn activity). Family Sharing does not reset or extend expiry. Each member must maintain their own account activity. Pooling at redemption counts as "burning" points, which resets the clock for the accounts whose points were used.
Can I use Family Sharing points to book for friends who are NOT in our group?
Yes — you can use Family Sharing points to book flights for any traveler, not just group members. The restriction is on which accounts' points can be pooled, not on who the passenger is. So you could use your combined family pool to book a flight for a friend if you wanted to gift them a trip.
Does Family Sharing work for partner airline redemptions (United, Lufthansa, etc.)?
Yes — Family Sharing works for all Aeroplan redemptions including Star Alliance partner flights, not just Air Canada. You can pool points to book on United, Lufthansa, Swiss, ANA, Turkish Airlines, and 40+ other partners.
