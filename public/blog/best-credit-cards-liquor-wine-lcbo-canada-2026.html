How Liquor Purchases Code on Credit Cards

Most standalone liquor stores — including LCBO, SAQ, BC Liquor Stores, and private wine shops — code as MCC 5921 (Package Stores — Beer, Wine, and Liquor). This is a distinct MCC from grocery stores (5411) and restaurants (5812).

No major Canadian credit card currently offers a specific bonus category for MCC 5921. This means the strategies are: (a) use a card with a broad "everyday spending" multiplier that captures everything, or (b) buy alcohol at grocery stores where the grocery rate may apply to the entire transaction.

The MCC 5921 nuance: Purchases at a standalone LCBO or SAQ store code as 5921 — not as groceries. However, the Beer Store, certain LCBO Express locations within grocery stores, and grocery chains that sell wine in Ontario (since the 2024 expansion) may code differently — the alcohol portion sometimes codes at grocery MCC 5411 rather than 5921, depending on how the retailer processes the transaction.

Ontario Grocery Store Alcohol — The 4% Opportunity

In 2024, Ontario significantly expanded where beer, wine, and cider can be sold — extending to major grocery chains, convenience stores, and big-box retailers. This created a rewards arbitrage opportunity: buying alcohol at grocery stores (where it codes as MCC 5411) instead of the LCBO (MCC 5921) can nearly double your earn rate on high-bonus-category grocery cards.

Scenario: $100/month on alcohol purchases in Ontario
 
At standalone LCBO (MCC 5921):
→ Scotia Momentum: 1% = $1.00/month = $12/year
 
At Loblaws/Metro with integrated alcohol (MCC 5411 grocery):
→ Scotia Momentum: 4% = $4.00/month = $48/year
→ 4x more rewards by shifting the same purchase to a grocery store
Test before assuming: Grocery store alcohol coding (5411 vs 5921) isn't 100% consistent even at the same store. The first time you buy alcohol at your local Loblaws, check the MCC on your statement before assuming you're earning 4%. Some stores split the transaction or use separate payment terminals for alcohol that code differently.

Best Strategies and Wine Club Tips

Wine Club Subscriptions

Many Canadian wine clubs (including LCBO's wine subscription service and private importers) charge your credit card monthly. These recurring charges are an opportunity to use a card with recurring payment bonuses. The Tangerine Money-Back Card's "Recurring Bill Payments" 2% category captures recurring wine club charges — check if your subscription qualifies.

International Wine Orders

Ordering wine from international wine merchants or importing cases directly incurs foreign transaction fees on standard cards. Use the Rogers Red Mastercard (1.5–2%, no FX on USD) or Scotia Passport Visa Infinite (no FX + travel insurance) for international wine purchases to avoid the 2.5% FX fee that would negate most reward earnings on standard cards.

Stocking Up Before Price Increases

When the LCBO or SAQ announces price increases (which happen periodically), buying ahead in volume is a common strategy. A large one-time purchase is a good opportunity to time your credit card welcome bonus spending threshold — a $500 LCBO run toward a $3,000 welcome bonus threshold earns both the regular category rewards AND contributes to a $500+ welcome bonus.
