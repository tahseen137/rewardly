Best Credit Cards for Families in Canada 2026

Families spend heavily on groceries, gas, recurring bills, and kids' activities. Here's how to earn the most on every household dollar — plus travel insurance for the whole family.

What Families Should Look for in a Credit Card

A family's spending profile differs significantly from a single person's. The right family card needs to perform well across groceries, gas, recurring bills (utilities, streaming, phone), and insurance. Secondary considerations include authorized user cards (so both spouses earn on the same account), travel insurance that covers children, and lounge access when travelling as a family.

Average Canadian family annual card spend (Stats Canada estimates): Groceries: $12,000–$16,000 · Gas: $3,000–$5,000 · Dining out: $4,000–$7,000 · Recurring bills: $3,000–$5,000 · Travel: $4,000–$10,000. Total: ~$25,000–$48,000/year.

The right card can earn 4% on groceries (and gas if using CIBC Dividend). A family spending $15K on groceries and $4K on gas: with CIBC Dividend (4%+4%) earns $760/year; with Scotia Momentum (4%+2%) earns $680/year. The right card can earn $600–$1,500+/year for a typical Canadian family.

Family-Specific Strategy Tips

1. Pool points with Aeroplan Family Sharing

Aeroplan's Family Sharing feature allows up to 8 family members to pool their Aeroplan miles into one account. Each member earns in their own account (credit card spend, flights, partner purchases), then transfers to the shared pool. This dramatically speeds up accumulation for family award redemptions.

2. Make both spouses authorized users on the same card

Most premium cards offer free additional user cards. When your spouse uses their authorized user card, points accumulate on your account. For the Scotia Gold Amex and TD Aeroplan VI, both spouses earning on one account doubles accumulation speed without doubling fees.

3. Don't forget recurring bill optimization

Netflix, Disney+, Spotify, hydro, natural gas, insurance premiums, internet, phone plans — Canadian families often pay $400–$800/month in recurring bills. Charging these to a 4% card (Scotia Momentum) or 2% card (Tangerine) earns significant rewards on autopilot, every month, with zero effort.

4. Shoppers Drug Mart is a family spending goldmine

PC Optimum bonus events at Shoppers are some of the best loyalty promotions in Canada — 20X points ($20 for every $100 spent) are routine during healthcare product events. Charge your Shoppers spend to the PC Financial World Elite for 4.5% baseline, then stack bonus Optimum events on top.

Annual fee math for families: If your family earns $800/year in rewards on a $120/yr card, your true "fee" is just $120 and your net benefit is $680. For most Canadian families spending $25K+/year on credit cards, a $120 annual fee card easily pays for itself in incremental earnings over a no-fee card within 2–3 months of spending.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many credit cards should a family have? Most families benefit from 2 cards: a primary card that earns on your biggest categories (groceries, gas) plus one supplementary card for a specific gap (e.g., the Simplii Visa for dining, or Home Trust for travel). More than 3–4 cards becomes difficult to track and dilutes spending across accounts, slowing point accumulation.
Does travel insurance cover children? Most premium travel credit cards cover dependent children under age 21 (or 25 if full-time students) who travel with the cardholder. The Scotia Gold Amex, TD Aeroplan VI, and PC Financial WE MC all include family emergency medical coverage. Always verify the specific age cutoffs and "travel with the cardholder" requirements in the insurance certificate.
Are free authorized user cards worth it? Yes — authorized user cards on the same account mean both spouses' spending earns toward the same rewards balance, doubling accumulation speed at no extra cost. The BMO eclipse rise even gives a 10% bonus on all points earned when you add an authorized user. Always ensure both spouses use the same primary card's authorized user (not separate cards) to pool rewards.
What's the best card if we shop at Costco? Costco Canada accepts only Mastercard (not Visa or Amex) in-store. Best Mastercards for Costco: MBNA Rewards World Elite (5 pts/$ everywhere including Costco), Tangerine World MC (2% if grocery category), BMO eclipse rise (5x if Costco codes as grocery). The PC Financial WE MC earns 3% at Costco (not the elevated 4.5% since Costco isn't a PC-banner store).
