Best Credit Card for Online Shopping in Canada 2026

Amazon.ca, US websites, subscription services, and e-commerce — which Canadian card earns the most?

📖 10 min read

Online shopping has become one of the largest spending categories for Canadian households — Amazon.ca, Etsy, US retailers, SaaS subscriptions, and more. The right credit card for online shopping depends on one crucial variable: are you buying from Canadian merchants (CAD prices) or US/foreign merchants (USD/EUR prices)?

For Canadian merchants, any good flat-rate or all-purchases rewards card works well. For US and foreign merchants, the 2.5% foreign transaction fee on most Canadian cards turns a 1.5% cash back card into a net -1% losing proposition. Choosing the right card for your mix of domestic and foreign online shopping can easily swing $200–$400/yr in your favor.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does Amazon.ca charge in CAD or USD?

Amazon.ca processes purchases in Canadian dollars (CAD). No foreign transaction fee applies. Your credit card's standard earn rate on all purchases applies. Amazon.com (the US site) charges in USD and will trigger the 2.5% FX fee on most Canadian cards.

Does using PayPal change which FX fee applies?

PayPal charges its own currency conversion fee when converting USD to CAD (typically 3%+ above the base rate). To avoid PayPal's conversion, select "Pay in USD" and let your credit card handle the conversion. A no-FX-fee card like Rogers Red or Home Trust will then apply more favorable exchange rate terms.

Does the Rogers Red 3% apply to all USD purchases?

The Rogers Red earns 3% on purchases made in a currency other than Canadian dollars — not just USD. EUR, GBP, and other foreign currency transactions also earn 3%, making it effective at offsetting FX costs on purchases from any international merchant.

What about Amex acceptance for online shopping?

Amex is accepted by most major online retailers (Amazon.ca, Apple, most subscription services) but not universally. Smaller merchants and some international sites may not accept Amex. If you encounter an Amex-only problem, have a Visa or Mastercard as a backup for those purchases.
