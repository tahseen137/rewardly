Best Air Miles Credit Cards in Canada 2026

BMO issues all Air Miles credit cards in Canada. Here's which tier actually earns the most — and the easiest ways to boost your balance without flying.

Air Miles is Canada's oldest loyalty program — over 11 million active collectors. But most Canadians don't know that BMO is the only bank that issues Air Miles credit cards, and that the program has two separate "banks" within it (Cash Miles and Dream Miles) that you have to manage separately.

This guide ranks all BMO Air Miles credit cards, explains which earn rates are actually competitive, and gives you the strategies to maximize your balance without the confusing fine print.

Air Miles 101: Air Miles splits into two "banks" — Dream Miles (for flights, vacations, experiences) and Cash Miles (for instant in-store redemptions at $10 off per 95 Cash Miles). By default, 75% of your miles go to Dream and 25% to Cash. You can adjust this split in your Air Miles account settings.

The no-fee BMO Air Miles Mastercard is the entry point for collectors. 1 mile per $20 spent is a weak base rate (equivalent to ~0.5% return), but the partner bonus miles make it worth carrying for Sobeys, Shell, and IGA purchases specifically.

The honest calculation: if you spend $400/month at Air Miles partners, you earn ~40 miles/month = 480 miles/year. A domestic flight typically costs 1,000–3,500 Dream Miles, so you're looking at 2–7 years to a free flight on the no-fee card alone. The value comes from stacking partner offers, not the base rate.

Is Air Miles Still Worth It in 2026?

Air Miles has faced criticism for years: devalued redemptions, expiring miles policies (since reversed), and fewer partner locations than the program's peak. Is it still worth collecting?

Yes, but only if:

  • You already shop at Sobeys/IGA/Safeway or fill up at Shell regularly
  • You use the Air Miles app to activate bonus offers before shopping
  • You redeem Cash Miles for in-store instant redemptions ($10 off per 95 miles) rather than holding out for flights

No, if:

  • You mostly shop at Loblaws/No Frills (use PC Optimum instead), Costco, or Walmart
  • You fill up at Canadian Tire gas bars (PC Optimum again)
  • You're optimizing for maximum cashback — Scotia Momentum or CIBC Dividend earn 4% on groceries vs Air Miles World Elite's effective 2.5%

Dream Miles Redemption Guide — Getting the Best Value

Dream Miles are most valuable when redeemed for flights through Air Miles' redemption catalogue. Key price points:

  • Short-haul domestic (e.g., Toronto–Ottawa): ~1,000–1,500 miles
  • Cross-country domestic (e.g., Toronto–Vancouver): ~1,500–3,500 miles
  • Southern US (e.g., Toronto–Fort Lauderdale): ~1,500–2,500 miles
  • Mexico/Caribbean: ~2,500–4,000 miles
  • Transatlantic (e.g., Toronto–London): ~4,000–8,000 miles

Air Miles flights book on Onyx or Air Canada (and sometimes WestJet) through the Air Miles travel portal. Availability can be limited — book at least 3–4 months in advance for the best award availability, especially during peak summer and holiday travel.

Maximizing Air Miles on the World Elite Card

  1. Use the card at Shell — 3 miles/$12 = the highest gas earn rate of any Air Miles card
  2. Load digital offers in the Air Miles app before every trip to a partner location
  3. Set your miles split to 100% Cash Miles if you prefer instant $10 redemptions over flight savings. Cash Miles at $10/95 miles ≈ 10.5¢/mile and don't expire while you have an active account
  4. Watch for Flash Miles events — periodically Air Miles offers "Flash Miles" deals where specific purchases earn 50× or 100× base rate
  5. Use Air Miles for hotel + car packages — sometimes better value than flights alone

Frequently Asked Questions

Do Air Miles expire in Canada?

Air Miles currently do not expire as long as you have an active collector account. In 2016, Air Miles announced a 5-year expiry policy that caused significant backlash and was reversed before it took effect. As of 2026, miles remain valid indefinitely with an active account and at least one qualifying transaction every 24 months.

Can I earn Air Miles at Costco?

No — Costco Canada does not participate in Air Miles. Costco is a Mastercard-only store (for credit cards), and only the Costco Mastercard with Triangle Rewards earns cashback there. If you shop heavily at Costco, PC Optimum and Air Miles aren't relevant for that spend.

What's the difference between Dream Miles and Cash Miles?

Dream Miles are redeemed for flights, vacation packages, experiences, and merchandise through the Air Miles catalog. Cash Miles give instant $10 off at partner locations (95 Cash Miles = $10). You control the split in your Air Miles account — by default it's 75% Dream / 25% Cash, but you can change this to 100% of either type.

Is the BMO Air Miles World Elite card worth the $120 fee?

For heavy Sobeys/Shell shoppers: yes. If you spend $800/month at Air Miles partner locations, you earn ~200 miles/month = 2,400 miles/year. A short domestic flight costs 1,000–1,500 miles, so you're looking at a free flight every 6–8 months — well worth the $120 annual fee. For light users who don't shop at Air Miles partners regularly, the no-fee BMO Air Miles Mastercard is a better fit.

Can I have both a BMO Air Miles card and a Scene+ card at Sobeys?

Yes — this is one of the best stacks available to Canadians. At Sobeys/IGA/Safeway, you can scan your Air Miles collector number AND pay with a Scotiabank Scene+ card, earning both Air Miles AND Scene+ points on the same purchase. The store's loyalty program and the credit card's points program operate independently.
