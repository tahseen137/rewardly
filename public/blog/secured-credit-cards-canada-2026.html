Best Secured Credit Cards in Canada 2026

New to Canada, recovering from bankruptcy, or never had a credit card? A secured card is the fastest path to a strong credit score — and to qualifying for rewards cards that earn real money back.

How Secured Credit Cards Work

A secured credit card requires a cash deposit — typically $200–$10,000 — that becomes your credit limit. The deposit protects the bank against non-payment, which is why secured cards are approved even with no credit history, bad credit, or recent bankruptcy.

The card works exactly like a regular credit card for purchases — it's accepted anywhere Visa/Mastercard is accepted. The issuer reports your payment history to Equifax and TransUnion monthly. Over 6–18 months of on-time payments, your credit score improves. At that point, you can apply for an unsecured card and get your deposit back.

Key principle: The deposit is not "spent" — it sits in a savings account and is returned when you close the card or graduate to unsecured. You're renting credit with your own money to build your history. The only cost is the annual fee (if any) and interest if you carry a balance.

How to Use a Secured Card to Build Credit Fast

1
Keep utilization below 30%. If your limit is $500, keep your balance below $150. Credit bureaus see high utilization as a risk signal. Lower is better — 10% is ideal. Pay down before the statement closes if needed.
2
Never miss a payment. Payment history is the single largest factor in your credit score (35% of your FICO score). Set up automatic minimum payments immediately, then pay the full balance manually before the due date.
3
Use it regularly — at least monthly. Accounts with no activity may be closed by the issuer. Use the card for small recurring purchases (Netflix, a coffee, one tank of gas) to keep it active.
4
Never carry a balance. The interest on secured cards (19.99%) erases any credit-building benefit. Pay the full statement balance every month, every time. Secured cards are tools, not loans.
5
After 6–12 months, check your score. You can monitor your Equifax score free through Borrowell. Once you're above 650–680, start applying for unsecured cards — beginning with basic no-fee options, then moving to rewards cards as your score improves.
6
Graduate, don't close. When you qualify for an unsecured card, ask your issuer if they'll "graduate" your secured card to an unsecured product. If yes, your credit account age is preserved (which helps your score). If not, close the secured card and open an unsecured card elsewhere.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do secured credit cards build credit as fast as regular cards?
Yes — secured cards report to credit bureaus identically to unsecured cards. Lenders and bureaus cannot distinguish between secured and unsecured accounts on a credit report. A positive history from a secured card is worth just as much as one from a premium rewards card.
Can I get a secured card if I'm in a consumer proposal or after bankruptcy?
Yes. Capital One Guaranteed Secured MC and Refresh Financial are no-credit-check options that approve regardless of your credit history. Home Trust also approves post-bankruptcy (typically 2+ years after discharge). Avoid applying to major banks for 2–3 years after bankruptcy discharge — the rejection further hurts your score.
What's the minimum credit score for a secured card?
For guaranteed-approval cards (Capital One, Refresh Financial), there is no minimum — they don't check. For Home Trust, informal guidance suggests a score as low as 540 may be approved. For Scotiabank Value Visa, you likely need 580+. For any big-5 bank product, expect 620+ minimum.
Is a secured card better than a prepaid card for building credit?
Yes, dramatically. Prepaid cards (like Prepaid Visa or Koho) do not report to credit bureaus and do not build credit history. Some prepaid products (Koho Credit Building) are designed as credit-building tools, but a secured card from an established issuer is the most effective and fastest path to credit history.
