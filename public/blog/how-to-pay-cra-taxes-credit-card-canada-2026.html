How to Pay CRA Taxes with a Credit Card Canada 2026

CRA doesn't accept credit cards directly — but third-party processors let you earn Aeroplan miles and cashback on your tax bill. Here's when it's worth the fee.

Can You Pay CRA with a Credit Card?

The Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) does NOT directly accept credit card payments for income tax, HST/GST, payroll deductions, or tax installments. However, third-party payment processors have partnerships that let you pay CRA obligations with a credit card — in exchange for a processing fee (typically 1.45–2.5%).

The most widely used processor in Canada is PaySimply (paymentsimply.com), which accepts Visa, Mastercard, and American Express for CRA payments. The processing fee is around 1.99–2.49% of the payment amount.

Step-by-Step: How to Pay CRA via PaySimply

1

Go to PaySimply.ca

Select "Pay CRA" or "Government of Canada" from the payment options menu.

2

Select your CRA payment type

Options include: Personal Income Tax (T1), HST/GST, Payroll Source Deductions (PD7A), Corporate Income Tax (T2), and Installment Payments.

3

Enter your SIN and the payment amount

For personal income tax, enter your Social Insurance Number. PaySimply passes this to CRA to credit the right account. For corporate payments, use your Business Number (BN).

4

Review the fee and confirm payment

PaySimply clearly shows the processing fee before you confirm. Calculate your expected rewards vs. fee before proceeding. The fee is charged to your card, not added to your CRA balance.

5

Save your confirmation number

PaySimply provides a payment reference number. Save this — it's your proof of payment. CRA typically posts the payment to your account within 3–5 business days.

Tax deadline warning: Don't pay your taxes via credit card at the last minute. CRA processes the date PaySimply posts the payment to them — not your card charge date. Submit 3–5 business days before the CRA deadline to avoid late-filing penalties. April 30 income tax deadline means you should pay by April 25–27 via PaySimply.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does paying taxes with a credit card count toward welcome bonus spend?
Yes — the charge appears on your credit card statement as a PaySimply merchant transaction. This counts toward your card's minimum spend requirement for a welcome bonus. If you owe $3,000 in taxes and need to hit a $3,000 spend threshold for a 50,000-point welcome bonus, a single tax payment can trigger the entire bonus. Just confirm this is your first major spend opportunity and the bonus terms don't explicitly exclude these processors.
Is the PaySimply fee tax-deductible?
The processing fee for paying personal income tax is NOT tax-deductible — it's a personal expense. However, if you're paying HST or business taxes as a self-employed person or corporation, the processing fee may be deductible as a business expense (similar to bank fees). Consult your accountant on the deductibility of the specific payment type you're making.
Does using a credit card to pay taxes affect my credit score?
Yes, if paying a large tax bill significantly increases your credit card utilization. A $10,000 tax payment on a card with a $15,000 limit pushes utilization to 67% — which can temporarily lower your score. If you're concerned, consider paying before the card's statement date (so the balance doesn't appear on your credit report), or pay the card balance immediately after the tax payment is made.
