Common Cashback Card Mistakes to Avoid

1. Carrying a Balance

Cashback cards have 20-22% APR. If you carry a $2,000 balance at 20% APR, you pay $400/year in interest. That wipes out ALL your cashback. Rule: never carry a balance.

2. Ignoring Annual Caps

Some cards cap category bonuses. Example: BMO CashBack World Elite earns 5% on groceries... but only on the first $500/month. After that, it drops to 1%. If you spend $800/month on groceries, you're leaving money on the table.

3. Not Using the Right Card at the Right Merchant

If you have 3 cards (PC Financial for Loblaws, Scotiabank for gas, Rogers for everything else), USE THEM CORRECTLY. Don't accidentally use Rogers at Loblaws and lose 3% back.

4. Paying Late

Late fees ($35-$45) and interest charges destroy cashback gains. Set up autopay for at least the minimum.

5. Not Checking for Better Offers

Banks change welcome bonuses quarterly. Right now (March 2026), Scotiabank Gold Amex is offering 40,000 Scene+ points = $400. That alone covers 3+ years of annual fees.

Cashback Card FAQs

What's the difference between cashback and points?

Cashback: You earn actual dollars (1% = $1 per $100 spent). Usually deposited to your account or statement credit.
Points: You earn loyalty points (1x, 5x, etc.) that you redeem for travel, gift cards, or cash. Points often have better redemption rates (e.g., Amex MR points = 1-2 cents each when transferred to airlines).

Can I have multiple cashback cards?

Yes. In fact, you should. Use PC Financial for groceries (4.5%), Scotiabank Gold for gas (5x), and Rogers for everything else (1.5%). Maximize returns on each category.

How often do I get paid?

Most cards let you redeem cashback anytime once you hit a threshold (usually $25-$50). Some auto-deposit annually. Check your card's terms.

Do cashback cards hurt my credit score?

Applying for a new card = hard inquiry (temporary 5-10 point drop). But having multiple cards with low utilization (<30%) = higher score long-term. Don't apply for 5 cards in one month.

Are there foreign transaction fees?

Most cashback cards charge 2.5% FX fees on USD purchases. Exception: Rogers World Elite has NO FX fees and gives you 3% back on USD = net 0.5% gain when buying in USD.
