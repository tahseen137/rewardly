How Uber and Lyft Code on Canadian Credit Cards

Here's the critical insight most people miss: Uber rides and Uber Eats are charged by separate entities with different merchant category codes (MCCs). This means a card that earns bonus points on dining may earn those points on Uber Eats deliveries — but not on Uber rides. And vice versa.

MCC Classification by Service

Uber Rides MCC 4121 Codes as Taxicabs & Limousines — NOT dining, NOT transit. Earns transit bonus on select cards, base rate on most.
Uber Eats MCC 5812 Codes as Eating Places & Restaurants — earns dining bonus rates. This is the big one for Cobalt users.
Lyft MCC 4121 Same as Uber rides — Taxicabs & Limousines. Transit bonus where available, base rate otherwise.

Key takeaway: Uber Eats earns dining bonus points. Uber ride-hailing earns transit bonus points (on a small number of cards) or just base rate. These are fundamentally different for rewards purposes — choose your card accordingly.

Best Card for Uber Eats (Dining = 5x)

BEST FOR UBER EATS — DINING CATEGORY

American Express Cobalt Card

Uber Eats codes as dining (MCC 5812), and Cobalt earns 5x Amex Membership Rewards on all dining — making it the undisputed winner for food delivery. On a $50 Uber Eats order, you earn 250 Amex MR points worth approximately $2.50–3.75 depending on redemption. At 5x, the Cobalt crushes every other option.

Important: Cobalt only applies to Uber Eats deliveries, not ride bookings. The monthly fee ($12.99/month = $155.88/year) is easily justified if you spend $260+ monthly on dining and food delivery combined. Annual fee: $155.88/yr.

Earn on Uber Eats: 5x Amex MR (~3.75%)  |  Network: Amex accepted by Uber Eats in Canada

RUNNER-UP FOR UBER EATS

Scotiabank Gold American Express

Scotia Gold earns 5x Scene+ on dining, which includes Uber Eats deliveries. Scene+ points are worth 1¢ each for travel and statement credits, making this 5x = 5% return — the same rate as Cobalt. Scotia Gold has the added benefit of no foreign transaction fees (handy if your Uber account charges in USD) and a flat $120 annual fee rather than monthly.

Between Cobalt and Scotia Gold for Uber Eats, the deciding factor is your primary loyalty program. Amex MR (Cobalt) transfers to Aeroplan, Avios, and others at excellent rates. Scene+ (Scotia Gold) redeems well for Scotiabank travel and statement credits. Both earn 5% effective on Uber Eats orders.

Earn on Uber Eats: 5x Scene+ (5%)  |  FX fee: None  |  Annual fee: $120

Best Card for Uber Rides & Lyft

BEST FOR UBER RIDES / LYFT — TRANSIT

BMO Eclipse Visa Infinite

The BMO Eclipse Visa Infinite earns 5x BMO Rewards on transit purchases, and its transit category explicitly includes rideshare services like Uber and Lyft (MCC 4121 — Taxicabs & Limousines). This makes BMO Eclipse the top-earning card for ride-hailing specifically, at 5x BMO Rewards per dollar.

BMO Rewards points are worth approximately 0.67¢ each when redeemed for travel, making the 5x earn = roughly 3.3% effective return on Uber/Lyft rides. Annual fee: $120/yr (first year free). Income requirement: $60K personal / $100K household.

Earn on Uber rides/Lyft: 5x BMO Rewards (~3.3%)  |  Annual fee: $120 (first year free)

Does Amex Work With Uber Canada?

Yes — American Express is fully accepted on Uber Canada, including both ride-hailing and Uber Eats. You can add any Canadian Amex card to your Uber account as a payment method. This means the Cobalt's 5x dining bonus applies to every Uber Eats order paid with Cobalt — one of the highest earn rates for food delivery available in Canada.

Verify your Uber payment method: If you have multiple cards saved in your Uber app, make sure the right card is selected as default. Many Canadians leave an old bank debit card as the default and miss all their credit card rewards. Check the "Payment" section in the Uber app and set your BMO Eclipse (for rides) or Cobalt (for Eats) as the primary method.
