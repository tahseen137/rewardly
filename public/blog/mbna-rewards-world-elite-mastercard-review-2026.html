The MBNA Rewards World Elite Mastercard earns 5 points per dollar on five major spending categories: groceries, restaurants, digital media, memberships, and household utilities — up to $50,000 in each category per calendar year. All other purchases earn 1 point per dollar. At roughly 1 cent per point toward travel, the 5× categories deliver approximately 5% back.

Most Canadians find that groceries, dining, digital subscriptions (Netflix, Spotify, software), memberships (gym, Costco, etc.), and utilities cover a large portion of their monthly spending. For Canadians who concentrate their credit spending in these five categories, the MBNA card delivers strong returns without needing multiple cards.

MBNA is a subsidiary of TD Bank. The card is issued by MBNA but draws on TD's infrastructure and is available online without a TD banking relationship.

Example monthly spend → points:
$800 groceries (5×) = 4,000 pts · $400 restaurants (5×) = 2,000 pts · $100 streaming/digital (5×) = 500 pts · $200 utilities (5×) = 1,000 pts · $500 other (1×) = 500 pts

Monthly total: 8,000 points → ~$80 in travel value at 1¢/pt. That's $960/yr — a net gain of $840 after the $120 fee.
⚠️ The $50,000 per-category cap: The 5× rate applies per category, not total. You can earn 5× on up to $50,000 of groceries AND up to $50,000 of dining in the same year. Once you hit $50K in any single category, that category drops to 1 pt/$ for the remainder of the calendar year.

World Elite Mastercard Benefits

Beyond MBNA's own rewards, the World Elite tier from Mastercard adds benefits that vary by property:

  • Mastercard Travel Pass — access to discounts and perks at select hotels and car rentals
  • Boingo Wi-Fi — unlimited access at 1 million+ Boingo hotspots worldwide
  • Mastercard Airport Concierge — 15% discount on airport meet-and-greet services
  • Zero Liability Protection — not responsible for unauthorized transactions

Notably absent: no Priority Pass lounge access (that generally requires a higher tier annual fee of $150+). If lounge access is a priority, look at the Scotiabank Passport Visa Infinite or National Bank World Elite instead.

Who Should Get the MBNA Rewards World Elite Mastercard

This card is ideal for Canadians whose credit spending is concentrated in groceries, restaurants, streaming/digital, memberships, and household bills. If those five categories represent 60–70% of your monthly spending — which is common for most Canadian households — the MBNA card delivers exceptional value at $120/yr.

You will love this card if you spend $800+/month on groceries and dining, pay several digital subscriptions, have a gym or club membership, and run your utility bills through credit. The 5× across all five simultaneously is uncommon in the Canadian market.

It is less ideal if you spend heavily outside these categories (gas, travel, hardware, clothing, etc. all earn only 1×), frequently spend abroad (the 2.5% FX fee erodes the 5× earn on foreign currency), want airport lounge access, or primarily want cash back rather than travel points.

Rewardly Verdict — 4.2 / 5

The MBNA Rewards World Elite Mastercard is one of Canada's best category rewards cards for everyday household spending. Five percent on groceries, restaurants, digital media, memberships, and utilities is an unusually broad set of high-value 5× categories — particularly strong because memberships and utilities at 5× are rare in the Canadian market.

The main compromises: only 1 pt/$ on gas, travel, and other non-category spending; no airport lounge access; and a 2.5% FX fee that erodes the 5× return on foreign currency purchases. For Canadians who spend primarily in CAD and can route a large share of monthly spending through those five categories, this card delivers exceptional returns at $120/yr. Frequently Asked Questions

What are MBNA Rewards points worth?

Points are worth approximately 1 cent each when redeemed for travel through the MBNA Rewards portal. Redemption for statement credits or gift cards typically yields 0.5–0.8 cents per point. Always redeem for travel to maximize value.

Is MBNA a real bank in Canada?

Yes. MBNA is a subsidiary of TD Bank Group — one of Canada's Big 5 banks. MBNA operates as a standalone credit card issuer and is federally regulated. Your card is backed by TD's infrastructure.

Can I transfer MBNA points to airline loyalty programs?

Currently MBNA Rewards does not offer direct transfers to airline loyalty programs like Aeroplan or WestJet Dollars. Points are redeemed through MBNA's own travel portal. This is a meaningful limitation compared to Amex Membership Rewards or TD Rewards, which can transfer to Aeroplan.

What is the income requirement?

You need a minimum personal annual income of $80,000 or a household income of $150,000. This is higher than most Visa Infinite cards ($60K personal) but standard for World Elite Mastercard tier products.

Which categories earn 5× on the MBNA Rewards World Elite?

The 5× earn rate applies to five categories: groceries, restaurants, digital media (streaming, apps, software), memberships (gym, Costco, clubs, etc.), and household utilities (hydro, gas, internet, cell phone). All other purchases — including gas stations, clothing, travel, and hardware — earn only 1 pt/$. Each category has its own $50,000 annual cap before dropping to 1×.
