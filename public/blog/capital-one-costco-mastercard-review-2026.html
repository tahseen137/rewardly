The Costco-Specific Context

Costco Canada warehouses and Costco.ca accept Mastercard only for credit card payments (Visa and Amex are not accepted). This creates a forced choice for Costco shoppers who want to earn credit card rewards on their warehouse purchases.

The relevant question for most Canadians is: Do I need a specific Costco-branded card?

The answer is no. Any Mastercard works at Costco — you could carry the Scotiabank Gold Amex (but it won't work at Costco) or a PC Financial Mastercard (works, earns PC Optimum points on Costco purchases). The Capital One Costco Mastercard is one option among many Mastercards, though it's the most visible card specifically marketed for Costco shopping.

Important: The Capital One Costco Mastercard earns only 1% on Costco purchases — the same rate as "all other" spending. If you want more than 1% on your Costco grocery spending, you need a different Mastercard that earns higher on groceries. The PC Financial Mastercard earns up to 30 PC Optimum points per dollar at Loblaws-banner stores — but does not earn bonus points at Costco (they're not an affiliated store).

The Gas Benefit — 2% at Costco Pumps

Costco gas is one of the most popular reasons Canadians hold Costco memberships — the pricing is routinely 5–15 cents per litre below branded stations. Using the Capital One Costco Mastercard at Costco gas adds another 2% cash back on top of the pricing advantage.

For a driver filling up 50L per fill-up, twice a month: $150/mo × 2% = $3/mo = $36/yr in gas cash back, on top of the pricing savings. This adds up meaningfully for regular Costco gas buyers.

How Cash Back Redemption Works

Unlike most cards that deposit cash back monthly or quarterly, the Costco Mastercard issues a Costco cash card once per year — redeemable in Costco stores or at Costco gas stations. This annual redemption model means you don't see your cash back until your card anniversary.

This is a drawback if you want flexible cash back. The rewards are Costco-ecosystem locked — you must redeem at Costco. This works perfectly for members who shop at Costco regularly, but is less ideal if you want to apply rewards broadly.

Travel Insurance

The Capital One Costco Mastercard includes purchase protection and extended warranty, but its travel insurance is limited compared to premium cards:

  • Purchase Protection: 90 days against theft or damage
  • Extended Warranty: Doubles manufacturer's warranty, up to 1 additional year
  • No emergency medical coverage
  • No trip cancellation/interruption insurance
  • No baggage coverage

For Costco travel bookings (Costco Travel offers competitive vacation packages), you should use a separate card with comprehensive travel insurance rather than the Costco Mastercard.

Is the Costco Mastercard the Best Card for Costco Shoppers?

Not necessarily. If most of your spending at Costco is on groceries and household goods (the typical Costco cart), you earn only 1% — no better than many other Mastercards. The card shines specifically for gas (2%) and dining (3%).

Best strategy for Costco shoppers: Hold the Rogers Red World Elite Mastercard (1.5% everywhere, no fee, Mastercard) for Costco warehouse purchases, and a separate rewards card for gas and dining if you're optimizing aggressively.

Rewardly Verdict — 3.5 / 5

The Capital One Costco Mastercard is decent but not exceptional. Its 3% dining and 2% gas rates are competitive for a no-fee card, but the 1% on Costco warehouse spending is disappointing — you'd expect better earning on the purchases that motivated applying for the card. The annual-payout-at-Costco redemption model is an extra layer of lock-in. It's a reasonable card if you're already a Costco member and want a single Mastercard to cover dining, gas, and Costco shopping, but more aggressive shoppers will do better pairing a higher-earn Mastercard with this card or replacing it with the Rogers Red WE MC.

Best for: Existing Costco members who want one Mastercard that covers the warehouse, gas, and dining in a single no-fee card.
