Who Should Choose the Amex Cobalt?

Pick Cobalt if:

You shop at a mix of grocery stores (not just Sobeys/IGA), you want to transfer points to Aeroplan or other airline programs, you're optimizing for maximum travel value from your points, you don't frequently travel internationally (so the 2.5% FX fee rarely applies), or you want Cobalt's purchase protection on gadgets and electronics bought online.

Who Should Choose the Scotiabank Gold Amex?

Pick Scotia Gold if:

You travel internationally 2+ times per year (0% FX fee saves 2.5% on every foreign purchase — $4,500 USD in travel spending saves you $112.50 just in FX fees), you primarily shop at Sobeys/Safeway/IGA and can unlock the 6x grocery earn, you want travel insurance included (25-day emergency medical, trip cancellation), or you prefer a lower flat annual fee ($120 vs. $191.88).

Can You Have Both?

The Power Move: Both Cards (Most Effective Option)

Many Canadian points enthusiasts carry both. Strategy: use Cobalt at non-Sobeys grocers and restaurants where earn is identical (5x both), earn Cobalt MR points you can transfer to Aeroplan. Use Scotia Gold for all international purchases (0% FX), Sobeys/Safeway grocery runs (6x vs. Cobalt's 5x), and as the primary card for travel bookings where its travel insurance applies. Annual fee total: ~$276 — worth it if combined annual rewards exceed $600, which they easily do at moderate spending levels.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do Cobalt MR points and Scotia Scene+ points ever interact?
No — these are completely separate loyalty programs. Amex Membership Rewards (earned by Cobalt) can be transferred to Aeroplan, Marriott Bonvoy, British Airways Avios, and others. Scene+ (earned by Scotia Gold) redeems for movies, Scotia travel booking, restaurant discounts, and select hotel/retail options. You cannot convert one to the other.
Both cards are American Express — will they be declined at the same merchants?
Yes — both are Amex cards with the same merchant acceptance issues. Costco Canada does not accept Amex. Some smaller retailers and service providers do not accept Amex. If merchant acceptance is a concern, add a no-fee Visa or Mastercard (Tangerine, BMO eclipse rise) as a backup for non-Amex merchants. This is especially important for Costco grocery runs — the Cobalt/Gold combo's main gap.
Is the annual fee difference worth it for the Cobalt?
If you mostly spend on food and dining and plan to transfer points to Aeroplan for premium travel redemptions, yes — the extra $35.88/year for Cobalt vs. Scotia Gold is easily recouped. Both cards earn 5x at non-Sobeys grocery stores, so Cobalt wins on point VALUE (not quantity) at Loblaws, Metro, or independent grocers — MR points are worth 1.5–2¢ each via Aeroplan versus 1¢ for Scene+. At Sobeys-banner stores, Gold actually beats Cobalt on quantity (6x vs 5x), but Cobalt's higher point value still wins for Aeroplan redeemers. The points flexibility (Amex MR's 1:1 to Aeroplan) adds significant value for travel-focused redeemers.
