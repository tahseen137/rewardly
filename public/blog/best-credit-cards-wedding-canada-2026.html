Best Credit Cards for Weddings in Canada 2026

The average Canadian wedding costs $30,000+. With the right card strategy, that's $600–$2,000+ in rewards — or a free honeymoon flight.

Why Weddings Are a Rewards Goldmine

A Canadian wedding involves massive, concentrated spending across multiple categories in a short window. Venue deposits, catering contracts, florals, photography, wedding attire, honeymoon flights and hotel — often $25,000–$40,000 or more, much of it paid by credit card within 12–18 months. This is an unusually strong opportunity to earn welcome bonuses, hit spend thresholds, and earn rewards on every dollar.

The biggest opportunity: Apply for a new card 12–18 months before your wedding date. Spend $3,000–$5,000 (common minimum spend for welcome bonuses) within 90 days using venue deposits and early vendor payments. Earn 60,000–100,000 Aeroplan points. Pool with your partner's matching card via Aeroplan Family Sharing. Book your honeymoon flights with points.

For most wedding vendors (photography, florals, venue hall, attire), you'll earn the base rate on most cards. Catering and rehearsal dinners are the exceptions — they code as dining, earning 5x on Amex Cobalt. This is one case where a flat-rate card like Rogers Red (2% everywhere) may outperform category cards on the majority of wedding vendors.

Best Cards for Wedding Spend

#1 Best Overall: Amex Cobalt (Catering + Honeymoon Dining)

5x MR on catering/dining · 2x on hotel · 1x on all other vendors

Annual fee: $156. Best for: catering company payments (usually a large invoice = big 5x earn), rehearsal dinner, and honeymoon dining. Amex MR transfers 1:1 to Aeroplan — use for honeymoon flights. Not ideal for the bulk of vendors (photography, florals, venue hall) where it earns only 1x.

#2 Best for Most Vendors: Rogers Red WE Mastercard (2% Flat)

2% cashback on ALL spend — no categories

Annual fee: $0 (or $29). Best for: all the vendors where no card earns more than base rate — photographers, florists, wedding planner, dress boutique, cake shop. 2% flat on $15,000 of "no-category" wedding vendors = $300 cashback. No annual fee makes it zero-risk to hold alongside your primary card.

#3 Best for Honeymoon Flights: TD Aeroplan Visa Infinite (Applied Early for Welcome Bonus)

3x Aeroplan on Air Canada · 1.5x on AC partners · 1x elsewhere · 60K welcome bonus

Annual fee: $139. Best for: the honeymoon flight booking itself (3x on Air Canada) and leveraging the welcome bonus. Apply 14–18 months before your wedding. Use venue deposit and catering payments to hit the $3,000–$5,000 minimum spend threshold in the first 90 days. Then pool 60,000 bonus points with your partner's matching welcome bonus via Family Sharing = 120,000+ points = 2 business class seats to Europe or Asia.

The Ideal Wedding Credit Card Timeline

  • 18 months before

    Apply for TD Aeroplan Visa Infinite (both partners)
    60,000 pt welcome bonus each. Use engagement party costs and early venue deposit to hit the $3,000 minimum spend within 90 days.

  • 12–15 months

    Apply for Amex Cobalt
    30,000–45,000 pt welcome bonus. Pay catering deposits (5x = $500 on $10K catering contract) and rehearsal dinner deposits through Cobalt.

  • 6–12 months

    Use Rogers Red for remaining vendors
    Photography, flowers, DJ, cake, venue hall — all earn 2% flat. $10,000 in misc vendors = $200 cashback.

  • 3–6 months

    Set up Aeroplan Family Sharing
    Pool TD + Amex → Aeroplan points. Both partners now have access to combined balance for the honeymoon booking.

  • After wedding

    Book honeymoon with combined points
    Use 3x earn on TD Aeroplan card for honeymoon flight purchase (or book with points if you have enough). Use Cobalt 2x for hotel bookings.

The Math on a $30,000 Wedding

One caution: Wedding vendors increasingly use Stripe or Square processing, which sometimes charges a merchant surcharge for credit card payments (1.5–3%). Ask each vendor before assuming you can charge everything. Some venues and independent vendors prefer e-Transfer to avoid card processing fees.
