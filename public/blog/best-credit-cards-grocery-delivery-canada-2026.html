The Grocery Delivery Coding Dilemma

Grocery delivery creates one of the most important MCC coding distinctions in Canadian credit card rewards. When you order groceries through a third-party platform like Instacart, the charge may code as the delivery platform (Instacart, MCC 5999 — Miscellaneous or 5411 — Grocery Stores) rather than the actual grocery store. Whether your card's grocery bonus applies depends on how the charge processes.

This means a card earning 5% on groceries might only earn 1% on Instacart orders — depending on the issuer's interpretation of the MCC code. The most reliable way to maximize grocery delivery rewards is knowing exactly how each service codes on your card, and choosing the right card accordingly.

The key question: Does grocery delivery code as "grocery" (5411) or something else (5999/online shopping)? The answer varies by platform and by card issuer. Instacart typically codes differently than ordering directly from Loblaws.ca.

Platform-by-Platform: How They Code

🛒

Instacart

Typically codes as: Instacart (MCC varies — sometimes 5411 grocery, sometimes 5999 misc)

Instacart's MCC coding is inconsistent across Canadian issuers. Some cards treat Instacart as grocery (5411) and apply the grocery bonus rate. Others code it as a service charge (5999) at base rate. The Amex Cobalt has historically recognized Instacart as food delivery (similar to Uber Eats), earning 5x points. Scotiabank Gold Amex has similarly given Instacart the grocery bonus. Test with a small order on your specific card.

Best card: Amex Cobalt or Scotiabank Gold Amex 5x dining/grocery (if coded correctly)
🔵

Walmart Grocery Delivery (walmart.ca)

Codes as: Walmart (MCC 5912 or 5411) — typically recognized as grocery

Ordering groceries directly from Walmart.ca typically codes under a Walmart MCC — which most Canadian cards that have "grocery stores" as a bonus category will recognize. The CIBC Dividend Visa Infinite earns 4% at grocery stores and typically recognizes Walmart grocery orders. Scotiabank Momentum VI earns 4% at grocery stores and is one of the most consistent cards for Walmart grocery orders.

Best card: Scotiabank Momentum VI or CIBC Dividend VI 4% cashback (grocery bonus)
📦

Amazon Fresh (via Amazon.ca)

Codes as: Amazon.ca (MCC 5999 or Amazon-specific) — NOT grocery

Amazon Fresh purchases process through Amazon.ca, which codes as online marketplace — NOT as grocery stores. This means grocery bonus cards do NOT earn the grocery rate on Amazon Fresh. Amazon.ca is not a bonus category on the BMO Eclipse VI either — it earns 1x BMO Rewards (0.67% effective for travel). The Tangerine World MC (2% if "online shopping" is chosen as a category) is the best option for Amazon Fresh orders. Amazon Fresh in Canada is limited in availability — check if it's offered in your area.

Best card: Tangerine World MC (2% — online shopping category) 2% cash back on Amazon Fresh orders, no annual fee
🏪

Loblaws Click & Collect / Grocery Gateway

Codes as: Loblaws/Loblaw Companies (MCC 5411 — Grocery Stores)

Ordering online at Loblaws.ca or No Frills, Zehrs, and Real Canadian Superstore online typically codes under the store's MCC (5411 — Grocery Stores). This means grocery bonus cards earn their full grocery rate on Loblaws-family online orders. Also, you earn PC Optimum points on Loblaws orders simultaneously — the best stack in Canadian grocery.

Best card: Scotiabank Momentum VI or CIBC Dividend VI (4% grocery) + PC Optimum points stacked 4% cashback + 10–30 PC Optimum pts/$1
🥑

Skip The Dishes (Grocery items)

Codes as: SkipTheDishes (food delivery MCC 5812 or 5999)

Skip The Dishes codes primarily as food delivery (similar to Uber Eats) regardless of whether you're ordering restaurant food or convenience grocery items. Cards with a dining bonus typically earn that rate on Skip orders. Amex Cobalt's 5x dining would apply. Note: "grocery delivery" through Skip is typically convenience items, not full weekly shopping.

Best card: Amex Cobalt or Scotiabank Gold Amex 5x dining category
