Why 2× everywhere is powerful

Most premium cards earn 3–6× in bonus categories and 1× everywhere else. If you spend heavily in non-bonus categories (professional services, utilities, rent via PayTM, contractor work, advertising), those 1× rates are dead weight. The Amex Gold earns 2× on everything — your lawyer's invoice, your SaaS subscriptions, your contractor payments, your life insurance. For professional and business-adjacent spend, 2× everywhere is often better than 5× dining + 1× everything else.

Key Benefits

✈️

$100 Annual Travel Credit

Applied automatically as a statement credit when you book travel (flights, hotels, car rentals) charged to your card. This reduces your effective annual fee from $250 to $150.

👥

Free Supplementary Cards (up to 9)

Add family members, partners, or employees to your account at no charge. Each earns 2× MR points on all their spending, credited to your account. For couples or families, this doubles or triples your point earn — significant at 2× base rate.

🔄

Points Transfer to Aeroplan (1:1)

Transfer Membership Rewards to Air Canada Aeroplan at a 1:1 ratio, with a 10% bonus on transfers of 10,000+ points. Your 2× everywhere effectively becomes 2.2× Aeroplan when you transfer in bulk — that's more than any standard card earns on Aeroplan spend directly (TD/CIBC Aeroplan earns 1× on most purchases).

🌍

Multiple Transfer Partners

Beyond Aeroplan, transfer to British Airways Avios, Marriott Bonvoy, Cathay Pacific Asia Miles, and others. MR points are among Canada's most flexible loyalty currency.

🛡️

Strong Purchase Protection

Extended warranty (1 year additional), 90-day purchase protection, and buyer's assurance. Purchase protection on high-value items is worth more than most cardholders realize — electronics, jewelry, appliances.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Amex Gold the same as Amex Cobalt?
No — two different cards. Amex Cobalt ($12.99/month, $155.88/year) earns 5× on dining and groceries but 1× elsewhere. Amex Gold ($250/year) earns 2× on everything, no bonus categories. Both transfer to Aeroplan. Cobalt is better for dining-heavy spenders; Gold is better for those with high non-food spending.
How does the Aeroplan 10% transfer bonus work?
When you transfer 10,000+ Membership Rewards points to Aeroplan at once, you receive a 10% bonus — 10,000 MR → 11,000 Aeroplan. On transfers of 100,000 MR, you'd receive 110,000 Aeroplan. This effectively improves your earn rate from 2× MR to 2.2× Aeroplan for large transfers.
Can I use the $100 travel credit at any travel booking?
The credit applies to eligible travel purchases charged to your card — typically flights, hotels, car rentals, cruises. It's applied automatically as a statement credit once qualifying travel charges appear. Check current American Express terms for the exact eligible merchant categories.
What's the difference between Amex Gold and Amex Platinum in Canada?
Amex Platinum ($799/year) adds airport lounge access (Centurion Lounges, Priority Pass), Fine Hotels & Resorts benefits (room upgrades, breakfast for 2, $100 property credit), automatic Marriott Gold and Hilton Gold elite status, and concierge services. Amex Gold is the middle tier — better than Cobalt's simplicity, but without the Platinum's luxury benefits.
