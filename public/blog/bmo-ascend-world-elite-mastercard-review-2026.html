BMO Rewards value: BMO Rewards points are worth ~0.67¢ each when redeemed for travel through BMO's travel portal. So 5x travel = ~3.35% on travel purchases, 3x dining = ~2%, 1x base = ~0.67%. Points can also be redeemed for merchandise, gift cards (at slightly lower value), or used for flight rebates.

The Ascend's $5M emergency medical limit exceeds many competitors at the same price point. Baggage coverage at $2,000/person is strong. Trip cancellation at $2,500/person is competitive. However, the 21-day medical window is shorter than the National Bank WE MC (60 days) or Desjardins Odyssey WE MC (60 days).

Lounge Access: 4 Complimentary Visits

The Ascend includes 4 complimentary DragonPass airport lounge visits per year. At ~$35–$45 USD per visit, that's $140–$180 USD in annual lounge value — partially offsetting the $150 annual fee. Additional visits: ~$32 CAD each.

Lounge access compared: The BMO Ascend gets 4 visits vs. the Scotiabank Passport VI's 6 visits (also $150/yr) and the Desjardins Odyssey WE MC's unlimited visits ($130/yr). If unlimited lounge access is a priority, the Desjardins card is a superior choice at a lower fee.

Pros and Cons

✓ What We Love

  • 5x BMO Rewards on all travel — strong category earn
  • $5M emergency medical — one of the highest limits available
  • $2,000 baggage insurance — above average
  • 4 lounge visits per year through DragonPass
  • 3x on dining AND entertainment — good for active social spenders
  • World Elite Mastercard benefits — 24/7 concierge, zero liability

✗ Where It Falls Short

  • No grocery earn bonus — 1x only; BMO eclipse rise earns 5x on groceries at no fee
  • $150 fee with no year 1 waiver
  • BMO Rewards redemptions complex vs. pure cash back
  • $80K income requirement limits accessibility
  • Only 4 lounge visits vs 6 (Scotia Passport) or unlimited (Desjardins) at same/lower fee
  • 21-day medical window shorter than competitors at same price
  • 2.5% FX fee — not a foreign-use card

Verdict: Good for Travel-Heavy Spenders, Outperformed in Daily Categories

The BMO Ascend WE MC is a well-constructed travel card — $5M medical, 4 lounge visits, and 5x on travel purchases are genuine strengths. But at $150/year with 1x on groceries, it's outperformed in everyday categories by the no-fee BMO eclipse rise. Best for cardholders who spend significantly on travel (flights, hotels) and want premium insurance, but can pair it with an eclipse rise for grocery and daily spending.
