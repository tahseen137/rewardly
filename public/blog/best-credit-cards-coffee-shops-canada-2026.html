Best Credit Cards for Coffee Shops in Canada (2026)

Tim Hortons, Starbucks, Second Cup, your local café — earn 5x on every cup with the right card.

Canadians buy more than 2.7 billion cups of coffee per year — and for habitual coffee drinkers, daily café visits represent a surprisingly significant credit card spend category. At $5–$8 per visit, 5 days a week, that's $1,300–$2,000/year at coffee shops. The right credit card earns 5x on this spend; the wrong one earns 1%.

Starbucks codes as 5812 or 5814. Independent cafes typically code as 5812. This means any card with a dining or food bonus category captures coffee shop spending at the bonus rate.

How Coffee Shop Purchases Code

Tim Hortons, McDonald's McCafé, and drive-through chains typically code as MCC 5814 (Fast Food Restaurants). Starbucks, Second Cup, Balzac's, and sit-down independent cafes typically code as MCC 5812 (Restaurants/Eating Places).

The good news for rewards optimization: virtually all Canadian credit cards that bonus "dining" or "food & drinks" capture both MCC 5812 and 5814 within their bonus category. The Amex Cobalt's 5x "Eat & Drink" category, the Scotia Gold Amex's 5x "Dining" category, and similar cards all earn bonus rates on Tim Hortons and Starbucks equally.

Starbucks app and mobile order edge case: When you pay through the Starbucks app using a linked credit card, your credit card typically earns rewards at the same dining MCC as paying in-store. However, if you load money onto a Starbucks gift card balance using your credit card, the transaction codes as a gift card purchase — not dining — and may not earn bonus category rewards. Pay through the app with your credit card linked directly, not pre-loaded on the Starbucks balance.

Starbucks Rewards & Tims Rewards vs. Credit Card Rewards

Both Starbucks and Tim Hortons have loyalty programs that earn rewards on their own purchases. The question many Canadians ask: should I use the in-app payment (earning loyalty stars/rolls) or pay with my credit card?

Starbucks Rewards

Starbucks Rewards earns 1 Star per $1 spent (or 3 Stars per $1 when you pre-load and pay via the app). 150 Stars = a free drink (roughly $7 value). That's approximately 4.6% return when paying via the pre-loaded app. However, pre-loading your Starbucks card via credit card may not earn dining category rewards — the pre-load codes as a gift card purchase.

Optimal Starbucks strategy: Link your Amex Cobalt directly to the Starbucks app (not pre-loading a balance, but using the card as the payment method). This earns 5x Cobalt MR (~10% effective) on the transaction while also earning Starbucks Stars for that payment. Best of both.

Tim Hortons Tims Rewards

Tims Rewards earns points on every purchase — 10 points per visit, with free items at 70 and 140 points. This is a relatively low earn rate in dollar terms (roughly 1–2% equivalent value). Paying with a dining bonus credit card (Cobalt 5x) significantly outperforms Tims Rewards in raw value, but you can earn both: scan the Tims app for Tims points, then pay with your Cobalt for credit card rewards. There's no conflict between the two programs.

Stack them all: At Tim Hortons, you can: (1) scan the Tims Rewards app → earn Tims points, AND (2) pay with your Amex Cobalt → earn 5x MR. These stack completely — no trade-off. At Starbucks: (1) pay with Cobalt linked directly as payment method → earn 5x MR AND Starbucks Stars. The only thing that breaks the credit card earn is pre-loading gift card balances.

Bottom Line

For coffee-focused credit card optimization, the Amex Cobalt is the clear winner — 5x MR on every coffee shop purchase at a potential ~10% effective return. For a daily coffee habit at $1,560/year, the $156 earned roughly covers the $191.88 annual fee with coffee spend alone, before counting the 5x earn on restaurants and other dining where the Cobalt also excels.

For those without an annual fee budget, the Rogers Red at 2% provides a guaranteed return on every coffee purchase with no category thinking required.
