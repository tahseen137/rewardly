The American Express SimplyCash Preferred is one of Canada's most straightforward cashback cards: 4% on groceries, 4% on gas, 2% on absolutely everything else. No earning caps. No annual cashback payout delay. No points redemption complexity. Just cashback deposited to your statement automatically.

The annual fee is $99 — cheaper than CIBC Dividend ($120) and Scotiabank Momentum ($120). The question is whether it earns more in practice. The 2% base rate on all other spending is the answer: nearly every dollar you spend earns at least 2%, making the card one of the highest average-earn cards in Canada.

The 2% on everything is the differentiator. CIBC Dividend and Scotia Momentum earn more on specific categories (2% drug stores, 2% transit, etc.) but drop to 1% on all other spending. Amex SimplyCash Preferred earns 2× more than either card on spending that falls outside their bonus categories.

Think about what earns only 1% on CIBC or Scotia: clothing, electronics, Amazon, home improvement, dentist bills, school supplies, pet supplies, entertainment. On SimplyCash Preferred, all of those earn 2% automatically. For diverse spenders, this is a significant advantage.

Welcome Offer — The Best in Class

The SimplyCash Preferred's welcome offer is unusually strong: 10% cashback on all purchases for the first 3 months, up to $2,500 in total spend (max $250 back). This structure means almost any spender can capture the full bonus — you don't have to concentrate spend on specific categories or make a large one-time purchase.

$250 cashback + $0 fee (sometimes offered in first year) = an immediate $349 effective value in year one. That's 353% return on the $99 annual fee.

The more diverse your spending, the bigger SimplyCash's advantage. Households with lots of spending outside groceries and gas (restaurants, online, entertainment, services) earn significantly more with SimplyCash's 2% base rate.

The Amex Acceptance Issue

The main practical limitation of any Amex card in Canada: not every merchant accepts it. Costco doesn't accept Amex in-store. Some smaller retailers and restaurants are Visa/Mastercard only. This matters more for a cashback card than a travel card, because you need to swipe it on everyday purchases to earn.

The workaround most SimplyCash Preferred holders use: keep it as the primary card for everyday spending, and carry a Visa or Mastercard as a fallback for Costco and any merchant that doesn't take Amex. The 2% base rate means you're still earning more than most fallback Visa/MC cards on the categories where you do use it.

Insurance and Purchase Protection

  • Purchase protection: 90 days against theft, loss, or accidental damage
  • Extended warranty: Doubles manufacturer's warranty up to 1 year
  • Buyer's assurance: 90-day purchase protection on most purchases
  • No travel insurance — this is a pure cashback card, not a travel card

The lack of travel insurance is expected for a $99 cashback card. If travel insurance is important to you, pair it with the Scotiabank Passport or a dedicated travel card, or purchase annual trip insurance separately.

Final Verdict

Rating: 4.3/5. The Amex SimplyCash Preferred is arguably Canada's most underrated cashback card. The 4% on groceries, 4% on gas matches the best in Canada, but the 2% on everything else is the real differentiator — households with diverse spending patterns consistently earn 30–60% more cashback annually than they would on CIBC or Scotia at the same fee tier. The annual payout and Amex acceptance limitations are the main drawbacks. For anyone whose spending is more diverse than "just groceries and gas," this card earns more — often by a large margin.

Frequently Asked Questions

When does Amex SimplyCash Preferred pay out cashback?

Cashback is paid out annually, typically in February, as a statement credit. This is different from Scotiabank Momentum (which pays monthly) and is one of the reasons some users prefer Momentum despite the higher fee. However, the cashback accumulates throughout the year and you can track it in real-time in your Amex online account.

Is there a spending cap on the 4% cashback?

No — the Amex SimplyCash Preferred has no earning caps on either the 4% gas/grocery rate or the 2% base rate. This is a meaningful advantage over some competitor cards that cap bonus earning at specific monthly thresholds.

Does the 4% apply at all grocery stores?

Yes — any purchase that codes as a grocery store earns 4%. This includes Loblaws, Sobeys, No Frills, Real Canadian Superstore, Metro, Walmart Supercentres (for the grocery portion), and independent grocery stores. Costco codes as a warehouse club (not grocery) so earns 2%.

What's the no-fee SimplyCash card?

The Amex SimplyCash (no Preferred) is the no-fee version: 1.25% on all purchases with no bonus categories. If you can't afford or don't want to pay $99, the no-fee SimplyCash is a decent fallback, but the extra 0.75% (to 2%) on everything plus 4% on gas/grocery with the Preferred card usually pays for the $99 fee if you spend $1,000+/month.
