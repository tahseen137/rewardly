Best Credit Cards for New Canadians & Newcomers 2026

No Canadian credit history doesn't mean no credit card. All major banks have newcomer programs that waive normal income and credit requirements for new permanent residents, international students, and recent immigrants.

Why It's Hard to Get a Credit Card as a Newcomer

Canadian credit cards require a Canadian credit history. New permanent residents (PRs), international students, and recent immigrants have no Canadian credit file at Equifax or TransUnion — regardless of their credit history abroad. Credit bureaus in different countries don't communicate, so a perfect credit score in India, the Philippines, or Brazil doesn't help you in Canada.

Good news: Most major banks in Canada have special "newcomer programs" that waive the credit history requirement for recent permanent residents and international students. You can get a premium credit card (not just a secured card) within weeks of arriving — as long as you meet immigration and income requirements.

What If You Don't Qualify for a Newcomer Program?

Not everyone qualifies for newcomer programs — some visa types, work permit holders, and visitors may not be eligible. In that case:

Option 1: Secured Credit Card

A secured credit card requires a security deposit (typically $500–$2,500) that becomes your credit limit. No credit history needed. Best options: Home Trust Secured Visa ($0 or $59/yr, lenient approval), Capital One Guaranteed Secured Mastercard ($59/yr, no credit check, guaranteed approval). See our full secured credit card guide.

Option 2: Nova Credit (US Credit History)

If you moved from the United States, Nova Credit can translate your US credit history for Canadian lenders. American Express Canada accepts Nova Credit reports from US immigrants. More institutions are adopting Nova Credit annually.

Option 3: Credit Builder Products

Koho's credit builder feature and similar fintech products let you build a credit file without a traditional credit card. Koho charges a monthly fee but reports payments to Equifax — useful for building a credit file quickly without a security deposit.

How to Build a Strong Credit Score in Canada: Timeline

Month 1

Apply for a newcomer credit card or secured card immediately

Credit history length matters. Start the clock as soon as possible. Even a no-fee newcomer card starts your Canadian credit file on day 1.

Months 1–6

Use the card for small regular purchases; pay in full monthly

Keep utilization under 30% of your credit limit. Pay the full balance every month — not just the minimum. Never miss a payment.

Month 6–12

Consider a second card (different type)

Adding a second card (e.g., Mastercard if your first was Visa) can help — different account types improve credit mix. The second card's credit limit also lowers your overall utilization.

Year 1–2

Credit score typically reaches 680–720 range

With 12+ months of on-time payments, zero missed payments, and low utilization, most newcomers reach a "good" credit score range sufficient for most financial products.

Year 2+

Upgrade to premium rewards cards

With a 680+ score and stable income, you can qualify for premium credit cards (TD Aeroplan VI, Amex Cobalt, Scotia Gold Amex) with the best rewards and travel insurance benefits.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can international students get a credit card in Canada?
Yes — most major banks include international students in their newcomer programs. Scotiabank StartRight, RBC Newcomer, CIBC Newcomer, and BMO all explicitly cover international students. Income requirements may be lower or waived for students. You'll need a valid study permit and proof of enrollment at a Canadian educational institution.
Does my credit history from my home country count in Canada?
Generally no — Canadian credit bureaus (Equifax and TransUnion Canada) maintain separate files from international bureaus. Your perfect credit score in India, China, or the US does not automatically transfer. Exception: Nova Credit can translate US credit history for American immigrants at participating Canadian lenders (currently American Express Canada). Canadian newcomer programs exist precisely to bridge this gap.
What documents do I need to apply for a newcomer credit card?
Typically: (1) Proof of immigration status — Permanent Resident card, Confirmation of Permanent Residence (COPR), or study/work permit. (2) Canadian SIN (Social Insurance Number). (3) Proof of address in Canada (bank statement, utility bill, or lease). (4) Proof of income (pay stub, employment letter) if the card has income requirements. Some banks may accept a Canadian bank account statement as additional verification.
What is a good first credit card for a newcomer to Canada?
The Scotiabank Scene+ Visa (no fee) through the StartRight program is an excellent first card — no annual fee, earns Scene+ points, no income requirement. If you have income and want stronger rewards, the BMO eclipse rise Visa (no fee, 5x on core categories) through BMO's newcomer program is outstanding. Both are available without Canadian credit history.
