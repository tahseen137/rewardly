What Is the Wealthsimple Cash Card?

The Wealthsimple Cash card is a prepaid Visa card issued through Wealthsimple, Canada's largest online investment platform (RRSP, TFSA, non-registered accounts). It's designed as a companion spending card for Wealthsimple's existing customers — your cashback can automatically be swept into your Wealthsimple investment account, which is its most differentiated feature.

Like KOHO and EQ Bank Card, the Wealthsimple Cash card is a prepaid card — not a credit card. You load funds into your Wealthsimple Cash account before spending. This means no overdraft risk and no interest charges, but also no automatic credit building and some merchant limitations (hotels, car rentals prefer credit cards for authorization holds).

The Auto-Invest Feature — What Makes Wealthsimple Cash Unique

Wealthsimple Cash's differentiator is that your 1% cashback can automatically flow into your Wealthsimple investment accounts — TFSA, RRSP, or non-registered. For someone who already uses Wealthsimple for investing, this creates a seamless loop: spending earns cashback that immediately becomes invested capital.

The round-up feature works similarly — every purchase is rounded up to the nearest dollar (or your chosen increment), and the rounding difference is invested automatically. A $4.75 coffee becomes $5.00, with $0.25 auto-invested. Over a year of daily coffee purchases, this might add $90–$180 in automatic investments from rounding alone.

Real value of 1% invest vs. 1% statement credit: At $2,000/month spending, 1% = $20/month = $240/year. If invested in a balanced ETF portfolio earning 7% annualized, $240/year in invested cashback grows to approximately $3,600 over 10 years due to compounding. The same $240 as a statement credit is $240 flat. Auto-investing the cashback is genuinely more valuable long-term — assuming you'd be saving/investing this money anyway.

Who Should Use the Wealthsimple Cash Card?

Best fit: Wealthsimple investors who want their everyday spending to feed their investment accounts effortlessly. If you already use Wealthsimple for your TFSA or RRSP, the Cash card creates a seamless "spend → earn → invest" loop at zero cost.

Also useful for: international travel where 0% FX fee beats most Canadian debit cards. If you're travelling to Europe or the US and need a card that won't add 2.5% on every purchase, Wealthsimple Cash delivers this at no cost (alongside KOHO and EQ Bank Card as comparable alternatives).

Not the best fit: Those who want the highest possible rewards rate (Rogers Red WE MC at 1.5% on a credit card beats 1%), those focused on credit building (use a traditional credit card), or those with large bonus-category spend where a 5x card (Cobalt) would dramatically outperform.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does the Wealthsimple Cash card build credit?
No — the Wealthsimple Cash card is a prepaid Visa, not a credit card. It does not appear on your credit report and does not contribute to your credit score (positively or negatively). If building credit is your goal, you need a traditional credit card that reports monthly to Equifax and TransUnion.
Can I use Wealthsimple Cash to pay at Costco Canada?
Costco Canada accepts Mastercard only — and the Wealthsimple Cash card is a Visa prepaid card. So no, Wealthsimple Cash cannot be used at Costco Canada. For Costco, you need a Mastercard (Rogers Red WE MC, PC Financial WE MC, MBNA cards, or other Mastercards).
How does the 1% cashback auto-invest work?
You configure auto-invest in the Wealthsimple app — choose which account (TFSA, RRSP, or non-registered) to sweep cashback into, and choose a portfolio. When cashback accumulates, it's automatically invested per your settings. Note: for RRSP contributions, make sure you have available contribution room — auto-investing into an RRSP still counts as a contribution against your annual limit.
