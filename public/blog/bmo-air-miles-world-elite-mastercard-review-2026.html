AIR MILES — What's a Mile Worth?

Before evaluating the card, you need to understand AIR MILES value. AIR MILES reward miles have two uses:

  • Dream Miles (travel): Flights, hotels, car rentals, vacation packages. Value varies; roughly 10–14 cents per mile for flight redemptions.
  • Cash Miles: In-store discounts at AIR MILES partners (Shell, Safeway, Rexall, Harvey's, etc.). Value is more transparent: roughly 10 cents per mile at many partners.

At 10–14 cents per mile, the effective earn rate on this card ranges from approximately 1–2% depending on where you spend. This is lower than top cash-back cards, but AIR MILES are valuable to collectors who shop regularly at partner stores (Shell, Safeway, Rexall, Metro, Loblaws) because those purchases stack: you earn from the store AND from the credit card.

The AIR MILES stacking advantage: When you use this card at an AIR MILES partner store (like Shell or Safeway), you earn AIR MILES from the retailer AND from your credit card. A $100 Safeway purchase might earn 10 miles from Safeway + 20 miles from the card = 30 miles total. This stacking is unique to coalition loyalty programs.

Dual Miles: Reward Miles vs. Cash Miles

AIR MILES offers two types of miles: Reward Miles (for travel redemptions, entertainment, merchandise) and Cash Miles (for in-store discounts). By default, miles earned on credit card spending go to your Reward Miles balance. You can opt to redirect some earning to Cash Miles depending on your redemption preference.

This flexibility is useful: if you're not planning a flight redemption but want to redeem toward your next Shell fill-up or Safeway grocery trip, Cash Miles let you use miles immediately at a known value.

Priority Pass Lounge Access

The BMO AIR MILES World Elite includes a Priority Pass membership. Like other cards with this benefit, you receive access to 1,400+ airport lounges worldwide. Check current terms for the number of complimentary visits included — BMO periodically updates this benefit.

Priority Pass membership at this price point (vs. no lounge access at the non-World Elite tier) is a meaningful upgrade for travelers who pass through major airports.
