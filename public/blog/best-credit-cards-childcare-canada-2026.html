Full-time licensed daycare costs $1,000–$2,500/month depending on province, child's age, and type of care (licensed daycare, home daycare, nanny). How Childcare Payments Code

Licensed daycare centres and regulated home daycares typically process credit card payments under MCC 8351 — Child Day Care Services. This MCC is not bonused by any major Canadian credit card's category structure — meaning you'll earn whatever base or flat rate your card offers, regardless of whether the card bonuses things like "recurring bills" or "utilities."

Nanny payments: If you pay a nanny directly (household employee), this typically can't be charged to a credit card at all — nannies are paid by bank transfer, cheque, or e-Transfer. You won't earn credit card rewards on nanny wages unless you use a payroll service that accepts credit card payment, which is uncommon and comes with processing fees.

YMCA / recreation centre childcare: May code as MCC 7941 (sports clubs) or 7011/8049 (other services). Still no specific bonus, but it varies.

Does Your Daycare Accept Credit Cards?

This is the critical question most families don't think to ask. A significant number of licensed daycares and home daycares in Canada do not accept credit card payment — they accept:

  • Post-dated cheques (very common in home daycares)
  • Interac e-Transfer (increasingly common)
  • EFT / pre-authorized debit
  • Credit card via Square / Stripe / Helcim (larger centres)

If your daycare doesn't accept credit cards, there's currently no mainstream Canadian workaround to put daycare fees on a credit card for rewards. Plastiq (which offered credit card bill payment as cheques) significantly reduced their Canadian operations. Paying a small processing fee (2.9%) to use a credit card when earning 2% back would result in a net loss — don't do this.

Ask before choosing a daycare: If two daycares are otherwise equal in quality and cost, choosing the one that accepts credit card payment means potentially $300–$600+/year in additional credit card rewards over the other. It's worth asking upfront during your daycare search.

CRA Childcare Deduction — Separate From and Complementary to Card Rewards

The federal Childcare Expense Deduction (Line 21400) lets you deduct up to $8,000/year for children under 7 (and $5,000 for ages 7–16) from your net income. This is a deduction, not a credit — it reduces taxable income, saving you 20–53% of the deducted amount depending on your marginal rate.

This is completely separate from your credit card rewards. You claim both: the CRA deduction on your tax return AND earn credit card cashback or points on the payments. They don't offset each other. A family in a 33% bracket deducting $8,000 saves $2,640 in taxes AND earns $360–$480 in credit card rewards — both from the same daycare spending.

Receipt requirement: The CRA requires receipts (or a T778 form from the provider showing provider name, SIN, amount paid, and child's name) to claim the childcare deduction. Your credit card statement showing the daycare payment alone is not sufficient — ask your daycare for an annual receipt letter each February.

Baby Supplies and Child Spending Beyond Daycare

Beyond daycare fees, families with young children have significant spending on:

Diapers, formula, baby food at grocery stores: Grocery bonus cards (BMO World Elite 5%, Scotia Momentum 4%) earn bonus rates on all of this if purchased at a grocery store. PC Optimum points stack at Loblaws/No Frills/Superstore. This is where category-bonus cards shine for families.

Children's clothing (MCC 5641 — Children's and Infants' Clothing Stores): Not specifically bonused by Canadian cards, but covered by flat-rate cards. Earn at base rate or use Rogers Red for 2%.

Extracurriculars, sports, lessons (MCCs vary): Swimming lessons, gymnastics, soccer registration often code as recreation or clubs (MCC 7997 or 8049). Not bonused, but recurring spend that adds up. Flat-rate card captures rewards consistently.

Pediatric dental/medical (MCC 8021/8011): Not bonused by most cards. Rogers Red (2%) or BMO Eclipse Rise for flat-rate coverage. See our dental credit card guide for specifics.
