WestJet Rewards Complete Guide for Canadians 2026

WestJet Dollars are the simplest airline currency in Canada — no points charts, no blackout dates, 1 WJD = $1 off any WestJet flight. Here's how to earn them fast and whether WestJet beats Aeroplan for you.

WestJet Rewards is refreshingly simple. Instead of points or miles on a confusing chart, you earn WestJet Dollars (WJD) — a currency where 1 WJD = exactly $1.00 off any WestJet or WestJet Vacations booking. No seat restrictions, no blackout dates, no award chart. If a seat is available for purchase, you can use WJD to pay for it.

Key distinction from Aeroplan: WestJet Dollars are fixed at 1¢ = $1 in flight value. Aeroplan points can be worth 1.5–4¢+ per point on premium cabin flights, but require understanding the award chart and finding "saver" space. WestJet is simpler; Aeroplan has higher ceiling value for strategic redeemers.

How to Earn WestJet Dollars

1. Flying WestJet

WestJet Rewards members earn WestJet Dollars on every WestJet flight based on the base fare and their tier status:

The WestJet RBC World Elite Mastercard earns 2% WJD on all purchases and 3% on WestJet purchases. Its headline benefit is an annual companion voucher for $0 (base fare + taxes) in WestJet Business — this alone is worth $400–$1,500 depending on the route and travel date. For couples who fly WestJet 1–2 times per year, the voucher frequently pays for the card fee many times over.

3. WestJet Partners

WestJet Dollars can be earned at a small number of partners: Budget and Avis rental cars, select hotels, and WestJet Vacations packages. The partner network is narrower than Aeroplan's — WestJet primarily monetizes through its credit cards and direct flying rather than a broad partner earn ecosystem.

How to Redeem WestJet Dollars

Redemption is the simplest of any Canadian airline currency: at checkout on westjet.com, select "WestJet Dollars" as your payment method and use any amount (no minimum). $1 WJD = $1 off the flight price. You can mix WJD with cash — use 50 WJD + pay the rest in cash, or use all WJD if you have enough.

Applies to: all WestJet flights (mainline + WestJet Link regional), WestJet Vacations packages, seat upgrades, checked bags. Does NOT apply to third-party charges or taxes/government fees (you must pay those in cash).

Best redemption timing: WJD are worth exactly 1¢ whenever you redeem — no premium for booking early or late, no bonus for business class vs. economy. The companion voucher (on the WestJet RBC WE MC) is where the outsized value hides — a $0 companion fare in business class can save hundreds to thousands on a single trip.

Choose WestJet if: You primarily fly WestJet domestically, have a travel companion (for the voucher), and value simplicity over maximizing premium cabin redemptions. WestJet Dollars work for everyone — you don't need to study an award chart.

Choose Aeroplan if: You want to fly business or first class internationally (Aeroplan can achieve dramatically higher per-point value on these), need access to more airlines, or are willing to invest time in understanding the award chart for maximum value.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do WestJet Dollars expire?
WestJet Dollars expire after 24 months of inactivity (no earning or redeeming). Using the WestJet RBC Mastercard for any purchase counts as activity and resets the clock. Flying WestJet or booking WestJet Vacations also maintains account activity.
Can I use WestJet Dollars on WestJet Vacations packages?
Yes — WestJet Dollars can be applied to WestJet Vacations packages (flight + hotel bundles). The fixed 1¢ value applies: $500 WJD = $500 off a vacation package. This is sometimes the highest-convenience use of WJD for families booking vacation packages.
How valuable is the $0 companion voucher on the WestJet RBC World Elite?
Extremely valuable for the right traveler. The voucher allows a companion to fly in WestJet Business (Premium cabin) for $0 base fare (you pay taxes and fees, typically $80–$250 depending on route). A WestJet Business seat from Toronto to Vancouver normally costs $600–$1,200 each way. One voucher use = card pays for itself 5–10x. Must book on the same itinerary as the primary cardholder.
Does the WestJet card earn WJD on non-WestJet travel?
Yes — the WestJet RBC World Elite earns 2% WJD on ALL purchases (not just WestJet), which is the core value for everyday spending. Book your flights on WestJet (3% earn) and use the card for groceries, gas, and dining (2% earn) to accumulate WJD year-round.
