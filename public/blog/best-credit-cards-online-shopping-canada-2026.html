Best Credit Cards for Online Shopping in Canada 2026

Amazon.ca, Walmart, eBay, Etsy, US websites — the right card protects every purchase, eliminates FX fees on USD transactions, and earns the most rewards on every click.

What Matters for Online Shopping Cards

Online shopping has three unique considerations beyond earn rate: (1) purchase protection — if an item arrives damaged or isn't as described and the merchant won't refund, your credit card's purchase protection is your last resort; (2) foreign transaction fees — buying from US-based Amazon, international Etsy sellers, or any non-CAD website triggers a 2.5% FX fee on most Canadian cards; (3) virtual card / fraud protection — online transactions carry higher fraud risk than in-person, and credit cards have better fraud liability protection than debit cards.

Key insight: Most Canadians shop on Amazon.ca (CAD transactions, no FX fee), but also on Amazon.com, eBay (often USD), Etsy (often USD), and global brands shipping from the US. If you spend $3,000/yr on USD online purchases, choosing a 0% FX card saves $75 vs. the standard 2.5% — enough to cover a basic annual fee.

Online Shopping Tips for Canadian Cardholders

Always use credit, never debit online: Credit card fraud liability is limited to $0–$50 under Canadian law with all major issuers (Visa, Mastercard, Amex zero liability). Debit card fraud recovery is slower and less guaranteed. For any online purchase, use a credit card — even a no-fee one.

Dynamic Currency Conversion online: Some US/international websites let you "pay in CAD" instead of their local currency. This is usually a currency conversion trap — the exchange rate offered is typically worse than your bank's rate plus the 2.5% FX fee. Choose to pay in the merchant's local currency (USD, EUR, etc.) and let your bank/card do the conversion (ideally with a 0% FX card).

File purchase protection claims quickly: Most purchase protection policies require you to report theft or damage within 45–90 days of the incident. For online purchases of high-value items, note your coverage period. Keep order confirmations and credit card statements as proof of purchase.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is it safe to use a credit card for online shopping in Canada?
Yes — credit cards are the safest payment method for online shopping. All major Canadian issuers (Visa, Mastercard, Amex) offer zero liability on fraudulent transactions. If someone makes an unauthorized charge on your credit card, you report it and it's reversed at no cost to you. By contrast, debit card fraud recovery can take 10+ business days and isn't always fully guaranteed. Never use your bank account number or debit card for online purchases when you can use a credit card instead.
Does Amazon.ca charge in USD or CAD?
Amazon.ca charges in Canadian dollars — no FX fee applies when shopping on the Canadian site. Amazon.com (the US site) charges in USD, which triggers the 2.5% foreign transaction fee on most cards. Many Canadian shoppers end up on Amazon.com for items unavailable on .ca — that's where a 0% FX card (Scotia Gold Amex, Home Trust Preferred Visa) becomes valuable.
Do subscription services (Netflix, Spotify, etc.) earn bonus points?
Many streaming subscriptions code as MCC 7841 (video rental) or MCC 7372 (software services). Some cards include subscriptions in their "entertainment" or "streaming" category for bonus earn: Amex Cobalt earns 3x on streaming via its expanded streaming category, and BMO eclipse rise earns 5x on entertainment/streaming. Note: National Bank WE MC earns 5x only on groceries and restaurants — streaming earns 1x on that card. Subscription services are a strong use case for category-bonus cards.
