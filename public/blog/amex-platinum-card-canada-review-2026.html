Who Should Get the Amex Platinum?

The Amex Platinum is built for a specific type of Canadian traveler: someone who flies frequently (8+ round trips/year), values airport lounge access, and stays at Marriott or Hilton properties regularly. If that's you, this card can deliver $1,500–$2,000+ in value per year — making the $799 fee a bargain. If you travel occasionally, there are far better-value cards.

Minimum profile for the Platinum to pay off: Flies 8+ times per year · Has access to lounges (Centurion, Priority Pass, or both) on most trips · Uses the $200 annual travel credit · Stays at Marriott or Hilton 3+ times/year (Gold status helps) · Values the concierge service

Annual Credits (Up to $500+/yr in Value)

✈️

$200 Annual Travel Credit

Statement credit automatically applied to travel purchases (flights, hotels, car rentals, cruises) charged to the card. Resets each calendar year. Easy to use — just put any travel on the card and the credit applies automatically. Worth $200 if you travel at all.

🏨

$100 Hotel Stay Credit

Applied to a prepaid hotel booking via Amex Travel per year. Limited to select properties, requires booking through Amex's portal. Worth $100 if you book a qualifying hotel annually.

🛂

$100 NEXUS Credit (every 4.5 years)

Reimburses one NEXUS application ($100 CAD) every 4.5 years (50 months). NEXUS provides expedited US/Canada border crossings. Amortized value: ~$22/yr. If you're applying/renewing NEXUS, this is a real benefit.

🍽️

$200 Annual Dining Credit

$200 in credits toward select Amex dining partners and experiences per calendar year. Partner list includes specific restaurant brands and delivery services. Worth $200 if you eat at participating restaurants.

Net cost after credits: $799 fee − $200 travel credit − $200 dining credit − $100 hotel credit − $22 NEXUS amortized = $277 effective annual cost — if you use all credits. Most heavy Platinum users actually come out ahead.

Airport Lounge Access — The Platinum's Crown Jewel

🏛️

Centurion Lounges — Unlimited Access

Access to all American Express Centurion Lounges globally — considered among the finest airport lounges in the world. Premium food, open bar, spa services (at select locations). Canadian cities covered: Toronto Pearson (Terminal 1, airside). US locations: New York (JFK, LGA), Los Angeles (LAX), San Francisco (SFO), Miami (MIA), Las Vegas (LAS), Seattle (SEA), Dallas (DFW), Philadelphia (PHL), Charlotte (CLT), Houston (IAH), Denver (DEN), Atlanta (ATL), Washington DC (IAD), Boston (BOS), Phoenix (PHX), Minneapolis (MSP). Unlimited visits per year — no guest fees for primary cardholder (guests: $50/each or free with qualifying spend).

🌐

Priority Pass — Unlimited Visits

Access to 1,300+ airport lounges worldwide via Priority Pass. Essential when Centurion Lounges aren't available. Unlimited visits for primary cardholder and one guest. Priority Pass normally costs $429 USD/yr for unlimited access — this card includes it free.

🍁

Plaza Premium Lounges — Unlimited

Access to all Plaza Premium airport lounges worldwide, including extensive Canadian coverage: Toronto Pearson (multiple terminals), Vancouver, Calgary, Ottawa, Halifax, Montreal, Edmonton, Winnipeg. This is the key lounge network for domestic Canadian travel — most Canadian airports have Plaza Premium lounges.

🏔️

Air Canada Maple Leaf Lounges — Complimentary

Unlimited access to Air Canada Maple Leaf Lounges when flying Air Canada (or Star Alliance partners) — no status required. A standalone Maple Leaf Lounge membership costs $550–$750/yr. This alone justifies the card for regular Air Canada flyers.

Lounge math: If you fly 8 round trips per year and use a lounge each time → 16 lounge visits. Priority Pass standalone unlimited costs $429 USD/yr (~$600 CAD). Air Canada Maple Leaf membership: $650/yr. Combined: $1,250/yr in standalone lounge costs vs. $799 Platinum fee (before credits). The lounge access alone pays for the card for frequent Air Canada flyers.

Hotel Elite Status

🌟

Marriott Bonvoy Gold Elite Status

Automatic Gold Elite without earning 25 nights. Gold benefits: 25% bonus Bonvoy points on stays, enhanced room upgrades (when available), late checkout (2pm, subject to availability), welcome gift (250 Bonvoy points). Not the highest Marriott tier (Platinum requires 50 nights), but meaningful if you stay at Marriotts regularly. Combined with Amex MR → Bonvoy transfers (1 MR = 1.2 Bonvoy), you can stack even more value.

🏩

Hilton Honors Gold Status

Automatic Hilton Honors Gold — normally requires 40 stays or 60 nights. Gold benefits: 80% bonus Hilton points on stays, complimentary breakfast (at many properties, especially internationally), 5th night free on reward stays, room upgrades. Hilton Gold's complimentary breakfast benefit alone can save $30–$60/day at international Hilton properties.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the Amex Platinum a credit card or charge card?
The Amex Platinum Canada is a charge card — your full balance must be paid in full each month. There's no preset spending limit (Amex assesses spend based on your account history and profile), but you cannot carry a balance. This means no revolving credit facility. The spend-in-full requirement is a dealbreaker for some and a feature for others (no interest charges).
Do supplementary cardholders get lounge access?
Supplementary cardholders get a Platinum Card of their own ($250/yr per additional cardholder) and receive full lounge access benefits independently. They also get their own $200 travel credit and $200 dining credit — meaning a couple with a primary and supplementary card gets $400 in travel credit and $400 in dining credit at $1,049 total annual fee. Many couples find this math compelling: $800 in guaranteed credits for $1,049 fee + unlimited lounge access for two.
Can I transfer Amex Platinum MR points to Air Canada Aeroplan?
Yes — Amex MR points transfer to Aeroplan at 1:1, plus Amex gives a 10% transfer bonus (transfer 10,000 MR → receive 11,000 Aeroplan). This is one of the best transfer ratios in Canada. Aeroplan business class redemptions at 2–3¢/Aeroplan point mean your Amex Platinum MR points can be worth 2.2–3.3¢ each through this route.
What's Fine Hotels + Resorts?
Fine Hotels + Resorts (FHR) is Amex's premium hotel booking program. Book eligible hotels through Amex Travel and receive: noon check-in (when available), daily breakfast for two, guaranteed 4pm late checkout, room upgrade (when available), $100 property credit (F&B, spa, or activities), and 2x MR points on the stay. FHR properties include Four Seasons, Ritz-Carlton, Aman, and 1,000+ luxury brands. The complimentary breakfast alone can be worth $100–$200/night at luxury properties.

✦ Rewardly Verdict

The Amex Platinum is Canada's best card for frequent high-end travelers. If you fly 8+ times/year, stay at Marriott or Hilton properties, and value lounge access — this card pays for itself several times over. The unlimited lounge network (especially combined Air Canada Maple Leaf + Plaza Premium for Canadian travelers) is unmatched. After credits, the effective cost is $277/yr for a card delivering $1,500–$2,500+ in measurable value. For everyone else, start with the Amex Cobalt ($156/yr) or Scotia Gold Amex ($120/yr) and upgrade when your travel profile matches.
