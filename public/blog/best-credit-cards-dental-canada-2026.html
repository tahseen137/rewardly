Best Credit Cards for Dental Bills in Canada 2026

The average Canadian dental cleaning and check-up runs $250–$400 with no insurance. A crown can hit $1,500. Here's how to earn something back.

Dental Bills in Canada: The Context

Canada's public health system does not cover routine dental care for most adults. Provincial programs provide limited coverage for children and seniors (the Canadian Dental Care Plan launched in 2024–2025 for eligible low-income Canadians), but for the majority of working-age adults, dental bills come out of pocket or through employer group benefits — with copays on top.

How Dental Bills Code on Credit Cards

Dental offices in Canada typically code as Health and Beauty Spas (MCC 7299), Doctors and Physicians (MCC 8011), or more commonly as Dentists and Orthodontists (MCC 8021). The good news for the Scotiabank Gold Amex cardholders: MCC 8021 falls within Scotiabank's broad definition of healthcare.

No major Canadian credit card offers a specific "dental" bonus category. However, the Scotia Gold Amex earns 1x Scene+ on healthcare (base rate — not a bonus category), while several cards earn their flat rates. The key differentiator for dental is whether the card treats medical/healthcare spending as a bonus category.

Best Credit Cards for Dental Bills

BEST OVERALL — HIGHEST FLAT RATE

Rogers Red World Elite Mastercard

Dental bills have no bonus category on any major card, which means flat-rate cashback cards win by default. Rogers Red earns 1.5–2% cashback on all purchases including dental (2% with Rogers/Fido/Shaw service; 1.5% without). On a $1,500 crown, that's $22.50–$30 back automatically. With no annual fee and wide Mastercard acceptance (all dental offices accept Mastercard), Rogers Red is the default recommendation for dental spend.

Earn on dental: 2% flat  |  Annual fee: $0  |  Income: $80K personal / $150K household

BEST FOR PHARMACY (DENTAL SUPPLIES)

CIBC Dividend Visa Infinite

The CIBC Dividend VI earns 2% cashback at drug stores and pharmacies (any chain — Shoppers Drug Mart, Rexall, Jean Coutu, London Drugs). While it doesn't help with dental office visits, it's the strongest fee card for dental-adjacent purchases: toothpaste, electric toothbrush heads, mouthwash, prescription medications post-procedure, and dental products at pharmacies. Note: Scotia Momentum earns only 1% at pharmacies (drugstores are not in its 4% tier).

Earn at pharmacies: 2%  |  Earn at dental office: 1%  |  Annual fee: $120/yr

BEST FOR AMEX USERS

American Express SimplyCash Preferred

Amex SimplyCash Preferred earns 2% cashback everywhere Amex is accepted. Most Canadian dental offices accept American Express — the 2% flat rate applies equally. On a $5,000 Invisalign treatment, that's $100 back. Annual fee: $99/yr, which is justified across all spending well before you get to dental-specific spend.

Earn on dental: 2% flat  |  Annual fee: $99/yr

Dental Bills at Pharmacies: A Key Distinction

An underutilized opportunity: many dental supplies — whitening products, prescription fluoride toothpaste, dental floss, night guard supplies, and even some post-procedure medications — are purchased at pharmacies, not dental offices. The CIBC Dividend Visa Infinite earns 2% at any drugstore — significantly better than the 1% most cards earn at dental offices. The PC Financial World Elite MC earns 4.5% PC Optimum specifically at Shoppers Drug Mart.

Similarly, the PC Financial World Elite Mastercard earns 45 PC Optimum points per $1 at Shoppers Drug Mart (equivalent to approximately 4.5% in redemption value at Shoppers) — the highest earn rate at any Canadian pharmacy. If you pick up dental supplies at Shoppers, PC World Elite is exceptional.

Does the Canadian Dental Care Plan (CDCP) Change Anything?

The federal Canadian Dental Care Plan, fully rolled out in 2025, provides coverage for eligible Canadians without employer dental benefits who meet income thresholds (under $90,000 household income). If you qualify for CDCP, your out-of-pocket dental costs are significantly reduced — which also reduces the amount you can put on a credit card.

CDCP note: If your dental work is fully covered by CDCP, you won't have an out-of-pocket balance to pay — meaning no credit card rewards opportunity on those visits. If you have partial coverage (copay), put the copay on Rogers Red or your flat-rate card. Verify your CDCP eligibility at canada.ca/dental.

Summary: Dental bills earn at the base flat rate on all cards (1–2%). Rogers Red (1.5–2%) wins for dental office visits. Scotia Momentum (4%) or PC World Elite (4.5%) wins for dental supplies at pharmacies. For large procedures, the timing strategy (welcome bonus) can generate 10–20x more value than ongoing earn rates alone.
