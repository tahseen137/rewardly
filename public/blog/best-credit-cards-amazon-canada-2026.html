Best Credit Cards for Amazon in Canada 2026

Amazon.ca and Amazon.com don't earn at grocery rates — they're general merchandise. The best card for Amazon depends on whether you're shopping .ca (CAD) or .com (USD). Here's what earns the most.

The Amazon MCC Problem: Why You Can't Stack Grocery Rates

Amazon Canada operates under Merchant Category Code (MCC) 5999 — "Miscellaneous General Merchandise Retailers." This is not a grocery, gas, or specialty category. So even if you're buying food on Amazon.ca, your 5x grocery card (Scotiabank Gold Amex) earns its base 1 point per dollar, not the elevated grocery rate.

Common misconception: Many Canadians assume Amazon.ca earns at their grocery rate because Amazon sells food. It doesn't. Amazon Fresh Canada is categorized differently than physical grocery stores — always check the actual MCC before assuming a bonus rate applies.

For Amazon purchases, you need cards that earn well on general merchandise or on all purchases everywhere — not category-specific cards. This changes which cards are optimal.

There's also the .ca vs. .com distinction: Amazon.ca charges in Canadian dollars (no FX fee), while Amazon.com charges in USD — adding a 2.5% foreign currency surcharge on most cards, which can erase your rewards.

Amazon.ca (CAD) — Best Cards

MBNA Rewards World Elite Mastercard

The MBNA Rewards WE MC earns 5 points per dollar on all purchases with no category restrictions — including Amazon.ca general merchandise. At ~1¢/point for travel redemptions, that's approximately 5% effective return on every Amazon purchase. $120/year annual fee.

Catch: 5 pts/$ only on the first $50,000/year in spending, then drops to 1 pt/$. For most Canadians, the cap is rarely hit. This is the highest flat earn rate on a Canadian credit card for general merchandise including Amazon.

Amazon.ca general merchandise earns only 1x MR on the Cobalt — its 5x rate applies to grocery stores, not Amazon. However, Amazon Prime subscription charges may earn at the streaming/grocery category on the Cobalt (verify with Amex — MCC classification can vary). For the subscription itself, the Cobalt is excellent. For product purchases, use the MBNA WE instead.

Where Cobalt wins on Amazon-adjacent spending: Amazon Fresh, digital content, streaming. Best combined approach: Cobalt for subscriptions, MBNA WE for product purchases.

Not a reason to choose this card for Amazon, but not a reason to use a different card if you already hold it.

Amazon.com (USD) — Best Cards

USD purchases incur a 2.5% foreign transaction fee on most Canadian cards. A card earning 2% on all purchases LOSES money (net -0.5%) on Amazon.com orders. You need a card that either (a) has 0% FX fee or (b) earns more than 2.5% in rewards to come out ahead.

#1

Rogers Red World Elite Mastercard

$0 Annual Fee · Cash Back · Best for .com
Best .com Card
3% on foreign currency

Rogers Red earns 3% cash back on all foreign currency purchases (including Amazon.com USD charges). The card also has a 2.5% FX fee — but 3% back minus 2.5% fee = net +0.5% real cash back on Amazon.com purchases. It's not a massive earn, but it's positive — unlike most Canadian cards which lose money on USD purchases after the FX fee.

Better yet: use the Rogers Red for Amazon.com to avoid the net loss, and use MBNA Rewards WE for Amazon.ca (5x, no FX implications).

Limited at some retailers: Amazon accepts Amex in Canada, but if you encounter any checkout issues, have a backup Visa/Mastercard ready.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does Amazon.ca charge in Canadian dollars? Yes — Amazon.ca charges are billed in Canadian dollars. No foreign transaction fees apply. The goods may be priced in USD on cross-border listings, but the final charge hits your card in CAD.
Is there a co-branded Amazon credit card in Canada? Unlike the US (which has an Amazon Prime Rewards Visa offering 5% back on Amazon.com), there is no Amazon-branded credit card in Canada as of 2026. Canadian Amazon shoppers must use general rewards cards.
Does Amazon Fresh Canada earn at grocery rates? Amazon Fresh Canada purchases have been inconsistently categorized — sometimes as grocery, sometimes as general merchandise, depending on the card issuer's MCC mapping. Check with your specific issuer. Don't assume grocery bonus rates apply until you verify with a test transaction.
Should I use Amazon Pay or Amazon Wallet? Loading money to an Amazon Wallet/gift card balance may earn rewards on the card used to load. But this is an advanced strategy — verify with your card issuer that gift card purchases earn rewards (some issuers exclude prepaid/gift card purchases from earning points).
