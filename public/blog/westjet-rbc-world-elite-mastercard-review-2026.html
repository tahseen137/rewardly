Who Is This Card For?

The WestJet RBC World Elite Mastercard has one ideal customer: someone who flies WestJet at least once or twice a year. If that's you, the card's two flagship perks — an annual companion voucher and free checked bags — cover the $119 fee easily, often within a single trip.

If you fly WestJet a few times a year, this card is almost certainly worth it. If you fly other airlines more often, or rarely check bags, the math is less compelling.

Welcome Bonus

Typical welcome offers include 600–1,100 WestJet Dollars with a minimum spend requirement (often $3,000–$5,000 in the first 3 months). WestJet Dollars work at a 1:1 rate — 600 WestJet Dollars = $600 off WestJet flights, hotels, or vacation packages.

Welcome bonuses vary by promotion. Check RBC's current offer before applying — they periodically run elevated bonuses around summer and holiday travel seasons.

WestJet Dollars (WJD) are simple: 1 WJD = $1 toward any WestJet purchase. No partner airlines, no transfer programs, no yield-maximizing required. You know exactly what you're getting.

The tradeoff: WestJet Dollars can only be used with WestJet. If you occasionally want to redeem on Air Canada or other carriers, this card gives you nothing. For multi-airline travelers, Aeroplan or Amex MR points are more flexible.

The Annual Companion Voucher — The Card's Real Value

Every year on your card anniversary, you receive a round-trip companion voucher for $0 base fare (taxes, fees, and surcharges still apply). A second traveler flies for free when you book a return WestJet flight at base fare.

Taxes and fees on a domestic round trip typically run $50–$120. For a Toronto–Vancouver round trip, that means your companion flies for approximately $80–$130 in fees — when the seat itself might cost $400–$800. This is substantial value.

The companion voucher alone often exceeds the $119 annual fee on its first use. If you fly with a partner, family member, or colleague, this is among the best single-card benefits in Canada.

Important: The voucher is for WestJet flights only, cannot be used on WestJet Link (Bearskin/Pacific Coastal codeshares), and must be redeemed within 12 months of issuance.

Free Checked Bags — Underrated for Families

The cardholder and up to 8 companions on the same booking get their first checked bag free on WestJet flights. WestJet's standard checked bag fee is $32.39 CAD each way.

Here's the math for a family of four on a round trip:

  • 4 passengers × 2 checked bags (round trip) × $32.39 = $259.12 saved

That's more than double the annual fee on a single family vacation. For couples, it's $129.56 per trip. Even solo travelers save $64.78 per round trip.

The 15-day emergency medical coverage is adequate for most leisure trips. Snowbirds going to the U.S. for 30+ days will need a top-up policy. Notably, this card has better medical limits ($5M) than most co-brand airline cards.

No Foreign Transaction Fees?

Unfortunately, the WestJet RBC World Elite Mastercard does charge a 2.5% foreign transaction fee. This is a meaningful weakness for international travel. Use a dedicated no-FX-fee card (like the Scotiabank Passport Visa Infinite or HSBC World Elite) when spending in foreign currencies.

Is It Worth the $119 Annual Fee?

For WestJet regulars: unambiguously yes. The annual companion voucher alone can save $200–$700 on a single booking depending on your route and timing. Add the free checked bags, and a family of four saves hundreds per trip beyond that.

The breakeven math is easy: if you use the companion voucher once and check one bag round-trip on one trip, you've likely already saved more than $119 in a year.

Rewardly Verdict — 4.3 / 5

The WestJet RBC World Elite Mastercard is the best WestJet-specific card available in Canada — because it's the only one. But it earns its stars on merit: the companion voucher and free bags deliver clear, easy-to-quantify value for anyone who flies WestJet at least once a year. The lack of FX fee waiver is a genuine annoyance. Pair it with a no-foreign-fee card for international trips.

Best for: WestJet loyalists, Canadian families flying domestically, beach vacation travelers on WestJet routes (Cancún, Jamaica, Dominican).

Skip if: You primarily fly Air Canada, travel internationally more than domestically, or fly less than once a year.
