Best Credit Cards for Pharmacy & Medical Spending Canada 2026

Canadians spend $2,000–$5,000 per year on out-of-pocket prescriptions, OTC medicine, dental, and health products. The right card earns 3–30x on this spending.

Why Pharmacy Spend Is a High-Value Category

Most credit cards don't offer bonus rates on pharmacy — it falls under "other" at 1x or 0.5x. But a few Canadian cards treat pharmacy/drug stores as a separate earn category and reward it at 3–5x. For families spending $200+/month on prescriptions and health products, this difference adds up to $70–$150/year in extra rewards.

Shoppers Drug Mart is especially lucrative because it has its own loyalty program (PC Optimum) that stacks on top of credit card rewards — and runs regular 20x bonus events. Stacking a high-earn credit card with PC Optimum bonus events is where the real value is unlocked.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does the Amex Cobalt earn bonus points at pharmacies?
No — the Cobalt's 5x category is food and dining (restaurants, grocery stores, food delivery), not pharmacies. Pharmacy purchases at Shoppers, Rexall, or independent pharmacies typically earn 1x MR points on the Cobalt. The Scotia Gold (5x) or PC Financial WE MC (4.5%) are better dedicated pharmacy cards.
Can I earn credit card rewards on prescription purchases?
Yes — most credit cards earn rewards on prescriptions the same as any other pharmacy purchase. The prescription drug is a charge to the pharmacy merchant, so your card earns points on it. The exception is some drug benefit cards that pay the pharmacy directly — in those cases, the card transaction goes through the drug plan, not your credit card. For out-of-pocket prescriptions, your credit card rewards apply in full.
Does dental spending earn bonus points?
Dental offices typically code as "health care services" or "medical and dental" merchants — not as drug stores. Most credit cards earn 1x on dental. There's no Canadian credit card that specifically bonuses dental spending as a category. Use your best flat-rate cashback card (Rogers Red at 2–3%) or Amex Cobalt (1x MR) for dental visits.
