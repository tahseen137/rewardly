Plum Rewards — What You Earn and How to Stack

Plum Rewards at a Glance

Indigo's loyalty program gives members 1 Plum point per $1 spent at Indigo/Chapters/Coles (worth 1¢ per point = 1% effective return). Plum Members Plus (typically achieved at $200+ annual spend) earns 2 points per $1 (2%).

Plum points can be redeemed only at Indigo stores or indigo.ca — they're non-transferable and store-specific. Occasional Plum Bonus Days offer 5x–10x multipliers on select categories.

The critical point: Plum Rewards and your credit card rewards are completely independent. You earn both simultaneously on every purchase. This means the right strategy is to always have your Plum card scanned AND pay with the best credit card for that type of purchase — no trade-off required.

Combined Plum (2%) + credit card (2–5%) = effective 4–7% total return on Indigo purchases for optimized shoppers.

How Indigo Purchases Code on Credit Cards

At physical Indigo and Chapters stores, purchases typically code under:

  • MCC 5942 (Book Stores) — most common for Chapters/Indigo standalone stores
  • MCC 5999 (Miscellaneous Retail) — sometimes used at Indigo lifestyle stores with heavy home goods
  • MCC 5200 or 5999 — Indigo's mixed-format stores (book + home + gift) may code as general merchandise

At indigo.ca (online), purchases typically code as general online retail — which is captured by cards with online shopping bonus categories. This is where some of the best earning opportunities arise.

MCC 5942 (Book Stores) — does any card bonus it? No major Canadian credit card currently offers a specific book store or MCC 5942 bonus category. The category is captured at base rate on most cards. The strategic play is therefore (a) cards with general online shopping bonuses for indigo.ca, or (b) the highest flat-rate card available.

Online (indigo.ca) vs. In-Store — Which Earns More?

The earn rate on credit cards should be identical whether you buy in-store or online at indigo.ca, since most cards don't differentiate by channel. However, a few considerations favour one over the other:

In-store advantages: Plum Bonus Days and in-store-only promotions (often exclusive to physical locations). You can also browse the full selection and pay with any card.

indigo.ca advantages: Regular online-only promo codes, occasional free shipping thresholds, and the ability to filter to Plum Bonus eligible items. Some cards with online shopping portal bonuses (like BMO's online shopping portal or American Express Offers) may add extra earn on online orders when you activate an Offer.

Amex Offers for Indigo: American Express Canada occasionally runs targeted Amex Offers for Indigo — spend $X at Indigo, get $Y back as a statement credit. These appear in the Amex Canada app under "Amex Offers" and are cardholder-specific. If you hold any Amex card, check regularly — these can add 5–10% in credit on Indigo purchases when available.

Plum Bonus Day Strategy

Indigo runs Plum Bonus Days (often 5x or 10x Plum points on select days or categories) via email and the Plum app. Stack a Plum Bonus Day with your best credit card for maximum total return. A 10x Plum day + 2% Rogers Red = 12% effective return on that purchase — worth timing big book hauls or holiday gift shopping around.

Birthday bonus: Plum Rewards members receive extra Plum points during their birthday month. Stack your birthday bonus with a high-credit-card earn day for maximum value on book purchases.

Gift Card Angle

Some grocery stores sell Indigo gift cards. If your grocery card earns 4% (Scotia Momentum) or has a high grocery earn rate, buying an Indigo gift card at the grocery store effectively earns your grocery rate on a book purchase. Availability varies by store and is not guaranteed.

Gift card caveat: Plum Rewards are typically NOT earned on gift card purchases (buying the gift card) — only on purchases made using the gift card (or regular payment) at Indigo. So the strategy is: earn 4% grocery rewards on the gift card purchase, then separately earn Plum points when you spend the gift card in-store.

Bottom Line

There's no Canadian credit card with a dedicated book store bonus category — so Indigo shopping is captured at base or general retail earn rates. That means the winners are flat-rate or general-retail cards:

For Rogers/Fido subscribers: Rogers Red Mastercard (2%) + Plum Members Plus (2%) = 4% total is the cleanest, simplest setup. No annual fee, no complexity.

For non-Rogers customers: BMO Eclipse Rise (5x ≈ ~1.7% travel) + Plum Members Plus (2%) = ~5.3% total is the highest combined earn rate, with the BMO Eclipse Rise standing out as a rare no-fee card with a strong everyday spending multiplier.

For Amex cardholders already using the Cobalt: keep using it (1x MR ≈ 2%) and watch for Amex Offers targeting Indigo — these periodic promotions can temporarily boost your effective earn rate significantly.
