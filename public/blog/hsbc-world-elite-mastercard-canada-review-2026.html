Card Overview

The HSBC World Elite Mastercard is HSBC Canada's flagship personal credit card. It's one of a small number of Canadian credit cards that combines three key features that globetrotters need: a meaningful earn rate on travel spending, complimentary airport lounge access, and zero foreign transaction fees.

HSBC Rewards Points are earned on all purchases and are most valuable when redeemed for travel through HSBC's Travel with Points portal (approximately 0.5¢/point for simple redemptions, up to 1.0¢/point for select travel bookings). The card also earns a flat $100 travel enhancement credit each year, which offsets the $149 annual fee significantly.

Effective annual fee after credits: $149 annual fee − $100 travel credit = $49 net cost per year, before any points earned on spending.

The 0% FX Fee Advantage

Most Canadian credit cards charge a 2.5% foreign transaction fee on purchases made in foreign currencies. On $10,000/year in US or international purchases, that's $250 saved annually with HSBC vs. a typical card.

Combined with the $100 travel credit, the HSBC WE MC's effective annual cost can be negative for heavy international travelers. Compare this to the Amex Cobalt ($191.88 fee, no lounge, no FX savings) or TD Aeroplan VI ($139 fee, no FX savings, 4 DragonPass visits).

Verdict — Best Premium Mastercard for Globe-Trotters

Six lounge visits + $100 travel credit + 0% FX fee at a $149 fee makes the HSBC WE MC one of the strongest international travel cards in Canada. Frequently Asked Questions

Do HSBC Rewards points expire?
HSBC Rewards points do not expire as long as the credit card account remains open and in good standing. If you close your HSBC World Elite Mastercard, any unredeemed points are forfeited. Redeem your points before closing the account, or transfer them to an eligible partner program if available.
Can I transfer HSBC Rewards points to airline miles?
HSBC Rewards points have limited transfer partners in Canada compared to Amex MR or Chase points. Transfer options and ratios change periodically — check the HSBC Rewards portal for the current list of airline and hotel transfer partners. The most common approach for Canadian HSBC cardholders is to redeem directly through HSBC's Travel with Points for flights and hotels, where the value is predictable at approximately 0.5¢–1.0¢ per point.
Is the HSBC World Elite Mastercard still available if I'm not an HSBC banking customer?
Yes — you do not need to hold an HSBC chequing or savings account to apply for the HSBC World Elite Mastercard. It's available to eligible Canadians with $80,000+ individual income (or $150,000+ household). Note: as of late 2023, HSBC Canada began a sale to RBC. Check the current product status at hsbc.ca/creditcards before applying, as the card may be rebranded or restructured under the RBC acquisition timeline.
