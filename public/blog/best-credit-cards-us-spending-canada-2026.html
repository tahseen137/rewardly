Best Canadian Credit Cards for US Spending & Travel 2026

Most Canadian credit cards charge a 2.5% foreign transaction fee on every USD purchase. On $10,000 in US spending, that's $250 straight to the bank. These cards eliminate the fee entirely.

The 2.5% FX Problem

When you use a typical Canadian credit card in the US (or for any USD purchase online), your bank applies a foreign transaction fee — typically 2.5% — on top of the exchange rate conversion. On a card earning 2% rewards, you're effectively earning -0.5% on every US purchase. The 2.5% fee eats every reward and then some.

What About Withdrawing USD Cash in the US?

Credit cards are for purchases — for USD cash, a different approach is better. Options for Canadians needing US cash:

Best approaches for USD cash:
1. Wise (formerly TransferWise) debit card — mid-market exchange rate, no FX fee, free ATM withdrawals up to a monthly limit. Best option for US ATMs.
2. Scotiabank USD Daily Interest account — hold USD in a Canadian bank account, withdraw at US Scotiabank partner ATMs.
3. Exchange before you leave — order USD from your bank before departure. Less convenient but predictable. Avoid airport exchange booths (worst rates).
4. Never use credit cards for ATM cash advances — cash advance fee (3–5%) + interest from day one + FX fee = extremely expensive.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does the FX fee apply to online USD purchases (Amazon.com, etc.)?
Yes — foreign transaction fees apply to ANY purchase in a foreign currency, whether online or in-person. If you buy from Amazon.com and the charge comes in USD, your Canadian card applies the 2.5% FX fee on that amount. Using a 0% FX card like Scotia Gold Amex or Home Trust Preferred eliminates this fee on Amazon.com, US subscription services, and all other USD-billed purchases.
Is American Express widely accepted in the US?
Yes — Amex acceptance in the US is significantly better than in Canada. Virtually all major retailers, restaurants, hotels, and online stores in the US accept Amex. The 5–10% of Canadian merchants who don't take Amex is not a meaningful concern in the US. The Scotia Gold Amex is an excellent primary card for US travel.
Should I pay in CAD or USD when offered the choice in the US?
Always choose USD (the local currency). When a US terminal offers to charge you in CAD, it's called Dynamic Currency Conversion (DCC) — the merchant's rate is typically 3–7% worse than your card's conversion rate. Always select the local currency; let your card's bank do the conversion at its published exchange rate. With a 0% FX card, there's no downside to paying in USD.
