The Scotiabank Momentum Visa Infinite is Canada's favourite grocery-and-gas cashback card — and with good reason. It earns 4% on four of the most common spending categories for Canadian households: groceries, gas, daily transit, and streaming. Monthly cashback payouts beat most competitors that pay annually, and the $120 annual fee is waived entirely in year one.

Earn rates in detail

4% — Groceries

Applies to purchases at grocery stores including Loblaws, Sobeys, Metro, Safeway, IGA, Longos, Farm Boy, and most independents. Walmart Supercentre does code as grocery for most Scotiabank purchases. Costco does not — Costco's warehouse stores are classified as warehouse clubs.

4% — Gas

Works at major chains (Petro-Canada, Shell, Esso, Husky, Pioneer, Ultramar) and most independent stations. Pay-at-pump (MCC 5542) and full-service (MCC 5541) both typically earn 4%. EV charging stations are included in this category.

4% — Daily transit

This is unusual and valuable. Covers TTC (Toronto), STM (Montreal), OC Transpo (Ottawa), TransLink (Vancouver), Calgary Transit, and similar municipal systems. Includes taxis and rideshares (Uber, Lyft). One of the few premium cashback cards to include this category.

4% — Streaming

Netflix, Disney+, Amazon Prime Video, Crave, Spotify, Apple Music, and most digital subscription services. If a service codes as "software" rather than "entertainment," it may not qualify — rare but possible.

2% — Drug stores

Shoppers Drug Mart, Rexall, Jean Coutu, Familiprix, and similar pharmacy chains. This includes non-pharmacy purchases at those locations.

2% — Recurring bills

Utility bills, internet, wireless, insurance, gym memberships billed monthly. Must be set up as pre-authorized debits or recurring charges to the card to qualify.

1% — Everything else

All other purchases earn 1%, which is standard for a card at this tier.

No earning caps: Unlike some competitors, the Scotiabank Momentum Visa Infinite has no monthly or annual cap on cashback earned at any tier. Spend $5,000/month on groceries and you earn $200 — no cutoff.

The verdict: BMO earns 1% more on groceries up to $500/month — but hits a $60K income barrier vs Scotia's $60K. If you spend heavily on transit and streaming, Scotiabank wins those categories. If you prioritize grocery earning above all else and hit $80K income, BMO takes the edge.

  • You fill up your car regularly at a gas station

FAQ

Is the first year really free?

Yes, as of 2026 the annual fee of $120 is waived for the first year — and for any supplementary cardholders added in the first year as well. After year one, the $120 fee applies automatically. You can call to cancel or request a retention offer before it charges.

Does Walmart count as grocery?

Walmart Supercentre and Walmart stores generally code as grocery (MCC 5411) for Scotiabank, so the 4% rate usually applies. Some people report inconsistency — if your Walmart purchase earned only 1%, contact Scotiabank customer service to clarify your specific location.

When is cashback paid out?

Scotiabank Momentum pays cashback monthly as a statement credit, typically around the 11th of the following month. If you earn $80 in January, expect to see it on your February statement.

What's the minimum income to apply?

$60,000 personal annual income, or $100,000 household income. Scotiabank does a credit check on application. Having existing Scotiabank accounts doesn't guarantee approval.

Can I combine cashback with Scene+ points?

No — the Momentum card earns cashback, not Scene+ points. If you want to earn Scene+ points, get the Scotiabank Scene+ Visa Infinite instead (3x on groceries, but less on gas). They're separate cards with separate earn structures.

Does the card have travel insurance?

Yes: emergency medical (15 days), trip delay/interruption insurance, rental car collision/loss damage waiver, and lost baggage insurance. Not as comprehensive as dedicated travel cards, but solid for a cashback card.
