Best Credit Cards for Black Friday Canada 2026

Canadians spend $4.5B+ during BFCM. The right card earns 2–5% back on every deal — plus purchase protection on electronics bought on sale.

Black Friday Card Strategy at a Glance

Black Friday (last Friday of November) and Cyber Monday have become Canada's biggest annual shopping events — rivalling Boxing Day. The right credit card strategy for BFCM depends on what you're buying: electronics and appliances code as "electronics retail" (no bonus on most cards), while online purchases from Amazon or direct brand sites often code normally. The two biggest wins: using a flat-rate card for electronics and a high-earn card for dining during the shopping day, AND ensuring your big purchases have purchase protection and extended warranty coverage.

🖥️ Electronics (BestBuy, Apple)

Rogers Red (1.5–2% flat)

Electronics stores don't earn bonus rates on most cards. Rogers Red 1.5–2% is the top choice. Cobalt earns only 1x here.

👗 Fashion (Nordstrom, H&M)

Rogers Red (1.5–2% flat)

Clothing stores also earn base rate. Rogers Red 1.5–2% flat wins again. No category card beats it at clothing retailers.

🍔 BF Day Dining (Food court, delivery)

Amex Cobalt (5x dining)

Lunch, dinner, and food delivery on shopping day earns 5x on Cobalt = 5x MR points. Switch to Cobalt for food.

📦 Amazon / Online

Amazon Rewards Card or Rogers Red

Check if you have an Amazon-specific card; otherwise Rogers Red 1.5–2% applies to all Amazon purchases.

Top 3 Cards for Black Friday 2026

#1 Rogers Red World Elite Mastercard — Best for Most BFCM Purchases

2% cashback everywhere · 3% on foreign currency (US-priced deals)

Annual fee: $0 (or $29). Rogers Red is the default BFCM card because most sale items — electronics, furniture, appliances, clothing — earn only base rate on category cards. At 1.5–2% flat (2% with Rogers/Fido/Shaw service), it outperforms every other card on these merchants. Additionally, if you're buying from US retailers (Amazon.com, US brand sites) on Black Friday, the 3% cashback on foreign currency transactions effectively offsets the 2.5% FX fee, making you a net winner vs. paying no FX fee on a base-rate card.

#2 TD Aeroplan Visa Infinite / CIBC Aeroplan VI — Best for Purchase Protection

1x Aeroplan · 90-day purchase protection · 1-year extended warranty

Annual fee: $139. Not the top earn card for electronics, but TD and CIBC Aeroplan Visa Infinite cards both offer 90-day purchase protection (covers damage and theft on items bought with the card) and 1 additional year of manufacturer warranty. For a $1,500 laptop or $800 television, this insurance alone can be worth more than the difference in earn rate. Always use a card with purchase protection for electronics $300+.

#3 Amex Cobalt — Best for Black Friday Dining and Streaming Deals

5x MR on dining & food · 3x streaming services

Annual fee: $156. When you're out shopping all day and stop for food — Cobalt is the switch. BFCM also brings discounts on streaming subscriptions (Apple TV+, Disney+, Spotify Premium). Cobalt earns 3x on streaming. If you're upgrading a streaming plan on sale, do it on Cobalt. Not the right card for electronics or clothing purchases (only 1x).

Purchase Protection — The Underrated BFCM Benefit

Why Purchase Protection Matters on Black Friday

What it covers: Most premium Canadian cards (TD, CIBC, Scotiabank VI, Cobalt) provide purchase protection that covers items against theft, loss, or damage for 90 days after purchase. If your new laptop is stolen or your TV is damaged in transit, file a claim with your card's insurance.

Extended warranty: Many premium cards also extend the manufacturer's warranty by 1 additional year. A 1-year warranty on a $1,500 laptop becomes 2 years at no cost.

Cards with the strongest coverage: TD Aeroplan VI, CIBC Aeroplan VI, Scotiabank Passport VI, Amex Cobalt, Scotia Gold Amex — all offer both 90-day protection AND extended warranty. Rogers Red and basic no-fee cards typically do NOT include purchase protection.

Key strategy: Split your Black Friday spending. Use Rogers Red for smaller clothing and accessory purchases ($50–$200 range). Use TD Aeroplan VI or Cobalt for electronics and appliances ($300+) where the purchase protection insurance adds real value, even at a lower earn rate.
