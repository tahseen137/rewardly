Best Aeroplan Credit Cards in Canada 2026

TD vs. CIBC vs. Amex MR transfers — which card earns the most Aeroplan points on your everyday spending? The definitive 2026 comparison.

How to Earn Aeroplan Points via Credit Cards

There are three ways to earn Aeroplan points with Canadian credit cards: (1) TD co-branded cards — TD issues the primary line of Aeroplan Visa cards (Infinite, Infinite Privilege), earning Aeroplan directly; (2) CIBC co-branded cards — CIBC also issues Aeroplan Visa cards as a co-issuer with TD; (3) Amex Membership Rewards transfers — MR points from Amex Cobalt, Gold, and Platinum transfer to Aeroplan at 1:1 with a 10% bonus, often delivering better earn rates on dining and everyday spending than the TD/CIBC co-branded options.

The Amex Cobalt edge: The Cobalt earns 5x MR on dining/food → transfers to 5.5x Aeroplan (1:1 + 10% bonus). No co-branded Aeroplan card comes close on dining. For maximum Aeroplan accumulation, the Cobalt + a TD or CIBC Aeroplan Visa (for Aeroplan Status miles and direct earning) is the optimal 2-card strategy.

Best Aeroplan Credit Cards — 2026 Rankings

#1

TD Aeroplan Visa Infinite Card

$139/yr (waivable) · Direct Aeroplan earn · Best for most Canadians
Best Direct Aeroplan Card
1.5 Aeroplan pts on grocery, gas, Air Canada | 1 pt everywhere else

The TD Aeroplan Visa Infinite is the go-to entry-level Aeroplan card. Earns 1.5 Aeroplan points/$1 on grocery, gas, and Air Canada purchases; 1 point everywhere else. Welcome bonus: 40,000 Aeroplan + $139 annual fee rebate in year 1 ($700+ value). $139/yr fee, waivable with TD All-Inclusive banking plan.

Key advantage over CIBC's equivalent: TD Aeroplan includes an Aeroplan Preferred Pricing benefit for award flight bookings through Air Canada — meaning TD card holders pay slightly fewer Aeroplan points for Air Canada awards than non-co-branded earners. Also includes Air Canada companion voucher (value varies), and $100 NEXUS rebate every 4 years.

Best for: Most Canadians accumulating Aeroplan. The fee waiver via TD banking, strong welcome bonus, and Preferred Pricing on award bookings make this the best direct-earn Aeroplan card for everyday users.
#2

American Express Cobalt Card

$156/yr · 5x MR → 5.5x Aeroplan via transfer · Best Earn Rate
Best Aeroplan Earn Rate
5x MR on dining/delivery → 5.5x Aeroplan (after 10% transfer bonus)

The Amex Cobalt is the highest-earning "Aeroplan card" in Canada despite not being co-branded — via the MR → Aeroplan transfer. On dining and food delivery (the Cobalt's 5x category): 5,000 MR → 5,500 Aeroplan (1:1 + 10% transfer bonus). On a $3,000/yr dining budget, that's 16,500 Aeroplan points per year from dining alone.

Limitation: MR points must be transferred to Aeroplan (usually within 48–72 hours), and Aeroplan points cannot be converted back to MR. Plan redemptions before transferring. Also: 1x MR on most non-dining spend (vs. 1.5x direct Aeroplan on the TD card's grocery/gas categories).

Best for: Heavy dining spenders who want to maximize Aeroplan points on restaurant and food delivery spending. 5.5x Aeroplan on dining is unmatched. Best paired with a TD or CIBC Aeroplan card for direct grocery/gas earn + Status qualification.
#3

CIBC Aeroplan Visa Infinite

$139/yr · Same direct earn as TD · CIBC banking preference
Best for CIBC Bank Clients
1.5 Aeroplan pts on grocery, gas, Air Canada | 1.5 pts on EV charging | 1 pt everywhere

The CIBC Aeroplan Visa Infinite nearly mirrors the TD version — 1.5 Aeroplan points on grocery, gas, and Air Canada purchases, 1 point everywhere else, same $139 annual fee. Key differentiator: 1.5x on EV charging stations (vs. TD's 1x) — increasingly important as electric vehicles grow in Canada. Also includes Aeroplan Preferred Pricing for Air Canada award redemptions.

Choose CIBC over TD if: you already bank with CIBC and qualify for fee waiver through CIBC Smart Plus or CIBC banking bundles. Both cards are equivalent for most Aeroplan earners.

Best for: CIBC bank clients who can waive the annual fee. Functionally equivalent to TD Aeroplan VI — choose based on your primary banking relationship. CIBC earns an edge on EV charging if you drive an electric vehicle.
#4

TD Aeroplan Visa Infinite Privilege

Best for Frequent Air Canada Flyers
2 pts on Air Canada | 1.5 pts on grocery/dining/gas/transit | 1.25 pts everywhere

The premium TD Aeroplan Visa Infinite Privilege doubles down on Air Canada spending: 2x Aeroplan on Air Canada purchases; 1.5x on grocery, dining, gas, and transit; 1.25x everywhere. $599 annual fee. Best for: Frequent Air Canada travelers who want lounge access and contribute card spend toward Aeroplan Elite Status. The $599 fee requires heavy Air Canada spending and lounge use to justify vs. the Infinite at $139/yr.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is it better to have a TD or CIBC Aeroplan card?
Functionally identical for most cardholders — same earn rates, same fee, same Aeroplan Preferred Pricing benefit. Choose based on your existing banking relationship: TD banking → TD Aeroplan VI (fee waivable with TD All-Inclusive). CIBC banking → CIBC Aeroplan VI (fee waivable with qualifying CIBC banking plan). If you bank with neither, TD has a slight edge due to broader banking product availability and the TD All-Inclusive fee waiver path.
Can I hold both a TD and CIBC Aeroplan card simultaneously?
Yes — there's no restriction preventing you from holding both a TD and CIBC Aeroplan credit card. Both deposit earned Aeroplan points directly into the same Aeroplan account (linked by your Aeroplan membership number). Some Aeroplan maximizers hold both — TD for primary grocery/gas earn and welcome bonus, CIBC as a supplementary card after meeting the TD cooling period. However, the incremental benefit of both co-branded cards vs. adding the Amex Cobalt is usually lower.
Do Amex MR points transfer to Aeroplan immediately?
Amex MR to Aeroplan transfers typically complete within 1–5 business days, not instantly. Do not transfer points while trying to book a specific award — the flights may sell out. Transfer points to Aeroplan proactively (accumulate enough for your target redemption, then transfer), and search award availability first using the Air Canada Aeroplan award search tool before initiating the transfer.
