Best Credit Cards for Transit & Commuting in Canada 2026

Your daily TTC, Presto, Uber, or metro commute can earn 3–5x rewards per dollar. Here are the best Canadian cards for transit and rideshare spending.

How Transit Purchases Code on Credit Cards

Transit spending uses multiple MCCs depending on how you pay: MCC 4111 (Local/Suburban Transit) covers most public transit (TTC, Presto, STM, OC Transpo, GO Transit, Canada Line); MCC 4121 (Taxicabs & Limousines) covers traditional taxis; MCC 4789 (Transportation Services) is a catch-all; Uber and Lyft typically code as MCC 4121 or 5999 depending on the transaction type. Many cards that bonus "transit" cover MCC 4111 + 4121 + ride-share apps.

Average Canadian commuter spending: Monthly transit pass in Toronto: $156 (TTC), Montreal: $94 (STM), Vancouver: $104–$187 (TransLink), Calgary: $109. At $1,400–$1,900/yr in transit spending, earning 5x instead of 1x saves ~$56–$76/yr in extra rewards — on a single category you're spending anyway.

Does Uber/Lyft Earn Transit Rewards?

Uber/Lyft MCC reality: Uber in Canada typically codes as MCC 4121 (Taxicabs) for ride-hailing and MCC 5812 (Restaurants) for Uber Eats. Lyft codes as MCC 4121. Most cards that bonus transit (MCC 4111) also include MCC 4121 — so Uber rides usually earn transit bonus rates. Uber Eats orders may earn the dining rate instead. Check your specific card's terms; MCC classification can vary by transaction type.

Presto card users: Loading money onto your Presto card via credit card typically codes as MCC 4111 (transit) — earning transit bonus rates. Pay for Presto top-ups with your transit-bonus card to maximize rewards on every tap. Same applies for OC Transpo e-tickets, STM Opus card online, and TransLink Compass card top-ups.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do monthly transit passes earn credit card rewards in Canada?
Yes — purchasing a monthly TTC Metropass, Presto pass, STM monthly pass, or any recurring transit pass with a credit card earns rewards at the applicable transit rate. The key is to pay directly with your credit card rather than pre-loading transit funds from a debit card. Use a card with transit bonus (Scotia Gold Amex at 5x, Amex Cobalt at 3x, Scotia Momentum at 2% cash back) for all transit pass purchases. Note: some transit authorities offer auto-reload — set this up with your bonus transit card.
Can I tap my credit card directly on the TTC?
Yes — the TTC and several other Canadian transit systems (including TransLink in Vancouver and OC Transpo in Ottawa) accept contactless credit card taps directly at fare gates. When you tap your Scotia Gold Amex directly at the TTC gate, the transit fare codes as MCC 4111 and earns the 5x Scene+ transit bonus rate. The Amex Cobalt earns 3x MR on the same tap. This is the simplest way to earn credit card rewards on transit — no loading a separate card, just tap your credit card.
