The RBC ION+ Visa is a quiet overachiever in Canadian credit cards. At just $48 per year, it earns 3 Avion points per dollar on six of the most common Canadian spending categories: groceries, dining, delivery, gas, daily transit, and streaming. That sweeps in almost everything most Canadians spend on regularly — your Uber Eats, your Netflix, your commute, your grocery run, your weekly fill-up.

RBC introduced the ION+ as a category-focused alternative to its premium Avion travel cards. It punches above its fee tier because RBC Avion points retain meaningful value through travel redemptions and British Airways Avios transfers.

The breadth of the bonus categories is the ION+'s biggest strength. Most category cards pick two or three buckets — the ION+ covers six, hitting the majority of household spending for most urban Canadians:

Avion Points Value

RBC Avion Rewards points are versatile. You can redeem them through multiple channels:

  • RBC Travel Portal — Book any flight and redeem points at roughly 1–1.2 cents per point. A $500 flight costs around 40,000–50,000 points.
  • British Airways Avios — Transfer at a 1:1 ratio (1 Avion point = 1 Avios). Avios are particularly valuable for short-haul flights booked on British Airways partner airlines (including American Airlines). A Toronto–Montreal Avios redemption can be as few as 7,500 Avios each way.
  • WestJet Dollars — 100 Avion points = $1 WestJet Dollar, which is a weak 1¢ conversion. Avoid this option.
  • Cash/Statement Credit — Approximately 0.5¢ per point. Poor value — always redeem for travel instead.
Best use of ION+ points: Transfer to British Airways Avios for short-haul partner award redemptions, or use the RBC travel portal for flexible travel credit. Avoid WestJet Dollar conversions and statement credits — value drops by 50%+.

Rewardly Verdict — 4.0 / 5

The RBC ION+ Visa is one of Canada's most underrated credit cards. At $48/yr, it covers groceries, dining, delivery, gas, transit, and streaming at 3× — a category spread that maps almost perfectly onto urban Canadian life. The $5M emergency medical is exceptional for its fee tier.

The main limitation is the Avion program: points are most valuable when redeemed for travel, and they don't transfer to Aeroplan. If you're building Aeroplan miles, you'll want the RBC Avion Visa Infinite or a CIBC/TD Aeroplan card. But for a practical everyday rewards card at a low annual fee, the ION+ is hard to beat. Frequently Asked Questions

Can I transfer RBC Avion points to Aeroplan?

Not from the ION+ card. The RBC Avion Visa Infinite and Avion Visa Business have Aeroplan transfer capability; the ION+ does not. If Aeroplan is your primary goal, you need the Avion Visa Infinite ($120/yr) or consider a CIBC or TD Aeroplan card instead.

What is the British Airways Avios transfer ratio?

1 Avion point = 1 British Airways Avios at a 1:1 rate. Avios are valuable for short-haul partner award bookings. The transfer is online through RBC Avion Rewards and typically processes within a few days.

Does the ION+ earn 3× at Costco?

No — Costco Canada only accepts Mastercard. The RBC ION+ is a Visa card and cannot be used at Costco warehouses.

How does the $48 annual fee compare?

It's one of the lowest annual fees for a category-rewards card in Canada. The Tangerine Money-Back card is no-fee but earns only 2% on chosen categories. Most other 3× category cards charge $120–$139/yr. The ION+ at $48 is unique in offering this earn rate at a mid-tier fee.

Can I hold both the ION+ and the Avion Visa Infinite?

Yes. Many RBC customers do exactly this: use the ION+ for everyday category spending (3×) and the Avion Visa Infinite for travel purchases and Aeroplan transfers. RBC allows multiple personal cards simultaneously.
