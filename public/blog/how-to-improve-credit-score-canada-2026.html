How to Improve Your Credit Score Fast Canada 2026

A 50-point improvement in your credit score can save you $15,000+ on a 25-year mortgage. Here's the fastest evidence-based path from fair to excellent in Canada.

Canadian credit scores range from 300–900 (both Equifax and TransUnion use this scale). Most lenders consider 660+ "good" and 760+ "excellent" — the threshold where you qualify for the best mortgage and car loan rates. For premium credit card approval, a score of 700+ is typically needed for entry-level rewards cards and 720+ for premium Visa Infinite and WE Mastercard products.

Fastest Improvements by Timeline

30 days

Pay Down Credit Card Balances (Biggest Quick Win)

Utilization updates when your bank reports to the credit bureau — usually at statement close. Paying your balance from $3,000 on a $5,000 limit card (60% utilization) to $1,000 (20%) can add 20–40 points within one billing cycle. If you have savings, temporarily pay down revolving credit card debt before the statement closes — even if you recharge it after the statement date.

30–60 days

Dispute Errors on Your Credit Report

Get your free credit report at Equifax Canada (equifax.ca) and TransUnion Canada (transunion.ca). Look for: accounts you don't recognize (identity theft), incorrect late payment marks, wrong balances, duplicate accounts. File a dispute online — credit bureaus must investigate and respond within 30 days. Errors affect 1 in 5 Canadians; fixing them can produce fast score improvements if a negative error is removed.

3–6 months

Set Up Autopay to Never Miss a Payment

Payment history is 35% of your score. One missed payment can drop you 50–100 points and stays on your report for 6 years. Set minimum payment autopay as a floor (ensures you're never late), then manually pay more. If you've had a late payment, its impact diminishes over time — after 2 years it has minimal weight, after 6 years it falls off entirely.

6–12 months

Request a Credit Limit Increase

If your credit card issuer will increase your credit limit (after 6–12 months of on-time payments), your utilization ratio drops even if spending stays the same. Example: $2,000 balance on $4,000 limit = 50% utilization. Same $2,000 balance on $8,000 limit = 25% utilization — a significant improvement. Request limit increases by phone or online — many banks offer them proactively for good customers without a hard inquiry.

12–24 months

Diversify Credit Mix (If Needed)

If you only have credit cards, adding an installment credit product (auto loan, personal loan, or being added as a co-signer on a mortgage) can improve your credit mix score. Don't take on debt purely for this — only if you genuinely need the product. Credit mix has the smallest weighting (10%) and isn't worth paying interest for.

Common Myths About Canadian Credit Scores

MythReality
"Checking my own credit score hurts it"False — checking your own score is a soft inquiry. Only hard inquiries (from lenders) affect your score, and those only drop it 5–15 points temporarily.
"Carrying a balance builds credit"False — you don't need to carry a balance or pay interest to build credit. Paying in full monthly (zero interest) while using the card is optimal.
"Income affects your credit score"False — your income doesn't appear in your credit score. A high earner with missed payments has a worse score than a lower earner with perfect payment history.
"Closing old cards improves your score"Usually false — closing old cards reduces average credit age and total available credit, both of which hurt your score. Keep old no-fee cards open with occasional small purchases.
"Equifax and TransUnion scores are always the same"False — different lenders report to different bureaus, and the bureaus use slightly different scoring models. Your Equifax score can differ by 20–50 points from your TransUnion score.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I get my free credit report in Canada?
Equifax Canada (equifax.ca) and TransUnion Canada (transunion.ca) both offer free credit reports. Equifax offers one free credit report per year by mail; TransUnion offers free access via their web platform. Free credit score monitoring is also available through many Canadian banks (TD MyCredit, RBC Credit Score, CIBC CreditSmart) and third-party services like Borrowell (uses Equifax) and Credit Karma Canada (uses TransUnion).
How long does it take to improve a bad credit score in Canada?
With consistent positive actions (on-time payments, low utilization), most people can move from "Poor" (560 range) to "Good" (660+) in 12–24 months. Going from "Fair" to "Excellent" takes 3–5 years of consistent positive history. The biggest single variable is payment history — even one missed payment adds years to your recovery timeline. Late payment marks remain on your Canadian credit report for 6 years from the date of the late payment.
Does using a prepaid card help build credit in Canada?
No — standard prepaid cards (KOHO, Wealthsimple Cash, EQ Bank Card) are not credit products and do not appear on your credit report. They neither help nor hurt your credit score. KOHO's Credit Builder add-on is an exception — it is specifically designed to report payment activity to Equifax. Traditional credit cards (any bank-issued Visa/Mastercard/Amex) are the most effective tools for building credit because they automatically report monthly usage and payments.
