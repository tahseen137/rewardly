Best Credit Cards for Sports Events & Concerts Canada 2026

From Leafs games to Taylor Swift tours — the right card earns on tickets, concessions, parking, and pregame dinner. Plus: which cards give presale access.

How Event Spending Codes on Credit Cards

Going to a game or concert involves several distinct spending categories: tickets (bought online, usually codes as "entertainment" or "ticketing services"), arena food and drinks (codes as "dining" or "food service"), merchandise (codes as "retail"), parking (codes as "parking" or "transportation"), and pre-game dinner at a nearby restaurant (codes as "restaurant/dining"). The most valuable category is dining, where Cobalt earns 5x — which applies to arena concessions at many venues.

Best Cards for Events

#1 Amex Cobalt — Best for Arena Concessions + Pre-Game Dining

5x MR on dining & food · 2x on transit

Annual fee: $156. Arena concession stands at Rogers Centre, Scotiabank Arena, Bell Centre, and most major Canadian venues code as dining/food service — which means Cobalt earns 5x on your beers and nachos. Paired with a pre-game dinner at a nearby restaurant (also 5x), Cobalt dramatically outperforms every other card for the in-venue experience. Note: Amex has limited acceptance at some venue merchandise booths — bring a backup card.

#2 Scotia Gold American Express — Best for Ticket Purchases

5x Scene+ on entertainment · 5x on grocery/dining · No FX fee

Annual fee: $120. The Scotia Gold Amex earns 5x Scene+ on "entertainment" purchases — which includes online ticket platforms like Ticketmaster, Live Nation, and StubHub when they code as entertainment merchants. A $500 ticket purchase earns 2,500 Scene+ points (worth $25 at Cineplex or Sobeys). Also earns 5x on dining before and after the show. No foreign transaction fee is valuable for US tour tickets purchased through US ticket sites.

#3 Rogers Red WE Mastercard — Best Backup for Merchandise and Parking

2% cashback everywhere, unlimited

Annual fee: $0. For any event spending that falls outside bonus categories — merchandise booth, parking garage, coat check, Uber home — Rogers Red earns 2% flat. Also the right card when Amex isn't accepted at a specific venue kiosk or merchandise stand. Always bring one as your fallback.

Presale and Priority Access Benefits

Cards with Concert/Event Presale Access in Canada

American Express Front of the Line: Amex cardholders (any Amex card) get exclusive presale access to concerts and events through the Amex Front of the Line program. This includes major tours, theatre productions, and sporting events. For high-demand shows where tickets sell out in minutes, Amex presale access can be the most valuable card benefit you never see listed in the earn rate table.

Scotiabank Scene+ / Scotia Cinema Events: Scotia cardholders occasionally get Scene+ presale and bonus points events at Cineplex venues, including live sports screenings and special events.

CIBC and TD: Some CIBC and TD premium cardholders receive access to exclusive events through their respective banking networks — typically communicated via email to eligible cardholders rather than publicized broadly.

Practical tip: If you attend major concerts regularly, the Amex Front of the Line benefit alone may justify the $156 Cobalt annual fee. Getting tickets to a sold-out show before the general public isn't something you can measure in earn rate — but it's real, recurring value for event-goers.

The Full Event Night Math

For a typical NHL/NBA/concert night: tickets $300 (Scotia Gold 5x = $15 value), arena food $60 (Cobalt 5x = $3 in MR points value), pre-game dinner $80 (Cobalt 5x = $4), parking $30 (Rogers Red 2% = $0.60), merchandise $50 (Rogers Red 2% = $1), transit home $10 (Cobalt 2x). Total night spend: $530. Total rewards across optimized cards: ~$24 in value, plus the accumulated points toward future travel. Without optimization (all on one 1% card), you'd earn $5.30. Difference: $18.70 per event night.
