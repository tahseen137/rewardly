How gas purchases are categorized

Credit card "categories" are based on Merchant Category Codes (MCCs) assigned by the payment network, not by you. Gas stations typically use:

  • MCC 5541 — Service Stations (full service)
  • MCC 5542 — Automated Fuel Dispensers (pay-at-pump)

Pay-at-pump transactions sometimes miss the gas category on cards that use MCC filtering — especially at independent stations. If you're getting fewer points than expected, try paying inside at the counter.

The Petro-Canada boost strategy

Petro-Canada has a loyalty partnership with RBC that unlocks an automatic 3¢/litre discount when you link an RBC credit card to the Petro-Canada app. Here's how to stack it:

  1. Link your RBC credit card to the Petro-Canada app (takes 2 minutes)
  2. Open the app at the pump, enter your location code
  3. Pay with your RBC card at the register or tap with the linked card
  4. Get: 3¢/L Petro-Canada discount and full credit card earn rate on the discounted price

At a 60L fill at $1.55/L after the discount: you'd pay $93 instead of $95.40, saving $2.40 upfront plus card rewards on top. Over 52 fillups/year: ~$125 in Petro-Canada savings alone.

Best combination: RBC ION+ Visa linked to Petro-Canada = 3x Avion points on gas plus 3¢/L discount. At Petro-Canada only, this beats a flat 4% cashback card on gas.

Should you use a gas-station credit card?

Canadian gas station co-branded cards (like the Canadian Tire Triangle card or Petro-Points card) aren't credit cards — they're loyalty program memberships. Use both: present your loyalty card for the station program, then pay with your high-earn credit card.

FAQ

Does paying at the pump vs. inside affect earning rates?

Sometimes. Pay-at-pump transactions use MCC 5542 (Automated Fuel Dispensers). Some cards process these as "gas" normally; others require MCC 5541 (full-service station). If your rewards seem off, pay inside at the counter to ensure MCC 5541.

Do electric vehicle charging stations count as gas?

On many cards: yes. CIBC Dividend Visa Infinite explicitly includes EV charging stations in the 4% gas/EV category. Scotiabank Momentum includes "public transit," which sometimes covers EV charging. Confirm with your issuer before relying on this.

What's the best card for gas and groceries combined?

CIBC Dividend Visa Infinite (4% on both gas and groceries, $120 fee waived year 1) or Scotia Momentum Visa Infinite+ (4% groceries + 2% gas, $120 fee waived year 1, good for grocery-heavy families).

Can I use multiple cards to maximize gas rewards?

Yes — but it's usually not worth it for gas alone. The difference between a 3% card and a 4% card on $200/month gas is $24/year. If you already have a 4% grocery card, use the same card for gas (if it also earns 4% there). Otherwise, get the card with the best combined rate across your full spending.

Does gas at Costco earn extra rewards?

Costco gas stations use MCC 5541 and are treated as gas by most cards. Costco Canada only accepts Mastercard in-store, so Visa cards won't work at the pump. A Mastercard with a strong gas rate (like Tangerine) works at Costco gas.
