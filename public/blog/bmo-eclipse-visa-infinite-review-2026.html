The 5× earn in those categories is best-in-class, and the Social Booster — an extra 10% on the first four authorized users' spending — rewards sharing your card with friends and family. At $120/year (effectively $70 after the $50 lifestyle credit), it's one of the most accessible premium rewards cards in Canada.

BMO Rewards points are worth approximately ~0.67¢ per point when redeemed through the travel portal (15,000 pts = $100). At 5×, you're earning roughly ~3.35% back on your four primary spend categories. For Canadians who order food delivery 2–4× a week (common in Toronto, Vancouver, Montreal), this card earns extremely fast.

The $50 Lifestyle Credit

$50 Annual Lifestyle Credit

Each cardmember year, you receive a $50 lifestyle credit applied automatically against eligible lifestyle purchases. This credit reduces your effective annual fee from $120 to $70. Eligible categories for the lifestyle credit include streaming services, ride-hailing, food delivery, gym/fitness memberships, and online shopping — essentially the categories you're already earning 5× on.

Social Booster — The Unique Differentiator

The Social Booster is BMO eclipse's most innovative feature. When you add authorized users to your account (up to 4), those users receive a 10% bonus on their spending — on top of the base earn rate. The bonus is paid to the primary cardholder's BMO Rewards account.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does the 5× rate apply to Instacart and grocery delivery apps?
Food delivery apps like DoorDash, Skip the Dishes, and Uber Eats typically code as food delivery (MCC 5812 or delivery-specific MCCs) and earn 5×. Instacart may code as grocery delivery or as a marketplace. BMO's terms include food delivery and grocery stores — check BMO's current terms for the most accurate category definitions.
How do I get the $50 lifestyle credit?
The $50 credit is automatically applied as a statement credit once you accumulate $50 in eligible lifestyle purchases each cardmember year. You don't need to redeem anything — it's applied automatically. Eligible categories typically include streaming services, ride-hailing, food delivery, transit, and gym memberships.
How many authorized users can I add for the Social Booster?
You can add up to four authorized users. Each user's spend earns a 10% bonus on top of the standard earn rate (so 5× becomes effectively 5.5× for AU spend, credited to your account). There's no additional annual fee for the first authorized user card.
Can I transfer BMO Rewards points to Aeroplan or other programs?
No — BMO Rewards points can't be transferred to airline or hotel loyalty programs. They're redeemed through BMO's travel portal, as statement credits, as gift cards, or into a BMO InvestorLine account. This is the card's biggest limitation vs Amex Cobalt.
Is BMO eclipse better than BMO World Elite Mastercard?
For everyday spenders: eclipse wins (5× on four categories vs 3× on travel/dining for World Elite). For travellers who prioritize lounge access and flexible travel booking: World Elite wins (4 DragonPass visits). The two cards target different profiles — eclipse is for everyday maximizers, World Elite is for frequent flyers.
