KOHO Plans Explained

Free Plan — Best Starting Point

$0/month

The KOHO free plan is one of the best no-fee spending accounts in Canada. It earns 1% cash back on groceries and dining, charges zero FX fees on international purchases (saving 2.5% vs. most bank debit cards), and provides a Visa prepaid card accepted anywhere Visa is accepted. Since KOHO is a prepaid card (not a credit card), you load money onto it before spending — eliminating overdraft risk and built-in credit card interest if you tend to overspend.

Best for: Those new to KOHO, students, budget-conscious spenders, or anyone who wants international spending without FX fees at zero cost.

Extra Plan — Best Value Upgrade

$9/month ($108/year)

The Extra plan doubles the earn rate on groceries and dining to 2%, plus adds one free international ATM withdrawal per month. At 2% on $400/month in groceries and dining, that's $96/year in cashback — nearly offsetting the $108 annual fee. If you spend more than $450/month on groceries + dining, the Extra plan is cash-flow positive vs. the Free plan.

Best for: Regular users who spend $500+ per month on groceries and dining and want the higher earn rate.

Everything Plan — Best for International Travellers

$19/month ($228/year)

The Everything plan includes all Extra perks plus unlimited free international ATM withdrawals and Credit Building included. For Canadians who travel frequently and need to withdraw local currency, unlimited international ATM withdrawals (each typically costs $3–$7 at other banks) can pay for the $10 premium over Extra quickly. Credit Building inclusion is also a meaningful addition for those who need it.

Best for: Frequent international travellers, those who use ATMs abroad regularly, or those who want Credit Building without an extra $7/month.

KOHO Credit Builder — How It Works

Credit Builder is KOHO's add-on feature (included in Everything, +$7/month on other plans) that helps Canadians build or repair credit without a hard credit inquiry. Here's the mechanism:

KOHO lends you a small amount (acting as a secured credit line). You repay it in automatic monthly installments, and KOHO reports your payment history to Equifax Canada. Because this is a secured arrangement and uses a soft inquiry (not hard), it doesn't require strong existing credit to qualify.

Building credit through Credit Builder works, but is slower than a traditional credit card because the credit limit is very small and the "credit utilization" benefit doesn't apply the same way. The real benefit is adding payment history for those who have no Canadian credit history at all (new immigrants, young people without any credit accounts).

Who it's for: New Canadians building their first credit history, people who have had credit issues and can't get a traditional credit card, or those who want credit building without the temptation of a credit card balance. If you already have a credit card that you pay in full monthly, KOHO Credit Builder adds limited incremental value over what you're already doing naturally.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is KOHO a real bank account?
No — KOHO is not a chartered bank and deposits are not directly CDIC-insured. KOHO partners with Peoples Trust Company, a CDIC member institution, to hold your funds. However, KOHO accounts are not formal "bank accounts" and the CDIC insurance applies to the partner institution's eligible deposits. KOHO recommends not storing your primary savings with KOHO — it's designed for everyday spending, not long-term savings.
Can I use KOHO to rent a car or book a hotel?
Hotels and car rental companies typically require a credit card (not prepaid) for incidental holds — they place a temporary authorization (e.g., $200–$500) on your card to cover potential damages or additional charges. Prepaid cards including KOHO are often declined for this purpose. For travel, carry a traditional credit card alongside KOHO for hotel check-in and car rental deposits, then use KOHO for day-to-day foreign spending where the 0% FX fee saves money.
Does KOHO report to credit bureaus?
Only through the Credit Builder add-on ($7/month on Free/Extra plans, included in Everything). Without Credit Builder, KOHO usage is not reported to Equifax or TransUnion and does not affect your credit score in any way — positively or negatively.
