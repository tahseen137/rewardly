Scotiabank vs TD Credit Card Rewards: Which Is Better in 2026?

Scene+ vs Aeroplan — 5x groceries vs premium flight redemptions. Which bank wins for Canadians?
February 28, 2026 · 8 min read · By Rewardly Team

Scotiabank and TD are two of Canada’s Big Six banks — both with strong credit card lineups, aggressive welcome bonuses, and competitive everyday earn rates. If you’re choosing between them, the right answer depends almost entirely on how you travel and where you spend.

Here’s an honest comparison of their flagship rewards cards in 2026.

The Cards: Who We’re Comparing

Scotiabank’s best rewards cards:

  • Scotiabank Gold American Express (travel, no FX fees)
  • Scotiabank Passport Visa Infinite (travel, no FX fees, lounge access)

TD’s best rewards cards:

  • TD Aeroplan Visa Infinite (Air Canada loyalists)
  • TD First Class Travel Visa Infinite (flexible travel)
  • TD Cash Back Visa Infinite (simple cashback)

We’re focused on the mainstream consumer cards — not premium tiers ($300+ annual fee) or business cards.

Earn Rates: Scotiabank vs TD

Scotiabank Gold American Express

  • 5x Scene+ points on groceries, dining, and food delivery
  • 3x points on gas, transit, and streaming
  • 1x point on everything else

TD Aeroplan Visa Infinite

  • 1.5 Aeroplan pts/$ on groceries, gas, and Air Canada purchases
  • 1.0 pt/$ on everything else

TD First Class Travel Visa Infinite

  • 8 TD Points/$ on travel booked through ExpediaForTD.com
  • 3 TD Points/$ on groceries, dining, and streaming
  • 2 TD Points/$ on everything else
Winner on everyday earn rates: Scotiabank Gold Amex by a clear margin. 5x on groceries is outstanding — higher than almost any card in Canada at this fee tier. A household spending $1,000/month on groceries earns 60,000 Scene+ points per year from that category alone.

Welcome Bonuses

Scotiabank Gold Amex: Up to 45,000 Scene+ points in year 1. At 1 cent/point for travel, that’s $450 in travel credit, plus first-year fee waiver.

TD Aeroplan Visa Infinite: Up to 50,000 Aeroplan points. At ~1.8 cents/point for flights, that’s roughly $900 in flight value — one of the best welcome bonuses in the Canadian market at this fee tier.

Winner on welcome bonus: TD Aeroplan, by a wide margin. Aeroplan points are worth more per point than Scene+ for travel redemptions, and 50,000 points can cover a significant portion of a domestic or transatlantic flight.

How Do You Want to Redeem?

This is where the comparison really diverges.

Best for Most

Choose Scotiabank if:

  • You want flexibility. Scene+ points redeem for any travel purchase at 1 cent/point — just book whatever you want, then redeem as a statement credit. No blackout dates, no transfer math.
  • You don’t have an airline loyalty. If you fly WestJet, Porter, Air Transat, and Air Canada interchangeably, Scene+ beats Aeroplan.
  • You want no foreign transaction fees. Both the Gold Amex and Passport Visa Infinite charge 0% FX fees — a real differentiator vs TD’s 2.5% on foreign purchases. Worth $200–$400/year for frequent international travelers.
  • You spend heavily on groceries and dining. At 5x Scene+, the Gold Amex earns faster than almost any Canadian card at this fee tier.

Choose TD if:

  • You fly Air Canada regularly. Aeroplan sweet spots — business class to Europe (55,000 pts), transpacific routes, partner airlines — offer premium value that simple travel credits can’t match.
  • You want the best domestic flight value. 50,000 Aeroplan points can cover a round trip domestically — roughly $500–$800 in flight value vs $450 in Scene+ credits.
  • You bank primarily with TD. TD customers can often access higher welcome bonus offers through existing customer channels.

Verdict: Which Is Better in 2026?

For most Canadians: Scotiabank Gold American Express

The 5x grocery earn rate is exceptional, no foreign transaction fees are a real advantage, and Scene+ is genuinely flexible. At $120/year, it’s also the cheapest premium card in this comparison.

For Air Canada loyalists: TD Aeroplan Visa Infinite

If you fly Air Canada 3+ times per year and are willing to learn Aeroplan redemptions, you’ll consistently get more value per dollar than any Scene+ redemption.

The real answer: carry both.

Many Canadian rewards maximizers carry a Scotiabank Gold Amex for groceries and international spending, and a TD Aeroplan card for Air Canada bookings. The two cards complement each other better than they compete.
