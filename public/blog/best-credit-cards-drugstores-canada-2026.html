Best Credit Cards for Drugstores & Pharmacies in Canada 2026

Shoppers Drug Mart, Rexall, Jean Coutu — the right card at the pharmacy can earn 4–5%+ back. Here's which card to pull out at the counter.

Why Drugstores Are a High-Value Rewards Category

Canadian drugstores are unusual spending categories because they sell a wide range of products beyond prescriptions: groceries, household goods, cosmetics, and gift cards — all coded under MCC 5912 (Drug Stores and Pharmacies) or occasionally MCC 5411 (grocery) at some Shoppers locations. Several premium credit cards offer bonus multipliers on this MCC, making your weekly Shoppers run genuinely rewarding.

MCC note: Most Shoppers Drug Mart and Rexall locations code as MCC 5912 (Drug Stores). A small number of Shoppers with extensive grocery sections may code as 5411 (Grocery) — benefiting grocery cards like PC Financial WE MC. Your card's transaction history will show which code applies at your specific store.

Major Canadian Drugstore Chains (2026)

🏪 Shoppers Drug Mart / Pharmaprix

Canada's largest pharmacy chain. Owned by Loblaw Companies. PC Optimum loyalty program. ~1,300 locations nationwide.

🏪 Rexall / PharmaChoice

Empire Company subsidiary. Be Well loyalty program. ~1,700 locations. Mostly Western Canada and Ontario.

🏪 Jean Coutu / PJC

Quebec-based chain, now Loblaw subsidiary. PC Optimum points. ~400 locations in Quebec, Ontario, NB.

🏪 London Drugs

Western Canada only. No national loyalty program. Strong electronics and cosmetics selection. ~79 stores BC/AB/SK/MB.

🏪 Lawtons Drugs

Atlantic Canada. Part of Sobeys/Empire. Air Miles partner at some locations. ~80 stores.

🏪 Guardian / IDA / Medicine Shoppe

Independent pharmacy networks. Coding varies — check your transaction history. Accept most cards.

Best Credit Cards for Drugstores

#1

Scotiabank Gold American Express

$120/yr · Scene+ · Amex
Best at Shoppers (when accepted)
5x Scene+ at Shoppers Drug Mart

The Scotia Gold Amex earns 5x Scene+ points on grocery and "everyday" spending — and Shoppers Drug Mart qualifies for the 5x grocery multiplier at most locations because Shoppers codes as grocery (MCC 5411) OR the 5x everyday earn applies. At 1 Scene+ pt = 1¢ on travel, that's effectively 5% back toward travel on every Shoppers purchase.

Annual fee: $120, waived first year. No FX fee (great for imported products billed in USD). No cap on the 5x earn. The only limitation: Amex isn't accepted everywhere — always carry a Visa/MC backup.

Best for: Anyone whose Shoppers Drug Mart accepts Amex and who shops there regularly. 5% back with no spending cap at a single chain is exceptional — particularly for high-value prescription fills and beauty/cosmetics purchases.
#2

BMO eclipse rise Visa

$0 Fee · BMO Rewards
Best No-Fee Option
5x BMO Rewards on drugstores (~1.7% travel)

The BMO eclipse rise earns 5x BMO Rewards on drugstores (MCC 5912) as one of its five primary bonus categories. At ~0.67¢/pt toward travel redemptions, that's ~1.7% back — with no annual fee. No income requirement, no cap on 5x earn.

BMO eclipse rise is unique in explicitly listing "drugstores" as a bonus category (alongside grocery, dining, gas, and streaming), making it the strongest Visa option for pharmacies nationwide — particularly Rexall and London Drugs where Amex acceptance is inconsistent.

Best for: Canadians who shop at Rexall, London Drugs, or smaller pharmacy chains where Amex isn't accepted, and want strong rewards with no annual fee.
#3

PC Financial World Elite Mastercard

$0 Fee · PC Optimum · $80K income
Best at Shoppers for PC Shoppers
4.5% PC Optimum at Shoppers Drug Mart

At Shoppers Drug Mart (and Jean Coutu), the PC Financial WE MC earns 4.5% in PC Optimum points (45 pts per $10 = 4.5%). PC Optimum points are redeemable at Loblaw-owned stores, Shoppers, and Esso/Mobil gas stations — and through PC Travel. No annual fee, $80K income requirement.

Stack with PC Optimum app offers: Shoppers regularly runs "20x the points" bonus events where app-loaded offers add 20x on specific products. Combined with the 4.5% base earn on the PC card, stacking events can push effective rewards well above 20% on select products.

Best for: Loyal PC Optimum members who shop primarily at Shoppers Drug Mart and want to stack credit card earn with PC Optimum points and app offers.
#4

CIBC Dividend Visa Infinite

$120/yr · Cash Back · Visa
Best Visa Cash Back for Drugstores
4% cash back on drugstores (no cap)

The CIBC Dividend VI earns 4% cash back on grocery and drugstore purchases combined — up to $80,000/year per category (effectively uncapped for most households). No complicated loyalty programs, no portal — straight 4% deposited as statement credit. Annual fee $120, waived if you hold a CIBC Smart Plus account.

Unlike the PC Financial card, CIBC Dividend works at any drugstore in Canada: Shoppers, Rexall, Jean Coutu, London Drugs, Guardian, independent pharmacies — as long as they code as MCC 5912 or 5411.

Best for: Canadians who shop at multiple pharmacy chains and want a simple Visa that earns 4% cash back everywhere — particularly those who already have a CIBC banking relationship (Smart Plus waives the fee).
#5

Scotiabank Momentum Visa Infinite

$120/yr · Cash Back · Visa
Strong Cash Back Alternative
4% on grocery and drugstore (shared $25K/yr cap)

Scotia Momentum VI earns 4% on grocery + drugstore combined up to $25,000/year — then 1%. A shared cap means heavy grocery shoppers may exhaust the 4% tier before reaching their drugstore spend. If your grocery + drugstore annual spend is under $25K, this is effectively 4% at the pharmacy with no tracking complexity.

Annual fee $120. The shared cap is a real limitation vs. CIBC Dividend VI (which has a per-category $80K cap), but if you're already loyal to Scotiabank or want Scene+ ecosystem compatibility, it's a solid option.

Best for: Existing Scotiabank customers whose combined grocery + drugstore spend stays under $25K/year, wanting 4% cash back with Visa acceptance.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does Shoppers Drug Mart code as grocery or drugstore?
It depends on the specific location. Many Shoppers locations code as MCC 5912 (Drug Stores & Pharmacies). Some Shoppers with larger grocery sections code as MCC 5411 (Grocery Stores, Supermarkets). Check your card's recent transactions to see which MCC your local Shoppers uses. For the BMO eclipse rise, both 5912 (drugstore category) and 5411 (grocery category) earn 5x, so you're covered either way.
Do prescriptions earn credit card rewards?
Yes — prescription fills paid by credit card earn rewards at the same rate as other pharmacy purchases, as long as the transaction processes through the credit card terminal. If your provincial drug benefit or insurance plan fully covers the prescription with zero co-pay, there's no card transaction and thus no rewards. Partial pay situations (where you pay a co-pay or dispensing fee) still earn on the amount charged to your card.
Does Rexall accept American Express?
Rexall locations vary — some accept Amex, many do not. Unlike Shoppers Drug Mart (which broadly accepts Amex), Rexall is more inconsistent. If Amex isn't accepted at your Rexall, BMO eclipse rise Visa earns 5x drugstores and is a strong alternative.
Can I stack PC Optimum points with a credit card at Shoppers?
Absolutely — this is one of the best reward-stacking opportunities in Canada. Present your PC Optimum card (or app) to earn PC Optimum points, and simultaneously pay with your credit card to earn credit card rewards. Both point types are issued on the same transaction. The PC Financial World Elite Mastercard is the only card that also earns PC Optimum points (as its primary reward), while also being a Mastercard.
