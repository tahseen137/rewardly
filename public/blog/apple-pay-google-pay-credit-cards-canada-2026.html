Apple Pay & Google Pay Credit Cards Canada 2026 — Best Cards for Tap-to-Pay

Your mobile wallet earns the same rewards as your physical card. Here's which cards earn the most when you pay with your phone — and what every Canadian should know about tap-to-pay.

The Key Thing to Know First

Using your credit card through Apple Pay or Google Pay earns exactly the same rewards as swiping the physical card — the card network and issuer don't distinguish between physical tap and mobile wallet payments for rewards purposes. What matters is which card you load into your wallet, not the device you use to pay. So the question isn't "which card works with Apple Pay" — virtually all Canadian credit cards do — it's "which card earns the most on my everyday spending."

Exception: BMO Eclipse Visa Infinite offers 1 additional BMO Reward point per dollar spent when you use the card through a mobile wallet (Apple Pay, Google Pay, or Samsung Pay). This is one of the only Canadian cards to actively reward mobile payment usage itself. It's a small bonus but worth noting if you already carry this card.

Apple Pay vs. Google Pay in Canada

Apple Pay

  • Works on iPhone 6 and later
  • Also on Apple Watch, iPad, Mac
  • Tap limit: $250 in most provinces (no PIN needed)
  • Above $250: you'll need PIN on many terminals
  • Accepted wherever contactless Visa/MC is accepted
  • Setup: Wallet app → add card → verify with bank

Google Pay / Google Wallet

  • Works on Android phones with NFC
  • Compatible with Wear OS smartwatches
  • Same $250 tap limit applies
  • Setup: Google Wallet app → add card → verify
  • Accepted wherever contactless Visa/MC is accepted
  • Works without unlocking phone (vary by bank)

Is Tap-to-Pay Safer Than a Physical Card?

Yes — significantly more secure for in-person payments

When you add a card to Apple Pay or Google Pay, your actual card number is never stored on your phone or transmitted to merchants. Instead, each transaction uses a device-specific virtual account number and a one-time transaction code. This means a data breach at a merchant can't expose your real card number. Physical card skimming is also impossible. From a fraud-protection standpoint, mobile wallets are the most secure way to pay in-person in Canada.

Setting Up Your Wallet: Quick Steps

Apple Pay setup:

  1. 1
    Open the Wallet app on your iPhone → tap the "+" button
  2. 2
    Scan your credit card or enter the number manually
  3. 3
    Your bank will verify via text, email, or app notification (varies by bank)
  4. 4
    Set your most-used card as default; others are accessible with one tap

Google Wallet setup:

  1. 1
    Download Google Wallet from the Play Store if not pre-installed
  2. 2
    Tap "Add to Wallet" → "Payment card"
  3. 3
    Scan or enter card details; bank verifies via OTP
  4. 4
    Enable NFC in Android settings if tap doesn't work (Settings → Connections → NFC)
