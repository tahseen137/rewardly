Why It Wins for Gas

The Costco gas bar offers two stacked advantages: prices are typically 5–12¢/litre cheaper than nearby stations, AND you earn 3% cashback with CIBC. That combination can be worth $300–$500/year for a family that drives regularly.

The 1% In-Store Problem — and the Workaround

The weak spot is in-store warehouse purchases earning only 1%. The fix: buy Costco Shop Cards (gift cards) on costco.ca at 2%, then use them in-store. You effectively double your in-store earn rate with minimal extra steps.

How You're Paid

Cashback accumulates all year and is issued as an annual Costco Cash Back certificate in February — redeemable in-store, like cash. It's not monthly deposits; you wait for the annual payout.

What About Amex Cobalt at Costco?

The Amex Cobalt earns 5x Membership Rewards at grocery stores — one of the best grocery earn rates in Canada. But Costco is not classified as a grocery store for Amex's merchant category system.

At Costco.ca, the Cobalt earns just 1 pt/$ (base rate). And in Costco warehouses, Amex isn't accepted at all. The Cobalt is an excellent card for Loblaws, Metro, or Sobeys — but it's not the right tool for Costco spend.

The Best Strategy for Most Costco Shoppers

  • Gas-heavy Costco spender (any telecom): CIBC Costco Mastercard — 3% gas, no fee, Mastercard accepted in-store
  • Rogers/Fido/Shaw customer with mixed Costco spend: Rogers Red World Elite — 3% effective on everything with bill redemption
  • Maximize in-store on CIBC Costco: Buy Costco Shop Cards on costco.ca (2%) and use in-store
  • Track it automatically: Use Rewardly to see which card earns most across all your spending — not just Costco

The single biggest mistake Costco shoppers make is using a Visa (doesn't work in-store), using a plain Mastercard earning 1% everywhere, or skipping the gas bar entirely. Fix any one of these and you're hundreds of dollars ahead per year.
