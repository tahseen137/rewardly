What Makes It Worth It

The annual free night award certificate redeemable at Marriott properties up to 35,000 Bonvoy points is the headline feature. A mid-tier Marriott (Courtyard, Residence Inn, Sheraton Select) in a major Canadian or US city typically costs 25,000–35,000 points — meaning a night worth $150–$250 each year. That's more than the $120 annual fee.

Additionally, Silver Elite status automatically gives you 10% bonus points on Marriott stays, late checkout (when available), and dedicated customer service. With the 15 Elite Night Credits included annually, you start each year 15 nights toward Gold Elite (50 nights = 4pm late checkout, 25% bonus points, breakfast at some properties).

Marriott Bonvoy Transfer Partners

Bonvoy points can transfer to 40+ airline partners at a 3:1 ratio (with a 5,000 mile bonus per 60,000 points transferred). While not the most generous transfer rate, the sheer number of partners — including Air Canada Aeroplan — adds flexibility to your hotel points.

2. American Express Platinum — Best for Luxury Hotel Benefits

At $799/year, the Amex Platinum isn't a "hotel card" per se — but its hotel benefits are unmatched in Canada. The Fine Hotels + Resorts (FH+R) program provides room upgrades, daily breakfast for two, 4pm late checkout, noon early check-in, and a $100+ property credit at 1,900+ luxury properties worldwide. That's easily $300–$500+ in value per stay.

Amex Platinum also provides automatic Marriott Bonvoy Gold Elite status and Hilton Honors Gold status annually — without needing to hold a co-branded card or stay a single night. Gold Hilton gives free breakfast at most properties, worth $40–$70/night per couple.

3. Scotiabank Passport Visa Infinite — Best Hotel Card for No-FX Travellers

For Canadians who stay at international hotels (where foreign exchange fees eat into value), the Scotiabank Passport VI is the smartest hotel card. You earn 3 Scene+ points per dollar on hotels, restaurants, and entertainment, with zero foreign transaction fees — saving 2.5% on every international hotel charge.

Scene+ points are worth 1¢ each for travel redemptions. 3 pts/$ = 3% return on hotel spend, plus no FX. Compared to a Marriott card losing 2.5% FX on international hotels, Scotiabank Passport effectively beats it at international properties (3% earn + 2.5% FX savings = 5.5% effective return vs Marriott's 5× = 2.8% at 3:1 airline transfer).

4. BMO World Elite Mastercard — Best Flexible Hotel Points

BMO Rewards points earn at 3× on travel purchases which includes hotels booked directly and on travel portals. Points are worth 0.67¢–1¢ each depending on redemption, making the effective hotel earn rate 2–3%. The card also includes 4 DragonPass lounge visits/year ($240+ value) and solid travel insurance.

General Hotel Rewards Strategy for Canadians

The Two-Card Hotel Strategy

Card 1 (Hotel loyalty): Marriott Bonvoy Amex — use for all Marriott stays (5× points), qualifying for the annual free night cert and Silver Elite status.

Card 2 (International stays): Scotiabank Passport VI — use at any non-Marriott hotel internationally (3× Scene+ with zero FX fee). No FX fee saves 2.5% on every foreign currency hotel charge.

Together, you maximize Marriott loyalty while avoiding FX drag at non-Marriott hotels. Net result: free nights from Marriott, no forex fees everywhere else.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is there a Hilton credit card in Canada?
No — Hilton Honors has no co-branded credit card available to Canadian residents. The US has Hilton Amex cards, but Canadian Amex cards don't include Hilton co-brands. Your best option for Hilton stays is a general travel card (like Amex Cobalt for 5× on dining, or a no-FX card for international Hiltons) combined with earning Hilton points directly through stays.
Is there an IHG credit card in Canada?
No — IHG One Rewards doesn't have a co-branded credit card in Canada. You earn IHG points through stays at Holiday Inn, InterContinental, Kimpton, etc. For Canadian cardholders, a general travel card works best for IHG stays.
What's the best card for booking hotels on Expedia or Hotels.com?
For third-party hotel bookings, a general travel card is best since you typically don't earn hotel loyalty points on OTA bookings. Amex Cobalt earns 2× on travel including Expedia and Hotels.com. Scotiabank Passport VI earns 3× on travel and waives FX fees — ideal for international hotel OTA bookings.
How do I get Marriott Gold Elite status in Canada without staying 50 nights?
Hold an American Express Platinum Card — it includes complimentary Marriott Gold Elite status (plus Hilton Gold) with no stay requirement. It's $799/year, but for frequent travellers, the combined benefits (lounge access, FH+R, hotel statuses) often justify it.
