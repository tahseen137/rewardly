Purchase Protection for Expensive Vet Bills

Several premium Canadian cards include extended warranty and purchase protection, but these don't apply to vet services — services are excluded. However, if you're buying expensive pet equipment (an orthopedic dog bed, a specialty food processor, a GPS tracker for your pet), cards with purchase protection cover these items for 90–120 days against damage or theft.

Cards with strong purchase protection: Amex Cobalt (90-day damage protection, 1-year extended warranty on eligible purchases), RBC Avion VI, TD Aeroplan VI. For pet equipment over $200, put it on one of these cards.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does Amex Cobalt earn 5x at pet stores like PetSmart?
No — PetSmart and Petco are specialty retail stores, not grocery stores. The Cobalt's 5x earn applies to supermarkets and grocery stores, not pet specialty retailers. PetSmart transactions on the Cobalt earn 1x MR points. For PetSmart, Rogers Red (1.5–2%) or a general flat-rate cashback card is better. The Cobalt only earns 5x on pet food you buy at actual grocery stores (Loblaws, Sobeys, Metro, Walmart grocery section, No Frills, etc.).
Are vet bills a deductible expense on Canadian taxes?
Generally no — personal pet veterinary costs are not tax-deductible in Canada for most Canadians. An exception: if your pet is a working animal (farm dog, service animal, guard dog for a business), the costs may be deductible as business expenses. Consult a tax professional for your specific situation. The credit card rewards on vet bills are one way to partially offset the cost without a tax break.
