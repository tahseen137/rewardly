RBC vs CIBC Credit Card Rewards: Which Is Better in 2026?

Avion vs Aventura — earn rates, fees, perks, and the honest verdict for Canadian cardholders.
February 28, 2026 · 8 min read · By Rewardly Team

RBC and CIBC are two of Canada’s most popular banks — and both have built flagship rewards programs that millions of Canadians carry in their wallets every day. RBC has Avion Rewards. CIBC has Aventura. On the surface they look nearly identical: similar fees, similar travel perks, similar “flexible points” positioning. But the differences matter more than the marketing suggests.

Here’s a straight look at both programs in 2026, so you can figure out which one actually earns you more.

The Cards: Who We’re Comparing

RBC’s best rewards cards:

  • RBC Avion Visa Infinite (flagship travel card)
  • RBC Avion Visa Platinum (lower fee option)

CIBC’s best rewards cards:

  • CIBC Aventura Visa Infinite (flagship travel card)
  • CIBC Aventura Gold Visa (mid-tier option)

We’re focused on the flagship Visa Infinite tier — the cards most Canadians actually apply for. No $600 ultra-premium tiers, no business cards.

Earn Rates: RBC Avion vs CIBC Aventura

RBC Avion Visa Infinite

  • 1.25 Avion points/$ on travel purchases
  • 1.0 point/$ on everything else
  • +20% bonus at Petro-Canada (link your card to get extra points at the pump)

CIBC Aventura Visa Infinite

  • 3 Aventura points/$ on travel booked through the CIBC Rewards Centre
  • 2 points/$ on groceries, gas, and drug stores
  • 1.5 points/$ on all other purchases
Winner on everyday earn rates: CIBC Aventura, and it’s not particularly close. A family spending $1,200/month on groceries and gas earns 28,800 Aventura points per year from those categories alone. On RBC, the same spending earns 14,400 points. That’s a meaningful gap over time.

Annual Fees

RBC is $19 cheaper per year at this tier. Not dramatic, but worth noting when comparing equivalent redemption value.

Welcome Bonuses

RBC Avion Visa Infinite: Up to 35,000 Avion points in year 1. At roughly 2 cents per point for flights, that’s approximately $700 in travel value — solid, though not a showstopper.

CIBC Aventura Visa Infinite: Up to 45,000 Aventura points — typically 15,000 points on first purchase, then 30,000 more after spending $3,000 in 4 months. At ~2 cents per point, that’s roughly $900 in travel value, plus a first-year fee rebate.

Winner on welcome bonus: CIBC Aventura by a clear margin. The higher point total and additional year-one perks make the Aventura the better card to open first if you’re starting fresh with either bank’s ecosystem.

Perks & Real-World Value

This is where the cards diverge most clearly — and where CIBC pulls further ahead.

CIBC Aventura Advantages

  • Priority Pass lounge access. 4 complimentary lounge visits per year — a perk the RBC Avion Visa Infinite simply doesn’t offer at this fee tier. Those 4 visits represent roughly $130–$200 in saved lounge fees annually.
  • NEXUS fee rebate. CIBC reimburses one NEXUS membership fee every 4 years (~$50 CAD) — genuinely useful for frequent cross-border travelers.
  • No booking restrictions. Aventura points redeem for any travel purchase without blackout dates. Book on any site, apply points as a statement credit.

RBC Avion Advantages

  • Lower annual fee. $120 vs $139 — relevant when comparing net value against a modest rewards gap.
  • Petro-Canada partnership. Link your RBC card to get a per-litre discount plus accelerated Avion earning. Real recurring value for Petro-Canada regulars.
  • Points don’t expire. Avion points have no expiry while your card is active — useful for slow accumulators.
  • Strong transfer partners. Avion points transfer to British Airways Executive Club, WestJet Rewards, and others — unlocking higher-value redemptions for savvy award travelers.

Who Should Choose Which Card

Choose RBC Avion Visa Infinite if:

  • You have loyalty to Petro-Canada and fill up regularly
  • You want to transfer points strategically to programs like British Airways Avios
  • You want a slightly lower annual fee
  • You accumulate slowly and want points that never expire
Best for Most

Choose CIBC Aventura Visa Infinite if:

  • You want better everyday earn rates on groceries and gas
  • You fly 2–4+ times per year and will actually use lounge access
  • You want a larger welcome bonus to offset first-year costs
  • You prefer simple “book anywhere, redeem anywhere” travel credits

Verdict: Which Is Better in 2026?

For most Canadians: CIBC Aventura Visa Infinite

The higher earn rates on groceries and gas, the larger welcome bonus, and the Priority Pass lounge access make the Aventura the stronger everyday card for most Canadian households — even at a $19/year premium.

For strategic award travelers: RBC Avion Visa Infinite

If you’re willing to learn airline transfer partners and optimize redemption value, Avion’s flexibility can unlock higher-value bookings than Aventura’s straightforward travel credits. The Petro-Canada link is also a genuine ongoing benefit for regular users.

The honest truth: most Canadians won’t optimize airline transfer partners. They want a card that earns fast on groceries, comes with meaningful perks, and lets them book travel however they want. That’s the Aventura.
