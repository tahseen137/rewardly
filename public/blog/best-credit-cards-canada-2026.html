🏆 Best Cash Back Card

🥇 Top Pick

Scotiabank Momentum Visa Infinite — $120/yr

4% cash back on groceries, 4% on recurring bills, 2% on gas and transit, 1% on everything else. For Canadians who spend heavily on groceries and subscriptions (Netflix, internet, phone), this card consistently wins.

Why it wins: 4% on groceries is the highest cash-back rate in Canada for that category. Grocery-heavy households (especially families) can earn $400–$600/yr in cash back.

Full Review →
Runner-up: CIBC Dividend Visa Infinite — 4% groceries, 4% gas, $120/yr. Better for high gas spenders; same grocery rate as Momentum with stronger gas rewards.
For simplicity: Rogers Red World Elite Mastercard — 1.5% everywhere (3% on USD purchases), no annual fee. Best no-fee flat-rate cash-back card.

🏆 Best Travel Rewards Card

🥇 Top Pick

Scotiabank Gold American Express — $120/yr

5 Scene+ points per dollar on dining, groceries, and entertainment (6× at Sobeys/IGA/FreshCo/Safeway). Zero foreign transaction fees. 3× on gas. Points transfer to Aeroplan at 1:1, meaning that 5 pts/$ on groceries = 5 Aeroplan pts/$ — among the highest Aeroplan-earning rates from any non-Aeroplan card. Strong travel insurance, $120/yr.

Why it wins: Rare combination of high earn rate + no FX fees + Aeroplan transfer. It outperforms dedicated Aeroplan cards on everyday spending while costing less.

Full Review →
Premium alternative: American Express Platinum Card ($799/yr) — Centurion Lounges, Fine Hotels & Resorts, automatic Marriott Gold + Hilton Gold. For frequent luxury travelers, it delivers $1,800+ in annual value.
Flexible redemptions: Scotiabank Passport Visa Infinite ($150/yr) — any airline, no blackout dates, 6 Priority Pass visits, no FX fees. Best for those who don't want to be tied to Air Canada or WestJet.

🏆 Best Card for Groceries

🥇 Top Pick

Scotiabank Gold American Express — $120/yr

6 Scene+ points per dollar at any grocery store in Canada. At ~1¢ per Scene+ point (or 1:1 Aeroplan transfer), that's ~6% effective return on grocery spending. No store restrictions — works at Sobeys, Loblaws, Metro, Costco (with limitations), No Frills, Farm Boy, independent grocers.

Why it wins: Highest grocery earn rate available on a widely-accepted card. The Amex limitation at some stores (Costco) is the only drawback.

Full Review →
If you shop at Loblaws/No Frills: PC Financial Mastercard — no annual fee, 30 PC Optimum points per $ at Loblaws banner stores (worth ~3%). Free and earns extremely well at PC-family stores.
Cash-back version: Scotiabank Momentum Visa Infinite ($120/yr) — 4% cash back on groceries. Better if you prefer dollars over points.

🏆 Best Card for Dining

🥇 Top Pick

Scotiabank Gold American Express — $120/yr

5 Scene+ points per dollar at restaurants, bars, and food delivery (DoorDash, SkipTheDishes, Uber Eats). Includes dining across all channels — sit-down, takeout, and delivery.

Why it wins: 5% effective earn rate on dining (at 1¢/pt Scene+) is among the best in Canada for straightforward redemptions. The Amex Cobalt competes on transfer value; but Scotia Gold also covers groceries with 0% FX fees.

Full Review →
Strong alternative: American Express Cobalt ($191.88/yr) — 5× Amex MR on dining + groceries. Amex MR points transfer to Aeroplan at 1:1 with a 10% bonus on 10,000+ transfers. Strong for reward maximizers.
Delivery-focused: BMO eclipse Visa Infinite ($139/yr) — 5× on food delivery, dining, grocery, and gas. Net $70/yr after $50 lifestyle credit. Best for food delivery power users.

🏆 Best No Annual Fee Card

🥇 Top Pick

PC Financial Mastercard — $0/yr

30 PC Optimum points per dollar at Loblaws banner stores (Loblaws, No Frills, Superstore, Shoppers Drug Mart), 45 pts/$ at Shoppers. PC Optimum has ~10,000 Canadian redemption points — this card is exceptional for PC loyalists. No annual fee, no income minimum.

Why it wins: Extraordinary earn rates at partner stores, zero cost. If you shop at Loblaws or Shoppers, this card earns more than most paid cards at those specific retailers.

Tangerine option: Tangerine Money-Back Credit Card — 2% on 2 (or 3) categories of your choice, 0.5% base, no annual fee. Flexible category selection works well for disciplined spenders.

🏆 Best Aeroplan Card

🥇 Top Pick

TD Aeroplan Visa Infinite — $139/yr

1.5 Aeroplan points per dollar on groceries, gas, and drugstores; 1 pt/$ on everything else. First checked bag free on Air Canada for cardholder + up to 8 companions. Preferred pricing on Aeroplan reward redemptions. Strong emergency medical ($1M, 21 days).

Why it wins: Among the "big 5" Aeroplan cards (TD, RBC, CIBC, BMO, Scotiabank Gold), the TD card offers the best combination of everyday earn rates and Air Canada perks.

High earner: Scotiabank Gold Amex ($120/yr) — Not an Aeroplan card, but earns 5 Scene+ pts/$ on dining + groceries and transfers to Aeroplan 1:1. Earns more Aeroplan points on everyday spending than any dedicated Aeroplan card.

🏆 Best Student Card

🥇 Top Pick

BMO CashBack Mastercard — $0/yr

3% cash back on groceries, 1% on recurring bills, 0.5% base. No annual fee, accessible with limited credit history. Students can build credit while earning cash back on grocery spending — the highest-return category for student spending patterns.

Why it wins: No annual fee + 3% groceries is a rare combination. Students building credit should not be paying annual fees, and this card earns well on the spending categories that matter to students.

🏆 Best Card for Newcomers to Canada

🥇 Top Pick

Scotiabank StartRight Program — Multiple Cards

Scotiabank's StartRight program allows newcomers to Canada (within first 3 years of landing) to apply for the Scotiabank Gold American Express, Scotiabank Passport Visa Infinite, or Scene+ Visa without Canadian credit history. No employment or income proof required in year one.

Why it wins: Most Canadian banks require established credit history. Scotiabank explicitly waives this for newcomers and offers strong cards (not just secured or starter cards).

How We Pick Our Winners

Rewardly evaluates cards against a Canadian household spending profile (groceries $800/mo, dining $200, gas $150, travel $100, subscriptions $150, other $600). We calculate annual rewards value, subtract the annual fee, and weight perks at realistic usage rates. Insurance coverage is assessed for actual medical and baggage limits, not just presence of coverage. We update this guide as card terms change.

How to Choose the Right Card for You

The guides above give you the best cards by category, but the "best for you" depends on your specific spending pattern. A card that earns 6% on dining is useless if you rarely eat out. A premium travel card with $799/yr in fees needs to deliver at least $799 in clear value for it to make sense.
