But with five major Aeroplan credit cards across TD and CIBC, plus the standalone Amex Aeroplan card, choosing the right one is anything but simple.

Our #1 Pick

TD Aeroplan Visa Infinite

Annual fee: $139 (waived first year)
Welcome bonus: Up to 50,000 Aeroplan points in year 1
Minimum income: $60,000 personal / $100,000 household

Earn Rates

  • 1.5 Aeroplan pts/$ on groceries, gas, and Air Canada purchases
  • 1.0 pt/$ on all other purchases
  • Bonus points stack on Air Canada flights booked directly

Why It Wins

Strong earn rate on everyday categories, meaningful welcome bonus, and comprehensive travel insurance that makes it a legitimate all-in-one travel card. The grocery and gas bonus rate (1.5 pts/$) is uncapped — a family spending $800/month on groceries and $200/month on gas earns 15,000 Aeroplan points per year just from those two categories.

Travel Insurance

  • Emergency medical insurance (up to $2 million, 21-day trips)
  • Trip cancellation/interruption, flight delay, lost baggage
  • Rental car and common carrier accident insurance

Best For

Families and professionals who fly Air Canada 2–4x/year and want Aeroplan on everyday spending without paying $599/year for premium benefits they won't fully use.

#2: CIBC Aeroplan Visa Infinite

Annual fee: $139 (waived first year)
Welcome bonus: Up to 50,000 Aeroplan points in year 1
Minimum income: $60,000 personal / $100,000 household

TD vs CIBC: What's the Actual Difference?

Nearly identical products — same earn rates, same annual fee, same welcome bonus structure, nearly identical travel insurance. CIBC adds EV charging at the 1.5x rate; TD's insurance is rated slightly higher by independent reviewers.

Bottom line: Bank with TD? Get the TD version. Bank with CIBC? Get the CIBC version. The financial difference over a year is negligible.

Best For

CIBC banking customers who want Aeroplan rewards on everyday spending without switching banks.

#3: American Express Aeroplan Card

Annual fee: $120 (waived first year)
Welcome bonus: Up to 40,000 Aeroplan points
Minimum income: No stated minimum

Earn Rates

  • 2 Aeroplan pts/$ on Air Canada and Air Canada Vacations
  • 1.5 pts/$ on food delivery (DoorDash, Uber Eats, Skip)
  • 1.0 pt/$ on everything else

What Makes This Card Different

Earns more on Air Canada purchases (2x vs 1.5x) and has strong food delivery earn rates — but underperforms on groceries and gas. Key limitation: Amex isn't accepted at Costco Canada and some smaller retailers.

Best For

Frequent Air Canada flyers who book directly, heavy food delivery users, and people who don't meet Visa Infinite income requirements.

#4: TD Aeroplan Visa Infinite Privilege

Annual fee: $599
Welcome bonus: Up to 75,000 Aeroplan points
Minimum income: $150,000 personal (or $200,000 household)

Premium Perks

  • Air Canada Maple Leaf Club lounge access (complimentary first year)
  • Priority check-in and boarding + free first checked bag (cardholder + companion)
  • Annual $100 Air Canada gift card
  • Emergency medical up to $5M, 31-day coverage
  • NEXUS fee rebate ($50 every 4 years)
The Math: $599 fee − $100 Air Canada gift card − lounge value (~$200+/year for frequent users) = net ~$299 for heavy Air Canada travelers. If you fly 6+ times/year and use the perks, it can justify itself. Otherwise, the $139 Infinite delivers 80% of the value at 23% of the cost.

Best For

High-income frequent Air Canada flyers who travel 6+ times/year and will genuinely use lounge access and priority benefits.

#5: CIBC Aeroplan Visa Infinite Privilege

Essentially identical to the TD Privilege — same $599 fee, same welcome bonus tier, similar earn rates and perks. Choose based on your primary banking relationship.

How to Choose the Right Aeroplan Card

Choose TD or CIBC Aeroplan Visa Infinite if: you spend heavily on groceries and gas, fly Air Canada 2–5x/year, and your income qualifies ($60K+ personal).

Choose Amex Aeroplan if: you book Air Canada directly and want 2x on those purchases, use food delivery heavily, or don't meet Visa Infinite income requirements.

Choose Privilege tier if: you fly Air Canada 6+ times/year, earn $150K+ personal, and will genuinely use lounge access and priority boarding.

Aeroplan Card vs Points-Transfer Cards

Aeroplan cards lock you into Air Canada's program — excellent if you fly Air Canada regularly. But if your travel includes frequent WestJet flights or diverse international airlines, a flexible points card might serve you better.

The American Express Cobalt Card (5x MR on food/dining) transfers to Aeroplan at 1:1 — so you're not giving up Aeroplan access, just adding flexibility.
