Canadian Credit Card Glossary 2026

Every credit card term explained clearly — from annual fee to zero liability. Bookmark this page and never be confused by credit card jargon again.

ABCDEF GHILMN OPRSTU WZ

A

Annual Fee
A fee charged by the card issuer once per year for holding the credit card. Ranges from $0 (no-fee cards) to $799+ (premium cards like the Amex Platinum). Waiving this fee in year 1 is a common promotional offer. Always calculate whether the rewards and benefits you earn exceed the annual fee before keeping a paid card.
Annual Percentage Rate (APR) / Purchase Interest Rate
The interest rate charged on unpaid balances, expressed as an annual rate. Standard Canadian rate: 19.99%. Low-rate cards (like the MBNA True Line) charge as low as 8.99%. Interest compounds daily — $1,000 carried for a year at 19.99% costs ~$220 in interest. This is why paying in full monthly is so important.
Example: If you carry a $2,000 balance at 19.99% for 3 months, you'll pay approximately $100 in interest — more than most rewards cards earn on $2,000 of spending.
Authorized User
A secondary cardholder added to the primary cardholder's account. The authorized user gets their own card (usually free) but the primary cardholder is legally responsible for all charges. Both spend on the same account, pooling rewards. Most Canadian premium cards allow 1–3 free authorized user cards.
Avion Rewards
RBC's proprietary loyalty program. Points earned on RBC Avion credit cards. Can be transferred to British Airways Avios (1:1 ratio) for international redemptions, or redeemed through RBC's travel portal at approximately 1¢/point. See also: British Airways Avios.

B

Balance Transfer
Moving debt from one credit card to another. Usually done to take advantage of a promotional low (or 0%) interest rate for a limited period. Canadian balance transfer offers typically run 6–12 months at 0–1.99% with a 1–3% transfer fee. The MBNA True Line is popular for balance transfers.
Example: You have $5,000 on a card at 19.99%. Transferring to a card with a 0% BT promo for 6 months (3% fee = $150) saves you approximately $430 in interest.
BONUSDOLLARS
Desjardins' loyalty currency. 1 BONUSDOLLAR = 1¢ in value, redeemable for travel, gift cards, or statement credits. Fixed value — no transfer partners, no complex calculations. Earned on Desjardins credit cards including the Odyssey World Elite Mastercard.
British Airways Avios
British Airways' loyalty currency. Transferable from RBC Avion Rewards (1:1) and Amex Membership Rewards (1:1). Avios are particularly valuable for short-haul and distance-based awards on Oneworld airlines — including Air Canada, British Airways, Iberia, and Cathay Pacific. Canada-London in business class can yield 2–3¢ per Avios in value.

C

Cash Advance
Using your credit card to withdraw cash from an ATM or bank. Usually charged a flat fee (typically $5 or 1–5% of amount, whichever is greater) PLUS the cash advance interest rate (22.99–24.99%), which starts accruing immediately with no grace period. Never use a credit card for cash advances unless absolutely necessary.
Cash Back
A rewards structure where the card returns a percentage of spending as cash — either as a statement credit, cheque, or automatic deposit. Simpler than points programs: 2% cash back on $1,000 = $20 in real money, no conversion math needed. Best for people who prefer simplicity and certainty.
CDIC Insurance
Canada Deposit Insurance Corporation. Federally insured protection for eligible deposits (savings, chequing, GICs) at CDIC member institutions up to $100,000 per deposit category. Relevant when banking with smaller institutions like Tangerine, Simplii, or EQ Bank — all CDIC members. Credit card balances are not deposits and are not CDIC insured.
Chip-and-PIN / EMV
The security standard used on all modern Canadian credit cards. The card's chip (EMV) creates a unique code for each transaction, making cards much harder to counterfeit than the older magnetic stripe. PIN entry (sometimes replaced by tap/contactless for purchases under $250) verifies the cardholder.
Contactless Payment (Tap)
Paying by tapping your card (or phone/watch with NFC) on a payment terminal. In Canada, most terminals support contactless. Transaction limits have been raised post-pandemic — typically $250 per tap without PIN. Works with Visa payWave, Mastercard contactless, and Apple/Google/Samsung Pay.
Credit Bureau / Credit Score
Canada has two credit bureaus: Equifax and TransUnion. They track your credit history and calculate a credit score (typically 300–900 scale). Lenders use this to decide whether to approve your credit card application. Scores above 700 are considered good; above 750 excellent. Check yours free via Borrowell (Equifax) or Credit Karma Canada (TransUnion).
Credit Limit
The maximum balance you're allowed to carry on your credit card. Set by the issuer based on income, credit score, and other factors. Exceeding your limit triggers an over-limit fee and damages your credit score. Keeping balances below 30% of your limit improves your score.
Credit Utilization
The percentage of your available credit you're currently using. If your limit is $10,000 and your balance is $3,000, your utilization is 30%. Lower is better for your credit score — aim for under 30%, ideally under 10%. Tip: pay your balance before the statement date to reduce the reported utilization.
CT Money (Canadian Tire Money)
Canadian Tire's loyalty currency, earned on Triangle credit cards and at Canadian Tire stores. 1 CT Money = 1¢, redeemable only at Canadian Tire-banner stores (Canadian Tire, Sport Chek, Mark's, Party City, Gas+). The Triangle Mastercard earns 4% CT Money at Canadian Tire stores and 1.5% at gas stations.

D

DragonPass / Mastercard Travel Pass
Airport lounge access program used by many Mastercard World Elite credit cards. Provides access to 1,300+ lounges globally. Some cards include complimentary visits (e.g., National Bank World Elite: 6/year; Desjardins Odyssey: unlimited); others charge per visit (~US$32). Requires registering your card in the DragonPass app.
Dynamic Currency Conversion (DCC)
A scam you should always decline. When paying in a foreign country, a merchant or ATM offers to charge you in Canadian dollars "for convenience." Always choose to be charged in the local currency — DCC rates are worse than your card's FX rate plus the foreign transaction fee. Say "charge me in [local currency]" every time.

E

Earn Rate
The rate at which you accumulate rewards per dollar spent. Usually expressed as: 3% cash back, 5x points per $1, or "2 Aeroplan miles per $1 on groceries." Higher earn rates in specific categories is the core value proposition of most rewards cards.
EMV
Europay, Mastercard, Visa — the international standard for chip payment technology. All Canadian credit and debit cards use EMV chips. See also: Chip-and-PIN.
Experian
A major US credit bureau. Not used in Canada — Canadian lenders use Equifax and TransUnion.

F

Flying Blue
Air France-KLM's loyalty program. Canadian relevance: Amex Membership Rewards transfers to Flying Blue 1:1. Flying Blue has good redemption options for European travel and occasionally runs "Promo Rewards" with discounted award prices.
Foreign Transaction Fee (FX Fee)
A surcharge (typically 2.5% in Canada) applied to purchases made in a foreign currency. On a $1,000 USD purchase, a 2.5% FX fee = $25 extra. Most Canadian cards charge this fee. Cards without FX fees include the Scotiabank Gold Amex, Rogers Red World Elite MC, and Home Trust Preferred Visa.
Example: Booking a $500 USD hotel on a standard card costs $500 USD × current exchange rate × 1.025 (2.5% FX fee). Using the Scotia Gold Amex saves you the $16+ FX surcharge.

G

Grace Period
The interest-free period between when you make a purchase and when interest starts accruing. In Canada, the typical grace period is 21 days after the statement date. If you pay your full balance by the payment due date, you never pay interest. Grace periods do not apply to cash advances (interest starts immediately).

H

Hard Inquiry (Hard Pull)
When a lender checks your credit report as part of a credit application (credit card, mortgage, car loan). Slightly lowers your credit score temporarily. Multiple hard inquiries in a short period can signal risk to lenders. Checking your own credit score is a "soft pull" and doesn't affect your score.
Household Income
Combined annual income of all adults in a household. Used as an alternative to personal income for World Elite Mastercard income requirements. Most World Elite MCs require $80,000 personal income OR $150,000 household income. Household income allows applicants with a non-working or lower-income spouse to qualify based on combined earnings.

I

Income Requirement
Minimum income level required to qualify for a credit card. Premium cards have higher requirements: Visa Infinite typically requires $60,000 personal / $100,000 household; World Elite Mastercard requires $80,000 personal / $150,000 household. No-fee and basic cards often have no minimum income requirement. Having sufficient income doesn't guarantee approval — lenders also assess credit score and debt load.
Interest Rate
See Annual Percentage Rate (APR). Standard Canadian credit card purchase rate is 19.99%. Some cards offer lower rates at the cost of fewer/no rewards (MBNA True Line: 8.99%).

L

Lounge Access
Complimentary or discounted entry to airport lounges. Provided via Priority Pass, DragonPass, or direct lounge partnerships. Lounges offer free food, drinks, Wi-Fi, showers, and a quieter environment than the main terminal. Premium cards (Amex Platinum, RBC Avion VI Privilege) offer unlimited access; mid-tier cards offer 6–8 visits/year; entry-level cards offer discounted access (~$32–45/visit).

M

MCC (Merchant Category Code)
A 4-digit code assigned to every merchant by Visa/Mastercard/Amex, indicating what type of business they are. Credit card rewards categories (e.g., "grocery," "gas") are defined by MCC codes. A store selling food but coded as "Warehouse Club" (Costco) may not earn at your card's grocery rate. Understanding MCCs helps you predict which earn rate applies at unusual merchants.
Example: Amazon charges at MCC 5999 (Miscellaneous General Merchandise), not a grocery or specialty code — so grocery-specific bonuses don't apply to Amazon purchases even if you're buying food.
Membership Rewards (Amex MR)
American Express's proprietary loyalty currency. Earned on Amex Cobalt, Gold Rewards, Platinum, and Business cards (not on SimplyCash). Key value: transfers to Aeroplan 1:1 with a 10% bonus (best transfer in Canada), British Airways Avios 1:1, Air France-KLM Flying Blue 1:1, Asia Miles 1:1, and Marriott 1:1.2. Statement credit redemptions (0.67¢/pt) are poor value — always transfer to partners for best use.
Minimum Payment
The smallest amount you must pay each billing cycle to avoid a late fee — typically the greater of $10 or 2–3% of your outstanding balance. Making only minimum payments is a trap: on a $3,000 balance at 19.99%, making minimum payments takes years to pay off and costs hundreds in interest. Always pay your full balance.

N

No-Fee Card
A credit card with a $0 annual fee. Examples: Tangerine World MC, BMO eclipse rise Visa, Rogers Red WE MC, PC Financial WE MC. No-fee cards are worth keeping indefinitely (closing them hurts your credit history length). Even a basic card earning 1% cash back should be kept open once you upgrade to a premium card.

O

Out-of-Province / Out-of-Country Medical Insurance
Emergency medical coverage provided by some credit cards for travel away from your home province or country. Not the same as provincial health coverage (OHIP, MSP, etc.). Good credit card travel medical covers hospitalization, emergency surgery, air ambulance, and repatriation. Coverage windows vary: 15 days (most basic) to 60 days (National Bank WE, Desjardins Odyssey). Limits range from $1M to $5M+.

P

PC Optimum
Loblaw Companies' loyalty program (merged with Shoppers Drug Mart Optimum in 2018). Points earned at Loblaws-banner stores, Shoppers Drug Mart, and via the PC Financial World Elite Mastercard. 10,000 PC Optimum points = $10 in grocery redemptions. Frequently offers bonus events (20X, 25X) that deliver excellent short-term earn rates.
Point Transfer
Moving loyalty points from one program to another. Key Canadian transfers: Amex MR → Aeroplan (1:1 + 10% bonus). RBC Avion → British Airways Avios (1:1). Scene+ does not transfer to airline programs. Transfer rates and bonuses change — always confirm current terms before transferring.
Priority Pass
The world's largest independent airport lounge access program with 1,400+ lounges. Available on premium Canadian cards (Amex Platinum offers unlimited visits; most others have limited visits). Requires registering on the Priority Pass app or website. Note: Mastercard cards typically use DragonPass (Mastercard Travel Pass), not Priority Pass.
Purchase Interest Rate
See Annual Percentage Rate. The rate applied to unpaid credit card balances on purchases. Standard 19.99% in Canada.

R

Redemption
Using accumulated rewards points or cash back. Common redemption types: statement credits, travel bookings, gift cards, merchandise, transfers to loyalty programs. Redemption value varies: travel portal redemptions are usually the best value for points programs; statement credits are often 25–50% less valuable per point than travel.
Rental Car Collision/Damage Waiver (CDW)
Insurance coverage for damage to or theft of a rental car, when the rental is charged to the credit card. Saves $15–25/day at the car rental counter. Most Canadian premium cards offer CDW for vehicles up to $65,000–$85,000 MSRP for 31–48 days. Important caveat: only covers the primary renter on most cards (National Bank WE MC uniquely covers additional drivers). Doesn't typically cover personal liability if you injure someone else.
Retail Price (MSRP) Cap for Rental CDW
The maximum vehicle value covered by your card's rental car insurance. Most mid-tier cards: $65,000 MSRP. Premium cards: $85,000 MSRP. Vehicles with higher MSRP are not covered — relevant if you're renting a luxury vehicle. Standard economy rentals (Toyota Corolla, Honda Civic) are always well under any cap.

S

Scene+
Scotiabank and Cineplex's joint loyalty program. Earned on Scotiabank credit cards and at Scene+ partners: Cineplex, Empire grocery stores (Sobeys, IGA, FreshCo, Safeway, Farm Boy), Harvey's, Swiss Chalet, and Scene+ Travel portal. 1 Scene+ point = 1¢. Best earning card: Scotia Gold Amex (6x at grocery/dining).
Secured Credit Card
A credit card backed by a cash deposit you make as collateral. Your credit limit equals your deposit ($500 deposit = $500 limit). Designed for people with no credit history or poor credit. Used to build or rebuild credit. Fees and interest rates are typically higher. Step up to an unsecured rewards card after 12–18 months of responsible use.
Statement Credit
A reward that reduces your credit card balance directly. Instead of receiving a cheque or gift card, the reward is applied as a negative charge on your statement. Common with cash back cards (Simplii, Tangerine) and some points program redemptions. Simpler than gift card redemptions but sometimes lower value per point than travel redemptions.
Statement Date
The date your billing cycle closes and your monthly statement is generated. Your balance on this date is what creditors typically report to the credit bureaus — important for credit utilization management. Pay your balance in full by the payment due date (usually 21 days after the statement date).
Supplementary Card
See Authorized User. A card issued to a secondary cardholder on the primary account. In Canada, often called a "supplementary" or "additional" card.

T

Trip Cancellation Insurance
Reimburses non-refundable travel expenses (flights, hotels, tours) if you must cancel your trip due to covered reasons: illness, injury, death of a family member, natural disaster. Typically covered reasons are spelled out in the certificate of insurance. Limits are per-person ($2,500–$5,000) and per-trip ($5,000–$10,000). Must purchase the travel with your credit card for coverage to apply.
Trip Interruption Insurance
Similar to trip cancellation, but covers expenses incurred when your trip is cut short mid-travel — return flights home, additional hotel nights, meals, etc. Usually carries higher per-person limits than trip cancellation.

U

Utilization Rate
See Credit Utilization. The ratio of current balance to credit limit, expressed as a percentage. Under 30% is good; under 10% is optimal for credit scoring purposes.

W

Welcome Bonus / Sign-up Bonus
Points, miles, or cash back awarded for meeting a minimum spend requirement in the first few months of card ownership. E.g., "Earn 60,000 Amex MR points when you spend $3,000 in the first 3 months." Welcome bonuses can be worth $300–$1,500+ in first-year value. Often the biggest single-year earning opportunity on any card.
Westjet Dollars
WestJet's loyalty currency. 1 WestJet Dollar = $1 CAD toward WestJet flights, vacation packages, and WestJet Vacations. Fixed value — simpler than points programs. Best earning cards: WestJet RBC World Elite MC (2% on WestJet/grocery/gas/transit, 1.5% everywhere else) and WestJet RBC Mastercard (1.5% on WestJet, 1% everywhere else).
World Elite Mastercard
The highest tier of Mastercard available in Canada, above Standard and World. Requires minimum income ($80,000 personal / $150,000 household). Comes with baseline World Elite benefits: Mastercard Airport Experiences (DragonPass), rental car insurance, zero liability. Examples: Desjardins Odyssey, National Bank World Elite, Rogers Red WE MC, Tangerine World MC, BMO eclipse Visa Infinite (Visa equivalent).

Z

Zero Liability
A guarantee from Visa, Mastercard, and Amex that cardholders are not responsible for unauthorized charges made to their card — provided they report the fraud promptly and haven't been negligent (e.g., sharing your PIN). Standard on virtually all Canadian credit cards. Report suspicious charges immediately by calling the number on the back of your card.

💡 Key Insight: The Three Numbers That Matter Most

Interest rate (don't pay it), earn rate (maximize it), and annual fee (ensure rewards exceed it). If you pay in full every month, the interest rate is irrelevant. Focus on maximizing your earn rate on your biggest spending categories while ensuring annual rewards clearly exceed the annual fee.
