Welcome Bonus

The standard welcome offer runs up to 20,000 Aeroplan points — typically 10,000 after first purchase + 10,000 after meeting a minimum spend threshold ($1,500–$3,000 in the first 3–6 months, depending on the current promotion).

At 1.5¢/point, 20,000 points = ~$300 in travel value. That's a 216% return on the $139 fee in year one alone (before earning on regular spend).

Watch for escalated offers. TD frequently runs promotions through Aeroplan.com, RFD, and card comparison sites that raise the bonus to 30,000–60,000 points. If you're not in a rush, waiting for an elevated offer can add $150–$600 in value.

Travel Insurance

The TD Aeroplan Visa Infinite comes with a solid travel insurance package for a card at this price:

  • Emergency travel medical: 21 days (up to age 64) — industry standard for this tier
  • Trip cancellation: Up to $1,500/person, $5,000/trip
  • Trip interruption: Up to $5,000/person
  • Flight/trip delay: After 4 hours, up to $500
  • Delayed/lost baggage: Up to $1,000
  • Car rental collision: Yes (waives CDW on rentals up to 48 days)
  • Common carrier accident: Up to $500,000

The 21-day medical window is the key limitation. If you travel for more than 21 consecutive days, you'll need to purchase additional top-up insurance. Seniors 65+ are not covered (standard for entry-level travel cards).

Perks and Benefits

  • Air Canada Café discount: 10% off food and non-alcoholic beverages at Air Canada Cafés in Canadian airports
  • Preferred boarding: Zone 2 priority boarding on Air Canada flights (does NOT include lounge access)
  • First checked bag free: One free checked bag for you and up to 8 companions on the same booking, on Air Canada flights. This is worth $30/bag — a family of 4 round-trip saves $240 in baggage fees, which alone covers the annual fee.
  • Complimentary Aeroplan status boost: Earn 1,000 Status Qualifying Miles (SQMs) per $10,000 spent, helping you reach Aeroplan 25K, 35K, or 50K status faster
  • Visa Infinite perks: Visa Infinite Dining Series, wine country experiences, hotel upgrades at select properties
The free checked bag perk is the sleeper value. At $30/bag × 2 people × 2 trips/year = $120 saved. That's 86% of the annual fee covered by one perk for a couple who flies Air Canada twice a year.

Final Verdict

Rating: 4.3/5. The TD Aeroplan Visa Infinite is a solid, reliable Aeroplan earner that delivers clear positive value for Air Canada flyers and gas/grocery spenders. Frequently Asked Questions

Does the TD Aeroplan Visa Infinite have lounge access?

No. The TD Aeroplan Visa Infinite does not include Priority Pass or Air Canada Maple Leaf Lounge access. For lounge access, you'd need to upgrade to the TD Aeroplan Visa Infinite Privilege ($599/yr), which includes 6 Maple Leaf Lounge passes and Priority Pass enrollment.

How much is 1 Aeroplan point worth?

Aeroplan points range from ~0.8¢ (merchandise/gift cards) to 7¢+ (Star Alliance business class sweet spots). For practical planning, use 1.5¢/pt for everyday economy flights — the typical value most cardholders achieve. Premium cabin redemptions on partner airlines can deliver 3–7¢ per point.

Can I share Aeroplan points with family?

Yes, through Aeroplan's Family Sharing feature. Up to 8 family members can pool points into a shared account. Note that Status Qualifying Miles (SQMs) cannot be shared — those remain individual. Pooled points can be used by any family member for award flights.

Is there a no-fee version of the TD Aeroplan card?

Yes — the TD Aeroplan Visa card has no annual fee and earns 1 Aeroplan point per $1.50 on all purchases (~0.67 pts/$1). It doesn't include the free checked bag benefit or travel insurance. It's worth getting to keep your Aeroplan earning during a fee-card's first-year-free promotion or while building credit.

How does the free checked bag work?

Your first checked bag (up to 23 kg) is waived for you and up to 8 travel companions on the same reservation, on Air Canada operated flights. You must charge the Air Canada ticket purchase to your TD Aeroplan Visa Infinite card. The benefit applies per trip segment — round-trip counts as 2 segments, so a couple saves $120 on a round trip.

Does the $139 annual fee get waived in the first year?

TD occasionally runs a first-year free promotion, typically during spring or fall campaigns. Check current offers on TD's website or through offer aggregator sites before applying. Even without the first-year waiver, the welcome bonus typically delivers 2–3× the annual fee in value.
