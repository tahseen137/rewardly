Best Credit Card for Subscriptions in Canada 2026

Netflix, Spotify, cell phone, internet, Amazon Prime — the right card can earn 2–4% back on the bills you pay every month without thinking about them.

Canadian households now spend an average of $450–$700/month on recurring subscriptions and services: streaming (Netflix, Disney+, Spotify, Apple TV+), cell phone plans ($80–$120/person), home internet ($75–$120), insurance premiums, gym memberships, and software subscriptions. Putting these on the right credit card is a passive way to earn $100–$300/year in rewards with zero extra effort.

How credit card networks classify subscriptions: Most streaming and digital subscriptions (Netflix, Spotify, Amazon Prime, Apple One) are classified as MCC 5968 (Direct Marketing – Continuity/Subscription Merchants) or MCC 5734 (Computer Software Stores). Cell phone bills are telecom (MCC 4813). Internet is typically utility (MCC 4899). This matters because some cards earn bonus rates only on "streaming" but not on telecom, or vice versa.

  • Netflix, Disney+, Spotify, Apple One, Amazon Prime, YouTube Premium

Key advantage: Scotia Momentum pays cashback anytime via the app (minimum $25 accumulated) — you can request your cash back whenever you'd like. Flexible, on-demand redemption rather than waiting for an annual payout.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does Netflix count as a "recurring bill" for credit card bonus categories?

Yes — Netflix is consistently classified as a recurring subscription on Scotiabank Momentum and Tangerine cards, earning 4% and 2% respectively. Similarly, Spotify, Disney+, Amazon Prime, and most streaming services qualify. Check your cardholder agreement or first statement to confirm each service codes correctly.

Does my cell phone plan earn bonus rewards?

On Scotiabank Momentum Visa Infinite, yes — your Rogers, Bell, Telus, Fido, Koodo, or any cell phone carrier bill earns 4% as a "recurring bill." On CIBC Dividend Visa Infinite, telecom bills also earn 2% (under the recurring bills category). Most other cards earn only the base rate (1%) on telecom.

Do annual subscriptions (like Amazon Prime annual) qualify?

Yes — annual subscriptions typically qualify as recurring bills/subscriptions even though they charge once per year rather than monthly. Amazon Prime, Microsoft 365 annual, and Apple One annual plans all qualify on Scotiabank Momentum and Tangerine.

What happens if Netflix raises its price — do I earn more?

Yes — percentage-based earn rates mean you earn proportionally more as subscription prices increase. If Netflix goes from $17 to $20/month, your 4% earn increases from $8.16 to $9.60 per year on that one subscription. Recurring bills are one of the few spend categories where you passively earn more as inflation hits.

Is the $120 Scotia Momentum fee worth it just for subscriptions?

Break-even is ~$250/month in recurring bills at 4% ($120 cashback = $120 fee). If your household pays $250+ in subscriptions + telecom + internet + insurance monthly, the card pays for itself before you earn a dollar on groceries or gas. Most Canadian families exceed this easily.
