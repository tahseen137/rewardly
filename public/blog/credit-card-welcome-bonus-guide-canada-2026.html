Credit Card Welcome Bonus Guide Canada 2026

The welcome bonus is often worth 5–10x the annual fee — it's the single biggest reward a card offers. Here's how to find the best current offers and make sure you actually earn them.

How Welcome Bonuses Work

Welcome bonuses (also called sign-up bonuses) are a one-time reward for new cardholders who meet a spending requirement within the first 3–6 months. The structure is typically: "Earn X points after spending $Y in your first Z months." Once you meet the spend, the points are credited to your account — sometimes instantly, sometimes on the next statement.

The golden rule: Never spend extra money to earn a bonus. The bonus is a reward for normal spending shifted to the new card — not a reason to buy things you don't need. Always meet the minimum spend with purchases you would have made anyway.

How to Hit the Minimum Spend Without Overspending

1. Pre-pay Regular Bills

Pre-pay insurance premiums, property taxes (if your municipality accepts credit cards), or annual subscriptions. Moving 3–6 months of regular expenses forward in time counts toward the minimum spend.

2. Time the Application Before a Large Planned Purchase

New appliance, furniture, home renovation, car repair? Apply for the card and time the purchase to fall within the first 3 months. The purchase you were going to make anyway hits the minimum spend.

3. Pay Rent via Credit Card (Via Chexy, Rentmoola, or similar)

Services like Chexy allow you to pay rent by credit card for a 1.75–2% processing fee. On a $1,500/month rent payment, $1,500 × 2% = $30 fee, but it counts toward minimum spend for a $500 bonus — strong ROI in year 1.

4. Float Your Expenses

Put everything on the new card for 3 months: groceries, gas, insurance auto-payments, phone bill, streaming. This alone should cover most $3,000 minimum spend requirements for a typical household without any manufactured spending.

5. Add an Authorized User

Some cards (check the terms) allow an authorized user's spending to count toward your minimum spend requirement. Adding a spouse or family member's purchases could double your monthly charge volume.

What NOT to do: Never buy prepaid Visa gift cards or load funds to accounts to manufacture spend — this violates most cards' terms of service and can result in the bonus being clawed back or your account being closed. Stick to genuine purchases.

Frequently Asked Questions

When is the best time to apply for a credit card in Canada?
Apply before a period of high natural spending: moving costs, home renovation, holiday shopping, back-to-school expenses. This makes it easy to hit minimum spend naturally. Some banks also run elevated welcome bonuses at specific times of year (though Canadian banks are less promotional than US banks). Check comparison sites for "highest ever" bonus tracking.
Can I get a welcome bonus multiple times on the same card?
Not on the same application — welcome bonuses are one-time per account. However, if you close the card and wait the issuer's required period (usually 24 months from account closure for Amex, varies by bank), you may be eligible again as a "new" cardholder. This is sometimes called "churning." Most Canadian banks are less aggressive than US banks about restricting this, but always check current terms.
What happens if I cancel a card before the welcome bonus is credited?
If you cancel before the bonus posts, you typically forfeit it. If you cancel shortly after the bonus posts, many issuers have "clawback" clauses allowing them to rescind the bonus points or require repayment of their cash value. In practice, claw-backs are uncommon, but holding the card for at least 6–12 months after the bonus posts is the safe approach.
