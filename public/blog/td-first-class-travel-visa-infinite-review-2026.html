The TD Rewards Points Math — Read This First

Before evaluating any TD Rewards card, you need to understand the redemption model. TD Rewards Points are redeemed at different rates depending on how you use them:

Redemption MethodValue per Point6 pts/$ grocery → effective rate

The headline earn rates (8×, 6×, 4×, 2×) are attractive, but the value-per-point is lower than Aeroplan or Amex MR. To get the best return, you must redeem through Expedia For TD or a similar TD travel booking tool.

Key insight: TD Rewards Points are best treated as a 3% grocery + dining travel rebate card, not a flexible points currency. If you're not booking travel, the earn rate is misleadingly high — the effective cash-back equivalent is lower than competing cards.

The grocery and dining rate of 6 pts/$ (~3% travel value) is competitive. Scotiabank Gold Amex offers 6 Scene+ pts/$ on the same categories, and Scene+ points transfer to Aeroplan at 1:1 — making the Scotia card arguably more flexible for the same earn rate.

Welcome Bonus

Typical offers include 50,000–100,000 TD Rewards Points on meeting minimum spend in the first few months. At 0.5¢ per point for Expedia For TD redemptions, 100,000 points = approximately $500 in travel. Watch for elevated welcome offers — TD frequently runs promos with first-year fee waivers.

Priority Pass Lounge Access

The TD First Class Travel Visa Infinite includes a Priority Pass membership with complimentary lounge visits. This is a meaningful differentiator from lower-tier cards — Priority Pass gives access to 1,400+ airport lounges worldwide.

Check the current card terms for the number of complimentary visits included per year, as TD periodically adjusts this benefit. Additional visits are typically charged at USD $32/visit through Priority Pass.

The Expedia For TD Lock-In

To get the best value on this card, you need to book travel through Expedia For TD. This is both a strength (integrated booking with point redemption) and a weakness (you're locked into Expedia's inventory and pricing).

Experienced travel hackers sometimes find better pricing directly with airlines or hotels, or through other booking platforms. With TD First Class, you give up that flexibility to maximize point value.

In practice, Expedia For TD has competitive pricing on most mainstream routes and accommodations. For most travelers, the portal lock-in is not a major inconvenience.

Who Should Get This Card?

Get it if: You bank primarily with TD, book travel frequently through Expedia, and want lounge access included. The Priority Pass is the card's strongest differentiator at this price point.

Skip it if: You want flexible points that transfer to multiple airlines. For that, the Scotiabank Gold Amex (Scene+ → Aeroplan 1:1) or Amex Gold (Amex MR → Aeroplan + more) give better flexibility for similar or lower annual fees.

Rewardly Verdict — 3.9 / 5

The TD First Class Travel Visa Infinite is a solid mid-tier travel card for TD loyalists. The earn rates look impressive on paper, but the TD Rewards redemption model requires Expedia For TD to deliver full value — a meaningful constraint. The Priority Pass lounge membership at this fee level is the card's clearest advantage. At $139/yr, it competes credibly but isn't the top choice if you value points flexibility.

Best for: TD banking customers, Expedia users, Priority Pass seekers without paying Amex Platinum prices.

Consider instead: Scotiabank Gold Amex ($120/yr, same earn rate, no FX, more flexible points) or CIBC Aeroplan Visa Infinite ($139/yr, Aeroplan, Star Alliance).
