Best Credit Cards for Gas Stations in Canada 2026

Earn up to 4% cashback on every fill-up — or stack a cashback card with gas loyalty programs to beat 4%. All stations ranked, no brand loyalty required.

Updated July 2026 · 7 min read

The average Canadian driver spends $1,500–$3,000+ per year on fuel. A card that earns 4% on gas versus a flat 2% cashback card could mean the difference of $45–$90+ per year in rewards on fuel alone. With some loyalty program stacking, active gas rewarders can earn the equivalent of 7–10¢ per litre back on every fill-up.

Gas station purchases in Canada almost universally code as MCC 5541 (Service Stations/Gas Stations) or MCC 5542 (Automated Fuel Dispensers) — two MCCs that several Canadian credit cards explicitly bonus. This makes gas one of the few categories where you can reliably predict your bonus earn rate.

Gas Station Loyalty Programs — Stack With Your Credit Card

Unlike grocery stores where you must choose between loyalty scan and credit card rewards, gas station loyalty programs and credit card rewards stack completely. Scan your loyalty card AND pay with your best credit card to earn both simultaneously.

Petro-Canada Petro-Points + RBC Petro-Canada Mastercard: The RBC Petro-Canada Mastercard is a co-branded card that earns triple Petro-Points at Petro-Canada stations AND 3¢/litre instant fuel savings. However, the non-gas earn rate is weak (1.25x Avion points). Most high-spend drivers earn more using a 3–4% cashback card (TD Cash Back VI at 3%, or CIBC Dividend at 4%) at any station, rather than the co-branded card's 3x points at Petro-Canada only.

The loyalty programs that stack best with a general rewards credit card:

  • Esso Extra (Imperial Oil): 1 point per litre at Esso and Mobil stations. Points redeemable for fuel discounts (400 points = 40¢/litre discount, effectively up to ~$7 off on a fill-up). Stack with any credit card.
  • Canadian Tire Money: Earned at Canadian Tire Gas+ stations. Standard rate is 4¢/litre in Canadian Tire Money, which can be spent at Canadian Tire stores. Stack with your credit card.
  • Circle K Fuel Rewards (Couche-Tard): Mobile app-based discount at Mac's / Circle K stations. Occasional 3-5¢/litre discount weeks. Stack with credit card at payment.
Best stack at Esso: Use Esso Extra app/card + CIBC Dividend Visa Infinite. You earn 4% cashback (CIBC Dividend) AND accumulate Esso Extra points simultaneously. On a 60-litre fill-up at $1.60/litre = $96 purchase: $3.84 cashback (4%) + 60 Esso points (~6¢ in fuel credits). Total: ~$4.44 back on a single fill-up.

Note on EV Charging at Gas Station MCCs

Some EV charging stations at Petro-Canada, Esso, and Shell locations code under the same MCC 5541 (gas stations) as the gas pumps. If your ChargePoint or FLO session codes as MCC 5541, your gas bonus card (CIBC Dividend, TD Cash Back VI) earns the full gas bonus rate on that charging session.
