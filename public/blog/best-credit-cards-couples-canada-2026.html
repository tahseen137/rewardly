Best Credit Cards for Couples Canada 2026

Two people, double the spend — the right strategies can earn you a free flight to Europe faster than either of you could alone.

The Core Strategies for Couples

Couples have a significant advantage over solo cardholders: combined household spending often qualifies for higher spend tiers, welcome bonuses can be earned twice, and programs like Aeroplan allow full point pooling through Family Sharing. The key is coordinating your card strategy rather than each person independently choosing cards.

Strategy 1: Welcome Bonus Stacking — The Fastest Win

Welcome bonuses are the highest-value play in Canadian rewards. Each partner qualifies independently for the same card's welcome bonus. If you're targeting Aeroplan, both of you can apply for the TD Aeroplan Visa Infinite (60,000 pts offer) in the same household — that's 120,000 Aeroplan points, enough for two roundtrips to Europe in economy. After the minimum spend period, pool the points via Aeroplan Family Sharing.

Strategy 2: Two-Card Combo Coverage

Instead of one partner holding two cards, split the coverage: Partner A holds a high-earn dining/grocery card (Cobalt 5x), Partner B holds a flat-rate travel card (Rogers Red 2-3%). For shared purchases, always use the higher-earning card. This eliminates annual fee overlap and maximizes earn rate across all spending categories.

Strategy 3: Supplementary Cardholder on High-Fee Cards

Many premium cards (TD Aeroplan VI, CIBC Aeroplan VI) allow supplementary cardholders at $0–$50/year extra. Instead of Partner B holding a separate card, they get a supplementary card on Partner A's account — all spend earns on one account, hitting category caps together and earning Aeroplan Elite status qualifying miles combined.

Aeroplan Family Sharing — The Points Pooling Playbook

Aeroplan Family Sharing allows up to 8 family members (must live at the same address OR be immediate family) to pool Aeroplan points into one account for redemptions. This is one of the most powerful tools for couples targeting business class flights.

Example: Partner A earns 65,000 Aeroplan points, Partner B earns 58,000. Through Family Sharing, they can combine to book one business class ticket to Europe (typically 75,000–90,000 pts) or two premium economy seats (45,000 pts each). Without pooling, neither could individually book the higher-tier redemption.

Supplementary Cardholder: When It Makes Sense

Adding your partner as a supplementary cardholder (instead of them holding their own card) pools ALL spend onto one account. This is best when you want to hit a single spend threshold together, qualify for Aeroplan status from a single account's ECCS, or when the secondary partner doesn't want to manage their own credit card. The downside: you only earn one welcome bonus, and the secondary cardholder doesn't build their own credit history. For most couples, having separate primary cards is better — both can earn welcome bonuses, and each builds their own credit file.
