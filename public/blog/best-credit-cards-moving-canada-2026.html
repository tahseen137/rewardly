Best Cards for Moving Expenses

#1 Rogers Red World Elite Mastercard — Best for Most Moving Costs

2% cashback everywhere, unlimited · No annual fee

Annual fee: $0 (or $29 monthly). The majority of moving costs — movers, truck, storage, furniture, home hardware — earn only base rate on most cards. Rogers Red's 2% flat outperforms all category cards on these vendors. On a $5,000 move (movers $2,500 + truck $500 + storage $500 + furniture/supplies $1,500), Rogers Red earns $100 cashback. A standard 1% card earns $50.

#2 TD Cash Back Visa Infinite — Best for Gas

3% on gas · 3% on grocery · 1% everywhere else

Annual fee: $139 (first year often free). Moving trucks are notorious gas burners. A 26-foot U-Haul gets 6–8 mpg and a Toronto-to-Ottawa move (450 km) can burn $150–$200 in gas. At 3% cashback vs. Rogers Red's 2%, you're earning $4.50 vs. $3 per $150 fill-up — marginal in isolation but real on a cross-province move. Better justification if you hold TD Cash Back already and it's your primary everyday card.

#3 Any New Card During Welcome Bonus Period — Biggest Win

All spend counts toward minimum spend threshold during welcome bonus period

Moving involves large, concentrated spend in a short window — often $3,000–$8,000 in 2–4 weeks. This is ideal for hitting a credit card welcome bonus minimum spend. If you apply for a TD Aeroplan Visa Infinite ($3,000 spend in 90 days = 60,000 Aeroplan points) and put your entire move through the new card, you'll hit the threshold in the first few weeks. The 60,000-point bonus alone is worth $600–$1,000 in travel — far exceeding any category-earn optimization on the same spend.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does rental truck insurance show up on my credit card?
Most credit cards with travel or rental car insurance don't cover moving trucks — they cover personal vehicle rentals. U-Haul, Penske, and Budget moving trucks are excluded from standard credit card collision coverage. You'll need to either purchase the rental company's damage waiver or rely on your home/tenant insurance policy if it covers moving. Check your card's specific certificate of insurance, not just the general marketing description.
Can I put my security deposit on a credit card?
Security deposits are usually paid by certified cheque or bank draft in Canada — most landlords do not accept credit cards for this. If your landlord does accept credit, the payment typically codes as a rent or property management transaction (1x on most cards, not a bonus category). Focus your credit card strategy on moving company, truck, storage, and furnishings rather than deposit transactions.
