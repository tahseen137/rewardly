Best Credit Cards for Home Renovation & Improvement in Canada 2026

Kitchen, bathroom, basement — the right credit card earns rewards on contractor invoices, Home Depot runs, and appliances, while protecting purchases with extended warranties and purchase protection.

How Home Renovation Spending Codes on Credit Cards

Unlike grocery or gas, home renovation spending doesn't have a single MCC. Purchases at Home Depot and Lowe's typically code as MCC 5251 (Hardware Stores) or 5211 (Lumber, Building Material Dealers). Appliance stores use MCC 5722. Contractors and trades often charge as MCC 1520 (General Building Contractors) or 7389 (Services). Most credit cards do not have a specific bonus for hardware or contractor MCCs — making your choice of card more about overall earn strategy and benefits.

Key insight: Since hardware stores rarely earn bonus points, the best card for renovation spending is either: (a) a card with the best base earn rate on everything, or (b) a card with exceptional purchase protection and extended warranty — which are highly valuable for appliances, tools, and contractor work.

The Most Important Benefit for Renovators: Purchase Protection

For renovation spending, purchase protection and extended warranty are arguably more valuable than the earn rate. Here's why:

🛡️

Purchase Protection (90–180 days)

Covers new purchases against theft, accidental damage, and loss for 90–180 days from purchase date. A $800 Milwaukee drill that breaks a month after purchase = covered. A $2,000 Samsung range damaged on delivery = covered. Amex cards: 90 days. Most Visa/MC premiums: 90 days. Some CIBC cards: 180 days.

⏱️

Extended Warranty (1–2 additional years)

Doubles the manufacturer warranty (up to 1–2 extra years) on eligible purchases made on the card. A $1,200 dishwasher with a 1-year warranty gets 2 years of coverage. Particularly valuable for appliances — refrigerators, washer/dryers, ovens, and dishwashers are renovation staples that break within 1–5 years. Extended warranty can save $200–$500 in repair costs.

File claims promptly: Purchase protection claims must typically be filed within 45–90 days of the incident. Keep your credit card statements and original receipts for all major appliance and tool purchases during your renovation.

Paying Contractors by Credit Card

Paying a contractor or trades person by credit card is possible and often advisable for large jobs — it adds purchase protection and creates a payment record. However:

Contractor credit card reality check:
• Many contractors add a 2–3% processing fee for credit card payments — which negates most reward earning
• Some contractors only accept cash, cheque, or e-Transfer
• For large amounts ($5,000–$50,000+), ask contractors upfront about credit card acceptance and fees
• If the processing fee is ≤ 1% and you earn 2% on the card, the net reward is still positive
• If the processing fee is 2.5%, there's no net benefit unless you have a 0% FX card (for contractors billing in USD)

Maximize contractor payments: Ask your contractor if they accept credit card payments with no surcharge (some larger renovation companies do, particularly for large projects). Use the card for materials directly purchased by you from retailers — no processing fee, and you earn rewards on every 2x4, tile, and fixture.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does Home Depot earn bonus points on credit cards?
Home Depot codes as MCC 5251 (Hardware Stores). Very few credit cards offer a bonus specifically on hardware stores. The Amex Cobalt's "Home & Living" category does include hardware/home improvement stores and earns 2x MR. Most other cards earn the base rate (0.5–1.5%) at Home Depot. The Triangle Mastercard (Home Depot co-branded) earns 1.5% in Canadian Tire money/Triangle Rewards at Home Depot — worth considering if you shop there heavily.
What's the best way to pay for a large renovation ($20,000–$100,000)?
For very large renovations, consider using your credit card for all materials and appliances (where there's no processing fee), then a combination of HELOC (home equity line of credit) or renovation loan for labour. Credit cards earn rewards and provide purchase protection on materials. For labour payments where contractors charge processing fees, HELOC at prime rate is typically cheaper than even a 0% promotional credit card offer (which may charge 3% upfront).
Is there a Canadian Tire credit card worth having for renovation spending?
The Triangle World Elite Mastercard earns 3–4% in Canadian Tire Money at Canadian Tire, Mark's, and Sport Chek — and 1.5% at Home Depot (since 2019). If you shop at Canadian Tire for tools, hardware, and supplies, the Triangle Mastercard is genuinely competitive for renovation spending. However, CT Money can only be redeemed at Canadian Tire/Mark's/Sport Chek, limiting redemption flexibility compared to Aeroplan or cash back.
