Best Credit Cards for Hotel Stays Canada 2026

Room upgrades, free nights, late checkout, elite status — the right hotel credit card turns a regular stay into a luxury experience. Here's what Canadian travellers need to know.

Two Types of Hotel Cards: Co-Brand vs. General Travel

Co-branded hotel cards (Marriott Bonvoy Amex, Hilton Honors Amex) earn points in a specific hotel loyalty program and often include automatic elite status. They're ideal if you're loyal to one hotel chain.

General travel cards (Amex Cobalt, Amex Platinum, Scotia Gold) earn transferable or flexible points that can be used for hotels across many chains, or applied to hotel bookings as statement credits. Better for travellers who stay at different hotel brands.

Should You Book Hotels Through the Card Portal or Directly?

This is the key strategic question for hotel rewards:

Book directly with the hotel chain if you want to earn hotel loyalty points (Bonvoy, Hilton Honors, IHG One Rewards). OTA bookings (Expedia, Hotels.com) typically do NOT earn hotel program points — you'd only earn credit card points. Book direct = earn both card points AND hotel loyalty points.

Book through Amex Fine Hotels + Resorts (for Amex Platinum) if you want complimentary upgrades, breakfast, and room credits — these benefits are only available through the FHR channel, not booking direct or through third parties.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do credit card hotel portal bookings earn hotel loyalty points?
Generally, no. When you book a hotel through a third-party portal (including credit card travel portals like RBC's or TD's), the hotel sees the booking as a wholesale/OTA reservation and typically does NOT credit hotel loyalty program points (Bonvoy, Hilton Honors, IHG, Hyatt). You'll earn your credit card's points or cash back, but not the hotel chain's points. Exceptions: Amex Fine Hotels + Resorts bookings do earn Marriott Bonvoy points if you have Marriott status attached to your Amex Platinum account. When in doubt, call the hotel directly before booking to confirm their policy.
What is the best way to use Marriott Bonvoy points?
The best Marriott Bonvoy redemptions in Canada are typically Category 4–6 properties (25,000–50,000 pts/night) where room rates are $200–$400+/night. A 50,000-point redemption at a $350/night Westin or Delta delivers ~0.7¢/point — significantly better than the base ~0.5¢/point for airline transfers. Peak/off-peak pricing means the same category property can cost 5–85% more or fewer points by date. Flexible dates are key to high-value Bonvoy redemptions.
