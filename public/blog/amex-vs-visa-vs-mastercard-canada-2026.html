Amex vs Visa vs Mastercard in Canada 2026

Which credit card network should you actually use? Acceptance, rewards, fees — compared.
Updated February 27, 2026 • 11 min read

The "best" credit card network depends on one thing: where you spend. Amex offers the best rewards — until you're standing at a small restaurant that doesn't accept it. Visa is accepted everywhere — but the rewards are often mediocre. Mastercard sits in the middle, solid on both fronts.

Here's the breakdown. No marketing fluff, just real acceptance data and reward comparisons from the Canadian market.

Acceptance: Where Can You Actually Use It?

Visa: The Universal Standard

Visa works everywhere. Gas stations in rural Alberta, corner stores in Nunavut, food trucks in Toronto. If a business accepts credit cards, they accept Visa.

Acceptance rate in Canada: ~99% (estimated based on Visa's merchant network data)

Mastercard: Nearly Universal

Mastercard's acceptance is almost identical to Visa. In 2026, most terminals that accept Visa also accept Mastercard. The gap that existed 10 years ago is gone.

Acceptance rate in Canada: ~98%

Amex: The Premium Outlier

Amex charges merchants higher fees (2.5-3.5% vs 1.5-2.5% for Visa/MC). Small businesses often reject it. You'll run into "sorry, we don't take Amex" at independent restaurants, cafes, and small retailers.

Acceptance rate in Canada: ~85% (lower outside major cities)

Where Amex works: Chains, major retailers, large restaurants, hotels, airlines, gas stations (Shell, Esso, Petro-Canada).

Where Amex doesn't work: Small independents, food trucks, farmers' markets, some Costco locations (Canada started accepting Amex in 2022, but not all warehouses are updated).

Fees: What Does Each Network Cost?

Annual Fees (Card-Specific, Not Network-Wide)

The network doesn't set annual fees — the issuer (RBC, TD, Scotiabank, etc.) does. But there's a trend:

  • Amex: Higher average annual fees ($120-$699 for premium cards)
  • Visa: Wide range ($0-$500)
  • Mastercard: Wide range ($0-$500)

Foreign Transaction Fees

Again, issuer-dependent. But the typical pattern:

  • Standard cards (all networks): 2.5% FX fee
  • Premium travel cards: No FX fee (Amex, Visa, MC all have no-FX options)

Merchant Fees (Why Some Places Don't Take Amex)

Merchants pay these fees, not you. But it affects where you can use your card:

  • Visa: ~1.5-2% per transaction
  • Mastercard: ~1.5-2.3% per transaction
  • Amex: ~2.5-3.5% per transaction

This is why small-margin businesses (restaurants, cafes) often reject Amex. They can't afford the extra 1% hit.

Perks & Benefits by Network

American Express

Strengths

  • Best rewards (earn rates + redemption value)
  • Premium travel perks (Priority Pass, hotel status, airline credits)
  • Strong purchase protection and extended warranty
  • Concierge services (Platinum and above)
  • Transfer partners (Aeroplan, Marriott, Hilton)

Weaknesses

  • Rejected by ~15% of Canadian merchants
  • Higher annual fees
  • Small businesses often don't accept
  • Not ideal for everyday small purchases

Visa

Strengths

  • Accepted everywhere (99%+ in Canada)
  • Wide range of cards (no-fee to ultra-premium)
  • Reliable for international travel
  • Visa Infinite perks (travel insurance, lounge access on select cards)

Weaknesses

  • Lower average rewards vs Amex/MC
  • Premium perks tied to specific cards, not the network itself
  • No Visa-wide transfer partners (card-specific programs only)

Mastercard

Strengths

  • Near-universal acceptance (~98%)
  • World Elite tier offers strong travel perks
  • Priceless Cities experiences (concerts, dining events)
  • Competitive rewards on premium cards

Weaknesses

  • Rewards slightly lower than Amex
  • World Elite perks require high-fee cards
  • Less brand prestige than Amex

The Optimal Strategy (What We Actually Recommend)

The 2-Card Setup

Card 1: Amex (Cobalt, Gold, or Platinum)
Use for groceries, dining, travel, and any merchant that accepts it. Maximize high-category earn rates.

Card 2: Visa or Mastercard (World Elite or Infinite)
Use when Amex isn't accepted. Your universal backup.

Why this works: You get Amex's superior rewards 85% of the time, and you're never stuck without a payment method.

Alternative: The 1-Card Setup (If You Hate Carrying Multiple Cards)

Best choice: Mastercard World Elite

Why? Near-universal acceptance + competitive rewards + premium perks. You'll earn slightly less than Amex, but you'll never be rejected.

Don't Do This:

Only carrying Amex. You will get stuck. Repeatedly. Especially outside major cities.

Which Network for International Travel?

Within North America: All three work fine. Amex acceptance is higher in the US than Canada.

Europe: Visa > Mastercard > Amex. Amex acceptance in Europe is spotty (under 70% in some countries).

Asia: Visa ≥ Mastercard >> Amex. Amex is rare outside major tourist areas.

South America / Africa: Visa only. Mastercard is hit-or-miss, Amex is non-existent.

Travel recommendation: Carry one Visa and one Mastercard. Leave Amex at home unless you're going to the US or staying at international hotel chains that accept it.

The Bottom Line

There's no "best" network. It depends on:

  • Where you spend: Major chains → Amex wins. Small businesses → Visa/MC required.
  • How much you travel: Frequent traveler → Amex for lounges and perks. Occasional traveler → Visa/MC is fine.
  • How much you optimize: Willing to carry 2-3 cards → Amex + Visa/MC. Want simplicity → Visa or Mastercard only.

Most Canadians should carry at least one Visa or Mastercard. Whether you add an Amex on top depends on if you value rewards enough to deal with occasional rejection.
