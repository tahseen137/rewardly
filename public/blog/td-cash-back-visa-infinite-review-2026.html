If you want straightforward cash back — dollars in your pocket, not points in a loyalty program — the TD Cash Back Visa Infinite is the card most Canadians should start with. Three percent on groceries and three percent on gas and recurring bills covers the two biggest everyday spending buckets for most families. Add recurring bills (streaming subscriptions, phone, internet) to that 3% pile and the annual savings add up fast.

TD is Canada's largest bank, and this card is its primary offer for everyday cash back earners. The first-year fee waiver essentially gives you a full year to see if the card pays for itself before you commit.

Earn Rates Explained

The 3% grocery rate applies to purchases at supermarkets, grocery stores, and most grocery chains. It does not apply to wholesale clubs like Costco (use the Capital One Costco Mastercard there) or department store food sections.

Gas earns 3% at service stations across Canada — not just one brand. The recurring bill category is where this card quietly shines: your monthly internet, Netflix, Spotify, phone plan, and utilities all earn 3% automatically, no activation required.

A household spending $700/mo on groceries, $150/mo on gas, and $200/mo on recurring bills earns approximately $408/yr from those categories alone — well more than the $120 annual fee.

📌 Tip: Set your phone plan, internet, and streaming services as auto-charge to this card. TD's system recognizes these as recurring bills automatically — no registration needed.

Cash Back Redemption

Cash back accumulates in your TD Cash Back Rewards account and can be redeemed at any time in increments of $25 via EasyWeb or the TD mobile app. You can apply it directly to your statement balance. There is no annual cap on how much cash back you can earn, and it does not expire as long as the account remains open.

This is meaningfully simpler than TD's Aeroplan or TD Rewards points cards, where redemption value fluctuates and requires planning around travel windows or transfer partners.

Travel Insurance Coverage

The TD Cash Back Visa Infinite includes a solid basic travel insurance package. Emergency medical covers up to $1,000,000 for the first 10 days of a trip. That 10-day window covers most leisure trips but falls short for extended travel — compare this to the TD First Class Travel card at 21 days or the National Bank World Elite at 60 days.

Who Should Get This Card

The TD Cash Back Visa Infinite is excellent for households who want simple, automatic returns from daily spending. The three grocery/gas/bills categories require no category activation, no annual enrollment, and no points math — your cash back just accumulates and you redeem when you want it.

It works particularly well for TD banking customers who want everything in one ecosystem, for families with predictable monthly grocery and gas bills, and for people who are skeptical of points programs and want real dollars back.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the $120 annual fee worth it?

At 3% cash back on groceries, gas, and recurring bills, a household spending $800/month in those categories earns $288/year from those categories alone — covering the fee more than twice over. If your combined spending in those three buckets is under $400/month, consider a no-fee card like Rogers Red or Tangerine instead.

Does the 3% grocery rate apply at Costco?

No. TD defines "grocery" as traditional supermarkets. Costco only accepts Mastercard and has its own co-branded Capital One card. Wholesale clubs typically do not qualify for the grocery bonus rate on any Visa card.

Can I have both the TD Cash Back card and a TD travel card?

Yes. Some TD customers pair this card for everyday cash back with a TD Aeroplan card for travel purchases where Aeroplan points are more valuable. TD allows you to hold multiple personal credit cards simultaneously.

What counts as a "recurring bill payment"?

Monthly subscriptions charged directly to your card (streaming services, phone plans, internet, insurance premiums, gym memberships) qualify. Utility payments via bill-pay through your bank do not — only direct-to-card charges from the merchant count.

How do I redeem cash back?

Log into TD EasyWeb or the TD app, navigate to your Cash Back Rewards summary, and choose to redeem. Minimum redemption is $25. It applies as a credit on your statement, effectively reducing what you owe.
