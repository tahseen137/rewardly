The BMO World Elite Mastercard is a heavy hitter in the Canadian travel card market — it offers airport lounge access (4 complimentary visits per year through Mastercard Travel Pass/DragonPass), 3× earning on travel and dining, and comprehensive travel insurance at a $150 annual fee. That's a compelling package compared to cards that charge $299+ for similar perks.

The 3× on travel, dining, and entertainment is one of the broadest triple-earn combinations available in Canada. Compare: the Amex Cobalt earns 5× on eating and drinking but only 2× on transit (not broad travel). The BMO World Elite catches everything from hotel bookings to Uber rides to concert tickets to Netflix.

The base 1 pt/$1 on everything else is competitive (effectively 1% return). For a card where travel + dining + entertainment cover most of your spend, you'll rarely need the base rate anyway.

BMO Rewards Points Value

BMO Rewards points are worth:

  • ~0.67¢/pt when redeemed for travel through BMO's travel portal (15,000 pts = $100)
  • ~0.33¢/pt for statement credits (15,000 pts = $50)
  • Less for merchandise and gift cards (avoid)

BMO Rewards is a fixed-value program — ~0.67¢/pt for travel is the ceiling. Unlike Aeroplan or Avion, there are no airline transfer partners where you can extract 2–5¢/pt from premium cabin sweet spots. This simplifies things (just book whatever flight you want and redeem against it) but caps your upside.

Lounge Access — The Standout Perk

The BMO World Elite includes 4 complimentary airport lounge visits per year through Mastercard Travel Pass powered by DragonPass. This is the most valuable perk for casual travelers — a lounge visit at a premium airport lounge typically costs $40–$65 CAD if purchased at the door.

4 free visits = $160–$260 in potential value per year — exceeding the entire $150 annual fee before factoring in your points earnings. If you fly round-trip twice per year and use a lounge each time, the card is effectively free.

After the 4 free visits, additional visits can be purchased at a discounted rate (~$32 USD) through DragonPass. Access covers 1,300+ airport lounges worldwide, including most major Canadian airports (Pearson, Vancouver, Montréal, Calgary).

Welcome Bonus

The typical welcome offer is 35,000 BMO Rewards points after spending $3,000 in the first 3 months. At 0.67¢/pt, that's ~$235 in travel value — more than 1.5× the annual fee in the first year alone.

BMO regularly runs escalated offers (up to 50,000 points) through comparison sites and during RRSP season. If you see 50,000 points, that's ~$335 in travel — a strong year-one return on the $150 fee.

Who Is This Card Best For?

  • Regular lounge users who fly 2–4 times per year and value relaxing before flights — 4 visits/year = the fee is covered before earning a single point
  • High dining and entertainment spenders — 3× on restaurants, concerts, Uber Eats, Cineplex, and streaming services accelerates earning fast
  • BMO banking customers who want integrated rewards
  • Simple travel redeemers who want to book any flight without managing airline partners

Final Verdict

Rating: 4.2/5. The BMO World Elite Mastercard delivers solid value at $150 through a combination of lounge access, broad 3× earning (~2% return at 0.67¢/pt), and solid travel insurance. The lounge access perk alone often covers the fee. Its main limitation is the fixed ~0.67¢/pt ceiling — advanced redeemers who want to extract premium value from airline transfers should look at Avion or Amex cards instead. For casual travelers who eat out frequently, see live events, and fly 2–4 times per year, this is a competitive all-round travel card at $150.

Frequently Asked Questions

What airports have lounges covered by BMO World Elite's DragonPass?

DragonPass covers 1,300+ lounges worldwide. In Canada, this includes major airport lounges at Toronto Pearson (YYZ), Vancouver (YVR), Montréal (YUL), Calgary (YYC), Ottawa (YOW), and Edmonton (YEG). Outside Canada, most major international airports are covered. Check the DragonPass app for the full list before your trip.

Does the BMO World Elite have a first-year free offer?

BMO regularly offers a first-year free promotion, especially in the January–March period and during back-to-school promotions in September. Check BMO's website or comparison sites for current offers. Even without a first-year waiver, the welcome bonus (~$235 in travel value) makes year one net-positive against the $150 fee.

How do I redeem BMO Rewards points for travel?

Log in to BMO Rewards online or through the BMO app, search for flights through the BMO Travel Portal, and pay with your points at ~0.67¢/pt. You can also book any travel directly and apply points as a statement credit against the charge — but this delivers only ~0.33¢/pt, so use the travel portal when possible. Points don't expire as long as your account is open.

Can I use BMO Rewards points for cashback?

Yes, but at a lower rate — approximately 0.67¢/pt (vs 1¢/pt for travel). If you know you'll want cashback eventually, a straight 1% cashback card would serve you better. Use BMO Rewards for travel to maximize their value.

What's the income requirement for BMO World Elite?

$80,000 personal income or $150,000 household income. This is higher than the TD Aeroplan ($60,000 personal) and RBC Avion ($60,000 personal) — which are both accessible at lower income thresholds. If you don't meet the World Elite income requirement, the regular BMO Rewards World Mastercard (no income requirement) earns at a lower rate with fewer perks.
