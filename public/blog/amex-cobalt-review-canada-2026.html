American Express Cobalt Card Review Canada 2026

5x on food and drinks, Aeroplan transfers, $155.88/year — but is it actually worth it?
Updated July 12, 2026 • 9 min read

The Amex Cobalt is one of the most talked-about credit cards in Canada, and for good reason. A 5x earn rate on dining and groceries is genuinely exceptional. But there's nuance: eligibility restrictions, a monthly billing model, and real limits on who gets maximum value from this card. Here's the full picture.

The Aeroplan transfer ratio is where this card shines. 5x points at dining × 2¢/point = effectively 10% back on restaurant and grocery spending when you redeem for Aeroplan flights. That's among the highest return rates of any Canadian credit card.

Annual Fee: The Real Cost

Fee Breakdown

$12.99/month — billed monthly, not annually. You can cancel any month without paying a lump sum. Total cost per year: $155.88 CAD.

The monthly billing model is intentional — it makes the Cobalt feel lower-commitment, and it means you're not locked in. That said, at $155.88/year it's not cheap. Here's how to justify it:

Who the Cobalt Is Actually Best For

Great fit if you:

  • Spend $300+ per month on dining, takeout, and groceries combined
  • Travel at least once per year and want to use points for flights
  • Are comfortable with the Amex acceptance gap (some independent stores don't take Amex)
  • Want to collect Aeroplan points without paying Aeroplan card fees separately
  • Like the flexibility of monthly billing

Not a great fit if you:

  • Shop primarily at Costco (warehouse stores don't earn 5x)
  • Never travel internationally — statement credit redemptions undervalue the points
  • Mainly want cash back (go with CIBC Dividend or Scotia Momentum instead)
  • Shop at merchants that don't accept Amex (check first)
  • Want a combined dining+grocery limit above $30,000/year at 5x

Transfer Partners: The Cobalt's Hidden Power

Amex Membership Rewards points transfer to these programs (all at 1:1 unless noted):

  • Aeroplan (1:1) — most valuable for Canadians flying Air Canada
  • Marriott Bonvoy (1:1.2 — 1,000 MR = 1,200 Bonvoy)
  • Hilton Honors (1:2)
  • British Airways Avios (1:1) — useful for short-haul flights
  • Singapore Airlines KrisFlyer (1:1)
  • Cathay Pacific (1:1)
  • Etihad Guest (1:1)

The Aeroplan 1:1 transfer is the headline. Aeroplan miles can be worth 2–3 cents each when redeemed for business class flights. Earning 5x Cobalt points on a $1,000 restaurant spend = 5,000 MR = 5,000 Aeroplan miles — potentially worth $100–$150 toward a business class ticket.

Cobalt Benefits Beyond Points

  • Travel emergency medical — out-of-province and international coverage up to $5 million (90 days)
  • $100 annual travel credit in the form of a monthly $12.99 bonus — essentially covers 1 month of fees
  • Purchase protection — 90-day coverage against theft and damage
  • Extended warranty — doubles manufacturer warranty up to 1 extra year
  • Amex Offers — targeted statement credits on spending at participating merchants
  • Front of the Line presale access — Amex presale tickets for concerts and events

The $30,000 Cap on 5x Earnings

This is important and often buried in the fine print. You earn 5x points on only the first $30,000 spent annually in eligible food and dining categories. After that, you drop to 1x. For most people this cap is irrelevant — you'd need to spend $2,500/month on dining and groceries to hit it. But heavy restaurant spenders and people who put catering bills on this card should track it.

The Rewardly Verdict

The Amex Cobalt is one of the best all-around travel rewards cards in Canada, full stop. The 5x rate on dining and groceries combined with Aeroplan transfer access is hard to beat at this price point.

It's not perfect — the Amex acceptance gap is real and the 5x category has limitations. But if you spend $300+ monthly on food and want to earn toward flights, there's no better card in Canada at under $200/year.

We rate it 4.6/5 for dining-focused travelers. For pure cash back, look at the CIBC Dividend instead.

How to Get Maximum Value from the Cobalt

  1. Know your 5x merchants. Add your most-visited grocery stores and restaurants to Rewardly. If they code as dining or grocery, you're getting 5x. If they code as something else, use a different card.
  2. Never redeem as statement credit. Transfer to Aeroplan or Marriott. The difference between 0.7¢ and 2¢/point is a 3x improvement in value.
  3. Stack with a no-fee backup card. Use the Cobalt for food and a flat 1.5% cashback card for everything else. Most Cobalt users pair it with the Rogers Red Mastercard for non-Amex purchases.
  4. Use Amex Offers. Log into your Amex account monthly and activate offers. These are often 10-15% statement credits at specific merchants and can easily cover a month of fees.
  5. Track your $30K cap. If you're hitting the limit, consider redirecting food spend to a secondary card for the remainder of the year.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does Amex Cobalt work at Costco?

No. Costco Canada is Mastercard-only and doesn't accept American Express at all. For Costco purchases, use a Mastercard that earns rewards — the Rogers Red Mastercard (1.5% everywhere) or MBNA True Line Mastercard are common choices.

Can I transfer Cobalt points to Aeroplan right away?

Yes. Amex Membership Rewards transfers to Aeroplan are available immediately at any time. There's no minimum transfer amount, though transfers are typically in increments of 1,000 points. Transfers usually post to your Aeroplan account within a few hours.

Is the Cobalt's $30,000 cap per card or per household?

Per card account. If you add a supplementary cardholder, their spending on food/dining also counts toward the same $30,000 cap.

Does the Cobalt have a welcome bonus?

Yes — Amex periodically runs welcome bonuses on the Cobalt, often 2,500 bonus points per month in your first 12 months (for a total potential bonus of 30,000 points). Promotions change, so check the current offer when applying.

What's the income requirement for the Amex Cobalt?

There's no minimum income requirement stated for the Cobalt. However, Amex will consider your credit score, existing debt, and overall creditworthiness. Good to excellent credit (700+) is typically needed for approval.

How does the Cobalt compare to the Scotiabank Gold Amex?

The Scotiabank Gold earns 6x on food (vs. 5x Cobalt) and costs $120/year (vs. $155.88 Cobalt). However, Scotia Scene+ points have fewer transfer partners and lower average value than Amex MR. If you want Aeroplan transfers, the Cobalt is better. If you prefer Scene+ cash-equivalent credits, the Gold may win for you.
