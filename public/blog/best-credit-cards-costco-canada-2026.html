Best Credit Cards for Costco Canada 2026

Costco Canada only accepts Mastercard. Here's which Mastercard earns you the most on your Costco grocery, electronics, and gas purchases.

Why Costco Only Takes Mastercard

Costco Canada has an exclusive payment partnership with Mastercard — they accept Mastercard, debit cards (Interac), and cash, but not Visa or American Express. This has been Costco Canada's policy since 2014 when they switched from Amex. If you want to earn credit card rewards on Costco purchases, you must hold a Mastercard.

Good news: Several no-fee and low-fee Mastercards earn strong rewards on all purchases (including Costco), and some travel Mastercards have Costco as a partner earn location. The right card can earn you 1.5–4.5% back on a $6,000/year Costco budget.

Top Mastercards for Costco Canada Shopping

#1PC Financial World Elite Mastercard

Annual Fee: $0 · Income: $80K individual or $150K HH

Earn at Costco: 10 pts/$1 (= 1% in PC Optimum value)
Note: Costco does not accept the PC Optimum loyalty card scan at the register (unlike Loblaws stores), so you cannot stack loyalty points. At Costco you earn only the card's base rate: 10 pts/$1 × 0.1¢/pt = 1% back. On a $6,000/year Costco budget: ~$60/year in PC Optimum value — redeemable at Loblaws, Real Canadian Superstore, and Shoppers Drug Mart. No annual fee means this is still worthwhile.

The card's headline rates (30 pts/$1 = 3% at Loblaws-banner; 45 pts/$1 = 4.5% at Shoppers) apply only at PC-banner stores, not Costco. The card ranks #1 here mainly because Costco only accepts Mastercard, and this is the strongest no-fee Mastercard available.

#2Rogers Red World Elite Mastercard

Annual Fee: $0 (with $25+ Rogers/Fido/Shaw monthly spend) · Income: $80K individual or $150K HH

Earn at Costco: 1.5% cash back (non-Rogers customers) or 2% cash back (Rogers/Fido/Shaw customers)
Rogers Red WE MC earns a flat 1.5% on all purchases for most cardholders, or 2% if you're an active Rogers, Fido, or Shaw subscriber with $25+/month spend. At Costco this is the same or better than PC Financial WE MC in flexible cash you can use anywhere — not locked to PC Optimum stores. The 3% earn rate applies only to Rogers-brand purchases (Rogers services/products), not general retailers like Costco.

#4WestJet RBC World Elite Mastercard

Annual Fee: $119

Earn at Costco: 1.5% WestJet Dollars on all purchases
Same 1.5% earn on Costco as the free cards above — but you pay $119/year. The annual companion voucher saves that fee if you fly WestJet, but purely for Costco rewards, the free Rogers or PC Financial card is a better deal. Best choice for Costco shoppers who already want the WestJet companion voucher benefit for travel.

#5BMO eclipse Visa Infinite

Annual Fee: $120

Earn at Costco: 1× (~0.67%) on all eligible purchases (Costco falls under "everything else" category)
The BMO eclipse Visa Infinite earns 5× BMO Rewards on dining, food delivery, streaming, and groceries — but Costco is typically coded as "Warehouse Clubs" (MCC 5300) which does NOT qualify as "grocery" on most Canadian cards. So you earn only 1× (~0.67%) at Costco. Avoid using BMO eclipse at Costco specifically; the flat-rate PC Financial or Rogers cards are better here.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does Costco Canada accept Visa?
No. Costco Canada does not accept Visa credit cards or Visa debit cards (with the exception of Interac Debit, which operates on a different network). Costco accepts Mastercard credit cards, Interac debit, and cash only. This policy has been in place since 2014 when Costco Canada's partnership with American Express ended and they switched to Mastercard as their exclusive credit card network.
Can I use the Cobalt or Scotiabank Gold Amex at Costco?
No — American Express cards are not accepted at Costco Canada. This means the top-earning grocery/dining cards in Canada (Amex Cobalt at 5x, Scotia Gold Amex at 5x Scene+) cannot be used at Costco at all. This is a significant limitation for Canadian rewards maximizers who shop at Costco regularly — you must hold a separate Mastercard as your Costco card.
Does Costco count as a grocery store for credit card bonus categories?
No. Costco Canada is coded as MCC 5300 (Wholesale Clubs) in the payment network, not as a grocery store (MCC 5411). As a result, credit cards that offer bonus rates at grocery stores (e.g., 5x on Amex Cobalt, 4% on CIBC Dividend Visa Infinite, etc.) do NOT provide those bonus rates at Costco. You earn the card's standard "everything else" rate on Costco purchases, making flat-rate Mastercards the practical choice for maximizing Costco rewards.
