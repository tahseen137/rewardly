What is a "Credit Card Tracker" — and Why Does It Matter?

The term "credit card tracker" gets used loosely. In practice, people searching for a tracker in Canada want one of two things:

  1. Optimization tools — Apps that help you use your existing cards better. They tell you which card earns the most rewards for each spending category. Examples: Rewardly, MaxRewards.
  2. Reward-first financial products — Cards or accounts designed to earn high cashback from day one. Examples: KOHO, Neo Financial.

These are fundamentally different tools solving different problems. An optimization tool can turn your existing stack of 3-4 cards into a finely tuned earning machine. A reward-first product gives you one excellent card to add to (or replace) your current stack.

Most Canadians need both: first optimize your current cards, then strategically add one or two specialist cards for high-value categories.

Here's the honest breakdown of each option.

Is MaxRewards available in Canada?

Partially. MaxRewards is primarily a US product. It supports Amex Canada cards and can auto-activate Amex Canada offers. However, the major Canadian banks (TD, RBC, CIBC, Scotiabank, BMO) are not supported. For most Canadians — who typically carry at least one Big Five bank card — MaxRewards is missing a significant portion of their wallet.

Is KOHO better than a traditional credit card?

It depends on your situation. If you can't get approved for a traditional credit card, have issues with overspending, or want a simple all-in-one spending + savings account, KOHO is excellent. If you have a good credit score and want maximum rewards, traditional credit cards (optimized via Rewardly) will generally outperform KOHO's 0.5-2% rates.

The average Canadian with 3+ credit cards and moderate spending ($3,000-5,000/month) typically leaves $400-800/year on the table by not optimizing which card they use where. The most common mistake: using a 1% base-rate card for groceries when you have a 5-6× card you're not using there.

No. Rewardly never asks for your banking credentials. You simply select which Canadian credit cards you own from a database of 410+ cards, and the app does the optimization based on the cards' known earning rates. No account connection required.
