The Canada Problem with CardPointers

Feature-by-Feature Breakdown

Card Recommendation Engine

Both apps answer: "Which card should I use for this purchase?"

CardPointers uses GPS location, merchant category codes, and real-time offer data to recommend which card earns the most at your current location. It's sophisticated — it knows that your Chase Sapphire earns 3x at restaurants and triggers a Sapphire recommendation when you walk into a restaurant.

Rewardly does this for Canadian cards. When you're at Loblaws, it knows your Scotiabank Gold earns 5x on groceries. At a gas station, it knows your TD Cash Back earns 3% at Canadian gas stations. The recommendation logic is tuned for Canadian merchant categories, Canadian earning multipliers, and Canadian card terms — not approximate mappings from American card logic.

Winner for Canadians: Rewardly — Canadian cards, Canadian categories, accurate earning rates.

AutoPilot / Real-Time Location Tracking

CardPointers' AutoPilot is genuinely impressive: it uses on-device location processing (privacy-focused) to push recommendations to your Apple Watch, lock screen widget, or CarPlay before you even need to think about which card to use. There's an AR camera feature that overlays card recommendations when you point your phone at a store.

Rewardly is category-based: you input your spending category or merchant, and it shows you the optimal card. No AR camera or Watch integration. For Canadians with TD, RBC, and CIBC cards, this category approach is actually more accurate — because the location-based approach requires accurate Canadian merchant data that CardPointers may not have.

Winner: CardPointers — for the technological execution. But only if it has your Canadian cards loaded, which is the unresolved question.

Offer Tracking and Auto-Activation
