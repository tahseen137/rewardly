Best Credit Cards for Clothing & Fashion in Canada 2026

Clothes don't have a bonus category on most cards — but where and how you shop changes everything about what you earn.

The Clothing Category Problem

Unlike groceries or dining, clothing and apparel do not have a dedicated bonus category on any major Canadian credit card. There is no card that earns 3x or 5x specifically for buying clothes. This means clothing purchases are treated as general merchandise or base-rate spend by almost all issuers.

However, where you buy your clothes matters for stacking opportunities. Department store purchases at Hudson's Bay or Simons can be stacked with store loyalty programs. Online fashion purchases code as clothing/apparel or general retail — no Canadian credit card has a dedicated "fashion" or general "online shopping" bonus category. Rogers Red's 2% flat rate is the best available for both in-store and online clothing purchases.

Key insight: For clothing purchases, the best strategy is not finding a "clothing card" — it's understanding whether you're buying in-store or online, and whether the store has its own loyalty program to stack with your credit card rewards.

Online Fashion: Where Your Card Actually Matters Most

BEST FOR ALL FASHION (ONLINE & IN-STORE)

Rogers Red World Elite Mastercard

No Canadian credit card earns bonus points specifically on clothing or fashion purchases — BMO Eclipse VI's 5x applies only to dining, grocery, gas, and transit, not general retail or online apparel. Rogers Red's 2% flat cashback on all purchases is therefore the top choice for both online and in-store fashion. Whether you're buying at Zara in the Eaton Centre or ordering from Aritzia.com, you earn the same flat 2% — no category tracking required.

Rogers/Fido/Shaw customers earn 2% on all CAD purchases. Non-Rogers customers earn 1.5%. Annual fee: $0 (income requirement applies).

Earn on all fashion (in-store and online): 2% flat  |  Annual fee: $0

BEST FOR AMEX POINTS COLLECTORS

American Express SimplyCash Preferred

Amex SimplyCash Preferred earns 2% cashback on all purchases wherever Amex is accepted. For in-store fashion at major Canadian department stores and clothing chains that accept Amex (most do, including Hudson's Bay, Simons, Nordstrom), this earns 2% — same as Rogers Red, but generates cashback as an Amex statement credit rather than Rogers bill credit.

Annual fee: $99/yr. At $99 fee, you break even versus a no-fee 1% card at about $10,000 in annual spend across all purchases. If you're spending $5,000+/year on clothing specifically, SimplyCash Preferred makes financial sense if you can't get Rogers Red (World Elite income requirement).

Earn on all fashion: 2% flat  |  Annual fee: $99/yr

The Hudson's Bay Exception: Stack Store + Credit Card

Hudson's Bay is worth special consideration because it has its own HBC Rewards loyalty program. Hudson's Bay Rewards (HBC) earns points on every purchase at Bay stores, which stack with your credit card rewards. You can earn HBC points AND credit card cashback simultaneously.

The former HBC credit card earned significant points but was discontinued. The HBC Mastercard issued by Capital One Canada earns HBC Rewards at a base rate, but for most Canadians the optimal approach is: use Rogers Red (2% cashback) as your payment card at Hudson's Bay, while also being enrolled in the HBC Rewards program (free, via their app). You earn both simultaneously — the store loyalty reward and the credit card cashback.

Stack example at Hudson's Bay: $200 Hudson's Bay purchase. HBC Rewards: ~200 points (≈$2 in HBC vouchers). Rogers Red cashback: 2% = $4. Total effective return: $6 on $200 = 3% without a store-specific credit card. Stacking is always better than either alone.

Purchase Protection: Why Clothing Is a Good Use Case

Many premium Canadian credit cards include purchase protection insurance that covers new purchases against damage or theft for 90–180 days. For clothing purchases, this means a coat damaged within 90 days of purchase (accidental damage) may be covered by your credit card insurance — not just your home insurance.
