How to Maximize Referral Income: 5 Strategies

1. Focus on Amex Cobalt Referrals

The Amex Cobalt is Canada's best everyday rewards card and an easy sell: 5x points on dining and groceries, no foreign transaction fees on the premium version, and a card that genuinely looks good. Its combination of strong welcome offer + ongoing earn rates means friends will thank you. Each referral: ~10,000‚Äì15,000 MR points.

2. Time Referrals Around Welcome Offer Elevations

Banks periodically increase welcome offers on their top cards (Amex regularly runs 80,000‚Äì100,000 point offers on the Platinum). When elevated offers are live, your referral becomes significantly more compelling ‚Äî your friend gets more, and your referral bonus may also increase. Track offer elevations at Canadian rewards blogs.

3. Segment Your Audience

Different cards appeal to different people. Frequent flyers want Aeroplan cards. Grocery spenders want Cobalt or PC Mastercard. Occasional travelers want no-fee cash back. Knowing your friend's spending profile before recommending means higher conversion and better outcomes for everyone.

4. Stack Referral Bonuses with Your Regular Earn

Referral points land in your rewards account the same as earned points. They can be combined with sign-up bonuses on new cards you're applying for, or used during transfer bonus windows (e.g., Amex periodically offers 30‚Äì40% transfer bonuses to airline programs). Never let them sit idle.

5. Refer to Multiple Cards

You can hold and refer from multiple cards simultaneously. If you carry both an Amex Cobalt and a TD Aeroplan Infinite, you have two separate referral programs running. Match the right card to the right friend rather than always defaulting to one link.

‚ö†Ô∏è Important: Only refer people who genuinely want and can responsibly use a credit card. Referring someone to a card they don't need or can't manage isn't a good play ‚Äî for them or for your relationship. The best referrals are ones where you're solving a real problem for someone.

Referral Bonuses vs. Welcome Bonuses: What's Better?

This is a common question: should your friend use your referral link or apply directly through a public elevated offer?

The answer depends on the specific offer at the time:

  • If the public welcome offer is higher than what's offered through referral links, they should apply directly ‚Äî and you can still refer someone else later.
  • If welcome offers are equivalent, use the referral link ‚Äî you both win.
  • Some elevated offers are only available through referral links (Amex has done this). Always check both routes before applying.

Bottom line: compare the total value on both sides before deciding. Referral links aren't automatically the best path for your friend, even if they're great for you.
