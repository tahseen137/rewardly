Best Credit Cards for Gas in Canada 2026

Canadians spend $2,000–$5,000/year at the pump. The right credit card earns 2–5% back on every fill-up. Here's what earns the most.

Why Your Gas Card Matters

At $1.50–$1.80/L for gasoline in major Canadian cities, a household driving 20,000km/year spends roughly $2,500–$3,500 on gas annually. Earning even 2% back = $50–70/year in free money. Earning 4% = $100–140/year. The right card pays for itself on gas alone.

The cards below are ranked by their gas station earn rate. Some are co-branded (only useful at specific chains); others earn at any gas station in Canada.

Key distinction: Most high-earn gas cards are either (a) gas station co-brands with great rates only at one chain (Petro-Points, Esso Extra), or (b) general-purpose cards with elevated gas categories everywhere. If you fill up at multiple chains, opt for the general category cards.

Top Gas Credit Cards in Canada (2026)

#1

Rogers Red World Elite Mastercard

No Annual Fee · Cash Back
Best No-Fee
1.5%on all gas (any chain, CAD) + 3% on foreign purchases

The Rogers Red World Elite earns 1.5% on all Canadian purchases including gas (no chain restriction), which isn't exceptional on its own. However, it pays 3% on all foreign currency purchases — so if you fill up at a US gas station (cross-border shopping trips), you earn 3%. For pure domestic gas spending, better options exist below. Rogers Red wins as the best overall no-fee card for Canadians who also spend in USD/EUR.

Best for: Commuters who make regular cross-border trips to the US, OR those who want a single no-fee card that also handles foreign spend well.
#2

Scotiabank Momentum Visa Infinite

$120/yr · Cash Back
4% Gas + Grocery
2%on gas (4% on groceries, streaming, recurring bills)

The Scotia Momentum Visa Infinite+ earns 4% on groceries, streaming, and recurring bills — but only 2% on gas. For families whose primary spend is grocery + bills rather than pure gas, this card earns more overall than a dedicated gas card. The $25,000/year cap applies to the 4% tier (grocery + streaming + recurring bills combined) — gas earns 2% separately and does not count toward that cap. Annual fee of $120 is waived in year 1.

Best for: Drivers who also do significant grocery shopping and want to maximize both categories with one card. For drivers focused on gas, CIBC Dividend VI earns 4% on both gas and groceries — a stronger choice for high-volume drivers.
#3

PC Financial World Elite Mastercard

No Annual Fee · PC Optimum Points
Best at Esso/Mobil
30 ptsper $10 (3%) anywhere + 45 pts/$10 at PC-banner gas (4.5%)

PC Optimum is one of Canada's most valuable loyalty programs for combined grocery + gas. The PC Financial World Elite earns 30 PC Optimum points per $10 everywhere (=3%), and 45 points per $10 at Esso and Mobil stations (=4.5%). Points are worth 1¢ per 10 points, redeemable only at Loblaws-banner stores and Shoppers Drug Mart. No annual fee, $80K household income required. If you fill up at Esso regularly and shop at Loblaws, this combination is unbeatable.

Best for: Esso/Mobil loyalists who also shop at Loblaws, Real Canadian Superstore, No Frills, or Shoppers Drug Mart.
#4

BMO eclipse rise Visa Card

No Annual Fee · BMO Rewards
5x Any Gas Station
5x ptsat any gas station (~1.7% toward travel)

The eclipse rise earns 5 BMO Rewards points per $2 spent at all gas stations — any chain. At ~0.67¢ per point for travel redemptions, that's approximately 1.7% effective return on gas. At $0 annual fee with no income requirement, this is the best no-fee travel rewards card for gas across all chains. The limitation: points are most valuable for travel; straight cash back seekers get better value from Tangerine (below).

Best for: No-fee card holders who fill up at various chains and want travel rewards on gas spend.
#5

Tangerine World Mastercard

No Annual Fee · Cash Back
2% Any Gas Station
2%cash back on gas (if selected as one of your 3–4 categories)

Gas is one of the 10 available Tangerine 2% categories. If you select gas as one of your 3 categories, you earn 2% cash back at any gas station in Canada. Pure, simple cash — no points program needed. Pair with Tangerine's Recurring Bills and Groceries selections for a comprehensive everyday card. Best for drivers who want guaranteed, simple cash back without program management.

Best for: Simple cash-back-focused drivers who want 2% at any station with no annual fee and no program complexity.
#6

Triangle Mastercard (Canadian Tire)

No Annual Fee · CT Money
Best at Gas+
1.5%at Gas+ / Triangle World Elite: 7¢/L at Gas+

The Triangle Mastercard earns 1.5% CT Money at all gas stations, with the enhanced Triangle World Elite MC offering 7¢/litre savings at Canadian Tire Gas+ stations — effectively 5–7% savings depending on gas price. The base Triangle MC earns 1.5% everywhere. If you have a Canadian Tire Gas+ near your regular route, the World Elite version is a compelling fuel card — you must spend at least $80K household income to qualify. CT Money is only spendable at Canadian Tire-banner stores.

Best for: Canadian Tire regulars with a Gas+ station near home or work. Triangle World Elite's 7¢/L at Gas+ is among the highest fuel savings available.

CIBC Dividend Visa Infinite: Honourable Mention

The CIBC Dividend Visa Infinite ($120/yr, waived year 1) earns 4% cash back on grocery AND gas combined — matching the Scotia Momentum VI's structure. The two cards are near-identical in gas/grocery earn rates; the choice between them often comes down to which bank you already deal with, welcome bonus, and ancillary benefits (both have solid travel insurance).

CIBC Dividend VI wins on: gas rewards (4% vs Scotia's 2%), Costco acceptance. Scotia Momentum VI wins on: recurring bills at 4%, streaming at 4%, transit at 2%. For pure gas optimization, CIBC Dividend is the stronger choice.

Gas Station Loyalty Programs: Stack Your Earnings

Most Canadian gas chains have their own loyalty programs that can be stacked with your credit card rewards:

  • Petro-Canada Petro-Points: Earn at Petro-Canada stations. Use the Petro-Canada app + any credit card for points + fuel savings. Linkable with Air Miles.
  • Esso Extra / PC Optimum at Esso: Earn PC Optimum points at Esso stations — stackable with the PC Financial World Elite for the highest combined Esso return.
  • Canadian Tire Gas+: CT Money from Triangle MC stacks with Gas+ prices (which are often already competitive).
  • Shell Go+ Rewards: Earn at Shell stations — stackable with any credit card.

The best gas stack: PC Financial World Elite MC at Esso + Esso Extra PC Optimum in-app = 4.5% credit card points + loyalty points on the same purchase.
