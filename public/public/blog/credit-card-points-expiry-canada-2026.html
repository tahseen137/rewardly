Do Credit Card Points Expire in Canada?

Aeroplan, Amex MR, Scene+, PC Optimum, Air Miles — complete expiry policy for every major Canadian rewards program.

Updated July 2026 · 8 min read

Millions of Canadians lose points and miles to expiry every year — not because they stopped earning, but because they didn't understand the rules. An inactive account, a cancelled credit card, or an overlooked program rule can erase years of accumulated rewards in an instant.

This guide covers the complete expiry policy for every major Canadian loyalty program — with specific rules, loopholes, and strategies for keeping your points alive indefinitely.

Aeroplan (Air Canada)

✈️
Aeroplan
Air Canada's loyalty program
⚠️ Conditional — expires after 18 months of inactivity
Expiry rule: Aeroplan points expire after 18 consecutive months of account inactivity. "Activity" is defined as any earning or redemption transaction in the account — a single purchase on an Aeroplan co-branded card (TD, CIBC, Amex) resets the clock.
How to keep points alive: The easiest method is to hold a co-branded Aeroplan credit card (TD Aeroplan Visa, CIBC Aeroplan Visa, Amex Cobalt) and make at least one purchase every 18 months. Even a $5 coffee resets the inactivity timer. You can also earn Aeroplan points through partner activities: shopping at participating retailers, dining at Aeroplan dining partners, or renting a car through a partner.
Status members: Active Aeroplan 25K, 35K, 50K, or Super Elite status members have extended protections — active status prevents expiry. Renewing status annually keeps points perpetually alive.
Aeroplan point reinstatement: If your points expire due to inactivity, Air Canada offers a reinstatement process — but it costs approximately $30 (USD) plus a reinstatement fee based on the number of points restored. It's far easier to maintain activity than to reinstate expired points.

American Express Membership Rewards (Canada)

💎
Amex Membership Rewards
American Express Canada
✓ Points do NOT expire while account is open
Expiry rule: Amex MR points do not expire as long as your Amex account remains open and in good standing. There is no inactivity requirement — you can have an active account for 10 years and earn zero points and they won't expire.
What causes loss: Closing your Amex account. When you cancel an Amex card that holds your MR balance, any unspent points are typically forfeited within a short window (30 days in most cases). If you downgrade from an MR-earning card to a non-MR card, your points may be transferred or lost depending on your account setup. Always redeem or transfer before cancelling.
Product change note: If you do a product change to a non-MR Amex card, your points should transfer to your existing MR account if you have another MR-earning card. If it's your only Amex card being cancelled, redeem or transfer all points first.

Scene+ (Scotiabank)

🎬
Scene+
Scotiabank / Empire
✓ Points do NOT expire while account is active
Expiry rule: Scene+ points do not expire as long as your Scene+ account is active and in good standing. You must maintain at least one earning activity (through a Scotiabank card, Empire stores, or Cineplex) to keep the account active, but there is no hard inactivity window announced for basic account holders.
What causes loss: Closing your Scene+ account or having your account suspended/terminated. Closing your last Scotiabank Scene+ credit card doesn't automatically close your Scene+ account — the account can exist independently as long as you maintain it through Empire store earnings or other partners.

PC Optimum (Loblaws)

🛒
PC Optimum
Loblaws / Shoppers Drug Mart
⚠️ Conditional — expires after 12 months of inactivity
Expiry rule: PC Optimum points expire after 12 consecutive months of account inactivity. Any earning transaction (in-store swipe, online purchase at Loblaws-banner stores, or Shoppers Drug Mart) resets the clock.
How to keep points alive: Shop at any Loblaws-banner store (Loblaws, No Frills, Zehrs, Valu-Mart, Real Canadian Superstore, Shoppers Drug Mart, PC Express) and scan your PC Optimum card at least once per 12-month period. A single transaction resets inactivity.

Air Miles

🌟
Air Miles
LoyaltyOne
⚠️ Conditional — expires after extended account inactivity
Expiry rule: Air Miles reversed their controversial 2016 "rolling 5-year expiry from earn date" policy after significant cardholder backlash. Current policy: miles do not expire as long as your account remains active (earning or redeeming). Extended inactivity can put miles at risk — maintain some activity in your account annually.
Dream vs. Cash miles: Air Miles has two buckets — Dream Miles (for travel and merchandise) and Cash Miles (for discounts at retailers). Keep both active through regular earning or redemption to maintain your balance.
Verify current terms: Air Miles has undergone corporate restructuring. Always confirm the current expiry policy at airmiles.ca — program terms may have changed.

RBC Avion / RBC Rewards

🔵
RBC Avion / RBC Rewards
Royal Bank of Canada
✓ Points do NOT expire while account is open
Expiry rule: RBC Rewards points (including Avion points) do not expire as long as your RBC credit card account remains open and in good standing. No inactivity requirement.
What causes loss: Closing your RBC account. Any unredeemed Avion points are forfeited when the credit card account is closed. Unlike Amex MR (which can exist on a separate program account), RBC Rewards are tied to your credit card.

BMO Rewards

🏦
BMO Rewards
Bank of Montreal
✓ Points do NOT expire while account is open
Expiry rule: BMO Rewards points do not expire as long as your BMO credit card account remains open. No inactivity requirement.
What causes loss: Closing your BMO credit card. Points are tied to the card account and are forfeited on account closure. Redeem before cancelling.

TD Rewards

🏛️
TD Rewards
TD Bank
✓ Points do NOT expire while account is open
Expiry rule: TD Rewards points do not expire as long as your TD credit card is open and in good standing. No minimum activity required.
Note on Aeroplan TD Cards: TD Aeroplan co-branded cards earn Aeroplan points (not TD Rewards). Those points are subject to Aeroplan's 18-month inactivity rule, not TD's policy.

How to Prevent Points Expiry in Canada

For Aeroplan: Calendar the 18-Month Rule

Set a calendar reminder 15 months after your last Aeroplan activity. Make one small qualifying transaction — even a $1 partner purchase — before the 18-month mark. If you hold a co-branded Aeroplan credit card and use it regularly, the clock is continuously reset.

For Air Miles: Check Your Oldest Miles Regularly

Log into your Air Miles account and look for the expiry date on your oldest miles. Air Miles sends expiry notifications by email — make sure your email is current on your account. If miles are expiring soon, redeem them for Cash Miles discounts at partner retailers (Sobeys, Shell, etc.) even if you don't have a specific redemption planned.

Air Miles minimum redemption: The minimum Air Miles redemption is typically 95 Dream Miles or a specified Cash Miles threshold. If you have a small number of expiring miles below the redemption minimum, you may not be able to redeem them in time. In that case, contact Air Miles member services — they have some flexibility for accounts facing imminent expiry.

For PC Optimum: Never Go 12 Months Without a Shoppers Run

Anyone shopping at Shoppers Drug Mart or any Loblaws-banner store even occasionally will naturally reset PC Optimum inactivity. If you've moved to a city with no Loblaws stores, check if Shoppers Drug Mart is still accessible — even buying vitamins or a snack once per year keeps your account alive.

Before Cancelling Any Credit Card: Redeem or Transfer First

This is the most important general rule. Before closing any rewards credit card (even if you're unhappy with it), log in and redeem or transfer all accumulated points. Bank points tied to cards (RBC Avion, BMO Rewards, TD Rewards) are forfeited when the account closes — you cannot reinstate them after the fact.

Common mistake: Cancelling a credit card and discovering later that you had 45,000 points worth $450 on it that were forfeited. This happens every day in Canada. Check your points balance before calling to cancel — always.
