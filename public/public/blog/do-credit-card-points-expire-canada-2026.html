Aeroplan — The Most Important Expiry to Know

Aeroplan is Canada's most popular travel rewards program and has the most consequential expiry rule. Your Aeroplan points expire if there is no earn OR redeem activity on your account for 18 consecutive months. This catches many Canadians off guard — especially if they earned a large welcome bonus and then paused travel for a period.

What resets the 18-month clock: ANY earn or redeem activity. This includes: earning points on a flight, earning points on a credit card purchase (TD, CIBC, Amex → Aeroplan transfer), transferring points to Aeroplan from Amex MR, redeeming ANY points for a flight or merchandise, donating points to charity, or purchasing points from Aeroplan. Even a $5 purchase on your TD Aeroplan card resets the clock for another 18 months.

Easy fix: If you have Aeroplan points and no upcoming travel planned, put a small recurring charge on your TD or CIBC Aeroplan card (a $10 Netflix charge works). This ensures at least monthly earn activity and your points never expire as long as the card is open.

Amex Membership Rewards — Never Expire, With a Catch

Amex MR points in Canada never expire while your card account is open. However, they vanish immediately upon card cancellation. This makes cancelling an Amex card without first transferring or redeeming your points an expensive mistake. Before cancelling any Amex card, transfer your points to Aeroplan or another transfer partner first. Once transferred, the points exist in the receiving program and follow those program's expiry rules.

If you're downgrading from a fee card to a no-fee Amex (like the Amex Green Card), points typically carry over. If you're cancelling outright with no replacement Amex, they're gone.

Air Miles — Expiry Policy

Air Miles reversed their controversial 2016 "rolling 5-year expiry from earn date" policy after major cardholder backlash. Under current terms, miles do not expire as long as your account remains active — maintain at least some earning or redemption activity annually to keep your balance safe. Air Miles has also undergone corporate restructuring, so verify the current expiry policy at airmiles.ca before making any redemption decisions.

5 Rules to Never Lose Your Points

Keep your Aeroplan card active: Put one small recurring charge on your TD or CIBC Aeroplan card. Any credit card earn on Aeroplan resets the 18-month inactivity clock.
Don't cancel an Amex card with a balance: Transfer your Amex MR points to Aeroplan or Marriott Bonvoy BEFORE cancelling. Transfers take 1–5 business days, so plan ahead.
Check your Air Miles expiry dates: Log into your Air Miles account and view point expiry dates by batch. Miles earned more than 4 years ago may be expiring soon regardless of account activity.
Keep bank-attached programs open: RBC Avion, CIBC Aventura, BMO Rewards, and TD Rewards all expire if you cancel the card. If considering a downgrade or product switch, verify if points carry to the new product.
Redeem before program changes: Loyalty programs do change their expiry rules (Aeroplan's rules have changed before). If your program announces a change that shortens expiry windows, use your points before the deadline.
