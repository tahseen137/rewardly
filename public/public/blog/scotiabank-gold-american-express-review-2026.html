Point Value

Scene+ points are worth 1¢ each for travel, movie, or entertainment redemptions. So 5× dining = 5% effective cashback on restaurant spend. At Sobeys-banner stores, 6× = 6% effective cashback — among the highest grocery earn rates in Canada.

Scene+ points can be redeemed for: travel through the Scene+ travel portal, movies at Cineplex, merchandise, or as statement credits (lower value). For maximum value, always redeem for travel.

Zero Foreign Transaction Fees

Like the Scotiabank Passport Visa Infinite, the Gold Amex charges no foreign transaction fees — a rare perk that saves you the standard 2.5% fee on all non-CAD purchases. For Canadians who travel internationally or shop at US online retailers, this adds 2.5% to the card's effective return on foreign purchases.

On a $3,000 international trip, that's $75 saved versus a standard card with FX fees. Combined with 5× on dining and entertainment while abroad, the Scotia Gold is exceptional for travel spending.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the Scotia Gold Amex accepted everywhere?
It's an American Express card, so it's not accepted at Costco, No Frills, FreshCo, or some other merchants. In Canada, approximately 85-90% of merchants accept Amex, with notable gaps at warehouse and discount stores. Consider keeping a Visa or Mastercard for those purchases.
Does the 5× dining rate apply to food delivery apps like DoorDash and Uber Eats?
Yes — food delivery typically codes as restaurant/dining (MCC 5812) and earns 5× Scene+ points on the Scotia Gold Amex. This includes DoorDash, Uber Eats, Skip the Dishes, and Instacart (when coded as delivery).
Does no FX fee apply to ALL foreign currencies, or just USD?
All foreign currencies — EUR, GBP, USD, JPY, etc. Unlike the Rogers World Elite Mastercard (which waives FX only on USD and select currencies), the Scotia Gold Amex waives the fee on any non-CAD transaction. This is a significant advantage for European and Asian travel.
Can I transfer Scene+ points to Aeroplan?
Yes — Scene+ points can transfer to Air Canada Aeroplan at a 1:1 ratio. This means your 5× Scene+ on dining and groceries translates to 5 Aeroplan pts/$, which can be worth 7.5–15¢ per point when redeemed for premium Air Canada flights. This transfer option adds significant value to the card for Aeroplan collectors.
What's the income requirement?
$12,000 minimum personal annual income — the lowest income threshold of any premium rewards card in Canada. This makes it accessible to students, part-time workers, and lower-income earners who wouldn't qualify for Visa Infinite or World Elite cards.
