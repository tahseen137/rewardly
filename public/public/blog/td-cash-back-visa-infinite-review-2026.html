Annual Fee Waiver — TD's Key Differentiator

The most compelling feature of the TD Cash Back Visa Infinite for existing TD customers is the annual fee rebate available with the TD All-Inclusive Banking Plan ($29.95/month). If you already pay for TD All-Inclusive (which includes unlimited transactions, $6.95/month fee waiver for an additional account, free safety deposit box, rebate on up to $120/yr in investment fees), the $139 credit card annual fee is fully rebated.

This makes the TD Cash Back VI effectively free for TD All-Inclusive customers — giving you 3% on grocery/gas/bills and 1.5% everywhere else at zero marginal cost. No other major Canadian cash-back card at this earn rate is waivable for existing banking customers.

Frequently Asked Questions

How is cash back redeemed on the TD Cash Back Visa Infinite?
TD Cash Back points accumulate as "Cash Back Dollars" in your account. You can redeem them in $25 increments as a statement credit starting every November (automatic annual redemption in the first week of November) or on request at any time. Unlike some cash-back cards that apply rewards automatically each month, TD requires you to initiate the redemption or wait for the November automatic payout. Set a reminder each November to claim your cash back if you prefer monthly redemptions.
Does Costco earn 3% on the TD Cash Back VI?
No — Costco warehouses in Canada code as MCC 5300 (Warehouse Clubs/Wholesale Clubs), not MCC 5411 (Grocery Stores). Most Canadian credit cards that bonus grocery stores do NOT include warehouse clubs. The TD Cash Back VI earns only 1.5% (the base rate) at Costco. Note: Costco Canada only accepts Mastercard — the TD Cash Back VI is a Visa card and is not accepted inside Costco warehouse stores, though it works for Costco.ca online purchases.
Is the TD Cash Back VI better than TD's Aeroplan card?
Depends on your travel frequency. If you fly Air Canada 4+ times per year, the TD Aeroplan Visa Infinite (same $139 fee, also waivable) is typically better — it earns Aeroplan points worth 1.4–3¢+ each on flights. If you rarely fly or prefer simple cash, the TD Cash Back VI's straightforward 3%/1.5% cash back avoids program complexity and delivers guaranteed value.

Rewardly Verdict

The TD Cash Back Visa Infinite is a good but not great cash-back card. At 3% grocery/gas/bills with a 1.5% base rate, it trails CIBC Dividend VI and Scotia Momentum VI at 4% in the same categories. But for existing TD All-Inclusive customers, the $139 fee waiver makes it free — and a free card earning 3% on your top categories beats a $120/yr card earning 4% once you factor in the fee math. If you bank with TD and have All-Inclusive, this is likely your best cash-back card. If not, consider CIBC Dividend VI or Scotia Momentum VI at 4%.
