Best Credit Cards for Charitable Giving in Canada 2026

Earn cashback or points on donations — and get a tax receipt. The right card earns 2% back while you give, with full CRA documentation for tax time.

Can You Earn Rewards on Charitable Donations?

Yes — if your charity accepts credit cards (most registered Canadian charities do), your donation earns rewards just like any other purchase. A $500 year-end donation to a registered charity earns $10 on a Rogers Red card (2% cashback) or 500 Aeroplan points on a TD Aeroplan card. The charity receives your full donation, issues a tax receipt, and your card rewards accumulate normally. There's nothing complex about this — it's simply using a credit card for a purchase that also generates a CRA-eligible donation receipt.

How donations code: Online donations via Visa/Mastercard on charity websites typically code as "charitable organizations" — a base-rate category on all Canadian cards. No card has an accelerated bonus category for donations. The best strategy is a high flat-rate card that earns 2% on everything, since no card gives 5x on charity giving.

Best Cards for Donations

#1 Rogers Red World Elite Mastercard — Best for Donations

2% cashback everywhere, unlimited

Annual fee: $0 (or $29). The clear leader since donations code as base-rate spend. 2% on a $1,000 annual donation total = $20 cashback. Over 10 years of consistent giving, that's $200 in cashback from donations alone. The cashback is real money — it can even be redirected to donate more. No annual fee makes it the obvious tool for charitable giving.

#2 TD Aeroplan Visa Infinite — Best if You're Accumulating Toward a Trip

1x Aeroplan on all base-category spend (including donations)

Annual fee: $139. Donations earn 1x Aeroplan points. On $1,000 in donations per year, that's 1,000 Aeroplan points (~$15 value). Not as high as Rogers Red's $20, but if you're already accumulating Aeroplan toward a specific flight goal, every point helps. Only makes sense if you already hold the card for travel — not worth applying specifically for donation earn rates.

#3 Amex Cobalt — For Year-End Donation Pushes

1x MR on base spend · Earns on most donation platforms

Annual fee: $156. Cobalt earns only 1x on donations (base rate) but has the advantage of Amex Front of the Line and transferable MR points. For year-end tax-planning donations (common in December), the Cobalt welcome bonus period can overlap — making year-end giving a convenient way to hit minimum spend thresholds while also getting your tax receipt. Note: some small charities don't accept Amex; have Rogers Red as backup.

Tax Considerations: Credit Card Donations and CRA

The Simple Version

Your donation is fully deductible: The full amount you donate by credit card is eligible for the CRA donation tax credit — regardless of any rewards you earn. Earning cashback on a donation does not reduce the donation amount for tax purposes.

The tax receipt: The registered charity will issue a donation receipt for the full amount you paid. For online donations by credit card, this receipt is typically emailed immediately. Keep it for tax filing.

Cashback is not income: CRA treats credit card cashback as a discount or rebate, not taxable income — even when earned on charitable donations. You don't need to report your donation cashback as income.

First-time donor? The First-Time Donor's Super Credit gives an additional 25% on the first $1,000 donated if neither you nor your spouse has claimed a charitable tax credit since 2007. This applies regardless of payment method.
