Earn Rate Reality Check — Which Card Earns More Aeroplan?

Both cards ultimately funnel into Aeroplan miles, but the path matters. The TD Aeroplan VI earns Aeroplan miles directly — no conversion step, no transfer ratio to worry about. The RBC Avion earns RBC Rewards Points, which transfer to Aeroplan at a rate that depends on your Avion tier (typically 1 RBC pt = 0.6–1 Aeroplan mile).

For a Canadian spending $2,000/month across a typical mix (grocery, dining, gas, travel), the TD Aeroplan VI earns approximately 30–40% more Aeroplan miles than the RBC Avion at the base conversion rate. The 1.5x on Air Canada, grocery, and gas — with no conversion step — is a strong differentiator.

Winner on Aeroplan Earn Rate: TD Aeroplan Visa Infinite

Direct Aeroplan earn (no conversion loss), 1.5x on Air Canada/grocery/gas, 50,000-mile welcome bonus. For Canadian travellers focused on Aeroplan redemptions, the TD Aeroplan VI earns more miles on identical spending.

Where RBC Avion Wins

The RBC Avion VI has one crucial advantage: flexibility. RBC Rewards Points can be redeemed for flights on ANY airline at a fixed rate of 1¢/point — not just Air Canada. This means you're not locked into Aeroplan availability. If you find a cheaper cash price on WestJet, United, or a budget carrier, you can use Avion points at a predictable value.

The Petro-Canada fuel savings (11.5¢/L discount when linked to RBC banking) can be worth $150–$300/year for regular drivers — a compelling non-travel benefit that the TD Aeroplan doesn't match.

RBC Avion also pairs naturally with RBC's banking products — if you already bank with RBC, the integrated experience (one app, combined statements, fee waivers through banking bundles) may tip the balance.

Key question to ask yourself: Do you fly Air Canada at least 2–3 times per year? If yes, the TD Aeroplan's 1.5x on Air Canada (direct Aeroplan, no conversion loss) and annual companion voucher likely makes it the better value. If you fly multiple airlines and want flexibility, the RBC Avion's any-airline redemption option is more useful.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I transfer RBC Avion points to Aeroplan?
Yes — RBC Rewards points can be transferred to Aeroplan. The conversion rate for Avion Visa Infinite cardholders is typically 1 RBC point = 1 Aeroplan mile (check current terms at RBC). However, you must hold an eligible Avion card and request the transfer through RBC Online Banking. Note that points transfers are typically one-way and cannot be reversed.
Is the TD Aeroplan VI the best Aeroplan card in Canada?
The TD Aeroplan Visa Infinite is the best mid-tier Aeroplan card at its price point ($139/year). The CIBC Aeroplan Visa Infinite is nearly identical (same fee, similar earn rates, issued by CIBC instead of TD). At the premium tier, the TD Aeroplan Visa Infinite Privilege ($599/year) offers lounge access, higher earn rates, and more insurance. For most Canadian travellers, the TD VI or CIBC VI is the sweet spot of value vs. cost.
