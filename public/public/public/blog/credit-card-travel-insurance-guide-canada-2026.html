Credit Card Travel Insurance Guide Canada 2026

Most Canadians have travel insurance sitting in their wallet and don't know how to use it. This guide explains what's covered, when it activates, and how to actually file a claim.

What Credit Card Travel Insurance Typically Covers

Premium travel credit cards (those with annual fees of $100+) typically include several types of travel insurance. Lower-fee and no-fee cards rarely include any. Here are the six core coverage types:

🏥 Emergency Medical

Covers hospital stays, doctors, prescriptions, and emergency evacuation abroad. The most valuable coverage — foreign hospitals can cost $10,000+/day.

✈️ Trip Cancellation

Reimburses prepaid, non-refundable travel costs if you cancel before departure due to covered reasons (illness, death of family member, job loss).

🔄 Trip Interruption

Covers additional costs if your trip is cut short — emergency flights home, lost hotel nights, etc. Usually a higher coverage limit than cancellation.

🚗 Car Rental Insurance

Collision Damage Waiver (CDW) replaces the rental agency's expensive daily coverage. Covers damage/theft to the rental vehicle.

🧳 Baggage Loss/Delay

Covers lost, stolen, or delayed checked luggage. Delay coverage typically kicks in after 6–12 hours and reimburses emergency purchases.

📱 Mobile Device Insurance

Covers phone/tablet loss, theft, or accidental damage for purchases made on the card. CIBC Visa Infinite cards include this; most others don't.

How to Activate Credit Card Travel Insurance

This is where most Canadians fail. Credit card travel insurance doesn't activate automatically just because you hold the card. You must meet specific conditions:

1

Charge the Trip to the Card (Most Important)

For trip cancellation/interruption: you must charge at least some portion of the trip cost to the credit card. Full or partial payment qualifies on most cards — but check your certificate for minimums. Some cards require the full fare to be charged.

2

Emergency Medical: No Charge Required (Usually)

Emergency medical insurance typically covers you just for being a cardholder — you don't need to charge the trip to the card. However, some cards (like TD Aeroplan VI) require that you be traveling on a ticket charged to the card for full benefits.

3

Car Rental: Decline the CDW at the Counter

To use credit card car rental insurance, you must decline the rental agency's own CDW (Collision Damage Waiver). Charge the rental to the card. Do NOT use points/miles to pay if your card requires full charge-to-card for coverage.

4

Depart Within Coverage Window

Emergency medical insurance has day limits: TD Aeroplan VI covers 21 days per trip, Scotia Passport VI covers 25 days, National Bank WE MC covers 60 days. Trips longer than your card's coverage window require supplemental insurance for the excess days.

Quick activation checklist: (1) Card is active with available credit. (2) You're a primary cardholder or authorized user listed in the policy. (3) Trip costs partially or fully charged to the card (for cancellation/interruption). (4) For car rental: CDW declined at counter, rental charged to card. ✓ That's it.

Pre-Existing Conditions: The Most Common Exclusion

The most common reason credit card travel insurance claims are denied: pre-existing conditions. The definition varies by card but typically means any medical condition for which you've been diagnosed, treated, or taken medication in the 90–180 days before departure.

Example of a denied claim: You have high blood pressure managed with medication. You go to Mexico, have a cardiac event, and are hospitalized for $80,000. The insurer reviews your prescription history, finds you've had hypertension for two years, and denies the claim as a "pre-existing condition." This is a real scenario that happens to Canadians every year.

If you have any chronic condition, check your card's Certificate of Insurance for the specific stability clause. Most require the condition to be "stable" (no change in medication, no symptoms, no medical visits) for 90–180 days immediately before departure.

Solution: If you have pre-existing conditions, buy supplemental travel insurance that explicitly covers your conditions — typically through an annual travel policy or a condition-specific rider. The credit card's coverage can still apply for unrelated emergencies.

Car Rental Insurance: What You Need to Know

Credit card car rental insurance is typically CDW/LDW only — it covers the rental car if it's damaged or stolen. It does NOT cover third-party liability (damage you cause to other vehicles or people). Always confirm you have separate liability coverage through your personal auto insurance or the rental's liability policy before declining everything at the counter.

The Right Conversation at the Rental Counter

Say this: "I'll decline the CDW/LDW — I'm using my credit card coverage. I'll keep the third-party liability protection." Most rental companies offer liability separately from CDW. Credit card covers the vehicle; liability is separate and usually worth keeping, especially abroad.

Which Cards Cover All Drivers?

Most credit cards cover the primary cardholder as the main driver. National Bank WE MC and Amex Platinum extend coverage to all listed drivers on the rental agreement. TD Aeroplan VI covers additional drivers only if the primary renter is the cardholder and the additional driver is listed at time of rental. Check your certificate before adding a spouse or friend as a driver.

How to File a Travel Insurance Claim

1

Call the Emergency Line Immediately

Every credit card travel insurance policy has a 24/7 emergency assistance line. For medical emergencies, call BEFORE receiving treatment if at all possible. The insurer may be able to guarantee payment directly to the hospital and coordinate care. The number is on the back of your insurance certificate (not the credit card itself).

2

Document Everything

Save all receipts, medical bills, doctor notes, and flight boarding passes. For trip cancellation, get a written reason from the cause (doctor's note for illness, official notification for job loss). For lost luggage, get a Property Irregularity Report (PIR) from the airline at the airport before leaving.

3

File Within the Time Limit

Most credit card insurance policies require claims to be filed within 30–90 days of the incident. Don't wait until you get home and forget — set a reminder the day you return. Late claims are typically denied regardless of merit.

4

Submit to the Insurance Administrator (Not the Bank)

Credit card travel insurance is underwritten by third-party insurers: TD uses TD Insurance/Allianz, RBC uses Aviva, Scotiabank uses Generali, BMO uses Allianz. Claims go directly to the insurer, not your bank branch. The administrator contact is in your Certificate of Insurance.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to register my trip to activate credit card travel insurance?
For most credit cards, no — coverage is automatic for cardholders. A few cards require you to call to register a trip for full medical benefits (particularly for travelers with pre-existing conditions). Check your Certificate of Insurance. Some cards offer optional "top-up" enrollment for longer trips or pre-existing conditions, which does require registration.
Does credit card travel insurance cover COVID-19?
As of 2026, most premium credit card travel insurance policies do cover COVID-19 as a covered medical emergency under emergency medical benefits, and some cover trip cancellation if you test positive shortly before departure. Policies evolved significantly post-pandemic. Read your current certificate — COVID exclusions that existed in 2020–2021 have largely been removed, but coverage terms vary by insurer.
Can I use credit card travel insurance if I booked with points?
This depends on the card. Some cards require that at least part of the trip be charged to the credit card (taxes, fees, or any portion) to activate trip cancellation/interruption. If you paid zero dollars (pure award redemption with no taxes charged to the card), cancellation insurance may not apply. Emergency medical insurance usually does not require payment of any trip cost — being a cardholder is sufficient.
Does travel insurance cover domestic travel within Canada?
Emergency medical insurance generally applies to travel outside your home province, not just outside Canada. Trip cancellation/interruption may also cover domestic travel if it's prepaid and non-refundable. Car rental insurance applies domestically as well. Check your certificate — coverage often specifies "travel more than 150km from home" as the threshold.
