Best Balance Transfer Credit Cards Canada 2026

The average Canadian credit card charges 19.99% APR. Moving that balance to an 8.99% or 0% promotional card can save hundreds in interest while you pay it off.

What Is a Balance Transfer?

A balance transfer moves existing credit card debt from a high-interest card to a new card with a lower interest rate. In Canada, most standard credit cards charge 19.99% to 22.99% APR. Balance transfer cards offer either a promotional 0% rate for a limited period (rare in Canada but sometimes offered as sign-up promotions) or a permanent low rate (8.99%–13.99% APR) that is significantly cheaper than standard cards.

How much can you save? On a $5,000 balance at 19.99% APR: you pay ~$83/month in interest alone. At 8.99% APR: only ~$37/month in interest. Saving $46/month in interest means $552/year — and faster debt paydown because more of each payment goes to principal.

How to Do a Balance Transfer in Canada — Step by Step

1. Apply for the low-rate card. You must first be approved. Most low-rate cards require good-to-excellent credit (680+ score). Note that applying triggers a hard inquiry on your credit report — a small, temporary score impact.

2. Request the balance transfer during or after application. On the application, specify the card(s) you want to transfer from and the amount. Alternatively, call the new issuer after approval to initiate the transfer.

3. Keep paying your old card until the transfer is confirmed. Balance transfers take 5–10 business days to complete. During this time, continue making minimum payments on the old card to avoid late fees and credit score damage. Stop only once the transferred amount shows on the new card and the old card shows a zero (or reduced) balance.

4. Stop using the old card for new purchases. If you continue charging the old high-interest card while carrying a transferred balance on the new card, you'll undo all the interest savings.

Balance transfer fees in Canada: Some Canadian cards charge a balance transfer fee of 1%–3% of the transferred amount. On a $5,000 transfer with a 2% fee, you'd pay $100 immediately. Check the fee before transferring — even with a fee, the interest savings usually outweigh it significantly.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does a balance transfer hurt your credit score in Canada?
A balance transfer involves a hard inquiry (when you apply for the new card), which may temporarily lower your score by 5–10 points. However, if the transfer reduces your overall credit utilization ratio (by effectively spreading debt across more available credit), it can actually improve your score over time. The key is: don't close the old card immediately (preserves available credit) and don't accumulate new debt on the old card after transferring.
Can I transfer a balance between cards at the same bank?
No — balance transfers between credit cards issued by the same bank are not allowed in Canada. For example, you cannot transfer an RBC Visa balance to an RBC Mastercard. You must transfer to a card issued by a different financial institution. This is why MBNA (owned by TD but issued as MBNA-brand cards) is popular — it accepts transfers from most other Canadian bank cards.
