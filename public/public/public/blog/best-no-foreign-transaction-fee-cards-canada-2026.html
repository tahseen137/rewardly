Best No Foreign Transaction Fee Credit Cards in Canada 2026

Stop paying 2.5% on every international purchase. The top no-FX cards ranked by rewards, perks, and total value.
March 6, 2026 · 8 min read · By Rewardly Team

Most Canadian credit cards quietly charge 2.5% on every purchase made in a foreign currency. Travel to Europe for two weeks, spend $4,000 — that's a $100 fee that most Canadians never notice. The right card eliminates this completely, and several of the best no-FX cards also come with strong rewards programs on top.

The Hidden Cost Most Canadians Pay: The standard 2.5% foreign transaction fee applies to hotel bookings, restaurants abroad, US Amazon purchases, USD-billed SaaS subscriptions, and any online merchant in a foreign currency. A family spending $300/month on US purchases quietly pays $90/year in unnecessary fees.

When Foreign Transaction Fees Hit You

Many Canadians don't realize the 2.5% FX fee applies beyond just travel:

  • While travelling: Hotels, restaurants, shops, Ubers, tours — anything billed in a non-CAD currency
  • Online shopping: Amazon.com, ASOS, US retailers, eBay US listings
  • Digital subscriptions: Netflix (often billed USD), Spotify, US SaaS tools, Adobe, Notion, etc.
  • App store purchases: Apps billed in USD on the App Store or Google Play

The Optimal Two-Card Strategy

💡 Expert Strategy: One Domestic + One International Card

Card 1 (Domestic): High-earn rate on your biggest spending categories in Canada. Amex Cobalt (5x groceries), PC World Elite (3% at Loblaws), or Scotia Gold Amex (5x groceries + no FX).

Card 2 (International): No-FX card for everything in a foreign currency — travel, US online shopping, international subscriptions. Home Trust Preferred Visa if you want $0 fee, Scotiabank Passport if you travel frequently.

This setup costs nothing extra (both cards in your wallet) and maximizes returns on domestic spend while eliminating FX fees abroad.
