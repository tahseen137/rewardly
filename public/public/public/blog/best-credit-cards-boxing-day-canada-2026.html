Best Credit Cards for Boxing Day Canada 2026

Boxing Day is Canada's biggest retail shopping day. On $1,500 in Boxing Day purchases, the right card earns $30–$150 back — plus purchase protection on expensive items.

Boxing Day vs Black Friday — Which Is Bigger in Canada?

Boxing Day (December 26) remains the largest single retail event in Canada — larger than Black Friday by most metrics. Canadians spend an estimated $6.5 billion on Boxing Day and the post-Christmas sales period. Electronics, appliances, clothing, and household goods are the top categories. Many major retailers now extend Boxing Day deals into "Boxing Week" (Dec 26 – Jan 2), giving you more time to shop strategically.

Best Cards for Boxing Day 2026

#1

Rogers Red World Elite Mastercard — Electronics & Appliances

$0 annual fee · 2–3% flat cashback on everything at BestBuy, Home Depot, LG, etc.
3% for Rogers/Fido/Shaw customers 2% on all purchases 0% foreign transaction fee

Electronics and appliances don't have a bonus category on any premium Canadian credit card — they fall under "everything else." Rogers Red earns 2–3% flat cashback, making it the best-in-class option for Boxing Day electronics deals at BestBuy, Home Depot, Walmart, The Brick, and online retailers. On a $2,000 television, that's $40–$60 cashback. Costco-only shoppers also benefit as Rogers Red is a Mastercard (Costco only accepts Mastercard).

#2

Amex Cobalt — Boxing Week Dining & Food

$191.88/yr · 5x on restaurant meals during the Boxing Week period
5x MR at restaurants 5x at grocery stores 1x on retail shopping

Boxing Day shopping typically includes a family lunch or dinner out. The Cobalt earns 5x MR on all restaurant spending — pair it with Rogers Red (for electronics) and use Cobalt for all food and dining during the holiday period. Not useful for actual retail Boxing Day purchases, but critical for the dining component of a holiday shopping trip.

#3

Scotiabank Gold Amex — Clothing & Entertainment

$120/yr · 5x Scene+ on entertainment category (which includes some retail)
5x Scene+ on entertainment purchases 5x on dining 0% foreign transaction fee

Some Boxing Day entertainment purchases (movie tickets, Cineplex gift cards, gaming purchases) may code as entertainment and earn 5x Scene+ on the Scotia Gold. Clothing and apparel typically earns at base rate. However, if you're planning to buy movie passes or gift cards to streaming services during Boxing Day sales, the Scotia Gold earns well here.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are Boxing Day online purchases treated the same as in-store?
Yes — credit card earn rates and purchase protection apply equally to online and in-store purchases. The merchant category code (MCC) is the same whether you shop on BestBuy.ca or in the BestBuy store. For foreign online purchases (US websites), cards with 0% foreign transaction fees (Rogers Red, HSBC WE MC) save you 2.5% on top of any rewards.
Can I use points to pay for Boxing Day purchases?
Yes, for some programs. Amex MR can be redeemed as a statement credit against recent purchases through the "Pay with Points" feature. TD Rewards and RBC Avion points also offer statement credit redemption. Aeroplan miles are not typically redeemable for retail purchases — they're best saved for travel. If you want to use points for Boxing Day, check your card's redemption options in the app before December 26.
