Best Business Credit Cards in Canada 2026

Self-employed, freelancer, or small business owner — the right business card earns points on every business expense, simplifies accounting, and the annual fee is fully tax-deductible.

Why Business Credit Cards Are Different

Business credit cards offer several advantages over personal cards for Canadian business owners: (1) higher credit limits appropriate for business spending volumes; (2) expense categorization and reporting tools (statements by category, quarterly summaries); (3) employee cards with individual limits and controls; (4) the annual fee is fully deductible as a business expense under CRA rules (unlike personal cards); (5) GST/HST tracked separately for input tax credit claims.

CRA note: Business credit card annual fees are deductible as a business expense. If you use a personal card for business expenses, only the proportional portion used for business is deductible. A dedicated business card simplifies bookkeeping and maximizes deductibility. Keep receipts for all purchases regardless of card.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the annual fee on a business credit card tax deductible in Canada?
Yes — business credit card annual fees are deductible as a business expense under CRA's Income Tax Act, provided the card is used exclusively or primarily for business. If you use the card partly for personal expenses, deduct only the proportional business-use portion. Keep records of business vs. personal use. Consult your accountant for your specific situation, particularly for home-office and mixed-use expenses.
Can I use a personal credit card for business expenses?
Technically yes, but it's not recommended. CRA accepts the deduction of business expenses paid by personal card, but you must keep every receipt and clearly document the business purpose of each expense. You can only deduct the proportional annual fee. Business cards simplify this significantly and reduce audit risk by clearly separating business and personal spending.
Are business credit card rewards taxable in Canada?
This is a nuanced CRA issue (covered in more detail in our taxable rewards guide). Generally: if you earn rewards on personal expenses, they're not taxable. If you earn rewards on business expenses that you've already deducted, CRA's position is that the rewards may reduce the deductible business expense (or represent taxable income). In practice, CRA rarely audits reward redemptions, but consult your accountant if you earn significant rewards on deducted business expenses.
