Aeroplan Elite Status Canada 2026 — How to Earn Status with Credit Cards

You don't need to fly constantly to earn Aeroplan Elite status. Credit card spending on TD and CIBC Aeroplan cards earns "Eligible Credit Card Spend" (ECCS) — which counts toward status qualification.

How Aeroplan Status Works

Aeroplan Elite Status is earned by accumulating Status Qualifying Miles (SQM) and Eligible Credit Card Spend (ECCS) in a calendar year (January 1 – December 31). Each status tier requires hitting a minimum SQM threshold AND a minimum ECCS threshold — or exceeding the SQM by a large enough margin to qualify on flights alone.

The key insight: ECCS from TD Aeroplan Visa Infinite and CIBC Aeroplan Visa Infinite credit cards counts toward status. Every $1,500 in eligible credit card spend = 1,000 Status Qualifying Miles. This lets you offset fewer flights with more card spend.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I get Aeroplan status without flying?
You cannot reach Elite Status on credit card spend alone — flights (SQM) are also required. However, ECCS from the TD or CIBC Aeroplan card can significantly reduce the number of flights required. For example, a 25K member who spends $37,500/year on their TD Aeroplan card earns 25,000 SQM from cards — meaning they only need to earn the remaining 0 SQM from flights, effectively reaching 25K status without the typical flight requirement. (The exact formula depends on Air Canada's published status calculator — verify with Aeroplan.)
When does ECCS from credit cards count?
ECCS accumulates throughout the calendar year and is credited to your Aeroplan status tracking quarterly (approximately). The annual ECCS count resets January 1. Status earned in one year applies to the following calendar year. So 2026 flying + spending earns 2027 status.
Is Aeroplan 25K status worth pursuing?
For Canadians who fly Air Canada 6–10 times per year, 25K status offers significant value: free checked bags (saves $35–$60 per flight), priority boarding, and upgrade waitlisting. The 2 checked bags per flight at $35 each = $70 per return trip. Flying 6 return trips saves $420 in bag fees alone — enough to justify mild effort to earn 25K status.
