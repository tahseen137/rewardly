Aeroplan vs WestJet Dollars vs Avion vs Scene+ — Which Canadian Program Is Best in 2026?

A clear comparison of Canada's four major loyalty programs — point value, earn rates, redemption sweet spots, and which one fits your travel style.

📖 13 min read

Canadians earn loyalty currency through credit cards, flying, shopping, and everyday spending — but the four major programs work very differently. Aeroplan and WestJet Dollars are airline-tied. RBC Avion Rewards and Scene+ are bank programs with flexible travel options. Choosing the wrong one means earning points you can't use well.

This guide cuts through the complexity. After reading it, you'll know which program is right for your travel style and how to maximize each one.

Aeroplan — Canada's Premium Travel Program

✈️ Aeroplan (Air Canada)

Best for: Frequent flyers who want premium cabin redemptions and maximum flexibility within the Star Alliance network.

Point value: 1–2.5¢+ depending on redemption. Business class to Europe can yield 2–3¢/point. Basic economy short-haul is closer to 1–1.3¢/point.

Partner airlines: Lufthansa, United, Singapore Airlines, ANA, and all 44 Star Alliance members. You can book partner airline flights using Aeroplan points at often-favorable rates.

Sweet spots: Round-trip business class to Europe on Air Canada Signature service (flat bed, premium meals) for 100,000–130,000 points. Short-haul North America from 7,500 points one-way on select Star Alliance partners.

Transfer into Aeroplan: Amex Membership Rewards (1:1 with 10% bonus), TD Rewards, CIBC Rewards, Marriott Bonvoy (3:1)

Best earning cards: Amex Cobalt (1 MR = 1 Aeroplan), TD Aeroplan Visa Infinite (direct earn), CIBC Aeroplan Visa Infinite (direct earn)

Aeroplan's strength is optionality. Because Air Canada belongs to Star Alliance, Aeroplan points can book flights on 45 airlines. The program has a dynamic-ish redemption chart (fixed tiers by distance zone) that allows skilled travelers to find enormous value on partner airline premium cabins that aren't well-optimized in other programs.

The complexity is real: understanding zone charts, partner availability, and the difference between Air Canada flights and partner flights requires research. For casual travelers who just want to book their summer vacation to Mexico, that complexity may not be worth it.

WestJet Dollars — Canada's Simplest Airline Currency

✈️ WestJet Dollars (WestJet Rewards)

Best for: Loyal WestJet flyers, particularly those based in cities where WestJet has strong route networks (Western Canada, sun destinations).

Point value: Fixed at $0.01 per WestJet Dollar — always. 1 WestJet Dollar = $1 off a WestJet purchase. No complexity, no charts.

Usable for: WestJet flights, checked bag fees, WestJet Vacations packages, onboard purchases. Cannot transfer to other programs.

Sweet spots: No sweet spots — it's a fixed-value currency. What you earn is exactly what you get: no upside from creative redemptions, no downside from value-destroying options.

Best earning card: WestJet RBC World Elite Mastercard (1.5–2% earn as WestJet Dollars, annual companion voucher)

WestJet Dollars is the least strategically interesting loyalty program in Canada — and that's a feature, not a bug. For someone who takes 2–4 WestJet flights per year and just wants their spending to reduce the cost of the next flight, this program delivers exactly that. Zero learning curve.

The limitation is lock-in: your WestJet Dollars only work at WestJet. If WestJet doesn't fly your route, the currency is useless for that trip. If you fly with multiple airlines, WestJet Dollars earned from non-WestJet credit card spending have limited reach.

RBC Avion Rewards — Flexible Mid-Tier Program

🏦 RBC Avion Rewards

Best for: RBC banking customers who want travel flexibility without Aeroplan complexity.

Point value: ~1¢ per point through RBC's travel portal. British Airways Avios transfer at 1:1 can yield 1.5–3¢/point for short-haul partner redemptions.

Transfer partners: British Airways Avios (1:1), WestJet Dollars (100 pts = $1 WestJet Dollar = poor value), the RBC travel portal

Sweet spots: Short-haul flights booked through British Airways Avios after transferring — Toronto–Montreal can be as low as 7,500 Avios one-way on American Airlines (Avios sweet spot).

Best earning cards: RBC ION+ Visa ($48/yr, 3× on 6 everyday categories), RBC Avion Visa Infinite ($120/yr, Aeroplan transfer, 1.25×)

Avion Rewards occupies a middle ground: more flexible than WestJet Dollars (you can book any travel through the portal), with a transfer path to British Airways Avios for more advanced redemptions. The Aeroplan transfer option (available on the premium Avion Visa Infinite) elevates the program significantly for serious points collectors.

The RBC ION+ Visa at just $48/yr makes Avion Rewards accessible to people who don't want a $120+ annual fee. Earning 3× on groceries, dining, transit, and streaming while building toward a travel redemption is a reasonable everyday strategy.

Scene+ — Best Everyday Value Program

🎬 Scene+ (Scotiabank + Cineplex)

Best for: Canadians who want simple, consistent value from everyday purchases (grocery, dining, entertainment) without the complexity of an airline program.

Point value: Fixed 1¢ per point across movies, Empire groceries, and Scene+ Travel. No variation, no charts.

Partners: Cineplex (all locations), Empire grocery stores (Sobeys, IGA, FreshCo, Farm Boy, Safeway, Foodland), Harvey's, Swiss Chalet, and Scene+ Travel portal.

Sweet spots: High earn rates on grocery + dining (6 pts/$ with Scotia Gold Amex) redeemed at face value at Sobeys/IGA = consistent 6% return on grocery purchases.

Best earning card: Scotiabank Gold American Express ($120/yr, 5 pts/$ dining/grocery, no FX fee)

Scene+ is the program that rewards everyday Canadians most consistently. It doesn't have Aeroplan's premium cabin sweetspots, but it also doesn't require researching partner award availability or understanding zone charts. If you shop at Sobeys and occasionally go to movies, Scene+ earns meaningfully on purchases you were making anyway.

Head-to-Head: Which Program Is Right for You?

You fly Air Canada frequently (6+ times/year) and want premium travel Pick Aeroplan. The Star Alliance network and business class sweet spots make it unbeatable for serious travelers. Stack Amex Cobalt for earning (MR→Aeroplan) with a TD or CIBC Aeroplan card for direct earn and perks.
You fly WestJet 2–4 times/year and mostly to sun destinations or Western Canada Pick WestJet Dollars. The companion voucher from the WestJet RBC World Elite Mastercard can save $250+ annually on its own, and the simple dollar-value system requires no management.
You want travel rewards but don't fly a specific airline Pick Avion Rewards (via RBC) or Scene+ (via Scotiabank). Both offer flexible travel portal bookings at 1¢/pt with no airline lock-in. Scene+ adds grocery and entertainment value; Avion adds British Airways Avios access.
You rarely travel but want to earn rewards on everyday spending Pick Scene+. Grocery, dining, and Cineplex are daily-life redemptions that don't require a flight. The consistent 1¢ value means you always know what you're getting.
You want maximum possible return per point, willing to do research Pick Aeroplan. When redeemed for premium cabin flights on partner airlines, Aeroplan points regularly achieve 2–3¢ each — double to triple the value of other programs.

Can You Use Multiple Programs at Once?

Yes — and most sophisticated Canadian points collectors do. Common combinations:

  • Amex Cobalt + TD Aeroplan VI — Cobalt earns Membership Rewards at 5× on dining/grocery and transfers to Aeroplan 1:1 (with 10% bonus); TD Aeroplan VI earns directly at airline and provides free checked bag benefit
  • Scotia Gold Amex + RBC ION+ — Gold Amex earns Scene+ at 5× on grocery/dining; ION+ earns Avion at 3× on gas/transit/streaming. Two programs, optimized for different categories
  • WestJet RBC WE MC + Rogers Red — WestJet card for WestJet-specific spending and the companion voucher; Rogers Red as a daily driver for all other purchases

The key is keeping the number of programs manageable. Running 3+ distinct programs simultaneously creates complexity that erodes the value of any single program. Most people maximize with 1–2 focused programs.

Amex Membership Rewards — The Bridge Program

One program not yet discussed is Amex Membership Rewards (MR) — the points currency earned on Amex Cobalt, Gold, and Platinum cards. MR is not a standalone redemption program but a transfer hub: you accumulate MR points and transfer them to Aeroplan, British Airways Avios, or other partners at a 1:1 ratio (with a 10% bonus to Aeroplan). This makes Amex cards uniquely powerful — you earn at high rates (5× on dining/grocery with Cobalt) and transfer to your preferred destination program when ready to book.

If you're deciding between Aeroplan-focused and MR-focused strategies, consider: do you want to lock in Aeroplan points year-round (TD/CIBC Aeroplan cards), or accumulate flexible MR and transfer to Aeroplan in batches (Amex Cobalt)? Both work. Many use both simultaneously.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I transfer Aeroplan points to WestJet or vice versa?

No. Aeroplan and WestJet Rewards are completely separate programs with no transfer path between them. Points in each program can only be redeemed within that ecosystem. If you accumulate points in both programs, treat them as separate budgets for different airlines.

Do Aeroplan points expire?

Aeroplan points expire after 18 months of inactivity. Any earn or redemption activity resets the clock. This is an important consideration — keep the account active with occasional purchases (even a small Starbucks purchase linked to Aeroplan) to preserve your balance.

Is WestJet Dollars better than Aeroplan for short-haul Canadian flights?

For simple short-haul Canadian flying (e.g., Toronto–Calgary), WestJet Dollars often provide equivalent or better value at lower complexity. A $400 flight costs 400 WestJet Dollars (simple 1:1). Aeroplan's equivalent redemption would be around 10,000–15,000 miles for the same flight — at 1–1.5¢/mile, comparable in dollar value but more complex to book and manage.

What's the best way to earn Aeroplan points from everyday spending?

The Amex Cobalt earns 5 MR pts/$ on dining and groceries, transferable to Aeroplan at 1:1 (plus 10% bonus). This is effectively 5.5 Aeroplan miles per dollar on restaurant and grocery spending — the highest rate available. Pair with a TD or CIBC Aeroplan card for non-bonus categories.
